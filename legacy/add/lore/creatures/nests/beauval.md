## Overview

**ZooParc de Beauval** was one of the largest and most prestigious zoos in Europe before The Silence. Home to 35,000 animals across 800 species, it featured:

- The only giant pandas in France
- Extensive great ape collection (gorillas, orangutans, chimpanzees)
- Large African savanna habitat (lions, elephants, giraffes)
- Tropical bird paradise
- Extensive reptile and aquarium facilities

On October 14, 2034, the zookeepers died. The animals did not.

## The First Months

### Week 1-4

Most enclosures held. Animals with food reserves survived. Those dependent on daily feeding began to weaken.

The primates figured it out first.

### Month 2-3

Fences designed for normal animals failed against increasingly desperate ones. The gorillas broke through first — not from strength, but from problem-solving. They watched, they learned, they adapted.

The lions followed the gorillas.

### Month 4-6

By the sixth month, Beauval was no longer a zoo. It was an open ecosystem. Predators hunted prey. Prey learned to hide. Everything learned to be quiet.

The Hush-affected neural disruption was already triggering adaptations.

## Known Mutations (Year 10 AS)

### The Pandas

The giant pandas were the first major surprise.

**Pre-Silence:** Gentle bamboo eaters, famously reluctant to breed.

**Post-Silence:** Something changed. The breeding reluctance vanished. The diet... expanded.

| Trait | Pre-Silence | Year 10 AS |
|-------|-------------|------------|
| Diet | 99% bamboo | Omnivorous (opportunistic) |
| Breeding | Notoriously difficult | Aggressive reproduction |
| Size | 100-150kg | 180-250kg |
| Temperament | Docile | Territorial |
| Coloration | Black and white | Black, white, and *something else* |

The "something else" is a reddish tint to the white fur. Expedition teams call them **Pandas Rouges** — a bad pun that stuck.

They are no longer cute.

**Threat Level:** HIGH — Deceptively fast, surprisingly aggressive, and they've learned to hunt cooperatively.

---

### The Gorilla Problem

The Beauval gorillas were always intelligent. Post-Silence, they became *frighteningly* intelligent.

The silverback Asato — the one Jonathan Reeves cried in front of — survived the initial months by organizing his troop. They secured food. They established territory. They developed what can only be described as tactics.

**Current Status:**
- Population: ~40 (from original 8)
- Territory: Northern section of former zoo + surrounding forest
- Behavior: Tool use, coordinated hunting, possible language development
- Interaction with humans: **Avoidance recommended**

The gorillas don't attack humans on sight. They observe. They assess. They remember.

This is somehow more terrifying.

**Threat Level:** EXTREME — Not because they're aggressive, but because they're smart.

---

### The Savanna Problem

Lions, elephants, giraffes, zebras — the African savanna exhibit was designed to simulate natural ecosystem dynamics.

Post-Silence, it achieved that goal in unexpected ways.

**Lions:**
- Pack size increased (12-15 vs. original 3)
- Hunting behavior adapted to new prey (including other zoo animals)
- Developed tolerance for Loire Valley climate
- Manes have become darker, almost black

**Elephants:**
- Survived by breaking out early and finding water
- Roam freely in southern Loire region
- Extremely aggressive when surprised
- Use infrasound communication at ranges that attract other creatures

**Giraffes:**
- Did not survive. Too specialized.

**Zebras:**
- Interbred with escaped domestic horses
- Created "striped horse" hybrids
- Faster than either parent species
- Very skittish, excellent early-warning system for predators

---

### The Aquarium Problem

Beauval's extensive aquarium facilities were... a mistake.

When the filtration systems failed, most fish died. But some tanks were connected to natural water systems. Some species escaped into the Cher river.

**Confirmed escapees:**
- Piranhas (somehow thriving in Loire tributary)
- Various catfish species (grew *significantly*)
- Unclear number of other species

**Rumored:**
- A crocodile. No confirmation. Probably fine.

---

### The Bird Paradox

The tropical bird exhibits were open-air. The birds simply... left.

Most died in the unfamiliar climate. But some species adapted. And some species bred with local birds.

The result: Loire Valley now has **parrots**.

Not many. Not dangerous. Just deeply weird.

They mimic sounds. Including, occasionally, human speech patterns they heard before The Silence.

Expedition teams report hearing "Hello?" and "Bonjour!" from the treetops near Beauval.

It's extremely unsettling.

## Expedition Notes

### Year 3 AS — First Contact

Initial expedition discovered the zoo had "opened itself." Found evidence of:
- Mass animal escape
- Predator-prey dynamics already established
- Signs of unusual animal behavior

Mission aborted after lion encounter. Two casualties.

### Year 5 AS — Survey Attempt

Aerial observation (climbing nearby water tower) revealed:
- Gorilla territory well-established
- Lions hunting in larger packs than normal
- "Large dark shapes" in meadow area (elephants)
- No human survivors or immune individuals

### Year 8 AS — Resource Assessment

Beauval facilities contain potentially valuable resources:
- Veterinary supplies
- Scientific equipment
- Metal and construction materials

**Retrieval assessment:** Not worth the risk.

## The Irony

In April 2031, Jonathan Reeves stood in Beauval Zoo, crying in front of the gorilla enclosure, thinking:

> "I know something you don't. I know the world is going to change. I know you'll survive — but into what?"

The answer: into this.

Asato's descendants rule a territory larger than the original zoo. The pandas Jonathan worried about have become apex predators in their own right. The animals "inherited a quieter world" — and they made it theirs.

Jonathan wanted them to thrive.

They did.

Just not the way he imagined.
