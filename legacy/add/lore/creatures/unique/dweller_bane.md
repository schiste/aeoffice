## Biological Profile

### Physical Characteristics

| Attribute | Standard Ratroach | Dweller Bane |
|-----------|-------------------|--------------|
| **Length (body)** | 30-40 cm | ~50 cm |
| **Length (tail)** | 15-25 cm (segmented) | ~12 cm (partially missing; doesn't want to talk about it) |
| **Weight** | 0.8-1.5 kg | ~2.8 kg |
| **Coloration** | Dark brown, chitinous patches | Pale grey-brown; patchy fur over dulled chitin |
| **Distinguishing Features** | Rat-roach hybrid traits | Clouded eyes (blind), vestigial antennae (deaf), missing tail segment, pronounced limp (left rear leg), segmented back plates, attitude |

### The Look

Dweller Bane looks like God started making a rat, got distracted, finished with cockroach parts, and then left it in a drawer for three decades.

His body is the worst of both worlds: patches of thin, grey fur alternate with dulled chitinous segments along his back and flanks. His face is mostly rat — the snout, the whiskers — but his back legs have that unsettling segmented quality, and there are vestigial antennae where his ears used to work. They twitch sometimes. It's deeply unpleasant to watch.

His eyes have gone completely milky. His chitin has lost its sheen. He moves with the slow, deliberate gait of something that has outlived everything that ever tried to kill it, knows it, and is vaguely annoyed about still being here.

**The Ratroach Question:**
> "Is it more rat or more roach?"
> — Everyone who sees one for the first time
>
> "Yes."
> — Everyone who's been here a while

### Morphology Details

**Body Type:** Quadruped (rodent-insectoid hybrid)

**Integument:**
- Type: Fur and chitin (hybrid)
- Texture: Patchy fur over segmented chitinous plates; rough, dulled with age
- Special Properties: Chitin provides natural armor on back and flanks; old scar tissue has reinforced weak points; distressingly crunchy if stepped on (don't)

**Thermoregulation:**
- [x] Warm-blooded (endothermic)

**Limbs & Locomotion:**
- Primary movement: Walking (slow, deliberate)
- Speed: Leisurely (1) — you could moonwalk away from this
- Special: Pronounced limp doesn't actually slow him down as much as you'd expect; he's had decades to adapt

**Dentition & Claws:**
- Teeth: Still sharp; ratroach incisors never stop growing, and he's had plenty of time to grow them
- Claws: Worn but functional; primarily used for gripping, not combat
- Weapons: Those teeth are his only real weapon, and he's not afraid to use them

## Sensory Systems

### The Sound Question

Dweller Bane has achieved what The Silent only dream of: he literally cannot hear The Silence. Whether this is ironic mercy or cosmic joke depends on your perspective.

**Hearing:**

| Attribute | Rating | Notes |
|-----------|--------|-------|
| Range (Hz) | 0 Hz | Completely deaf |
| Sensitivity | None | Ears are decorative at this point |
| Directional | N/A | Can't direct what doesn't exist |
| Vibration Detection | **Exceptional** | Compensatory adaptation; feels footsteps through floor |

**Sound Behavior Classification:**
- [x] **The Deaf** — Lost hearing entirely; hunts by other senses
- Note: His deafness predates most documentation; unclear if it's mutation, injury, or age

**Reaction to Common Sounds:**

| Sound Type | Reaction |
|------------|----------|
| Human speech | Cannot hear; ignores |
| Drums/rhythm | Cannot hear; might feel vibration if very close |
| Crystal resonance | Cannot hear; no reaction observed |
| Silence (prolonged) | His natural state; comfortable |
| Sudden loud noise | Cannot hear; but feels air pressure changes |

**How He "Sees":**

Dweller Bane navigates primarily through:
1. **Vibration sensing** — Feels footsteps, movement, even heartbeats if close enough
2. **Smell** — His sense of smell is extraordinary, even for a rat
3. **Whisker mapping** — Uses whiskers to build spatial awareness
4. **Memory** — He's lived in these tunnels for decades; knows every stone

### Other Senses

**Vision:**
- Quality: Blind (0)
- Adaptations: None needed; he's moved past vision as a concept
- The eyes remain because removing them seemed like too much effort

**Smell:**
- Quality: Excellent (enhanced through compensatory adaptation)
- Can detect: Food, water, other rats, humans, fear (allegedly), "people who are about to do something stupid"

**Vibration Sensitivity:**
- Can feel footsteps from ~10 meters through stone
- Can distinguish between human gaits, rat movement, and "someone trying to sneak"
- This is how he always knows you're coming

## Ecology & Behavior

### Diet & Feeding

**Classification:**
- [x] Omnivore
- [x] Scavenger

**Primary Food Sources:**
1. Mushroom scraps from nearby farms
2. Dropped food from settlers (he knows the schedules)
3. Cave insects and invertebrates
4. Whatever he wants, honestly; who's going to stop him?

**Hunting/Foraging Method:**
He doesn't hunt. He waits. Food comes to his territory eventually. He's been doing this for decades. He has patience you cannot comprehend.

**Will Eat Humans?**
- [ ] No — not prey
- Note: He will absolutely *bite* humans who disturb him, but it's personal, not nutritional

### Habitat & Distribution

**Preferred Environment:**
- [x] Underground/Caves
- [x] Abandoned structures (recording studios, apparently)

**Geographic Range:**
- Common: Storage Room B, Studio Echo
- Uncommon: Adjacent rooms when foraging; the old vocal booth; under the mixing desk
- Absent: Anywhere the Crystal's hum is too strong; he prefers the quieter corners

**Territory Size:** Approximately 40 square meters of storage room + adjacent crawlspaces

**Shelter:**
- Type: Nest (elaborate, decades in the making)
- Location: Inside a gutted amplifier cabinet from the studio's pre-Silence days; accessible only through a gap in the back panel
- Description: A surprisingly comfortable arrangement of stolen cloth, shredded acoustic foam, and what appears to be a 2029 tour t-shirt for a band no one remembers

### Reproduction

Not applicable. Dweller Bane has outlived all potential mates and shows no interest in the younger generation. He is the end of his particular line, and he seems fine with that.

Locals joke that he's "too mean to die and too grumpy to breed."

### Social Structure

- [x] Solitary

**Group Terminology:** N/A — there is only one Dweller Bane

**Hierarchy:** He is the undisputed master of Storage Room B. Other ratroaches avoid his territory. He has earned this through a combination of longevity, territorial aggression, and what can only be described as "sheer bloody-mindedness."

- Slow
- Can't see
- Can't hear
- Old

## Origin & History

### The Mystery of His Age

Ratroaches don't live 30-40 years. Regular ones might reach 8 years in stable conditions — their chitinous armor and hybrid resilience give them an edge over pure rats. But 30+ years is absurd even by post-Silence standards.

Dweller Bane is at least 30. Possibly older. Possibly *much* older. The chitin doesn't help with dating — it just makes him look like a piece of particularly resentful furniture.

**Theories:**
1. **Hush-related mutation** — The same virus that changed everything else may have altered his aging process
2. **Crystal proximity** — The studio sits on a Crystal; long-term resonance exposure may have effects
3. **He's simply too stubborn to die** — The leading theory
4. **He was here before The Silence** — Impossible. Probably. Almost certainly. ...Right?

### Known History

**Pre-Year 300 AS — Unknown:**
No one knows how long Dweller Bane has lived in Studio Echo. The building was abandoned for 266 years. When the Hero arrives in Year 300 AS, there he is — already ancient, already blind, already deaf, already annoyed at being disturbed.

The studio's records (water-damaged, partial) mention "vermin in the walls" as early as 2031. This is almost certainly different vermin. Almost certainly. The ratroach mutation hadn't even happened yet. ...Probably.

**Year 300 AS, Day 1 — First Contact:**
When the Hero first enters Studio Echo to claim the Crystal, Dweller Bane is encountered in Storage Room B. He's not hostile. He's not friendly. He's just *there*, like he's always been there, like he'll always be there.

The Hero's first choice in the post-apocalypse: deal with this rat, or leave him alone.

Most choose to leave him alone. The ones who don't learn why the others did.

**Year 300 AS, Day 1 — The Naming:**
The Hero (or an early settler) names him "Dweller Bane" — a creature that has been the bane of whoever dwelt here before. The name sticks. It fits.

### Why He Matters

Dweller Bane is the **first creature** the Hero encounters. Before the mutated wildlife, before the faction conflicts, before the weight of 300 years of post-apocalyptic history — there's this rat.

He's a tutorial. He's a tone-setter. He's a choice.

**The Fallout:**
> *Every vault dweller remembers their first radroach. Every Studio Echo settler remembers Dweller Bane.*
>
> *He's not dangerous. He's not important. He's just... there. Waiting. Judging.*
>
> *Welcome to the apocalypse. Mind the ratroach.*

## Cultural Significance

### In Studio Echo

**Common Names/Slang:**
- "Dweller Bane" — The name that stuck
- "Le Fléau" — Shortened French version
- "That Bastard Rat" — Affectionate
- "Grandpa" — Used by settlers who've accepted him
- "The Original Resident" — He was here first, after all

**Idioms/Sayings (emerging):**
> "Stubborn as Dweller Bane" — Meaning: Impossibly persistent; won't give up even when it makes no sense

> "He's been here longer than Dweller Bane" — Meaning: Sarcastic way of saying someone's been somewhere a very long time (impossible to actually achieve)

> "Even Dweller Bane wouldn't eat that" — Meaning: The food is truly terrible

**Folklore/Myths:**
As Studio Echo grows, stories about Dweller Bane will grow too. Some will claim he was the studio's original mascot, kept by musicians in the Before. Others will insist he's somehow connected to the Crystal — a guardian, or a warning.

The water-damaged 2031 reference to "vermin in the walls" will become legend. Was it him? *Could* it be him? The math doesn't work. The mutation timeline doesn't work. But neither does a 40-year-old ratroach, and here we are.

### Utility

- [ ] Edible — Technically yes, but no one has ever been hungry enough to try, and the social consequences would be severe
- [ ] Materials — Nothing useful
- [ ] Domesticable — He has domesticated the *settlement*, not the other way around
- [x] Trainable — In the sense that *you* can be trained to avoid his territory

**Actual utility:** Pest indicator. If Dweller Bane is acting strangely (moving from his usual territory, refusing food), something is wrong in the tunnels. He's functioned as an informal early warning system twice — once for a tunnel collapse (Year 283) and once for a gas pocket (Year 294).

### The Irony

The Sounding Five worried about protecting animals. They calibrated Hush to spare them.

Dweller Bane can't hear. He can't see. He lives in eternal silence and darkness.

He's also the happiest, most well-adapted creature in the cave system. He has no predators, abundant food, a comfortable nest, and absolutely no idea that the world ended.

Sometimes survival looks like this: old, blind, deaf, and completely unbothered.

## Player Encounter Notes

### First Encounter Design

Dweller Bane is intended as the **first creature** a new player encounters — the radroach moment, the tone-setter, the "welcome to the apocalypse" handshake. He serves several purposes:

1. **Introduction to creature mechanics** — Low threat, but demonstrates that creatures exist and have behaviors
2. **Tutorial in observation** — Players who watch him learn about vibration sensitivity, territory, feeding patterns
3. **Tone-setting** — He's absurd, he's memorable, and he's not particularly dangerous
4. **Your home has history** — Even in "your" space, things were here before you

### Encounter Trigger

Dweller Bane is encountered in Storage Room B of Studio Echo when the Hero:
- First explores the studio after claiming the Crystal
- Enters the storage room looking for supplies or equipment
- Makes too much noise or vibration near his amplifier cabinet nest

He's not blocking anything critical. He's just... there. Existing. Judging.

### Behavior During Encounter

- If player is quiet and doesn't approach the amplifier cabinet: **Ignores them completely**
- If player makes significant vibration: **Emerges, sniffs air, gives a look of profound disappointment, relocates**
- If player approaches his nest: **Defensive posture, warning squeaks (he can't hear them but muscle memory persists), will bite if touched**
- If player attempts capture/removal: **Escape via pre-planned routes through the walls; he's had 266 years to map this building**

### Rewards for Leaving Him Alone

- He stays out of your way (mutual respect)
- Later settlers find him charming; leaving him be becomes tradition
- Optional: Leaving food offerings improves his disposition; he might eventually tolerate your presence
- Easter egg: If you leave him alone for long enough, he'll eventually relocate to a less-trafficked area on his own

### Rewards for... Not Leaving Him Alone

- Rat bite (minor damage, infection risk if untreated)
- He relocates somewhere more annoying (the kitchen storage, probably)
- A story you'll be telling for years
- The knowledge that your first act in your new settlement was picking a fight with a blind, deaf, elderly ratroach — and losing

## Quick Identification Guide

### "Is That Dweller Bane?" Checklist

- [ ] Storage Room B in Studio Echo (or adjacent spaces)
- [ ] Unusually large for a ratroach (~50cm body length)
- [ ] Pale grey-brown, patchy fur over dulled chitinous segments
- [ ] Completely milky/clouded eyes
- [ ] Vestigial antennae where ears should be (twitch occasionally)
- [ ] Pronounced limp (left rear leg)
- [ ] Missing portion of tail
- [ ] Moving slowly and deliberately with "I was here first" energy
- [ ] Did not react to sound at all
- [ ] Gave you a look that made you feel judged despite being blind

### Similar Creatures (Avoid Confusion)

| Creature | Key Difference |
|---------|----------------|
| Standard ratroach | Much smaller (30-40cm), reacts to sound, has functional eyes, runs away, chitin is shinier |
| Young ratroach | Moves quickly, has functional senses, still has survival instincts |
| Regular rat | No chitin, no segmentation, no vestigial antennae, looks like a normal rat because it is one |
| Large roach | No fur, no mammalian features, doesn't give you an existential crisis |

### Warning Signs You're in His Territory

- Nest material visible in the old amplifier cabinet
- Faint smell of "old ratroach" (distinctive, musty, slightly chitinous, unforgettable)
- Small droppings near the mixing consoles (he has routes)
- That feeling of being watched despite knowing he can't see you
