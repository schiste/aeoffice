## The Problem

When The Silence fell, approximately 0.003% of humans proved immune to Hush's effects. Scattered across the globe, these survivors could still hear normally, still process sound, still function.

They all died anyway.

Without communication, without infrastructure, without medicine, without each other — the immune survivors fell one by one to:
- Disease (no hospitals, no medicine, no diagnosis)
- Other survivors (desperate, paranoid, competing for resources)
- The mutating fauna (which didn't care about immunity)
- Starvation (supply chains don't survive apocalypse)
- Accidents (no one to help when you fall)
- Age (time remains undefeated)
- Loneliness (a slower death, but death nonetheless)

By Year 42 AS, the last immune human died.

The problem is: **everyone claims their Last Human was THE Last Human.**

## The Scholarly Debate

In Year 287 AS, the Acoustic Engineers compiled a "definitive" list of Last Humans from oral histories, written records, and archaeological evidence.

They found **47 documented "Last Humans"** across Europe alone.

**Year 300 AS Update:** Following the discovery of The Witness's journals (Year 297), The Cartographer's maps (Year 298), and The Bernard Watcher's logs (Year 299), the count has been revised upward to **67 documented "Last Humans"** in France alone, with another 60+ across the rest of Europe. The supplementary archives contain an additional 20 cases from the "Middle Years" (Years 11-20 AS) and 10 documented "Special Cases" involving disputed, fraudulent, or philosophically problematic claims.

The Choir of Resonance disputes this number. They claim 94.

The Silent Watchers refuse to participate in the debate on principle. (Though they quietly published a paper suggesting the number is actually 112.)

The Archive has begun maintaining a separate index of "Special Cases" — the disputed, fraudulent, technical, and philosophical claims that don't fit standard categories. These include regional champions ("Last Human of the 5th Arrondissement"), collective claims ("The Mutual Last Humans Society"), and retroactive designations.

The Nomads have their own list and it's different from everyone else's. And ours. And theirs from last year.

### The Years 31-42 AS Problem

The final decade saw the highest concentration of "Last Human" claims. Archaeological evidence suggests:
- Year 31: ~40 immune survivors remained in France
- Year 35: ~15 remained
- Year 38: ~8 remained
- Year 40: ~6 remained
- Year 41: 3-4 remained (Bernard, Simone, The Bernard Watcher, possibly Almost-Bernard)
- Year 42: 2 remained (Simone, possibly the Corsican)

As The Philosopher noted: "The closer they got to actually being last, the more certain they became. And somehow, still wrong."

## Documented "Last Humans" (France)

### Year 1 AS: The Optimist of Marseille

**Location:** Marseille harbor district
**Full name:** Luc-Espérance Ferrier-Bonheur (his parents were optimists too)
**Occupation (pre-Silence):** Dockworker, amateur graffiti artist, self-proclaimed "vibes consultant"
**Claimed status:** "The Last Human (Three Weeks In)"

**Background:** Luc was born to a family of relentless optimists. His father, who lost three businesses to bankruptcy, named each failure "a learning opportunity." His mother once described a house fire as "aggressive redecorating." When The Silence fell and Luc found himself alone in a shipping container (he'd been napping), his first thought was: "Finally, some peace and quiet."

**Evidence:** Spray-painted on container ship in letters two meters tall: *"LUC = LAST HUMAN. YEAR 1. YOU'RE WELCOME, FUTURE."* Below it, in smaller text: *"P.S. — If you're reading this and you're also human, I called it first."*

**What happened:** Two days after painting his declaration, Luc heard drums from a nearby warehouse. He investigated, expecting to find "musical ghosts" (his words). Found 47 people maintaining a shift-rotation noise system using shipping containers and engine parts.

They laughed at him for six weeks. Then they made him repaint the container. His revision read: *"LUC = FIRST PERSON TO CLAIM LAST HUMAN STATUS. TECHNICALLY STILL A RECORD."*

**Notable quotes:**
- *"In my defense, it HAD been very quiet."*
- *"I didn't say I was right. I said I was first."*
- *"The 47 of you could have introduced yourselves BEFORE I climbed that container."*

**Fate:** Lived until Year 34 AS. Died of old age, surrounded by people who still called him "Last Human Luc" as a joke. His final words: *"I was optimistic, not accurate. There's a difference."*

**Legacy:** The container still exists. It's been repainted 14 times. Each painter adds their own "Last Human" claim as tradition.

**Scholarly assessment:** Excellent early example of premature declaration; foundational text for the field of "Confident Wrongness Studies"

### Year 2 AS: Margot the Methodical

**Location:** Former university library, Toulouse
**Full name:** Dr. Margot Émilie Renard-Précision (she added "Précision" to her surname after her dissertation defense; it was legally binding)
**Occupation (pre-Silence):** Chief Librarian, University of Toulouse; holder of three PhDs (Library Science, Statistics, "Being Correct")
**Claimed status:** "I have conducted a census. Population: 1. Margin of error: ±0."

**Background:** Margot was the kind of person who alphabetized her spice rack, then created a database to track usage patterns. She once returned a birthday card to her mother with corrections in red ink. When The Silence fell, she was in the rare books section, cross-referencing 16th-century marginalia. She didn't notice for three days.

Her census methodology was, by her own admission, "flawless within stated parameters." The parameters were the problem.

**Evidence:** 47-page handwritten document titled *"A Comprehensive Census of Post-Silence Human Population: Toulouse Metropolitan Area (Six Block Radius)"*. Included:
- Detailed maps of her search area (to scale)
- Statistical analysis of empty buildings
- A 12-page methodology section
- A conclusion stating she was "definitively, provably, statistically the last human"
- A bibliography (citing herself four times)

**What happened:** The day after filing her report in the library's official catalog system (she maintained it alone, for herself), 200+ survivors from the metro tunnels emerged to scavenge the library for fire fuel.

They found her census. A former statistics professor in the group graded it: *"Sample size insufficient. Search radius laughable. Methodology flawed. Conclusion unsupported. Grade: D-. See me after class. — Prof. Henri Vachon, Metro Tunnel #7"*

Margot's handwritten response, found beneath: *"Prof. Vachon died in 2031. His opinion is therefore inadmissible. — Dr. Renard-Précision"*

Someone else added: *"He's standing right here."*

Margot's final note: *"...oh."*

**Notable quotes:**
- *"My methodology was sound. Reality was the outlier."*
- *"I searched six blocks thoroughly. How was I to know humanity survived in block seven?"*
- *"The metro is not technically within my search parameters."*

**Fate:** Joined the metro survivors. Lived until Year 19 AS. Spent her final years creating a proper census — radius: 400km. She found 23 survivor communities. Her final census concluded: *"Population: approximately 4,000. Margin of error: ±significant. I was very, very wrong. — Dr. Renard-Précision (Humbled)"*

**Legacy:** Her original census is displayed in the Archive with the grading intact. The phrase "within stated parameters" is now academic shorthand for "technically correct but completely useless."

**Scholarly assessment:** Academic rigor does not prevent embarrassment; proof that methodology is only as good as its assumptions

### Year 3 AS: The Cousins of Carcassonne

**Location:** Medieval fortress, Carcassonne
**Full names:**
- Étienne Romulus Dubois-Têtu (the "Têtu" means "stubborn" — it was a family trait)
- Isabelle Rema Dubois-Têtu (same surname, same stubbornness, distant enough relation that family reunions were "technically optional")

**Occupation (pre-Silence):**
- Étienne: Medieval reenactment enthusiast, professional grudge-holder, accountant
- Isabelle: Medieval reenactment enthusiast, amateur grudge-holder, also accountant (different firm; they didn't speak about this either)

**Claimed status:** Both claimed simultaneously; neither acknowledged the other's existence for five months despite living in same fortress

**Background:** The Dubois-Têtu family had been feuding since 1847 over a disputed inheritance involving a goat. No one remembered which goat or what the dispute was, but the grudge remained sacred. Étienne and Isabelle were second cousins who had met exactly twice: at a funeral (they sat on opposite sides) and at a wedding (they left through different exits).

When The Silence fell, both independently decided that Carcassonne's medieval fortress was the obvious survival choice. Both arrived within hours of each other. Both saw the other from a distance. Both pretended they hadn't.

**Evidence:**
- North tower, carved in Gothic script: *"LAST MAN — ÉTIENNE ROMULUS DUBOIS-TÊTU. HERE SINCE DAY 1. DO NOT DISTURB."*
- South tower, carved in identical Gothic script: *"LAST WOMAN — ISABELLE REMA DUBOIS-TÊTU. HERE SINCE DAY 1. THE NORTH TOWER IS HAUNTED, IGNORE IT."*

**What happened:** For five months, they maintained elaborate schedules to avoid each other. Étienne scavenged Mondays, Wednesdays, Fridays. Isabelle scavenged Tuesdays, Thursdays, Saturdays. Sundays they both stayed inside, seething.

Then a Drum Tribe arrived.

**The exchange:**
- Drum Tribe Leader: "We found two people. Both claim to be the last human."
- Étienne: "I am. She's in the south wing."
- Isabelle: "I am. He's in the north wing."
- Drum Tribe Leader: "You know each other?"
- Both: "No."
- Drum Tribe Leader: "You have the same surname."
- Both: "Coincidence."
- Drum Tribe Leader: "It's a 200-meter walk between your towers."
- Both: "We like our privacy."
- Drum Tribe Leader: "Have you considered... talking?"
- Étienne: "To HER?"
- Isabelle: "To HIM?"
- Both: "Absolutely not."

**Notable quotes:**
- *"The north tower is a separate sovereign territory."* — Isabelle
- *"I acknowledge no claims south of the courtyard."* — Étienne
- *"We're not ignoring each other. We're respecting boundaries."* — Both, separately, when asked

**Fate:** Both joined the Drum Tribe. Both requested "opposite ends of the caravan." They maintained their silence for another 12 years. In Year 15, during a creature attack, Étienne saved Isabelle's life. Her response: "I suppose I owe you a debt. I'll acknowledge your existence on Tuesdays."

They died within a week of each other in Year 31 AS. Their graves are on opposite sides of the cemetery. The headstones face away from each other.

**Legacy:** "Pulling a Carcassonne" means ignoring obvious solutions due to personal stubbornness. The fortress is now a historical site. Guides point out both towers and explain: "They could see each other's cooking fires every night. Neither ever mentioned it."

**Scholarly assessment:** Peak human stubbornness; proof that even the apocalypse cannot overcome family drama

### Year 4 AS: Philippe the Broadcaster

**Location:** Radio station NRTL, Nantes
**Full name:** Philippe Jean-Jacques Moreau-Voix (the "Voix" meant "Voice" — his great-grandfather was also a radio host)
**Occupation (pre-Silence):** Overnight DJ at NRTL ("Nantes Radio: The Loneliest Hours"), voice actor for regional advertisements, man who talked to himself professionally

**Claimed status:** "I've been broadcasting for 91 days. No response. Ergo: last human. QED. Checkmate. I rest my case."

**Background:** Philippe had been talking into microphones since age 7, when he started a "radio show" for his stuffed animals. By 2034, he'd spent 23 years on overnight radio — the 2-6 AM slot when only insomniacs and truckers listened. He was used to talking to no one. The Silence, in some ways, felt normal.

When the station's daytime staff died, Philippe didn't notice for two days. He assumed they'd quit. "Finally," he reportedly said, "I get the good time slots."

**Evidence:** 91 consecutive broadcasts, all recorded on the station's backup system:
- Day 1: *"If anyone can hear this, please respond. This is Philippe Moreau-Voix, your voice in the darkness."*
- Day 30: *"Still alone. But I've been alone on the overnight shift for years. This is different but also not."*
- Day 60: *"I've started naming the pigeons. Marcel is my favorite. He doesn't respond either."*
- Day 91: *"I am definitively the last human. My evidence: 91 days of broadcasting with zero response. Science."*

**What happened:** Philippe's radio didn't work. The Filter blocked all transmission beyond 100 meters. He was essentially doing a podcast for pigeons.

Meanwhile, in the basement: 34 survivors had been living there since Week 2, maintaining a generator for sound-based protection. They could hear Philippe through the floor. Every day. Every broadcast. Every pigeon update.

**Why they didn't go upstairs:**
- Week 1: "We should tell him."
- Week 2: "He seems happy. Let's wait."
- Week 4: "He named the pigeons. We can't interrupt that."
- Week 8: "I genuinely hate the sound of his voice now."
- Week 12: "We thought he was annoying."

**The Tragedy:** Day 93, the generator failed. The basement survivors evacuated through a back tunnel. No one told Philippe. They'd grown so accustomed to ignoring him that it didn't occur to anyone.

Philippe died on Day 94, still broadcasting. His final recorded words: *"Day 94. Still the last human. Marcel the pigeon seems concerned. I wonder if—"*

The recording cuts off. Scholars debate whether he saw something, lost power, or simply ran out of things to say for the first time in his life.

**Notable quotes:**
- *"The beautiful thing about radio is you never know if anyone's listening. Now I know. They're not."*
- *"Marcel, you're the best audience I've ever had. You don't change the station."*
- *"To the future: I was here. I talked. No one answered. Story of my career, honestly."*

**Legacy:** The station is now a memorial site. His 91 recordings are preserved in the Archive. They're used in training for "how to maintain morale in isolation" (positive example) and "why you should check basements" (cautionary tale).

The basement survivors, when interviewed decades later, expressed regret. "We should have told him. We just... really didn't want to hear his voice anymore." One added: "In our defense, you haven't heard Day 47. He did impressions for three hours."

**Scholarly assessment:** A tragedy of miscommunication; proof that annoying people are still people; the origin of the phrase "We thought he was annoying" as an excuse

### Year 5 AS: The Roadtrip Twins

**Location:** Abandoned tour bus, A7 Autoroute near Lyon
**Full names:**
- Océane Janis Joplin Fontaine (their mother was a rock fan)
- Théodore "Théo" Jim Morrison Fontaine (their father was also a rock fan; they compromised on nothing)

**Occupation (pre-Silence):**
- Océane: Travel blogger ("Océane's Oceanic Adventures" — she'd never seen an ocean)
- Théo: "Aspiring influencer," professional passenger, voted "Most Likely to Miss the Point" in secondary school

**Claimed status:** "We drove 400 kilometers. Saw nobody. We're the last two humans. Road trip!"

**Background:** The Fontaine siblings had been planning a cross-country road trip for years. The apocalypse seemed like the perfect opportunity. "No traffic," Océane reasoned. "Free gas," Théo added. Neither considered that "no people" might include "people who could help them."

They'd survived The Silence in a recording studio (their mother was a session musician; she died in the booth, mid-take). They emerged three weeks later, stole a tour bus, and headed south with zero plan.

**Evidence:** Dashboard video diary (47 minutes, recovered Year 89 AS):
- Minute 1: *"Day 1 of the End Times Road Trip! We're going to find other survivors!"*
- Minute 15: *"Still no one. Lyon was empty. Or at least the highway was."*
- Minute 30: *"Théo thinks he saw smoke. I think it was clouds. We agreed not to check."*
- Minute 45: *"Four hundred kilometers. Nobody. I think we can officially say: everyone's dead. Just us."*
- Minute 47: *"Wait, what's that sound—"*

**What happened:** Their route accidentally threaded between three major survivor settlements. They passed within 5km of a 200-person farming commune ("thought the tractors were automated"), 8km of a factory community ("assumed the smoke was a fire, didn't investigate"), and 2km of a Drum Tribe camp ("heard drums, assumed ritual haunting").

They finally parked next to an Unplugged cave entrance to sleep. Drums audible from 200 meters.

**The Exchange:**
- Océane: "What's that sound?"
- Théo: "Wind, probably."
- Océane: "Rhythmic wind?"
- Théo: "The apocalypse is weird. Let's not investigate."

They drove away the next morning. Ran out of fuel 20km later. Had to walk back.

**The Return:**
When they arrived at the cave entrance on foot, a sentry stopped them.
- Sentry: "Where did you come from?"
- Théo: "We drove past yesterday."
- Sentry: "You heard the drums?"
- Océane: "We thought it was wind."
- Sentry: "...what kind of wind is that rhythmic?"
- Théo: "We didn't want to investigate."
- Sentry: "Why not?"
- Océane: "Horror movie rules. Don't investigate strange sounds."
- Sentry: "That's... that's not how this works."

**Notable quotes:**
- *"In our defense, we weren't very smart BEFORE the apocalypse."* — Océane
- *"The road trip was successful. We found survivors. Eventually."* — Théo
- *"Horror movie rules say don't investigate drums. We were trying to survive."* — Both

**Fate:** Joined The Unplugged. Lived for 30+ years in the caves. Océane became a cartographer ("making sure no one takes the scenic route again"). Théo became a sentry ("I know what it looks like when someone's about to make a dumb decision"). Both died in Year 37 AS, within a month of each other.

**Legacy:** "Taking the scenic route" now means missing obvious solutions. Their dashboard video is shown to new Unplugged members during orientation as an example of "what not to do." The 47-minute video has been edited into a 3-minute highlight reel titled "Peak Human Obliviousness."

**Scholarly assessment:** Geographic illiteracy saves lives (eventually); proof that confidence and competence are unrelated

### Year 6 AS: Grand-père Julien

**Location:** Domaine Beaumont-Divin, Burgundy
**Full name:** Julien Bacchus Beaumont-Divin (the "Divin" was added by his grandfather, who believed wine was literally sacred)
**Age at Silence:** 73
**Occupation (pre-Silence):** Third-generation winemaker, philosopher of fermentation, professional argument-starter at dinner parties

**Claimed status:** "I have survived six years by drinking wine and shouting at birds. I am certainly the last human. The universe agrees — it keeps providing."

**Background:** The Beaumont-Divins had made wine in Burgundy since 1743. They'd survived the Revolution (by hiding in the wine cellar), two World Wars (same method), and the 2028 European Wine Crisis (they refused to acknowledge it). Julien assumed The Silence was simply "another thing the wine cellar would handle."

He was 73 when The Silence fell. He'd already been living alone on the vineyard for eight years, ever since his wife passed and his children moved to Paris ("betraying 280 years of tradition for an apartment with a balcony"). He didn't notice The Silence for a week. He assumed the lack of visitors was "finally some peace."

**Evidence:** 200+ wine bottles, all empty, all carefully labeled "PROPERTY OF THE LAST HUMAN — JULIEN B-D." He maintained a wine diary throughout:
- Year 1: *"The 2029 Pinot Noir is excellent. No one to share it with. Their loss."*
- Year 3: *"Opened the 2018 reserve. Still no visitors. Perfect."*
- Year 5: *"I've noticed the wine consumption seems higher than my drinking pace. The universe provides."*
- Year 6: *"The universe provided 47 bottles this month. Miraculous."*

**What happened:** A foraging party from a settlement 15km away had been stealing his wine for five months. Their system was elegant: sneak in at night, take 2-3 bottles, leave no trace. They assumed the vineyard was abandoned.

Julien noticed the missing wine but attributed it to "divine providence" — the universe rewarding him for surviving.

**The Discovery:**
Month 6, Julien stayed up late (the 2032 Burgundy required attention). He caught three foragers mid-heist.

- Julien: "YOU!"
- Foragers: [frozen]
- Julien: "You've been stealing my wine!"
- Forager 1: "We thought you were dead!"
- Julien: "I LABELED THEM 'PROPERTY OF THE LAST HUMAN!'"
- Forager 2: "Yes! We thought it was a memorial!"
- Julien: "The bottles were being DRUNK!"
- Forager 3: "We assumed squirrels."
- Julien: "SQUIRRELS?"
- Forager 3: "...alcoholic squirrels?"
- Julien: "You could have ASKED!"

**Notable quotes:**
- *"The universe provides. Apparently, via theft."*
- *"I've been alone for six years. The wine has been excellent company. You have been less excellent."*
- *"In my defense, who expects 47 bottles of divine providence to be THEFT?"*
- *"You thought SQUIRRELS drank a 2018 Burgundy? That's a €200 bottle. Squirrels have no palate."*

**Fate:** Julien grudgingly joined the settlement ("They've already drunk half my cellar. I might as well supervise."). He became their official winemaker and lived until Year 24 AS, dying peacefully in the new vineyard he'd established. His final words: *"The 2058 vintage isn't ready. Don't you dare open it before Year 30."*

They opened it in Year 25. It was excellent. Everyone agreed Julien would have been furious.

**Legacy:** His "Last Human Reserve" wines are still traded as artifacts. The Domaine Beaumont-Divin is now a pilgrimage site for wine enthusiasts. His phrase "the universe provides" is used sarcastically when someone discovers they've been stolen from.

**Scholarly assessment:** Proof that isolation breeds delusion; also, proof that good wine survives all circumstances

### Year 7 AS: Brother Thomas of Avignon

**Location:** Papal Palace ruins, Avignon
**Full name:** Brother Thomas Quasimodo Bellanger (he chose "Quasimodo" himself upon taking vows — "a reminder of humility and bells")
**Occupation (pre-Silence):** Benedictine monk, bell-ringer, man who genuinely enjoyed 4 AM wake-up calls

**Claimed status:** Never claimed. Others assumed. He found the assumption baffling.

**Background:** Thomas had joined the monastery at age 22 after a "religious experience" involving a church bell, three bottles of communion wine, and what he described as "a very loud conversation with God." He'd spent 31 years ringing bells, praying, and perfecting his bell-maintenance technique.

When The Silence fell, Thomas was in the bell tower (obviously). He heard the deaths below but didn't stop ringing — "The bells are for the living AND the dead. Possibly more for the dead today."

He climbed down after three hours. Found the other monks deceased. Found 2,000+ people in the catacombs beneath the palace who had survived by hiding near the resonant stone chambers. They assumed he knew about them. He assumed they'd introduce themselves eventually.

**Evidence:** Found maintaining a bell tower, ringing bells on schedule for seven years. When a separate group of survivors arrived from outside:

- Outsiders: "Are you the last human?"
- Thomas: "What? No. There are thousands of us."
- Outsiders: "WHERE?"
- Thomas: "In the catacombs. We've been here since Month 1."
- Outsiders: "You've been ringing bells for SEVEN YEARS?"
- Thomas: "Yes. For everyone."
- Outsiders: "We thought you were alone."
- Thomas: "Why would I ring bells if I was alone?"
- Outsiders: "...to signal survivors?"
- Thomas: "I WAS signaling survivors. The ones in the catacombs. Why would I signal people I didn't know existed?"
- Outsiders: "...fair point."

**Notable quotes:**
- *"The bells are my duty. I ring them for God. God hears. Everything else is secondary."*
- *"Of course there were people in the catacombs. Where else would people go during an apocalypse? That's basic."*
- *"You assumed I was alone? I was ringing bells on a schedule. Alone people don't keep schedules."*

**Fate:** Continued ringing bells until Year 28 AS, when arthritis made it impossible. Trained three successors. Died Year 31 AS, peacefully, in the bell tower. His final request: "Ring the big one. The one that sounds like God clearing His throat."

**Legacy:** "Brother Thomas logic" means being correct for reasons you didn't intend. The bell tower is still maintained. They ring the "God's throat" bell on his death anniversary.

**Scholarly assessment:** The only "Last Human" who wasn't wrong because he never claimed anything; accidental prophet of obvious truths

### Year 8 AS: "Premier Pierre"

**Location:** Wine cellar, Bordeaux region
**Full name:** Pierre Premier Pierre-Dupont (yes, really — his parents were unimaginative and the midwife was drunk)
**Occupation (pre-Silence):** Wine cave tour guide, amateur philosopher, man with an unfortunate name

**Claimed status:** "The First Last Human" (he was very proud of the wordplay)

**Background:** Pierre Premier Pierre-Dupont had spent his life explaining his name. "Yes, Pierre Pierre. No, it's not a typo. Yes, my parents knew. No, I don't know why." He'd worked in Bordeaux wine caves for 25 years, giving tours to tourists who never remembered his name but always laughed at it.

When The Silence fell, he was deep in a wine cave, mid-tour. His tourists died. He didn't. Eight years of isolation followed, during which he developed a rich philosophical framework around being "first" at something — specifically, being first to declare himself last.

**Evidence:** Carved into wine cellar wall in increasingly elaborate script: *"Je suis le dernier. Pierre. An 8."*

Below it, a longer inscription: *"Pierre Premier Pierre-Dupont, First to Be Last, Last to Be First. Let the record show I claimed it before anyone else. Even if others were also last, I was FIRST last. Priority matters."*

**Notable quotes:**
- *"My whole life, people laughed at my name. Now I'm Pierre the First Last Human. Who's laughing?"*
- *"Being last is easy. Being FIRST last? That takes initiative."*
- *"I survived eight years alone with my name. Nothing can break me now."*

**Fate:** Found by foragers in Year 9 AS. Upon learning others existed, his response: "Fine, I'm not last. But I was FIRST to be last. That still counts."

He died in Year 22 AS, having founded the "First Last Human Society" — a philosophical movement dedicated to the importance of being first at things, even incorrect things.

**Legacy:** His carving is preserved. The "First Last Human Society" still exists, though most members join ironically. His name is invoked whenever someone wants to be wrong before anyone else.

**Scholarly assessment:** Probably not actually the last. Definitely named Pierre. Definitely proud of both.

### Year 9 AS: The Three Lighthouses (The Beatles of Lastness)

**Location:** Brittany coast (Pointe du Raz, Phare d'Eckmühl, Île Vierge)
**Full names:**
- Marcel John Lennon Phare (Pointe du Raz) — his mother was English, a Beatles fanatic, and "confident he'd be famous"
- Simone-Not-That-One Paul McCartney Gardien (Phare d'Eckmühl) — she added "Not-That-One" legally in Year 15 AS to avoid confusion with The Other Simone
- Laurent George Harrison Lumière (Île Vierge) — "the quiet one" of the three, which is saying something given they never spoke

**Occupation (pre-Silence):** All three were lighthouse keepers. Brittany had a proud tradition of eccentric lighthouse keepers. These three exceeded expectations.

**Claimed status:** Each declared themselves "The Last Lighthouse Keeper" and by extension "The Last Human"

**Background:** The three lighthouses were visible to each other on clear days — a geometric triangle of stubbornness. Before The Silence, Marcel, Simone-Not-That-One, and Laurent had a "lighthouse keeper network" where they'd signal each other with lights. After The Silence, each assumed the others had died.

**Why didn't they check?**
- Marcel: "The sea is dangerous. I don't swim."
- Simone-Not-That-One: "I don't have a boat. I never needed one. The lighthouse came with supplies."
- Laurent: "I assumed they were ghosts. Ghost lights. Very common in Brittany folklore."

For nine years, they signaled into the void, unaware the others were signaling back.

**Evidence:** All three kept meticulous logs:
- Marcel's log, Year 9: *"The other lighthouses still glow. Automated systems, probably. Or ghosts. I am the last keeper."*
- Simone-Not-That-One's log, Year 9: *"Pointe du Raz is still lit. Must be electrical. The Île Vierge too. I'm the only living keeper."*
- Laurent's log, Year 9: *"The others burn. Phantom lights. I alone remain human."*

**What happened:** A fishing collective sailing the coast spotted all three lighthouses on the same day. Each had a flag flying:
- Pointe du Raz: "LAST HUMAN HERE — MARCEL"
- Phare d'Eckmühl: "NO, LAST HUMAN HERE — SIMONE (NOT THAT ONE)"
- Île Vierge: "YOU'RE BOTH WRONG — LAURENT"

**The Exchange (when brought together on the fishing boat):**
- Fisher Captain: "Can you see each other's lighthouses?"
- All three: "Yes."
- Fisher Captain: "Have you tried... visiting?"
- Marcel: "The sea is dangerous."
- Simone-Not-That-One: "I don't have a boat."
- Laurent: "I assumed they were ghosts."
- Fisher Captain: "You've been signaling each other for nine years."
- Marcel: "No, I've been signaling the void."
- Simone-Not-That-One: "Same. The void just happened to signal back."
- Laurent: "I thought it was my own light reflecting off clouds."
- Fisher Captain: "...for nine years?"
- Laurent: "Brittany has unusual weather."

**Notable quotes:**
- *"We're lighthouse keepers. We keep the light. We don't go visit other lights. That's not in the job description."* — Marcel

### Year 41 AS: Bernard the Confirmed

When The Silence fell, Bernard was auditing a small business in the Périgord countryside. The owners died. Bernard survived. His first thought: "Finally, no one to argue with."

By Year 35, he had legally added "Dernier-Homme" to his surname (he'd found a surviving government office in Bordeaux and insisted on the paperwork). By Year 40, he'd built an elaborate signage system around his cave announcing his status. By Year 41, he had met Simone five times and categorically refused to acknowledge her existence as relevant.

**Evidence:** Note found in cave, framed and prominently displayed:

*"I am the last human. Don't let anyone tell you otherwise. I met Simone but she was weird so she doesn't count. She grows vegetables, which is suspicious. She also corrects my grammar, which is unforgivable. I am the last. Bernard Napoléon Victor Hugo Charlemagne Dernier-Homme. Confirmed."*

**The Five Meetings (reconstructed from both parties' journals):**

**Meeting 1 (Year 40):** Simone discovered Bernard's cave while foraging. Bernard declared himself last human. Simone pointed out she was standing right there. Bernard: "You don't count." Simone: "Why not?" Bernard: "You're too calm about this."

**Meeting 2 (Year 40):** Simone brought vegetables as a peace offering. Bernard ate them, then declared himself the last human again. Simone: "Bernard, I grew those vegetables." Bernard: "Last humans can receive gifts from non-humans."

**Meeting 3 (Year 41):** Simone attempted a formal introduction. "I'm Simone. I'm human." Bernard: "You say that, but you're too organized. Humans aren't that organized after the apocalypse."

**Meeting 4 (Year 41):** Simone brought more vegetables and a lengthy explanation of why Bernard was wrong. Bernard's journal: *"Simone came by again. She has a theory about 'human categorization.' I stopped listening when she used the word 'epistemologically.' Definitely not human."*

**Meeting 5 (Year 41):** Their final meeting. Simone: "Bernard, I'm going to say this clearly: You are not the last human." Bernard: "I am." Simone: "I'm a human." Bernard: "Prove it." Simone: "I'm standing here. I'm speaking French. I brought you tomatoes." Bernard: "Anyone can grow tomatoes." Simone: "THAT'S WHAT HUMANS DO." Bernard: "The lady doth protest too much."

**Notable quotes:**
- *"Saw that Simone woman again. Still doesn't count."*
- *"Simone brought me vegetables. I am still the last human. The vegetables were good though."*
- *"Simone says I'm an idiot. This proves she doesn't count. Actual humans would recognize my importance."*
- *"If Simone is human, why is she so annoying? Checkmate."*
- (Final entry) *"I'm dying. I am the last human. Simone will probably outlive me, but she's weird, so it doesn't count. Tell history I was right."*

## Cultural Impact

### The "Last Human" Insult

In post-Silence culture, calling someone "a Last Human" is a mild insult meaning:
- Someone who thinks they're more important than they are
- Someone who won't stop talking about themselves
- Someone who ignores obvious evidence

**Usage:**
> "Stop being such a Last Human about this."
> "He's very Bernard about his opinions."
> "Don't pull a Three Brothers on me."
> "You're being provisional about it." (meaning: hedging like The Philosopher)
> "She's got Simone's patience." (meaning: dealing with an idiot gracefully)

### The "Last Human" Toast

At funerals and memorial gatherings, it's traditional to raise a glass and say:
> "To the last of us. Whoever that was."

Some add: "May they have been less annoying than Bernard."

### The Holidays

**Year 42 Day** (observed in Month 8) commemorates Simone's approximate death date. It's a day of remembrance for all immune survivors who couldn't survive the new world.

**Bernard Day** (one week earlier) is observed in some communities as a joke holiday. Traditional activities include:
- Claiming to be the last at something despite obvious evidence
- Bringing someone vegetables and insisting they "don't count"
- Building elaborate signs about your own importance

**The Day of Almost** (Month 7) commemorates The Witness, Almost-Bernard, and all those who came close to being last. It's considered the most bittersweet of the Last Human holidays.

### The Bernard-Simone Rivalry

The Bernard-Simone debate has spawned:
- 3 separate academic societies (pro-Bernard, pro-Simone, "They Were Both Wrong")
- Annual reenactments of their meetings (comedic)
- A persistent folk tale that they're still arguing in the afterlife
- The saying: "Like Bernard and Simone" (meaning: two people in obvious proximity who refuse to acknowledge each other's legitimacy)

**The Five Vegetable Theory:** Scholars debate whether Simone's five documented vegetable deliveries to Bernard were:
1. Genuine attempts at community
2. A long-form practical joke
3. Evidence gathering (she was documenting his ridiculousness)
4. Passive-aggressive commentary
5. All of the above

## Ongoing Research

The question "Who was the last human?" has spawned:

### Recent Discoveries (Years 295-300 AS)

**The Witness's Journals (Year 297 AS):** Changed everything and nothing. Confirmed Bernard was as ridiculous as reported. Confirmed Simone was as patient as claimed. Confirmed there were AT LEAST three people in the same valley system in Year 37-41. Resolved nothing about who was actually last.

**The Cartographer's Maps (Year 298 AS):** Proved the exponential collapse theory. Showed immune survivors clustered more than previously thought. Revealed Almost-Bernard's tragedy (30km from Bernard the entire time). Created new arguments about regional vs. global "lastness."

**The Bernard Watcher's Logs (Year 299 AS):** Pure comedy gold. No scholarly value. Everyone read them anyway. "Bernard yelled at a tree" is now a meme.

### Unsettled Questions

1. Was the Corsican candidate actually last? (Island vs. mainland debate)
2. Did the Nomads' "Year 44 François/Philippe" exist? (No evidence, but they insist)
3. Which of the Three Brothers was actually last? (Forensic analysis ongoing; each faction has a favorite)
4. Was The Philosopher correct that "lastness" is epistemologically unknowable? (Philosophy department says yes; everyone else says "stop being provisional")
5. Should The Witness count even though he never claimed to be last? (New debate emerging)

New evidence occasionally surfaces. The debate will never end.

This is considered healthy.

## A Note on "Humans" vs. "Survivors"

Technically, the Crystal-enabled survivors (the player's ancestors) are also human. But in common usage:
- **"Human"** or **"Last Human"** refers to immune survivors who died out
- **"Survivor"** or **"Resonant"** refers to Crystal-enabled descendants
- **"Pre-Silence Human"** refers to the original 8 billion

This linguistic distinction emerged around Year 100 AS and is now standard.
