## The Middle Years Problem

Years 11-20 AS represent what historians call "The Exhaustion Decade" — the period when immune survivors stopped actively searching for others and started accepting their solitude.

This makes documentation particularly tragic. Many of these "Last Humans" were within days' travel of each other. Some passed within kilometers, separated only by mistiming or bad luck.

The Archives contain 23 documented cases from this decade alone. Here are ten of the most notable.

---

## Year 11 AS: Doctor Rousseau the Methodical

**Location:** Grenoble, former medical district
**Claimed status:** "The Last Rational Human"
**Circumstances:** Dr. Amélie Rousseau, epidemiologist, spent Years 1-10 AS documenting the collapse with scientific precision. She maintained detailed logs of survival patterns, infection rates (before understanding Hush), and population estimates.

Her Year 11 conclusion, written in a leather journal found in Year 134 AS:

> *"Based on observed die-off rates and resource depletion patterns, I calculate the probability of another immune survivor in this region at 0.02%. I am, statistically speaking, the last human. I will continue documentation for the hypothetical future observer. Science demands it."*

**How claim was disproven:** Three months after writing this, a trader named Yves passed through Grenoble on a supply run. He found her journals but not her body. On the final page, he added in rough handwriting:

> *"Found your math. You're wrong. Also you left bread out and it went moldy. — Yves, Year 11 (still alive)"*

**Fate:** Dr. Rousseau's body was never found. Yves's addition sparked a centuries-long debate about whether she saw his note before she died.

**Legacy:** Her journals remain the most comprehensive early documentation of the collapse. The Acoustic Engineers consider her a patron saint. The phrase "statistically speaking" is used in her honor when making overconfident claims.

## Year 12 AS: The Lighthouse Keeper's Apprentice

**Location:** Île d'Ouessant, Brittany
**Claimed status:** "The Last Person Who Knows How Lighthouses Work"
**Circumstances:** A young woman (name unknown, called herself only "Apprentice" in her writings) maintained the lighthouse on Brittany's westernmost island for three years after The Silence. She kept meticulous records of ships that never came, signals that went unanswered, and the slow realization that no one was listening.

Her Year 12 entry:

> *"Day 1,089. No ships. No signals. The lamp still burns but no one sees it. I think I am the last person who remembers how to trim a wick properly. This feels like an absurd thing to be last at, but here we are. I'm going to the mainland tomorrow. If there's no one there, that's it. I'm calling it."*

**How claim was disproven:** She did go to the mainland. She found no one in the immediate area and returned to the island. What she didn't know: Jean-Michel of Saint-Malo (documented in Year 12 AS) was only 47 kilometers away, also scanning the coast for survivors.

They never met.

**Fate:** Died Year 14 AS. Cause unclear. Body found in lighthouse with a note: *"I kept the light on as long as I could. Two years longer than I needed to, probably. Habit."*

**Legacy:** Her lighthouse burned for another six months on stored oil before finally going dark. Some claim Jean-Michel saw the light go out and knew what it meant. Others say he was already dead by then. The timeline is disputed.

## Year 13 AS: Luc the Librarian

**Location:** Bibliothèque Sainte-Geneviève, Paris
**Claimed status:** "The Last Literate Human" (later amended to "The Last Human Who Gives a Shit About Books")
**Circumstances:** Luc Moreau, former librarian, survived in the library's basement reading room. He spent years cataloging which books survived, which were lost, and writing annotations for a future he didn't believe would come.

His claim appears as a bookmark in Volume 3 of the Encyclopédie:

> *"Year 13. If you're reading this, congratulations — you've disproven my theory that I'm the last literate human. Please continue the catalog. It's under 'M' for 'Moreau.' Don't reshelve it in the wrong place. I'm dead but I'll still be furious."*

**How claim was disproven:** The Schoolteacher of Clermont-Ferrand (documented Year 15 AS) later found and continued his catalog. She added:

> *"Luc — Your organizational system is chaos. I've started over. No hard feelings. — Mme. Dubois, Year 16, ALSO literate"*

**Fate:** Died Year 13 AS, approximately three months after writing his claim. Cause: structural collapse. A shelf fell on him. The irony was noted in The Schoolteacher's later writings.

**Legacy:** His catalog was incomplete but survived. The current Archives owe him a foundational debt. His ghost is said to haunt the stacks, reshelving books in alphabetical order.

## Year 14 AS: The Optimist

**Location:** Nice, Côte d'Azur
**Claimed status:** "Definitely Not the Last Human, But Worth Checking In Case"
**Circumstances:** A survivor known only as "Théo" (from his signature) left cheerful notes in public places across Nice for four years, hoping someone would find them and respond. They included:

- *"If you find this, I'm at the blue house on Rue de France! There's wine! — Théo, Year 10"*
- *"Update: rats got the wine. I'm at the hotel now. Third floor. — Théo, Year 11"*
- *"I saw smoke yesterday! If that's you, I'm still at the hotel! — Théo, Year 12"*
- *"The smoke was a wildfire. I'm an idiot. Still at hotel. — Théo, Year 12"*

His Year 14 note marked a tonal shift:

> *"I think I might be the last person in Nice. Maybe the coast. Maybe everywhere. This is a terrible realization to have in such a beautiful city. If you're reading this, I was wrong and I'm so, so happy about it. I'll be at the beach. — Théo"*

**How claim was disproven:** He wasn't the last. A survivor named Isabelle had been living in Cannes (32 km away) the entire time. She found his notes in Year 16 AS while scavenging Nice. By then, Théo was gone.

She added to his final note:

> *"Théo — I was in Cannes. We were so close. I'm sorry. I hope the beach was nice. — Isabelle, Year 16"*

**Fate:** Unknown. His body was never found. Some believe he went into the sea. Others think he left Nice after all. The Romantic Historians prefer the beach theory.

**Legacy:** His notes are considered the most emotionally devastating artifacts of the early collapse. Couples sometimes visit the blue house on Rue de France and leave their own notes. It's become a tradition.

## Year 15 AS: The Cartographer

**Location:** Massif Central region, mobile
**Claimed status:** "The Last Human With a Decent Sense of Direction"
**Circumstances:** A survivor named Élise spent Years 11-15 AS traveling in expanding circles from Clermont-Ferrand, mapping survivor signs and safe routes. Her stated goal: "Find everyone left."

Her Year 15 journal entry:

> *"Five years. Twelve settlements found. All dead by the time I arrived. I'm either very unlucky or very late. I've mapped 400 kilometers in every direction. I've found supplies, bodies, and notes from the dead. But no living people. I think... I think I'm done searching. I think I'm the last."*

**How claim was disproven:** She gave up one valley too soon. Literally. Over the next ridge was a settlement of four immune survivors who had banded together in Year 9 AS. They survived until Year 19 AS.

She never knew.

**Fate:** Died Year 17 AS. Cause: infected wound. Her maps survived and were found in Year 83 AS. They're still used by historians to track early survivor movements.

**Legacy:** The phrase "one valley too soon" is used in her memory. It means giving up right before success. The Cartographers' Guild considers her a martyr.

**Notable artifact:** Her final map shows her route ending 2.3 kilometers from the ridge settlement. The proximity is considered cosmically unfair.

## Year 16 AS: The Corresponder

**Location:** Strasbourg, former postal district
**Claimed status:** "The Last Human With Anyone to Write To"
**Circumstances:** A postal worker named Henri continued delivering mail for years after The Silence, even though no one was alive to receive it. He wrote letters to himself from fictional senders, created responses, and maintained a full postal route schedule.

His psychiatry is debated. His dedication is not.

His Year 16 self-sent letter:

> *"Dear Henri, I regret to inform you that you are the last human on your route. Possibly all routes. This makes you the last postman. Perhaps the last person. Please consider this letter your termination notice. Sincerely, The Universe. P.S. — You did good work."*

**How claim was disproven:** He hand-delivered this letter to an empty house. Inside that house: another survivor named Sophie, who had returned only hours after he left. She found the letter, recognized the fresh bootprints, and chased after him.

They met three streets later.

**Fate:** Henri and Sophie survived together until Year 23 AS, when Henri died of pneumonia. Sophie died Year 29 AS. They're buried together with the letter between them.

**Legacy:** Their story inspired the tradition of "last letters" — writing to someone even if you think no one will read it. Several matches have been made by people finding and responding to last letters centuries later.

**Cultural note:** "Return to sender" has a darker meaning now.

## Year 17 AS: The Philosopher

**Location:** Montpellier, university district
**Claimed status:** "The Last Human Capable of Abstract Thought"
**Circumstances:** A philosophy professor (signed documents as "Prof. Roussillon") spent years writing treatises on the meaning of being the last human. He explored epistemology, ontology, and the ethics of survival in solitude.

His Year 17 conclusion:

> *"Cogito, ergo sum. I think, therefore I am. But if I am the last, and no one remains to verify my existence, do I truly exist? Perhaps I am merely the last consciousness experiencing the memory of humanity. This seems very important. Or not important at all. I can't tell anymore."*

**How claim was disproven:** A scavenger named Baptiste found his writings in Year 18 AS. At the bottom of the page, he wrote:

> *"You exist, you pretentious asshole. I'm reading this, aren't I? Also you're wrong about Descartes. — Baptiste"*

The professor never saw Baptiste's note. But the philosopher community debates whether the act of being read, even posthumously, validates existence. It's a whole thing.

**Fate:** Died Year 17 AS, approximately two months after his final entry. Cause: starvation. He apparently forgot to eat while contemplating existence.

**Legacy:** His work is simultaneously revered and mocked. The phrase "very important or not important at all" is used when someone is overthinking something obvious.

## Year 18 AS: The Twins

**Location:** Avignon
**Claimed status:** Both claimed "The Last Human (Not Counting My Sister)"
**Circumstances:** Twin sisters (names lost, called themselves "A" and "B" in writings) survived together through Year 18 AS. They argued constantly about who would be "the last last human" when one of them inevitably died first.

Their competing notes, found side-by-side:

> *"A's note: When I die, B will be the last human. She's younger by 11 minutes. But I was born first, so technically I was the first of the last. I win on technicality."*

> *"B's note: When A dies, I'll be the last human. The 11 minutes don't count, we're the same age. But I'll outlive her because I'm smarter. She eats mushrooms she can't identify. It's only a matter of time."*

**How claim was disproven:** They both died Year 18 AS. Same week. Different causes. A: mushroom poisoning. B: fell off a ladder trying to hang a "B is the Last Human" banner.

**Fate:** Archaeologists found them in the same house, three rooms apart. The banner never got hung.

**Legacy:** The Sisters' Paradox is a common debate topic: if two people die without knowing who went first, who was "the last"? Philosophy students write papers about it. No one agrees.

**Cultural impact:** "Don't eat the mushroom" is now slang for "you're being stubborn and you're going to regret it."

## Year 19 AS: The Musician

**Location:** Lyon, Croix-Rousse district
**Claimed status:** "The Last Human Who Remembers How Music Sounded"
**Circumstances:** A violinist named Céline survived by playing music to herself in the silent world. She couldn't hear it properly through The Filter, but she played anyway — by muscle memory and vibration.

Her Year 19 diary entry:

> *"I played the Chaconne today. Bach. I remember how it's supposed to sound — the resonance, the harmony, the beauty. But all I feel is vibration now. Dead notes in dead air. I think I'm the last person who remembers music as music, not just survival noise. When I die, Bach dies again. For real this time."*

**How claim was disproven:** The Hermit of Lyon (documented Year 23 AS) was living in tunnels beneath the same district. He could feel her playing through the stone. He claimed it kept him alive — the rhythm helped him track time.

They were separated by 40 meters of limestone. They never met.

**Fate:** Céline died Year 19 AS. The Hermit's logs record: *"The music stopped today. I think someone died. I think I'm more alone now than I was yesterday."*

**Legacy:** Her violin was found in Year 89 AS during The Hermit's excavation. It's displayed in the Resonance Museum with a note: "She played. He listened. Neither knew."

**Artistic impact:** The piece "Chaconne for the Last Human" is performed annually on Year 19 Day. Musicians play to empty rooms in her honor. Some claim they can feel her listening.

## Year 20 AS: The Chronicler (Annotated)

**Location:** Toulouse
**Claimed status:** "The Last Human (I've Checked, It's Definitely Me This Time)"
**Circumstances:** A historian named Marc spent years documenting other "Last Humans" before concluding he was, himself, the last. He left an extensive index of previous claims and why they were wrong.

His Year 20 entry:

> *"I've found evidence of 14 people who claimed to be the last human. They were all wrong, obviously, because I found their notes. I've searched for two years. I've found no one living. By my own methodology, I must conclude: I am the last human. I've checked. It's definitely me this time. —Marc, Year 20"*

Directly below his note, in different handwriting:

> *"No you're not. —Julien, Year 21"*

Below that, in yet another hand:

> *"Marc — you had good methodology but bad timing. Also your handwriting is terrible. —Julien, Year 21, DEFINITELY not the last because I met Élodie in Carcassonne last month"*

**How claim was disproven:** See above. Thoroughly. Publicly. With commentary on handwriting.

**Fate:** Marc died Year 20 AS, never knowing he'd been contradicted. Julien and Élodie survived until Years 26 and 27 AS respectively. They continued the chronicle Marc started.

**Legacy:** The Annotated Chronicle is one of the Archives' most treasured documents. It contains 27 "Last Human" claims, each annotated by later survivors. The final entry (Year 42 AS, Simone) is annotated by historians in Year 156 AS with: *"Ok this one might actually be it."*

**Scholarly impact:** Marc's methodology is still taught in Archives training. His conclusion is used as a cautionary tale. The phrase "I've checked, it's definitely me this time" is academic slang for overconfidence.

## Statistical Analysis (Disputed)

Of these 10 documented "Last Humans" from Years 11-20 AS:

- **2** were within 50km of other survivors when they made their claims
- **3** gave up searching within months of potentially finding others
- **4** had their claims disproven within their lifetime (they just never found out)
- **1** had their claim disproven in writing while their corpse was probably still warm
- **7** died alone despite being wrong about being last
- **10/10** believed they were absolutely, definitely the last

The Archives conclude: "Loneliness is not evidence."

## Cultural Legacy: The Middle Years

The Years 11-20 AS "Last Humans" are considered the most tragic because they died in the gap — after the initial chaos settled but before communities formed. They could have been found. They just... weren't.

**Common sayings from this period:**

- "Don't pull a Cartographer" — giving up one step before success
- "Checking your math" — referencing Dr. Rousseau's statistical confidence
- "Writing on the wall" — leaving notes for people who'll never read them (from Luc)
- "One valley away" — being close but not close enough
- "The Musician's Gap" — being separated by something crossable but never crossing it

**The Middle Years Toast:**

Raised at gatherings, specifically for Years 11-20 victims:

> "To those who searched and stopped searching. You were never as alone as you thought. Someone was always one valley away."

## The Note Wall

In Year 198 AS, the Archives created a memorial wall listing all documented "Last Humans" from Years 11-20 AS. Visitors are encouraged to leave notes responding to their claims.

The most common note left:

> "You weren't the last. But we remember you anyway."
