# Greg the Anxious Dinosaur (Children's Book Series)

> **Quick Reference:** The bestselling children's book series about a T-Rex with anxiety disorder — accidentally became the dominant framework for understanding mental health in Year 300.
>
> **Type:** Children's picture books
> **Author:** Michelle Park-Okonkwo
> **Illustrator:** David Huang
> **Publisher:** Penguin Young Readers
> **Published:** 2026-2034 (23 books)
> **Year 300 Status:** "The Wisdom of Greg" — foundational mental health texts; Greg is considered a philosopher

---

## The Series

### Origin

**Michelle Park-Okonkwo** was a child psychologist who struggled to explain anxiety to young patients. In 2025, she doodled a worried T-Rex during a session.

**The child's response:** "Why is the dinosaur scared? He's a T-Rex."

**Michelle's answer:** "Even T-Rexes get scared sometimes. His name is Greg."

**The child:** "I like Greg. He's like me."

The first book was published the following year.

### The Concept

**Greg** is a Tyrannosaurus Rex who experiences anxiety, and the books follow his journey learning to cope.

**Why a T-Rex?**
- Children love dinosaurs
- A T-Rex "should" be scary and powerful
- Greg's anxiety contrasts with expectations
- The message: anxiety can affect anyone, even apex predators

## Cultural Impact

### Sales & Reception

**By 2034:**
- 47 million copies sold worldwide
- Translated into 38 languages
- #1 prescribed children's book by therapists (survey, 2032)
- Michelle Park-Okonkwo: "I just wanted to help kids. I didn't expect Greg to become... this."

### Professional Adoption

Therapists, schools, and hospitals adopted Greg:

**Common practices:**
- "The Greg Method" for introducing anxiety concepts to children
- Greg plush toys in therapy offices
- "Greg Says" breathing exercises in schools
- Hospital waiting rooms stocked with Greg books

### Adult Adoption

Unexpectedly, adults embraced Greg too:

**@AnxietyMemes (2029):**
> "I'm 34 and a children's book about a dinosaur articulated my experience better than any therapist."

**The Adult Greg Movement:**
- Greg merchandise for adults (subtle designs)
- Greg quotes used in adult therapy contexts
- "Greg energy" became shorthand for "acknowledging anxiety without shame"

### The Greg Philosophy

Michelle's simple language created accidentally profound statements:

| Greg Quote | Adult Application |
|------------|-------------------|
| "My brain tells me scary things. Sometimes my brain is wrong." | Cognitive distortions aren't truth |
| "Hard days still end." | Impermanence of suffering |
| "I can be scared and brave at the same time." | Courage isn't absence of fear |
| "My feelings are big but they fit inside me." | Emotional capacity |
| "Other dinosaurs feel scared too." | Universal experience of anxiety |

## Year 300 Mythology

### "The Wisdom of Greg"

The Greg books are among the most influential texts in post-Silence mental health:

| Reality | Year 300 Belief |
|---------|-----------------|
| Children's books | Philosophical treatises |
| Greg (character) | Ancient sage/philosopher |
| Michelle Park-Okonkwo | "The Scribe of Greg" |
| Anxiety disorder | "The Inner Storm" |
| Coping techniques | "Greg's Practices" |
| The T-Rex form | Proof that even the powerful struggle |

### Why Greg Survived

1. **Simple language:** Translatable across dialects
2. **Visual instructions:** David Huang's illustrations needed no text
3. **Universal themes:** Anxiety exists in every era
4. **Practical guidance:** Breathing exercises actually work
5. **Waraq printing:** All Penguin books used durable paper by 2030

### The Practices

Year 300 settlements teach "Greg's Practices":

**The Breathing:**
> "In through the tiny arms. Out through the big mouth."
(Deep breath in, slow exhale)

**The Naming:**
> "What is your brain telling you? Say it out loud. Now ask: is it true?"

**The Waiting:**
> "Hard days still end. Sit with me. We will wait together."

**The Acknowledgment:**
> "Other people feel scared too. You are not alone. You are never alone."

### Greg as Philosopher

Year 300 scholars debate Greg's "teachings":

**The Bresme School:** "Greg teaches acceptance. Feel the fear. Continue anyway."

**The Lyon School:** "Greg teaches community. We can be scared together."

**The Tarn School:** "Greg teaches patience. Hard days still end."

**All agree:** "Greg was wise. Greg understood the human condition. That Greg was a dinosaur is irrelevant."

## Quest Hooks

### The Complete Collection

**Hook:** A settlement has 22 of 23 Greg books. They desperately want to find the missing one: *Greg's Panic Attack*.

**Reality:** The book exists somewhere. It contains specific guidance for acute anxiety.

**Implications:** Finding it would be genuinely helpful. The Hero might need to search ruins, trade with other settlements, or discover it's been misattributed.

### The Scribe's Testimony

**Hook:** A fragment of Michelle Park-Okonkwo's interview surfaces. She said something about Greg that scholars can't interpret.

**Reality:** She probably said "He's just a character I made up." This would challenge the Greg-as-philosopher belief.

**Implications:** Does the Hero share this? Does it matter that Greg was fiction if the practices work?

### The Greg Schism

**Hook:** Two settlements disagree on Greg's core teaching. One emphasizes acceptance; one emphasizes community. Tensions are rising.

**Reality:** Greg taught both. The books are complementary, not contradictory.

**Implications:** The Hero might need to find books from both "schools" and demonstrate they're part of one series.
