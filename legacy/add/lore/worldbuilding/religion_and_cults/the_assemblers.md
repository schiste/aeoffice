# The Assemblers

> **Quick Reference:** The world's fastest-growing Year 300 religion, based entirely on IKEA assembly instructions and Dr. Astrid Lindblom's satirical book — she wrote it as a joke. They know. They don't care.
>
> **Type:** Religious movement
> **Founded:** Approximately Year 60 AS (formal organization)
> **Sacred Texts:** *The IKEA Prophecies*, assembly manuals, *I Was Joking About the IKEA Prophecies*
> **Adherents (Year 300):** ~15,000 across European settlements
> **Symbol:** The Allen Key (L-shaped, representing adaptation)

---

## Origins

### The Rediscovery

**Year 43 AS:** A survivor in the Lyon region discovered a warehouse containing:
- 200+ IKEA flat-pack furniture boxes (unopened)
- A waterlogged but readable copy of *The IKEA Prophecies*
- Multiple Allen keys

**Her name:** Mathilde Rousseau (no relation to the Lyon settlement founder)

**Her reaction:** "These instructions are... beautiful. Look at how they communicate without words. This is how we should write."

### The First Assembly

Mathilde began assembling furniture from the boxes. As she worked, she read *The IKEA Prophecies* aloud to her settlement.

**What happened:**
- People found the process meditative
- Completing furniture provided tangible accomplishment
- The book's analysis felt profound (even though it was satire)
- Mathilde's readings became weekly events

**Within 5 years:** The "Assembly Circles" had spread to three other settlements.

### The Lindblom Question

When someone found *I Was Joking About the IKEA Prophecies*, the movement faced a crisis:

**The debate:**
- Faction A: "She said it was satire. We should stop."
- Faction B: "Many prophets denied their divinity. Her denial proves her wisdom."
- Faction C: "Does it matter? The practice helps us."

**Resolution (Year 67 AS):** The Council of Assembly ruled:
> "Truth is found in the practice, not the intent. Dr. Lindblom wrote wisdom whether she meant to or not. Her denial is itself instruction: remain humble. Do not claim to know more than you know. We are all still assembling."

**Faction C won.** The movement grew.

## Beliefs

### Core Tenets

| Tenet | Meaning | Practice |
|-------|---------|----------|
| **The Assembly** | Life is components requiring assembly | Work with what you have |
| **The Instructions** | Guidance exists; seek it | Study manuals, guides, wisdom |
| **The Allen Key** | Every problem has a tool | Find the right approach |
| **The Missing Screw** | Some suffering is inevitable | Accept imperfection |
| **The Finished Product** | Purpose comes through completion | Finish what you start |
| **The Lindblom Humility** | Even the wise deny their wisdom | Don't claim certainty |

### The Sacred Numbers

From IKEA instructions:
- **4:** Number of legs on most furniture (stability)
- **6:** Standard Allen key size (the most common)
- **16:** Typical screw count in small furniture (completeness)
- **47:** Steps in the most complex preserved manual (the full journey)

### The Names

IKEA product names became mantras (with creative etymology):

| Name | "Meaning" (Assembler interpretation) |
|------|--------------------------------------|
| KALLAX | "Source" — where things begin |
| BILLY | "Protector" — shelters knowledge (books) |
| MALM | "Ore" — raw potential |
| LACK | "Absence" — simplicity, minimalism |
| POÄNG | "Point" — the present moment |
| HEMNES | "Home-ness" — belonging |

**Note:** These etymologies are wrong. KALLAX is a place name in Sweden. BILLY was named after an IKEA employee. The Assemblers know this. They use the meanings anyway.

## Practices

### The Weekly Assembly

**Every seventh day:**
1. Community gathers
2. A Reader presents a passage from *The IKEA Prophecies* or a manual
3. Discussion of meaning and application
4. Communal work: building, repairing, or maintaining
5. Sharing a meal at the completed workspace

### The Life Rituals

**Birth — The First Assembly:**
- Newborns are presented with an Allen key (symbolic)
- Their name is recorded alongside an IKEA product name
- "May your life be well-assembled. May you find your tools."

**Coming of Age — The First Build:**
- Youth must assemble a piece of furniture alone
- No help permitted
- Struggle is expected and valued
- Completion marks adulthood

**Marriage — The Joint Assembly:**
- Couple assembles furniture together
- Must complete without arguing (or arguing constructively)
- The finished piece becomes their first marital possession

**Death — The Disassembly:**
- The deceased's assembled works are distributed
- Their name is added to "The Record of Completions"
- "They assembled well. Their work remains."

### The Pilgrimage

**The Dream:** Reach Älmhult, Sweden — site of the first IKEA store.

**The Reality:** Sweden is far, dangerous, and the store is ruins.

**The Compromise:** Some Assemblers make pilgrimages to any IKEA ruins they can safely reach. A warehouse outside Lyon is the closest "holy site" for most French Assemblers.

## Hierarchy

| Title | Role | Selection |
|-------|------|-----------|
| **The Reader** | Interprets texts; leads rituals | Elected by community |
| **The Keyholder** | Maintains tools; resolves disputes | Appointed by Reader |
| **The Assembler** | Any practitioner | Self-declared |
| **The Apprentice** | Learning the practice | Paired with experienced Assembler |
| **The Disassembler** | Handles end-of-life matters | Volunteer, respected role |

### The Council of Assembly

Annual gathering of Readers from all settlements to discuss:
- Interpretation questions
- New discoveries (manuals, texts)
- Disputes between communities
- The ongoing question of Lindblom's intent (never resolved)

## Controversies

### The Crocs Schism

**The dispute:** Should Assemblers wear Crocs?

**The Crocs Devotion argument:** "Crocs are holy because they survived."

**The Assembler argument:** "Crocs come pre-made. Nothing is assembled. They violate our principles."

**Current status:** Most Assemblers refuse to wear Crocs. Some consider it apostasy. The schism is minor but real.

### The Flatpack Fundamentalists

**A hardline faction** insists that only original IKEA products can be assembled — no improvised or local-made furniture.

**Mainstream Assembler position:** "Assembly is the practice. The brand is secondary."

**Fundamentalist position:** "The manuals came from IKEA. The furniture should too."

**Current status:** Fundamentalists are a minority but maintain separate communities.

### The Lindblom Denial Debate

**Three schools of thought:**

1. **The Literalists:** "She said it was satire. We should acknowledge that and practice anyway."

2. **The Mystics:** "Her denial was the final teaching. True prophets always deny."

3. **The Pragmatists:** "Who cares? The practice works. Move on."

**The debate continues.** It's the Assemblers' equivalent of theological disputation.

## Relations With Other Beliefs

### The Bresme Caves

The Bresme community includes Assemblers, but they're a minority. Relations are cordial.

**Bresme Cave Code recognition:**
> "The Assemblers practice with integrity. Their rituals of construction and maintenance benefit all. They are welcome."

### The Lindquist Family

The Lindquists are NOT Assemblers. They have their own cult (Vinyl/Lectromusic). The two groups coexist but don't mix.

**Lindquist view of Assemblers:** "Fercute that they build things. S'diff that they worship a furniture manual."

**Assembler view of Lindquists:** "Their practice is alien to us. We wish them well in their search for The Studio."

### The Gas Priests

Some overlap exists — both groups emerged from pre-Silence "absurdist sports/literature." Occasional interfaith dialogue.

**Shared belief:** "The ancients were strange. Their strangeness produced wisdom."

## Year 300 Status

### Population

- **~15,000 practitioners** across European settlements
- **Largest communities:** Lyon region, Northern France, parts of Germany
- **Growing presence:** The Bresme caves, coastal settlements

### Current Focus

1. **Preservation:** Maintaining surviving manuals and furniture
2. **Teaching:** Spreading assembly skills (useful regardless of faith)
3. **The Pilgrimage Question:** Is reaching Älmhult possible?
4. **The Lindblom Archives:** Finding more of her writings

### The Future

The Assemblers are well-positioned for post-Silence life:
- Practical skills (building, repair)
- Community rituals that produce tangible results
- Theology that embraces uncertainty
- No claims of exclusive truth

Whether Lindblom would approve is unknown. The Assemblers believe she would appreciate the irony.
