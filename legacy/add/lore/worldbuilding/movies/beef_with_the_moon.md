## The Film

### The Premise

**Plot (such as it is):**

**Gerald** (Nicolas Cage), a municipal accountant in Dayton, Ohio, becomes convinced the moon has personally wronged him. He can't articulate how. He doesn't know why. But he knows.

The film follows Gerald's 2-year campaign to "settle the score with the moon," including:
- Staring at the moon for hours while muttering
- Writing angry letters to NASA (they respond politely; he's furious)
- Attempting to sue the moon in small claims court (dismissed)
- Starting a support group: "People Who Have Beef With The Moon" (7 members)
- Eventually traveling to Cape Canaveral to "confront the moon directly"

**The ending:** Gerald stands on a beach at night, pointing at the moon, and delivers a 9-minute monologue. The moon does not respond. Gerald says, "This isn't over." Credits roll.

**Resolution:** None. The beef continues. The film just... ends.

### The Monologue

**Gerald's final confrontation (excerpts):**

> "You've been up there my whole life. Watching. Pulling at the tides. Pulling at ME. Don't pretend you don't know what you did. New Year's Eve, 1997. I know you saw. I know you remember.
>
> And what do you do? You just hang there. Waxing. Waning. Like it's nothing. Like I'm nothing.
>
> Well, I see you too, moon. I see you every night. And I want you to know: I'm not afraid anymore. I'm not grateful for your light. I didn't ask for your phases.
>
> We're not done, you and me. We'll never be done. Because I remember. And I know — I KNOW — that somewhere in that gray, dead, beautiful face of yours... you remember too."

**The film ends with Gerald still pointing. The credits begin. There is no post-credits scene.**

## Cultural Impact

### "I Have Beef With The Moon"

The phrase entered common usage immediately:

| Usage | Meaning |
|-------|---------|
| "I have beef with the moon" | Unexplained, possibly irrational grievance |
| "That's very Gerald of you" | Obsessive focus on something others don't understand |
| "Moon-beefing" | Holding a grudge against something that cannot respond |
| "The moon doesn't care, Gerald" | Said to someone complaining about immutable circumstances |

### The Support Group

After the film, real "People Who Have Beef With The Moon" groups formed:

**Online communities:**
- r/BeefWithTheMoon (230,000 members)
- Facebook groups in multiple languages
- Discord servers dedicated to "lunar grievances"

**What they discussed:**
- Times the moon "seemed wrong"
- Full moon anxiety
- "I don't trust the far side"
- "The tides are personal, actually"

**Were they serious?** Nobody knew. Including them.

### Nicolas Cage's Campaign

Cage did not break character during the entire press tour:

**Interview excerpt (Jimmy Fallon, 2032):**
> **Fallon:** "So Nicolas, what drew you to this role?"
> **Cage:** "The moon, Jimmy. The moon drew me. It's been drawing me my whole life. Gerald and I... we understand each other. We know what the moon did."
> **Fallon:** "...What did the moon do?"
> **Cage:** *long stare at the ceiling* "You know, Jimmy. You know."

**Awards consideration:** Cage was nominated for Best Actor. He lost. He said, "The Academy is clearly in league with the moon."

### The Dayton Response

Dayton, Ohio — Gerald's hometown — embraced the film:

**City actions:**
- "Gerald Day" declared (August 15)
- Statue proposal (rejected, but close)
- Tourism boost: "Visit Dayton. We Have Beef With The Moon."
- The real small claims court where "Moon v. Gerald" was filed became a tourist attraction

## Year 300 Mythology

### "The Lunar Grievance"

The film is interpreted as documentation of an ancient war:

| Reality | Year 300 Belief |
|---------|-----------------|
| Absurdist comedy | Historical record of human-lunar conflict |
| Gerald (character) | "The Challenger" — one who dared to confront the sky |
| The 9-minute monologue | "The Grievance Speech" — sacred accusation |
| "New Year's Eve 1997" | The unknown incident that started the war |
| "We're not done" | Promise of continued resistance |

### The Beliefs

Some Year 300 communities believe humanity had genuine grievances with the moon:

**Theories:**
- The moon controlled human behavior (true: tidal influence, possibly circadian)
- The moon witnessed shameful acts and refused to forget (parallel to Crows Remember)
- The silence was connected to lunar alignments (no evidence, but compelling)
- Gerald was a prophet who saw the truth

### The Ritual

**The Lunar Address:**

Some settlements maintain a practice of "addressing grievances to the moon":

**The Process:**
1. On full moons, individuals may speak to the sky
2. They voice complaints, frustrations, things they cannot change
3. The moon does not respond
4. This is expected and accepted
5. The act of voicing is the point

**Psychological benefit:** Surprisingly effective. Externalizing frustration to a non-responsive entity provides catharsis.

### The Bresme Version

**The Bresme Cave Code mentions the moon (Article 34):**
> "The moon watches but does not answer. Some have grievances. They may speak them. The moon's silence is not indifference; it is patience. If you have beef with the moon, voice it. Then continue living."

**The Lindquists' variation:**
> "Oofda, the moon. Yabetja, I've got beef. Codworse, it never answers. But forcry, sometimes you just gotta say it out loud."

## Quest Hooks

### The 1997 Incident

**Hook:** Gerald mentioned "New Year's Eve 1997" in his monologue. A scholar obsesses: what happened that night?

**Reality:** Nothing. The screenwriters made it up. There was no incident.

**Implications:** Some mysteries have no answers. The scholar must accept ambiguity — or invent their own explanation.

### The Lunar Address

**Hook:** A settlement has stopped practicing The Lunar Address. An elder believes this has brought misfortune. They want to restore the ritual.

**Reality:** The ritual has no mystical power. But communal catharsis might have real benefits.

**Implications:** Does the Hero support "superstition" that genuinely helps people? Or insist on "truth"?

### The Challenger's Heir

**Hook:** Someone claims to be descended from "Gerald of Dayton" and wants to continue his work.

**Reality:** Gerald was a fictional character played by Nicolas Cage.

**Implications:** Faith in fictional lineage. The Hero must decide how to handle someone's sincere belief in something that wasn't real.
