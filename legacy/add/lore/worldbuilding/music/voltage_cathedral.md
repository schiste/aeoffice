## Pre-Silence Reality

### The Band

**Voltage Cathedral** was an obscure French electro duo active from approximately 2019 to 2034. They were:

- Moderately successful in the European underground electronic scene
- Known for atmospheric, bass-heavy productions
- Had a small but devoted fanbase (including one very devoted American)
- Rumored to record at Studio Echo in the Loire Valley

**Musical style:**
- French electro (in the tradition of Justice, Kavinsky, Gesaffelstein)
- Dark, atmospheric soundscapes
- Heavy bass and distorted synths
- Occasional vocals (processed beyond recognition)

**Discography (approximate):**
- *Cathédrale* (2020) — Debut EP
- *Neon Communion* (2022) — First full album
- *Black Mass Frequency* (2024) — Second album
- Various singles and remixes (2019-2034)

**Commercial success:** Limited. They had a cult following but never broke into mainstream. Their biggest streaming numbers came from a track that was used in an obscure French film.

### The Members

**Names uncertain.** The duo maintained anonymity (common in French electro), performing in masks and rarely giving interviews.

**What's known:**
- Two French men, probably from the Paris area
- Met in music school or through the club scene
- One handled production, one handled live performance
- Both died in The Silence (almost certainly)

**Kaylee's obsession:**
- She discovered them through a Spotify algorithm in 2021
- Flew to Europe three times to see them perform (2022, 2023, 2024)
- Attempted to find their real identities (unsuccessful)
- Believed they were recording at Studio Echo in October 2034
- Was camping outside the studio when The Silence began

### The Studio Echo Connection

Voltage Cathedral had reportedly booked studio time at Studio Echo in the Loire Valley. The session was listed as **10/14** — which could mean:

- October 14th (mm/dd — American format)
- 14th of October (dd/mm — European format)
- Some other date entirely

Kaylee interpreted it as October 14th and flew to France to stake out the studio. Whether the band was actually scheduled to be there is unknowable — but if they were, they never arrived.

## Year 300 Mythology

### The Gods of Lectromusic

In Lindquist family tradition, Voltage Cathedral has been elevated to divine status:

| Original | Year 300 Belief |
|----------|-----------------|
| Electro duo | Twin gods of sacred music |
| Band name | Divine title meaning "Temple of Power" |
| Their music | The lost sacred sound — "lectromusic" |
| Studio Echo | Their holy temple |
| Their masks | Divine faces, too sacred for mortals |
| Their anonymity | Proof they were not of this world |

**Core beliefs:**
- Voltage Cathedral were messengers from a higher realm
- They communicated through sacred frequencies ("lectromusic")
- The Raver was chosen to receive their teachings
- Studio Echo was their temple on Earth
- Finding the Studio would bring the family closer to the divine

### The Lindquist Pantheon

| Entity | Role | Evidence |
|--------|------|----------|
| **Voltage Cathedral** | Supreme gods of music | Kaylee's devotion |
| **The Raver (Kaylee)** | Prophet/saint who received their call | Founded the family |
| **Big Wayne** | Ancestor god who blessed the journey | Kaylee's stories |
| **The DJ** | Forbidden art — mortals attempting divine work | The DJ Incident |

### Sacred Imagery

The Lindquist family has preserved fragments of Voltage Cathedral imagery:

**What survives:**
- A faded concert poster (recovered from Kaylee's belongings)
- Screenshots on Kaylee's dead phone (inaccessible)
- Kaylee's descriptions, passed down orally
- The name itself, preserved across generations

**Year 300 interpretation:**
- The poster is a sacred icon, displayed during rituals
- The image shows "the Twin Gods in their holy masks"
- The text (in French, unreadable to most) is considered divine script
- Artists have created interpretive drawings based on descriptions

### The Music

No one in Year 300 has heard Voltage Cathedral's music. This has made it more sacred, not less.

**What the Lindquists believe "lectromusic" sounds like:**
- Some say it was "the sound of pure energy"
- Others describe "rhythms that moved the body without thought"
- Some believe it was "the voice of the gods, speaking in frequencies"
- The DJ Incident (Kaylee's failed performance) is seen as proof that only the worthy can channel it

**The reality:**
- It was pretty good French electro
- Heavy bass, atmospheric synths, processed vocals
- Nothing supernatural — just music
- Kaylee loved it because it made her feel something

## Quest Hooks

### The Revealed Sound

**Hook:** The Hero discovers playable recordings of Voltage Cathedral at Studio Echo.

**Reality:** It's just music. Good music, but just music.

**Implications:**
- The Hero can finally hear what the family worshipped
- Do they share it with the Lindquists?
- Will the reality (just music) destroy the faith?
- Or will the family find it even more meaningful when they can actually hear it?

### The Unmasking

**Hook:** Documents at Studio Echo reveal the real identities of Voltage Cathedral.

**Reality:** Two French guys named something like Jean-Pierre and Mathieu. Normal people who made electronic music.

**Implications:**
- The gods have names. Mundane names.
- This could shatter Lindquist theology
- Or it could humanize the divine: "They were like us, but touched by something greater"

### The October 14 Question

**Hook:** Studio Echo's booking records survive. Was Voltage Cathedral actually scheduled for October 14, 2034?

**Possibilities:**
1. **Yes** — They were coming, but The Silence stopped them. Kaylee's instincts were correct.
2. **No** — The session was for a different date. Kaylee misread it. Her survival was pure accident.
3. **Ambiguous** — Records are damaged. The truth remains unknown.

**Implications:**
- If yes: The Raver was truly guided to the right place
- If no: The entire Lindquist mythology is built on a date format confusion
- If ambiguous: The faith can persist, but doubt remains

### The Album That Never Was

**Hook:** Voltage Cathedral was in the middle of recording their third album when The Silence hit.

**Reality:** Partial recordings might exist somewhere — unfinished tracks, demos, works in progress.

**Implications:**
- The Hero could discover "new" sacred music
- Incomplete recordings raise questions: Is unfinished divine work still divine?
- The final track might cut off mid-song — the moment The Silence began

## The Irony

Voltage Cathedral were not gods. They were two French guys who made electronic music in masks because it was cool and because they valued privacy.

They probably:
- Argued about mixing decisions
- Had day jobs until the music paid enough
- Complained about venue sound systems
- Never knew a random American woman was obsessed with them

But to Kaylee, they represented something transcendent. And because she survived and they didn't, her feelings became scripture.

The music itself — if anyone ever hears it — is probably pretty good. Might even be great. But it's not divine. It's just art, made by people, for people.

That might be enough.
