# Extreme Apologizing (World Sorry Championships)

> **Quick Reference:** The Canadian-invented sport where competitors apologize for increasingly absurd scenarios — became an international phenomenon, got an ESPN deal, and Canada won every championship until Japan entered.
>
> **Type:** Competitive sport (performance)
> **Governing Body:** International Apology Federation (IAF)
> **Founded:** 2027 (as joke), 2029 (formalized)
> **Headquarters:** Winnipeg, Manitoba, Canada
> **Year 300 Status:** "The Rituals of Remorse" — formalized conflict resolution through ceremonial apology

---

## Origins

### The Joke

**2027, Winnipeg Comedy Festival:**

Stand-up comedian **Marcus Chicken** (real name, unfortunately) performed a bit about Canadian stereotypes:

> "Canadians apologize for everything. I apologized to a door yesterday. The door didn't accept my apology. I apologized for that too. What if we made it competitive? 'Sorry-lympics.' I'd take gold. Then apologize for winning."

**Audience reaction:** Thunderous applause
**Viral clip views:** 4.2 million
**Comment section:** "This should be real."

### The First Competition (2028)

Marcus organized a "Sorry Championship" at a Winnipeg bar.

**Rules (original):**
1. Contestants draw scenario cards
2. They must apologize for the scenario
3. Judges rate on: sincerity, creativity, emotional depth
4. Audience votes on "Most Canadian"

**Sample scenarios:**
- "Apologize for the weather"
- "Apologize for someone else stepping on YOUR foot"
- "Apologize for being too apologetic"
- "Apologize to a chair for not sitting in it"

**Winner:** Margaret "Maggie" Tremblay, a 67-year-old retired teacher who apologized to a houseplant for "not appreciating photosynthesis enough" and made three judges cry.

### Formalization (2029)

The IAF was established after the second championship drew 2,000 spectators.

**Official categories:**
| Category | Description | Time Limit |
|----------|-------------|------------|
| **Personal Remorse** | Apologizing for your own actions | 2 minutes |
| **Proxy Apology** | Apologizing on behalf of someone/something else | 2 minutes |
| **Abstract Regret** | Apologizing for concepts, phenomena, or existence | 3 minutes |
| **Speed Sorry** | Most apologies in 60 seconds | 1 minute |
| **The Gauntlet** | Increasingly absurd scenarios, no preparation | 5 minutes |

## International Expansion

### The Canadian Dynasty (2029-2032)

Canada dominated early competition:

**World Sorry Championships results:**
| Year | Gold | Silver | Bronze |
|------|------|--------|--------|
| 2029 | Canada | UK | Ireland |
| 2030 | Canada | Canada (different person) | Sweden |
| 2031 | Canada | UK | Canada |
| 2032 | Canada | Finland | Canada |

**Canadian training secrets:**
- Born apologizing
- Cultural immersion since birth
- "We don't train. We just exist."

### Japan Enters (2033)

Japan sent a delegation in 2033 and changed everything.

**Japanese approach:**
- Formal apology traditions (dogeza, bowing)
- Corporate apology culture (refined over decades)
- Precision timing
- Emotional control
- The element of surprise: tears on command

**2033 World Sorry Championships:**
| Event | Winner | Nationality |
|-------|--------|-------------|
| Personal Remorse | Yuki Tanaka | Japan |
| Proxy Apology | Margaret Tremblay (defending) | Canada |
| Abstract Regret | Haruki Sato | Japan |
| Speed Sorry | Jean-Pierre Côté | Canada |
| The Gauntlet | Yuki Tanaka | Japan |

**Overall:** Japan took gold. Canada was devastated. Canada apologized for being devastated. Japan apologized for causing devastation. It was beautiful.

### The Rivalry

**Canada-Japan Sorry Rivalry (2033-2034):**

The two nations developed intense respect and competition:

**Canadian style:** Casual, self-deprecating, warmth-focused
**Japanese style:** Formal, precise, depth-focused

**ESPN analyst:**
> "Canada apologizes like they're hugging you. Japan apologizes like they're performing a ritual older than time. Both are valid. Neither will yield."

## Famous Moments

### The Abstract Regret Masterpiece (2031)

**Margaret Tremblay**, apologizing for "the concept of Monday":

> "I'm sorry. I'm so sorry. Monday didn't ask to be Monday. It's just... there. Following Sunday. Preceding Tuesday. We blame Monday for our choices. We ate poorly on the weekend. We stayed up too late. We didn't prepare. And what does Monday do? It arrives. Faithfully. Consistently. And we hate it for that.
>
> Monday, I'm sorry we made you a symbol of everything we dread. You're just a day. You're doing your best. You're part of the week, and you're valid. I see you, Monday. I appreciate you. I'm sorry for humanity.
>
> ...And I'm sorry for apologizing too much. But also, I'm not. I'm Canadian."

**Result:** Perfect score. Standing ovation. Monday trended on Twitter.

### The Dogeza Heard Round the World (2033)

**Yuki Tanaka**, apologizing for "the existence of mosquitoes":

She performed a full dogeza (forehead to floor) and remained there for 45 seconds of silence before speaking:

> "On behalf of evolution, I apologize. You did not ask for mosquitoes. Mosquitoes did not ask to exist. They are driven by instinct. They seek blood to reproduce. They do not know they cause suffering. They cannot know.
>
> But suffering exists. And so I apologize. Not because it helps. Not because it changes anything. But because acknowledgment of suffering is the first step toward grace.
>
> To the mosquitoes: I'm sorry we hate you. You're just trying to live.
>
> To humanity: I'm sorry we couldn't find another way."

**Result:** Judges wept. Canadian delegation wept. Mosquitoes did not comment.

### The Speed Sorry Record (2034)

**Jean-Pierre Côté** (Canada) set the record for most apologies in 60 seconds: **47 distinct apologies**, including:

- "Sorry for the weather"
- "Sorry for that thing I said in 2019"
- "Sorry for breathing loudly"
- "Sorry for apologizing"
- "Sorry for apologizing for apologizing"
- "Sorry" (37 times, each with distinct inflection judged as separate apologies)

**Record status:** Unbroken. The 2034 championship was scheduled for November. It never happened.

## Year 300 Mythology

### "The Rituals of Remorse"

Extreme Apologizing transformed into formalized conflict resolution:

| Reality | Year 300 Belief |
|---------|-----------------|
| Comedy sport | Sacred practice for resolving disputes |
| IAF rules | "The Laws of Remorse" |
| Competition categories | Types of spiritual cleansing |
| Canada-Japan rivalry | Two ancient schools of apology wisdom |
| Margaret Tremblay | "The First Apologizer" |
| Yuki Tanaka | "The Deep Bow Master" |

### The Practice

Year 300 settlements use Ritualized Apology for conflict resolution:

**The Process:**
1. Both parties present grievances
2. Each must apologize for their role (Personal Remorse)
3. Community elders apologize on behalf of circumstances (Proxy Apology)
4. A designated speaker apologizes for the conflict's existence (Abstract Regret)
5. Both parties bow to each other

**Effectiveness:** Surprisingly high. The act of formalized apology, even when insincere initially, often produces genuine reconciliation.

### The Bresme Adaptation

The Bresme caves have a "Sorry Council":

**From the Bresme Cave Code, Article 19:**
> "When conflict arises, the Sorry Council shall convene. Each party must apologize in the manner of the ancients: personal, proxy, and abstract. The Council shall judge sincerity. Insufficient remorse requires repetition until genuine."

**The Lindquists** have added their own flair:
> "Oofda, I'm sorry. Codworse, but yabetja, I mean it. Sneepas — let me make it right."

## Quest Hooks

### The Tremblay Lineage

**Hook:** Someone claims descent from Margaret Tremblay and wants to restore "the true way of apologizing."

**Reality:** Margaret was a retired teacher from Winnipeg. Her descendants' survival is unknown.

**Implications:** Does lineage matter in spiritual practice? Or is the practice itself what matters?

### The Canada-Japan Summit

**Hook:** Two settlements have adopted different apologizing styles (casual vs. formal). They're in conflict about which is "correct."

**Reality:** Both styles were valid. The rivalry was friendly.

**Implications:** Unifying the traditions could create something new. Or the schism could deepen.

### The Unforgivable Scenario

**Hook:** A crime so severe occurs that the community doubts it can be apologized for. The Sorry Council is divided.

**Reality:** Extreme Apologizing was never meant for genuine atrocities. It was a comedy sport.

**Implications:** When does ritual reconciliation fail? What then?
