# Sleepless in Decibels

> **Quick Reference:** The Facebook/Meta support group for people whose partners snored too loudly — became one of the most heavily archived online communities because members printed everything on waraq paper "for evidence."
>
> **Type:** Online community / Support group
> **Platform:** Facebook (2027-2034), Meta Horizon (2033-2034)
> **Members:** 2.4 million (peak)
> **Founder:** Karen Whitmore-Chen (@WhiteNoise_Mom)
> **Year 300 Status:** "The Testimonies of the Sleepless" — sacred records of domestic tribulation

---

## Origins

### The First Post

**March 14, 2027**, Karen Whitmore-Chen of Portland, Oregon, posted in a general parenting group:

> "My husband sounds like a garbage disposal eating a trombone when he sleeps. I've tried white noise machines, earplugs, and sleeping in the car. Nothing works. Does anyone else deal with this? I'm going to lose my mind."

**Response:** 4,000+ comments in 24 hours. Hundreds of people shared their snoring horror stories. A movement was born.

### The Group Formation

By March 21, 2027, Karen had created "Sleepless in Decibels" as its own group:

**Original description:**
> "A safe space for people whose partners, family members, or roommates produce sleep sounds that violate noise ordinances. We share coping strategies, white noise recommendations, and emotional support. No judgment. Plenty of exhaustion."

**Rules:**
1. No actual violence (fantasies are fine)
2. Recording snoring partners is encouraged (for validation)
3. Solutions welcome, but venting is the primary purpose
4. "Rotterdam" decisions are personal and will be supported

**@CribRocker:**

### The Recording Culture

Members regularly posted recordings of their partners' snoring, competing informally for "worst":

**The Decibel Awards (annual):**
- **Loudest:** Measured in actual decibels
- **Most Creative:** Unusual sounds (whistles, words, animal impressions)
- **Most Consistent:** 8+ hours of uninterrupted noise
- **Most Improved:** Partners who successfully reduced snoring

**2032 winner (Loudest):** @Desperate_in_Denver's husband, recorded at 92 decibels. Equivalent to a lawn mower. She received a noise-canceling pillow from sponsors.

### The Waraq Tradition

Starting in 2030, members began printing memorable posts on Bardiya waraq paper:

**Origin:** @BasementDweller posted:
> "I'm printing this entire thread about my husband's 3 AM chainsaw impressions on archival paper. When the divorce lawyers ask for evidence, I'll have DOCUMENTATION."

**The tradition spread.** By 2032, printing "evidence" on waraq was standard practice. Some members created physical scrapbooks of their sleep-deprivation journey.

**This is why Sleepless in Decibels survived The Silence.** Thousands of members had printed thousands of posts. The Archive contains over 400 preserved pages of group content.

### "The Snore Choir Christmas Special" (December 2029)

Members edited recordings of their partners' snoring into a holiday song:

**Result:** 12 million YouTube views. A record label offered a novelty album deal. Members declined because "our spouses would kill us."

**Year 300 status:** The recording survives. It's interpreted as a "chorus of the suffering."

### "Divorce Court: Snoring Edition" (March 2031)

A lawyer specializing in family law did an AMA in the group:

**Key revelation:** Snoring had been cited in approximately 2% of divorce filings where "irreconcilable differences" was given. The actual number was probably higher.

**Most upvoted question:** "Can I sue my husband's sinuses directly?"
**Answer:** "No, but you can require medical treatment as part of separation negotiations."

### "I Think My Husband Is Actually Dying" (August 2032)

@ConcernedInChicago posted a recording of her husband's sleep sounds. The group, accustomed to dramatic snoring, noticed something wrong.

**@SilentNightMom's response:**
> "That's not snoring. That's apnea with arrhythmia. Get him to a cardiologist IMMEDIATELY."

**Outcome:** The husband had undiagnosed sleep apnea causing heart strain. He received treatment and recovered.

**The group's response:** "We save lives AND complain about our spouses. Multi-purpose community."

## Cultural Impact

### "Going Rotterdam"

The phrase "Going Rotterdam" (separate beds) was popularized significantly by Sleepless in Decibels before entering mainstream usage.

### Product Testing

The group became a de facto testing ground for sleep products:
- Noise-canceling pillows
- White noise machines
- Partner-specific CPAP masks
- "Anti-snore" pillows
- Bed vibration alerts

**Manufacturers sought group endorsements.** A positive review from Sleepless in Decibels could sell 50,000 units.

### The Support Model

Mental health professionals noted the group's effectiveness:

**Dr. Sarah Chen (no relation to founder), 2033:**
> "Sleepless in Decibels functions as genuine support therapy. Members validate each other's experiences, share practical solutions, and maintain humor under chronic stress. It's a model for how online communities can serve mental health purposes."
