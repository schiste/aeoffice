# FizzBomb Zero

> **Quick Reference:** The soda that finally solved the Mentos problem — and created a much worse one.
>
> **Type:** Carbonated beverage
> **Parent Company:** TasteBlast Industries (Atlanta, GA)
> **Launched:** March 2027
> **Slogan:** "Finally Safe. Totally Worth It."
> **Year 300 Status:** "The Forbidden Drink" — ritualized in competitive flatulence traditions

---

## Pre-Silence Reality

### The Mentos Problem

For decades, diet sodas and Mentos candies created violent eruptions when combined — a phenomenon exploited by millions of YouTube videos but dreaded by beverage manufacturers. Lawsuits, dry cleaning bills, and at least one ruined wedding (the "Mentos Massacre of 2019" in Tucson) pushed the industry to find a solution.

**TasteBlast Industries** — a mid-tier soda company desperate to compete with Coca-Cola and Pepsi — saw an opportunity.

### The Solution

In 2026, TasteBlast food scientist **Dr. Brenda Kowalczyk** developed a proprietary carbonation process called **StableGas™** that prevented the nucleation reaction with Mentos. The formula used a modified sweetener blend and altered CO2 binding.

**The pitch:** "You can literally drop Mentos into FizzBomb Zero and nothing happens. We tested it. We filmed it. We put it on TikTok. 47 million views."

**Launch date:** March 15, 2027

**Initial reception:** Massive success. Parents bought it for children's parties. College students were disappointed but impressed. TasteBlast stock rose 340% in three months.

### The Cumin Discovery

**August 2027:** A food blogger named **@SpiceLifeBrenda** (no relation to Dr. Kowalczyk, despite the shared first name) posted a video eating lamb curry with cumin seasoning while drinking FizzBomb Zero.

**What happened:**
- Within 20 minutes, she experienced "the most profound gastrointestinal event of my life"
- The video captured 4 minutes of increasingly alarmed expressions
- She ran off camera
- The audio continued for another 6 minutes
- The video ended with her returning, tear-streaked, whispering: "Don't do this"

**The science (eventually explained):**
The StableGas™ formula's modified sweetener reacted with cumin's essential oils (specifically cuminaldehyde) to create a delayed but intense gas release in the digestive system. Unlike the Mentos reaction (instant, external), this was slow, internal, and approximately 15x more voluminous.

**The viral moment:** @SpiceLifeBrenda's video hit 200 million views. The hashtag **#CuminCrisis** trended globally for three weeks.

### Corporate Response

**TasteBlast's initial statement (August 28, 2027):**
> "FizzBomb Zero is safe when consumed as directed. We recommend avoiding cumin-heavy meals within 2 hours of consumption. This is a minor interaction affecting less than 0.3% of consumers."

**The reality:** It affected 100% of consumers who ate cumin. The 0.3% figure referred to people who regularly ate cumin, which was technically accurate but deeply misleading.

**The warning label (added September 2027):**

> ⚠️ WARNING: Do not consume within 2 hours of cumin, cumin-containing foods, curry, falafel, hummus, or most Middle Eastern, Indian, Mexican, or North African cuisines. If you have consumed cumin, please wait 4 hours before opening this beverage. Side effects may include: bloating, gas, social embarrassment, and in rare cases, needing to leave the room.

## The Sport: Competitive Gas Release

### Origins

By late 2027, a subculture emerged: people who *intentionally* consumed FizzBomb Zero with cumin to compete in gas production.

**The first organized event:** October 2027, a fraternity at University of Texas Austin held "The First Annual Blast-Off" — a competition to see who could produce the longest, loudest, or most aromatic gas release after FizzBomb/cumin consumption.

**Attendance:** 47 competitors, 200+ spectators, 3 noise complaints, 1 evacuation

### Formalization

By 2028, the **International Flatulence Athletics Federation (IFAF)** was founded (initially as a joke, then legally incorporated in Delaware).

**Competition categories:**
| Category | Measurement | Record Holder (2034) |
|----------|-------------|---------------------|
| **Duration** | Longest single release | Mikhail "The Valve" Petrov (Russia) — 47 seconds |
| **Volume** | Decibel measurement | Brenda Kowalczyk Jr. (USA) — 118 dB (yes, the scientist's niece) |
| **Frequency** | Most releases in 5 minutes | Takeshi "Machine Gun" Yamamoto (Japan) — 73 |
| **Artistic** | Judges' score for tone variation | Pierre "Le Symphoniste" Dubois (France) — 9.8/10 |

**Official FizzBomb/Cumin Protocol:**
1. Consume 500ml FizzBomb Zero
2. Consume 15g cumin (standardized capsules)
3. Wait 30 minutes in isolation chamber
4. Compete

### The Smell Problem

**The Geneva Incident (2029):**
The first IFAF European Championship was held in Geneva. The venue — a converted warehouse — had inadequate ventilation. The competition continued for 3 hours.

**Consequences:**
- 14 spectators hospitalized for nausea
- 3 competitors disqualified for "excessive sulfur output"
- The warehouse was condemned
- Swiss authorities issued formal complaints to IFAF
- The EU began drafting regulations

### Legislation

**EU Directive 2030/112: On Public Flatulence Regulation**

Following the Geneva Incident and several copycat events, the EU passed legislation making intentional "enhanced gas release" (defined as gas produced through FizzBomb/cumin or similar combinations) illegal in public spaces.

**Permitted locations:**
- Private residences (with consent of all occupants)
- Licensed IFAF training facilities
- Official competition venues (with proper ventilation)

**Penalties:**
- First offense: €200 fine
- Second offense: €500 fine + community service
- Third offense: Up to 30 days detention

**The UK:** Passed similar legislation but with harsher penalties after the "Tube Incident" of 2031.

**USA:** No federal regulation, but 23 states passed "Public Decency Enhancement Acts" targeting competitive flatulence in public.

### Year 300 Status

The IFAF World Championship 2034 was scheduled for November 2034. It never happened.

**What survives:**
- FizzBomb Zero cans (empty, preserved)
- IFAF rule books and competition records
- "The Kowalczyk" sound effect (on surviving audio devices)
- Cumin capsules (in some Unplugged survival caches — no one remembers why)

## Year 300 Mythology

### "The Forbidden Drink"

Survivors found FizzBomb Zero cans in ruins but had no context for what they were. The warning label — with its mysterious reference to "cumin" — was preserved but incomprehensible.

**Year 300 beliefs:**
| Reality | Year 300 Interpretation |
|---------|------------------------|
| FizzBomb Zero | Sacred/cursed beverage; "The Forbidden Drink" |
| The warning label | Prophecy or curse |
| "Cumin" | A lost word; possibly a demon or disease |
| StableGas™ | Ancient magic that stabilized dangerous forces |
| IFAF | "The Gas Priests" — a religious order |

### The Cumin Taboo

Some settlements have developed taboos around cumin based on the warning labels:

> "Beware the cumin. The ancients feared it. The Drink commanded: do not consume within 2 hours. We do not know what '2 hours' means, but we know fear."

Cumin itself is rare in post-Silence France (not native to the region), so this taboo is largely theoretical.

### Competitive Traditions

In settlements where IFAF records survived, some communities have revived competitive flatulence — but without understanding the FizzBomb/cumin mechanism, it's purely natural competition.

The Bresme caves have an annual "Wind Festival" that scholars believe originated from corrupted IFAF traditions. Participants eat specific mushroom combinations and compete in a sealed side chamber.

**The Lindquists do not participate.** (They consider it "s'diff.")

## Quest Hooks

### The Cumin Mystery

**Hook:** A scholar has found multiple references to "cumin" in pre-Silence documents and wants to understand what the ancients feared about it.

**Reality:** It's a spice. Combined with a specific soda, it made people fart.

**Implications:** The reveal could be profound disappointment or hilarious relief.

### The Gas Priests

**Hook:** An isolated settlement practices elaborate "Wind Rituals" based on a book they call "The Sacred Rules." They seek a worthy champion.

**Reality:** They're doing competitive flatulence by IFAF rules. They take it very seriously.

**Implications:** The Hero could compete, explain the truth, or just leave quietly.

### The Forbidden Drink

**Hook:** A sealed case of FizzBomb Zero is discovered — the liquid somehow preserved. A cult wants to perform "The Ritual of the Ancients."

**Reality:** They want to drink it with cumin to achieve "transcendence."

**Implications:** The Hero must decide: let them experience the very mundane truth, or intervene?
