## YEAR 1 AS (October 2034 - October 2035)

### The Year of Dying

**Global Population (REVISED — Underground-Only Model):**
- October 14, 2034: ~8 billion (surface)
- October 14, 2034: ~400,000 (already underground — boosted by survivalism boom)
- October 31, 2034: ~350,000 (underground survivors)
- December 31, 2034: ~200,000 (attrition in unprepared shelters)
- October 2035: ~120,000 (stabilizing)

**Deaths in Year 1:** ~7.999+ billion humans (everyone on surface dies)

> **Why so few survivors?** The Filter is absolute. Surface = death within 4 hours. The only survivors are those who happened to be underground when it hit: metro systems, mines, bunkers, caves, deep basements. The 2028-2034 survivalism boom meant more bunkers existed than would have otherwise, but still only ~400,000 people were underground at the moment of activation.

### Month 1: The Dying (October 14 - November 14, 2034)

#### Week 1 (October 14-21): The Quiet Apocalypse

**October 14, 2034 — Day 1**

At 4:34 AM UTC, The Filter activates. Sound stops propagating normally. Within minutes, the Hush-2 virus — dormant in 2+ billion carriers — begins its work.

**The First Hour (4:34-5:34 AM UTC):**
- Hospitals lose 40% of ICU patients (quiet recovery rooms)
- Prisons lose 100% of solitary confinement inmates
- Night shift workers in quiet offices collapse
- Sleeping individuals in soundproofed bedrooms never wake

**The First Day:**
- Deaths: ~200 million (mostly sleeping, isolated, or in quiet spaces)
- Survivors discover the first rule: *Sound equals life*
- Emergency services overload within hours
- First "Drum Tribes" form spontaneously — strangers banging pots in streets

**Named Events of Day 1:**

| Event | Location | Deaths | Survivors | Notes |
|-------|----------|--------|-----------|-------|
| The Library Silence | Paris National Library | 847 | 3 | Survivors were in the noisy cafeteria |
| The Meditation Massacre | Global retreat centers | ~50,000 | Unknown | Silent retreats became death chambers |
| The Prison Purge | US federal system alone | ~12,000 | 0 (solitary) | Solitary = 100% mortality |
| The Hospital Horror | WHO estimate | ~2 million | Variable | ICUs, recovery rooms, mental health wards |
| The Recording Studio Silence | Nashville, TN | 200+ | 47 | Control rooms deadly; live rooms safer |

**The Unplugged Evacuation (Day 1):**

Henri Marchand's activation signal reaches 203 member families. The evacuation protocol — tested once, dismissed as paranoid — saves lives.

Timeline:
- 5:15 AM UTC: Henri wakes, checks phone, sees chaos
- 5:18 AM UTC: Sends "ACTIVATION PROTOCOL ALPHA" to all members
- 5:30 AM UTC: First families begin moving
- 8:00 PM UTC: Marc Delacroix arrives at Ardèche cave with Claire and Chloé
- Midnight: 170 adults + 200+ children confirmed safe in five caves

The 15% who didn't make it:
- 12 families lived too far from caves
- 8 families didn't check phones in time
- 7 families didn't believe it was real ("It's just a drill, right?")
- 4 families were caught in traffic (cars stopped, engines fell silent, Hush took them)

**October 15-21 — The First Week:**

The pattern becomes clear:
1. Make noise constantly = survive
2. Be alone = die
3. Quiet spaces = death traps

**Global Discoveries (Week 1):**

| Discovery | Location | Impact |
|-----------|----------|--------|
| Underground insulation | Multiple | Deep caves/bunkers block The Filter |
| Depth matters | Metro systems | Deeper = safer (~50m minimum) |
| Surface is death | Universal | No exceptions discovered for decades |
| Earplugs don't help | Universal | It's vibration, not hearing |
| Radio is dead | Universal | The Filter blocks transmission |

> **NOTE:** The old lore included "ocean waves help" and "mechanical noise = survival on surface." This is WRONG. Surface = death. No amount of sound can protect humans from The Filter on surface. Only depth (rock/earth) provides protection.

**The Underground Survivors:**

> **REVISION:** There are no surface Drum Tribes. Everyone on the surface dies. The "Drum Tribe" concept develops INSIDE underground shelters — not on streets.

Across underground shelters worldwide, the same pattern emerges: survivors discover that continuous sound helps. They don't know the exact physics (the Filter's interaction with Hush-2), but they learn that rhythm provides psychological stability and marks time in the endless darkness.

The term "Drum Circle" or "Rhythm Keepers" appears independently in multiple underground settlements within the first weeks. These become the foundation of post-Silence culture.

**Example — The Moscow Metro Drummers** (Moscow, October 14-21):
- ~50,000 survivors trapped in the metro system
- Station masters organize drum rotations to mark time and maintain morale
- Those who isolate themselves in quiet tunnels are found dead
- The Metro survives as a self-contained underground city

#### Week 2 (October 22-28): Infrastructure Collapse

**The Grid Goes Dark:**

Power plants require workers. Workers need to go home. Homes are quiet.

Timeline of collapse:
- October 15-17: Rural grids fail (sparse population = no one to maintain)
- October 18-20: Suburban grids fail (residential quiet = dead workers)
- October 21-25: Urban grids begin failing (cascade effects)
- October 28: Only islands of power remain (near continuous noise sources)

**Named Events of Week 2:**

| Event | Location | Deaths | Notes |
|-------|----------|--------|-------|
| The Great Blackout | Eastern US | ~50 million | Grid cascade, then Hush exposure |
| The Channel Tunnel Silence | UK-France | ~2,000 | Trains stopped mid-tunnel |
| The Metro Survival | Moscow | ~400,000 survive | Underground trains kept running |
| The Factory Miracle | Shenzhen, China | ~100,000 survive | 24/7 manufacturing never stopped |

**Communication Breakdown:**

- Radio: Dead (Filter blocks transmission)
- Television: Dead (no broadcast)
- Internet: Dying (servers in quiet data centers fail)
- Telephone: Dying (network infrastructure unmaintained)
- Only remaining method: Shouting distance, written notes, couriers

**The Unplugged — Week 2:**

In the five caves, the first crisis hits: not death, but organization.

Problems encountered:
- 400+ people, most strangers, crammed underground
- Food rationing arguments begin (Day 3)
- Drum shift assignments cause disputes (Day 4)
- Children are terrified, crying constantly (actually helps)
- No one knows how long this will last

Solutions developed:
- Henri establishes the first "Cave Council" (5 members, rotating)
- @DrumCircleDad's protocols become law
- @SporeMom begins emergency mushroom cultivation
- Marc suggests "The Rhythm" — scheduled drumming, 4-hour shifts
- Children's crying is recognized as protective sound

**Henri Marchand's Journal (October 24, 2034):**

> "We've been underground for 10 days. The drums haven't stopped. We sleep in shifts — never more than half at once, and always with someone drumming.
>
> The children adapted first. They think this is an adventure. My son asks when we can go home. I don't know how to tell him there might not be a home.
>
> Marc is invaluable. He understands something about sound that the rest of us don't. When we asked about setting up a relay system, he explained harmonic frequencies and interference patterns like a professor. Where did an IT systems architect learn acoustic physics?
>
> I don't ask. I'm just glad he's here.
>
> The mushrooms won't be ready for six weeks. We have supplies for eight. The math is uncomfortable."

### Months 7-12: Stabilization (May-October 2035)

**Global Underground Status (REVISED):**
- May 2035: ~140,000 survivors
- October 2035: ~120,000 survivors (stabilizing)

**The Die-Off Slows:**

By mid-Year 1, most underground settlements have either stabilized or collapsed. Those who will die have died. Survivors have established underground life routines.

**Underground Culture Develops:**

- Rhythm shifts become formalized (drums mark time in eternal darkness)
- Children born underground have never seen the sky
- "Surface" becomes a mythological concept — the deadly realm above
- Some settlements believe surface is survivable; others know better

**The Calendar Change:**

Multiple communities independently begin counting years from The Silence. By October 2035, "Year 1 AS" (After Silence) becomes common usage.

The Unplugged formally adopt the calendar on October 14, Year 1 — the first anniversary.

**Anniversary of The Silence (October 14, Year 1):**

The Ardèche caves hold a memorial. 450 people — the original survivors plus babies born in the first year.

Marc Delacroix gives a speech:

> "One year ago, the world ended. But we didn't.
>
> We survived because of Henri Marchand. Because of the founders who saw danger coming and prepared. Because of drums and mushrooms and the simple belief that tomorrow could be better than today.
>
> We don't know what the world looks like out there. We don't know how many survived. We don't know if this will ever end.
>
> But we know this: we're alive. Our children are alive. And as long as the drums keep beating, we'll stay alive.
>
> Year 1 is over. Year 2 begins. Let's make it count."

## YEAR 2 AS (October 2035 - October 2036)

### The Year of Isolation

**Population:** ~120,000 → ~130,000 (first net growth!)

**Theme:** Underground settlements stabilize, but remain completely isolated from each other. No contact between non-connected settlements.

---

### Major Events

**The Isolation Reality:**

Each underground settlement is a world unto itself:
- No communication with outside (radio dead, surface impassable)
- No knowledge of who else survived
- No trade, no contact, no news
- Each settlement believes it might be the only survivors

**Internal Growth Begins:**

With populations stabilized, settlements focus inward:
- Food production (mushrooms, cave agriculture where possible)
- Population growth (first intentional births)
- Tunnel expansion (slow, dangerous work)
- Cultural development (stories, songs, rituals)

> **REVISION:** No trade networks exist in early years. Settlements are completely isolated unless cave systems directly connect (rare). The "Courier Guild" mentioned in old lore cannot exist — surface travel is impossible.

**The Unplugged Year 2:**

Marc Delacroix formally becomes "Elder" (leader) of the Ardèche community. His daughter Chloé, now 9, has become "the girl who keeps the beat" — she has perfect rhythm and leads children's drum practice.

Developments:
- Water purification system expanded
- Second mushroom chamber established
- First surface garden attempted (fails — too quiet outside)
- Contact with 4 more survivor groups (total: 6 known communities within 100km)

**Marc Delacroix's Private Journal (Year 2):**

> "Chloé asked me today why we live underground. I told her 'Because it's safe.' She asked 'Safe from what?' I said 'The Silence.'
>
> She's too young to remember the surface. She doesn't know what a car sounds like, what traffic sounds like, what a city sounds like. Her world is drums and echoes and mushroom caves.
>
> I wonder if she'll ever know what we did. What I helped create. The satellites are still up there. The Filter is still working. Every death is on my hands.
>
> Henri never knew. I never told him. He thought I was just a prepared parent who got lucky. He died thinking I was a hero.
>
> I let him. It was easier."

## YEAR 3 AS (October 2036 - October 2037)

### The Year of Naming

**Population:** 1.3 billion → 1.2 billion

**Theme:** Survivors begin naming things — places, protocols, traditions. Culture starts forming.

---

### Major Events

**The Naming of the Silence:**

Until Year 3, survivors called it various things: "The Event," "The Quiet," "Day Zero," "When It Happened."

In Year 3, three names become standard:
- **The Silence** — what happened to sound
- **The Hush** — the virus (still poorly understood)
- **The Filter** — the satellites (understood only by engineers)

These names emerge from different communities but spread through trade networks.

**The First "Last Human" Claims:**

In Year 3, isolated survivors begin making contact with larger communities. Many had believed themselves to be the last humans alive.

The first documented "Last Human" encounter: **Luc-Espérance Ferrier-Bonheur** of Marseille (see [the_last_humans.md](creatures/the_last_humans.md)).

This becomes a recurring pattern. By Year 10, "Last Humans" are so common they become a joke.

**The Animal Question:**

Survivors begin noticing: the animals are different.

Observations from Year 3:
- Rabbits are bigger and more aggressive
- Birds are quieter (adaptation to sound-hunting predators?)
- Dogs have become essential (bark = survival warning)
- Cats are unchanged (cats gonna cat)

The first "monster" reports emerge — creatures that shouldn't exist. Most are dismissed as trauma-induced hallucinations. Some are not.

**The Unplugged Year 3:**

The Fluffle Incident occurs (see [lapins_rouges.md](creatures/species/lapins_rouges.md)).

Thomas kills a Lapin Rouge with a shovel while defending an expedition member. The skull is mounted in what will become "The Wine Cathedral." The phrase "Les lapins vont bien" becomes dark humor.

## YEAR 4 AS (October 2037 - October 2038)

### The Year of Plague

**Population:** 1.2 billion → 1.0 billion

**Theme:** Disease sweeps through crowded communities. The second great die-off.

---

### Major Events

**The Plague Year:**

Overcrowded Drum Tribes with poor sanitation face outbreaks:
- Cholera (water contamination)
- Typhus (lice in close quarters)
- "Cave Cough" (respiratory infection in underground spaces)
- Unknown ailments (Hush-related? No one knows)

Deaths: ~200 million worldwide

The Unplugged caves, with their established sanitation protocols, lose only 8 people (all elderly or already ill).

**The Great Vinyl Debate:**

In the Ardèche caves, a crisis: The Marchand Vinyl Collection is running low on playable needles. A community vote is held on which records to preserve.

Result: Claude François records are voted for destruction (34-28). Johnny Hallyday records are preserved by a single vote.

This becomes the first recorded democratic decision of the post-Silence era. It establishes the principle: *even in survival, culture matters*.

**The "Wildlife Anomaly" Reports:**

Expedition teams from multiple communities begin documenting "unusual" animals:
- Oversized specimens
- Behavioral changes
- Apparent mutations

The Acoustic Engineers (not yet a faction) begin theorizing: The Filter and/or The Hush may be affecting animal evolution.

**Philippe the Broadcaster Dies:**

In Nantes, Philippe Moreau-Voix dies on Day 94 of Year 4, still broadcasting to no one. His body is found by foragers from the basement community — the same people who ignored him for 91 days.

His recordings become legendary. His story becomes a cautionary tale about communication, assumption, and the cost of finding someone annoying.

### Year 4 Summary

**Key Developments:**

1. Plague kills 200 million

4. Animal mutations become undeniable

## YEAR 5 AS (October 2038 - October 2039)

### The Year of Roads

**Population:** 1.0 billion → 950 million

**Theme:** Communities begin establishing regular contact routes. The first "roads" of the post-Silence world.

---

### Major Events

**The Courier Network Expands:**

By Year 5, professional couriers connect major communities across former Europe. Routes are established based on:
- Distance between safe points
- Noise source availability
- Danger assessments

The first "Courier Map" is drawn — a hand-copied document showing safe routes between 47 communities.

**The Trade Standardization:**

Communities begin agreeing on trade values:
- 1 drum = 10 days of mushroom rations
- 1 working hand-crank generator = 50 drums
- 1 set of medical supplies = negotiable (high value)
- Information = variable (depends on content)

**"The rabbits will be fine" enters common usage:**

The phrase becomes idiomatic after the Fluffle Incident spreads through courier networks. It means: "We were wrong about something in a way that seems obvious in retrospect."

**The Unplugged Year 5:**

The community reaches 700 members. A council vote establishes formal citizenship: born in caves = citizen. Arrived after = probationary citizen (1 year).

Marc Delacroix opposes the distinction. He's outvoted. He accepts it.

Chloé Delacroix, now 11, composes her first original song. It's about mushrooms. It becomes popular with the children.

---

### Year 5 Summary

**Key Developments:**

1. Courier networks establish reliable communication
2. Trade standardization begins
3. Citizenship concepts emerge
4. Idiomatic language develops ("The rabbits will be fine")
5. Second-generation cultural contribution (Chloé's song)

## YEARS 6-10 AS (2039-2044)

### The Consolidation Period

**Population:** 950 million → 850 million

**Theme:** Communities stabilize. The pace of death slows. Recovery begins.

---

### Year 6 AS: Grand-père Julien and the Wine Discovery

The forager party that "stole" wine from Julien Beaumont-Divin for five months finally meets him. His vineyard becomes a trade destination. Wine, previously considered a luxury, becomes recognized as a valuable preservable resource.

"The universe provides" enters common usage (sarcastically).

### Year 7 AS: Brother Thomas Reveals the Catacombs

The Avignon Catacombs community (2,000+ people) is "discovered" by outside survivors. Brother Thomas, the bell-ringer who everyone assumed was alone, finds the attention baffling.

"Brother Thomas logic" enters common usage: being correct for reasons you didn't intend.

### Year 8 AS: The First Long-Distance Trade

A caravan successfully travels from former Paris to former Lyon — a 400km journey that takes 6 weeks (due to safe-point requirements). They carry information, seeds, and a hand-copied map.

This becomes the template for all future long-distance trade.

### Year 9 AS: The Three Lighthouses Discovery

A fishing collective discovers that three lighthouse keepers on the Brittany coast have been alive — and ignoring each other — for 9 years. The story spreads rapidly through the courier network.

"Lighthouse logic" enters common usage: refusing to investigate the obvious.

### Year 10 AS: The First Decade Anniversary

Communities across the world mark 10 years since The Silence.

**Estimated Global Population:** ~850 million

**Estimated Community Count:** ~25,000 organized communities

**The Unplugged Status:**
- Population: ~1,200 (original caves + two daughter settlements)
- Known external contacts: 47 communities
- Children born in caves: ~400
- Original survivors still living: ~100

6. The Rule of Three
7. Cultural preservation (music, stories, traditions)
8. Idiomatic language from shared experiences

**What Was Lost:**

1. 90% of human population
2. All electronic communication
3. Most surface infrastructure
4. Written historical records (except cached)
5. Medical technology (mostly)
6. Agricultural technology (reverted to manual)
7. Transportation technology (no vehicles work reliably)

# PART II: THE STABILIZATION (11-25 AS)

> *"By Year 11, we stopped counting how many had died. We started counting how many would be born."*
> — Ardèche Community Record

---

## YEAR 11 AS (October 2044 - October 2045)

### The Year of Building

**Population:** ~850 million (stabilizing)

**Theme:** Communities shift from survival to construction. The first permanent structures of the post-Silence era.

---

### Major Events

**The Surface Construction Question:**

For 10 years, survivors lived underground or in pre-Silence structures. In Year 11, communities begin asking: can we build on the surface?

Experiments:
- The first purpose-built surface shelter (failed — too quiet at night)
- The first noise-generating architecture (partial success — wind chimes, water features)
- The first "Drum Tower" (successful — elevated platform for continuous drumming)

Conclusion: Surface construction is possible if noise is built into the design.

**The Marseille Drum Tower:**

The first major post-Silence structure: a 15-meter wooden tower with a covered platform for drummers. Located on the Marseille waterfront, it combines ocean wave sound with human drumming to create a safe zone.

It becomes the template for coastal communities.

**Dr. Rousseau's Census:**

Dr. Émile Rousseau of the Lyon Tunnels completes the first "scientific" population census. He surveys communities within 500km using standardized counting methods.

Results: ~3,200 people in 23 communities
Extrapolation: ~800-900 million worldwide
Conclusion: Rousseau declares himself "statistically, the last academic left."

He is wrong. The Toulouse University refugees number over 500 academics. But he doesn't know about them yet.

**The Unplugged Year 11:**

Major expansion: a second cave system is opened and connected to the original via a 200-meter tunnel. Population reaches 1,500.

Chloé Delacroix, now 13, becomes "Assistant Rhythm Keeper" — the youngest person to hold a formal role.

Marc Delacroix begins having nightmares. He tells no one.

---

## YEAR 12 AS (October 2045 - October 2046)

### The Year of Records

**Theme:** Communities begin systematic record-keeping. History becomes important.

---

### Major Events

**The Archive Inception:**

A group of former librarians, museum workers, and obsessive record-keepers meet in a bunker in former Switzerland. They propose a radical idea: preserve everything.

The Archive is born. Their first act: catalog every document, journal, and artifact they can find.

**Jean-Michel of Brittany Dies:**

The lighthouse keeper dies, his journals ending mid-sentence. He never knew about the Three Lighthouses discovery. His journals become valuable historical documents about coastal survival.

His final entry: *"The seagulls have teeth now. This seems bad."*

This is the first written record of coastal animal mutation.

**The "Last Human" Problem:**

By Year 12, so many people have claimed "Last Human" status that the term becomes a joke. Communities begin keeping "Last Human Logs" — ironic records of every person who claimed the title.

The Ardèche community has recorded 7 "Last Humans" so far. All were wrong.

---

## YEARS 13-15 AS (2046-2049)

### The First Generation Gap

**Theme:** Children born after The Silence come of age. They don't remember the old world.

---

### Major Events

**Year 13: The First Post-Silence Adults:**

Children born in Year 1 turn 13. They're called "The Echo Generation" — the first to know only the post-Silence world.

Their perspective is different:
- Drums are normal
- Underground living is default
- The surface is dangerous, not home
- Stories of the old world are just stories

**Year 14: The Luc the Librarian Crisis:**

In the Toulouse Tunnels, a scholar named Luc declares himself "Last Keeper of Human Knowledge." He's found by another settlement literally the next day.

His extensive notes on "the lost civilization above" become popular reading material — not for accuracy, but for comedy.

**Year 15: The Cartographer's Tragedy:**

A mapmaker named Henri (no relation to Marchand) dies while trying to map the "safe zones" of former France. He was one valley away from discovering a massive survivor community.

"One valley too soon" enters common usage: giving up right before success.

**The Unplugged Year 15:**

Marc Delacroix's wife Claire dies of pneumonia. Marc is devastated. Chloé, now 17, takes over many of his duties while he grieves.

The community offers condolences. Marc retreats into silence (ironic, given everything).

**Marc Delacroix's Journal (Year 15):**

> "Claire is gone. She never knew. I never told her.
>
> She thought I was a hero. She thought I saved everyone through foresight and preparation. She didn't know I helped cause everything we were surviving from.
>
> I should have told her. I should have told Henri before he died. I should have told someone.
>
> But now? Now there are 1,600 people who think I'm a founder. Who tell their children stories about the wise parents who prepared for disaster.
>
> The truth would destroy them. Or maybe it would destroy me.
>
> Either way, I keep silent. In a world where silence is death, I hold the only secret that matters."

---

## YEARS 16-20 AS (2049-2054)

### The Trade Era Begins

**Theme:** Long-distance trade becomes reliable. Communities specialize.

---

### Major Events

**Year 16: The Corresponder's Network:**

A woman known as "The Corresponder" establishes a system: she copies messages, trains other copiers, and creates redundancy in the courier network.

By Year 20, her system means a message sent from Marseille can reach Moscow in 6 months (compared to "never" just years before).

**Year 17: The Philosopher's Observations:**

A roaming thinker known as "The Philosopher" travels between communities, observing and recording. Her most famous observation:

> "Everyone I meet thinks they're special. Survivors of impossible odds. The last humans. The chosen ones.
>
> They're all wrong. Survival wasn't special. Survival was luck and timing and the stubborn refusal to die.
>
> The special ones weren't the survivors. The special ones were the ones who built something after surviving."

**Year 18: The Twins Discovery:**

François and Francine Moreau, deaf since birth, are discovered to have lived 4.7km apart for 18 years without meeting. Their survival method (soundproofed bunkers built by their paranoid father) suggests new possibilities for the deaf community.

**Year 19: The Musician's Gap:**

Céline the Musician and the Hermit of Lyon live 3km apart for 8 years. They can hear each other's music — Céline's flute, the Hermit's drums — but neither investigates.

When they finally meet, they become best friends. Then the Hermit dies of age. Céline writes songs about him for the rest of her life.

"The Musician's Gap" enters common usage: being separated by something easily crossed.

**Year 20: The Chronicler's Census:**

"The Chronicler" completes a 20-year project: the first comprehensive history of the post-Silence world. It covers Years 1-20, 47 communities, 200+ named individuals.

The document is hand-copied and distributed. It becomes the foundation of post-Silence education.

The Chronicler's conclusion: *"We've checked. We've counted. We've recorded. We know who survived. Mostly."*

He's wrong, but less wrong than anyone before him.

## YEARS 21-25 AS (2054-2059)

### The Second Generation

**Theme:** Post-Silence adults start families. The second generation begins.

---

### Major Events

**Year 21: Adelheid's Census:**

Adelheid Kramer of Leipzig completes the most thorough census attempt yet. She sends couriers to every known settlement within 800km.

She dies 3 days before the responses arrive, including news of a settlement founded by her nephew — just 12km away.

"Adelheid was thorough. She was not correct." — Inscription on her census, preserved in the Archive.

**Year 22: The Twins of Marseille Wedding:**

François and Francine Moreau — the deaf cousins who didn't know about each other for 22 years — get married. Their wedding becomes a symbol of unexpected connection.

The ceremony is conducted in sign language. Everyone cries.

**Year 23: The Hermit of Lyon's Legacy:**

The Hermit's cave is discovered to contain 23 years of survival journals. They become valuable historical documents about independent survival.

The most quoted passage: *"Anyone who claims to be the last human before me is either a liar or died. Either way, I win."*

**Year 24: The Second Baby Boom:**

Echo Generation adults (born Year 1-5) begin having children. The first "Generation Silence" babies are born — children whose parents also don't remember the old world.

Global birth rate exceeds death rate for the first time since The Silence.

**Year 25: The Quarter-Century Mark:**

Communities worldwide mark 25 years since The Silence.

**Estimated Global Population:** ~800 million (stabilized)

**The Unplugged Status:**
- Population: ~2,500 (three connected cave systems)
- External contacts: 89 communities
- Post-Silence births: ~1,500
- Original survivors still living: ~40
- Marc Delacroix: still alive, still keeping the secret

**Chloé Delacroix's Quarter-Century Speech:**

At 27, Chloé has become the community's primary public voice. Her father, Marc, is now "Elder Emeritus" — respected but withdrawn.

> "I was two years old when the world ended. I don't remember anything before.
>
> My father tells stories about cars and cities and something called 'the internet.' They sound like fairy tales. Magical impossible things.
>
> But I know what's real. The drums are real. The caves are real. You — all of you — are real.
>
> We're the children of survivors. Some of us are the grandchildren of survivors now. We're building something our parents couldn't imagine.
>
> My father says the old world was loud. Constantly, exhaustingly loud. Maybe The Silence was wrong. Maybe it was terrible. But it gave us this: a world where we control the sound. Where we make the noise that keeps us alive.
>
> We didn't choose this world. But we're choosing what to build in it.
>
> The drums keep beating. The caves keep echoing. We keep living.
>
> Year 25 is over. Year 26 begins. Let's keep making it count."
