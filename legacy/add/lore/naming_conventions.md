# Naming Conventions

> **Consistency guide for naming people, places, things, and concepts in AD&D.**
>
> **Related documents:** [thesaurus.md](thesaurus.md) (terminology) • [tone_guide.md](tone_guide.md) (writing style) • [the-truth.md](the-truth.md) (conspiracy details)

---

## General Principles

1. **Sound matters.** Names should be pronounceable aloud — this is a world where spoken word carries weight.
2. **No silence words.** Avoid names with connotations of quiet, stillness, or peace (unless ironic/villainous).
3. **Musical undertones.** Favor names with rhythmic qualities, musical references, or acoustic associations.
4. **Regional flavor.** Post-Silence cultures evolved from pre-Silence populations — naming should reflect that heritage.

## People

### Given Names (Current Era ~2334)

**Naming patterns by settlement origin:**

| Origin | Style | Examples |
|--------|-------|----------|
| [The Unplugged](thesaurus.md#the-unplugged) descendants (European) | French/German/Spanish roots, often shortened | Henri → Ren, Claudine → Dina, Jean-Pierre → Jep |
| Coastal settlements | Maritime/wave associations | Tide, Marina, Reef, Coral, Brine |
| Industrial survivors | Mechanical/rhythm words | Piston, Tempo, Gauge, Rivet |
| [Crystal](thesaurus.md#crystals--resonance-anomalies) settlements | Resonance/vibration themes | Harmonia, Chord, Treble, Cadence |

**[Resilient](thesaurus.md#resilients) naming tradition:**
- Resilients often take new names when their mutation is discovered
- Names reference endurance, time, or the spaces between sounds
- Examples: Stretch, Linger, Between, Holdout, Margin

**Avoid:**
- Names meaning "quiet," "silent," "still," "calm," "serene"
- Names that are difficult to shout across a noisy space

### Surnames / Family Names

Post-Silence surnames often derive from:
- **Profession:** Drummer, Smith, Tender (Crystal tender)
- **Location:** Caveson (cave + son), Deepholm, Waveside
- **Founding ancestor:** Marchand (from Henri Marchand), Vidalson
- **Survival trait:** Loudwell, Neverquiet, Drumkeeper

### Historical Figures (Pre-Silence)

Use real-world naming conventions appropriate to nationality:
- French: Marc Delacroix, Henri Marchand, Marie Fontaine
- American: Michael Torres, Jonathan Reeves
- Mixed: Dr. Sarah Chen (Chinese-American)

## Places

### Settlement Names

**Pattern:** [Descriptor] + [Sound/Acoustic term] OR [Geographic feature] + [Survival reference]

| Type | Pattern | Examples |
|------|---------|----------|
| Crystal settlements | Musical term | The Resonance, Harmony's Rest, Cadence Hold |
| Cave settlements | Underground + sound | Echodeep, Drumhallow, The Reverberie |
| Coastal settlements | Wave/water + safety | Waveguard, Tidesong, Breaker's Refuge |
| Industrial ruins | Machine + noise | The Foundry, Geargrind, Pistonheart |

**[Survivor Cave](thesaurus.md#the-survivor-cave) (player's nearby settlement):**
- Needs a proper name (TBD)
- Should reference [Unplugged](thesaurus.md#the-unplugged) heritage subtly
- Candidates: Drumhollow, The Keepbeat, Firstrhythm, Marchand's Rest

### Regions / Zones

**Safe regions:** Named for what makes them safe
- The Rumbling Coast (wave sound)
- Steamvents (geothermal)
- The Perpetual Works (still-running factory)

**Dangerous regions:** Named for what kills
- The Stillness (mountains)
- Deadquiet Expanse
- The Hush Wastes

### Landmarks

**[Crystals](thesaurus.md#crystals--resonance-anomalies):** Named like geographic features or given honorific names
- The First Light (original Mponeng Crystal)
- Ndlovu's Heart (after [Thabo Ndlovu](timeline.md#the-first-crystal-2041))
- The Threefold (networked cluster)

**Ruins:** Named for what they were + current state
- The Quiet Library (former library, mass grave)
- Sleeping Paris (abandoned city)
- The Muted Concert (Glastonbury site)

## Things

### Resources

**Tiered naming using musical metaphor:** (see [thesaurus.md](thesaurus.md#bassline--chorus--harmonics) for gameplay details)

| Tier | Name | Association |
|------|------|-------------|
| Basic | [Bassline](thesaurus.md#bassline--chorus--harmonics) | Foundation, low frequency, essential |
| Mid | [Chorus](thesaurus.md#bassline--chorus--harmonics) | Power, harmony, community effort |
| Advanced | [Harmonics](thesaurus.md#bassline--chorus--harmonics) | Complexity, overtones, mastery |

**Crafting tiers (TBD):**

Crafting tier naming is intentionally deferred and will be redesigned alongside itemization/gear.

### Equipment / Items

**Weapons:** Named for the sound they make or enable
- Thunderstrike (loud impact weapon)
- The Screamer (alarm device)
- Bellringer (heavy hammer)

**Tools:** Named for function + acoustic property
- Echoscope (detection device)
- Resonance Probe (Crystal tool)
- Drumwright Kit (instrument repair)

**Artifacts:** Named by discoverers or by effect
- Henri's Silence (the encrypted hard drive — ironic, see [the-truth.md](the-truth.md#the-survivor-cave-descendants-of-the-the-unplugged))
- The Telegram Fragments (chat log pieces from [Sleepless in Decibels](thesaurus.md#sleepless-in-decibels))
- Founder's Drum (original [Unplugged](thesaurus.md#the-unplugged) percussion)

### Buildings / Structures

**[Base](thesaurus.md#the-base) buildings:** Musical/acoustic terms
- Fire Pit (central gathering, sound source)
- [Crystal](thesaurus.md#crystals--resonance-anomalies) Circle (main resonance structure)
- Resonance Chamber (amplification)
- Mix Console (control/efficiency)
- Workshop (crafting)
- Bunks (housing — practical term, affects [Vibes](thesaurus.md#vibes))

**Upgrades:** [Base name] + tier descriptor
- Fire Pit → Bonfire → Beacon
- Workshop → Forge → Foundry

## Factions

### Naming Pattern

Faction names should be:
- **Simple:** One or two words, easy to remember
- **Evocative:** Suggests their philosophy
- **Speakable:** Can be shouted in warning or greeting

| Faction | Name Origin |
|---------|-------------|
| [The Archive](thesaurus.md#the-archive) | They archive knowledge |
| [The Choir](thesaurus.md#the-choir) | Religious, communal singing |
| [The Engineers](thesaurus.md#the-engineers) | Technical, problem-solvers |
| [The Silent](thesaurus.md#the-silent) | Embrace silence (threatening) |
| [The Resonants](thesaurus.md#the-resonants) | Believe in Crystal resonance network |

|---------|--------------|--------------|

| Silent | The Transcended | Aspirant, Initiate |

## Creatures / Enemies

### Naming Philosophy

Enemies should be named for:
- The threat they pose to sound/survival
- Their behavior or appearance
- What survivors call them (folk names)

**Examples:**
- **Dampeners:** Creatures that somehow suppress local sound
- **Echohunters:** Track prey by sound
- **Stillborn:** Mutated creatures from the early Silence
- **Hushlings:** Small, numerous, attracted to noise

### Boss / Unique Enemy Names

Pattern: [Descriptive title] + [Individual name or location]
- The Silent King of Deadquiet
- Echobane the Listener
- The Dampener of Marchand's Pass

## Forbidden / Reserved Terms

**Never use casually:**
- "Silence" (except as threat or historical reference)
- "Quiet" (same)
- "Peace" (has death connotations now)
- "Rest" (ambiguous — could mean death)
- "Still" (same)

**Use with care:**
- "Sleep" (survival requires noise even while sleeping)
- "Alone" (being alone = death)
- "Deaf" (tragic historical association)
