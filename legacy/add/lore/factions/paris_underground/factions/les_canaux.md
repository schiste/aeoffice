# Les Canaux ("The Channels")

> **Quick Reference:** The sewer-dwellers who became Paris's connective tissue — messengers, smugglers, guides, and the only people who can go anywhere.
>
> **Territory:** The sewers — 2,400km of connections beneath all factions
> **Population:** ~4,000-5,000
> **Government:** None (officially). La Confrérie des Canaux (guild-like structure)
> **Founded:** Year 12 AS (emerged from refugees and outcasts)
> **Year 300 Status:** Essential, despised, everywhere

---

## Origin Story

### Day 1: Nobody Chose the Sewers

Nobody woke up on October 14, 2034, and said "I want to live in a sewer."

But people ended up there anyway.

**Who was in the sewers at 5:34 AM:**
- ~300 égoutiers (sewer workers) on night shift
- Unknown number of homeless using access tunnels
- A few urban explorers
- Rats (millions)

When The Silence hit, these weren't survivors — they were afterthoughts. The metro tunnels had people. The catacombs had cataphiles. The sewers had... whoever couldn't fit anywhere else.

### Years 1-10: The Unwanted

**The Refugee Waves:**

| Wave | When | Who |
|------|------|-----|
| First | Weeks 1-4 | Overflow from metro tunnels (too crowded) |
| Second | Months 2-6 | Exiles (kicked out of other groups) |
| Third | Years 1-3 | War refugees (fleeing violence above) |
| Fourth | Years 3-10 | Criminals, outcasts, those with nowhere else |

**Year 1 reality:**
- ~2,000 people scattered through sewer tunnels
- No organization
- No resources
- No respect

**Year 5 innovation:**
Someone realized the sewers connected *everything*. Metro tunnels, catacombs, building basements, every faction's territory. The sewers weren't a place to live — they were roads.

**Year 12: La Confrérie**
The sewer-dwellers formalized into "La Confrérie des Canaux" — a guild of guides, messengers, and smugglers. Anyone could join. Everyone needed them.

### The Great War (Year 78-82)

Les Canaux were neutral — or rather, they sold services to all sides.

**Services provided:**
- Message running (both sides)
- Troop movements (guided, for pay)
- Smuggling (weapons, food, medicine)
- Intelligence (sold to highest bidder)

**The War made them rich.**

When the Treaty of Platform A was signed, Les Canaux had become indispensable. Everyone hated them. Everyone needed them.

## Year 300: The Rats of Paris

### What They Are

Les Canaux aren't a faction in the traditional sense. They don't control territory — they ARE the territory. They don't have borders — they have access. They're the circulatory system of underground Paris.

### Governance: La Confrérie

**Officially:** No government. No leaders. No rules.

**Actually:** La Confrérie is a loose guild structure.

**Structure:**

| Role | Function |
|------|----------|
| **Les Passeurs** | Guides through sewer routes |
| **Les Courriers** | Message runners |
| **Les Porteurs** | Goods transporters |
| **Les Éclaireurs** | Scouts, map-makers |
| **Les Anciens** | Experienced members, informal leaders |

**No hierarchy:**
Les Anciens have influence, not authority. They advise. They mediate disputes. They can't command.

**Entry:**
Anyone can join. You just have to survive the initiation — spending 72 hours alone in the sewers with no guide. Most don't make it. Those who do are "Canaux" forever.

### The Sewer Network

2,400 kilometers of tunnels beneath Paris.

**Main arteries:**
- Grand Égout (under major boulevards)
- Collectors (medium tunnels)
- Branches (smaller passages)
- Service tunnels (tiny, expert only)

**Navigation:**
Les Canaux navigate by:
- Smell (different areas have distinct odors)
- Sound (water flow patterns)
- Touch (wall textures)
- Memory (routes memorized like songs)

Maps exist but are intentionally incomplete. Full knowledge is power.

### Settlements

Les Canaux don't have cities. They have "nodes" — meeting points, rest areas, storage depots.

**Major nodes:**

| Node | Location | Function |
|------|----------|----------|
| **Le Confluent** | Where main collectors meet | Social hub, market |
| **La Cache** | Location secret | Main storage |
| **Les Quais** | Along the Seine underground | Boat staging |
| **Le Carrefour des Rats** | Central location | Meeting point |
| **Les Sources** | Near clean water | Sacred |

## Daily Life

### Living Conditions

**The truth:** Sewer living is hard.

**Environment:**
- Constant water presence (wet everything)
- Smell (you stop noticing after Year 1)
- Disease risk (mitigated by experience)
- Cramped spaces
- Darkness

**Advantages:**
- Temperature stable (13-15°C year-round)
- Access to all Paris
- Safety in numbers
- Freedom

### The Smell

Les Canaux smell. There's no avoiding it.

**Adaptation:**
- They don't notice it anymore
- Other factions definitely notice
- Bathing areas exist (clean water nodes)
- "Sewer perfume" (strong herbs) used for formal occasions

**Social impact:**
Les Canaux are easy to identify. This is both protection (people respect what they represent) and limitation (they're never fully welcome anywhere).

### Movement

Les Canaux don't stay in one place.

**Life pattern:**
- Move constantly
- Follow routes
- Rest at nodes
- Never truly settle

**Why:**
- Staying still means vulnerability
- Movement is income (paid for travel)
- Flexibility is survival

### Food and Water

**Water:**
- Clean water sources exist (filtered groundwater)
- These are sacred, protected, never revealed to outsiders

**Food:**
- Mushrooms (grown in drier tunnels)
- Rats (primary protein source)
- Trade goods (from other factions)
- "What we find" (scavenging)

**Rat cuisine:**
Yes, they eat rats. They've developed extensive recipes:
- Rat jerky (preserved, portable)
- Rat stew (node kitchens)
- "Sewer wine" — fermented mushrooms in... conditions. It's vile. It works.

### Children

Les Canaux have children. It's complicated.

**Birth:**
- Usually at nodes with older women present
- Dangerous (sanitation issues)
- High infant mortality historically (improved over time)

**Childhood:**
- Children learn routes from age 5
- "Path training" — memorizing passages
- By age 12, can navigate major routes alone
- Full Canaux status at 16 (if they survive)

**Education:**
Oral tradition. Routes are taught as songs. History is story.

## Culture & Customs

### The "Rat" Identity

Les Canaux reclaimed "rat" as their symbol.

**Why rats:**
- Rats survive anywhere
- Rats know all the paths
- Rats are underestimated
- Rats are essential (they eat waste, they're food)

**Naming:**
Children receive "rat names" at adolescence:
- Rat-Gris (Gray Rat)
- Queue-Longue (Long Tail)
- Dent-Fine (Sharp Tooth)
- Yeux-Noirs (Black Eyes)

### Philosophy: La Merde

"La merde monte, la merde descend." (Shit rises, shit falls.)

**Meaning:**
- Everything cycles
- Today's powerful are tomorrow's forgotten
- Nothing is permanent
- Acceptance of life's nature

**Practical application:**
- Don't get attached to status
- Don't trust power
- Survive, adapt, flow

### Language

Les Canaux speak standard French but with extensive specialized vocabulary.

**Sewer terms:**

| Term | Meaning |
|------|---------|
| **Couler** | To move (through tunnels) |
| **Le Flux** | The direction/flow |
| **Déboucher** | To emerge (into another territory) |
| **La Source** | Clean water point |
| **Le Barrage** | Blocked passage |
| **Remonter** | To go upstream/toward surface |
| **Plonger** | To go deeper |

**Greetings:**
- "Que tes eaux coulent" (May your waters flow)
- "Bon flux" (Good flow — like "good luck")
- "Je coule" (I'm going — goodbye)

### Navigation Songs

Routes are memorized as songs.

**Example (simplified):**
> "Sous Châtelet, trois fois gauche,
> Suivre le son du grand courant,
> Au rat mort, plonger profond,
> Remonter quand l'eau chante..."

(Under Châtelet, three times left, / Follow the sound of the great current, / At the dead rat, dive deep, / Rise when the water sings...)

These songs are never written. They're taught mouth to ear. Outsiders can't learn them.

### The Grande Traversée

Annual race through the sewers.

**Route:** One end of Paris to the other. Entirely underground.
**Distance:** ~15 kilometers (through sewers, much longer than direct)
**Duration:** Winners complete in 4-6 hours

**Rules:**
- No guides
- No maps
- No surface breaks
- First to emerge at destination wins

**Prize:** Nothing. Prestige. The winner's name is sung into navigation songs for a year.

## Services

### What Les Canaux Sell

| Service | Description | Price Range |
|---------|-------------|-------------|
| **Passage** | Guided travel between factions | Varies by danger |
| **Messaging** | Guaranteed delivery, confidential | Medium |
| **Smuggling** | Goods moved without inspection | High |
| **Intelligence** | Information from across Paris | Very high |
| **Storage** | Secure caches, location secret | Medium |
| **Extraction** | Rescue from dangerous situations | Very high |

### Pricing

Les Canaux don't have fixed prices.

**Factors:**
- Danger of route
- Urgency
- Client's ability to pay
- Relationship with client
- What else they're carrying

**Payment accepted:**
- All faction currencies
- Barter goods
- Favors (dangerous — they remember)
- Information (currency of choice)

### Rules

**The Canaux Code (unwritten but absolute):**

1. **Message delivered is message sealed.** Contents never revealed.
2. **Payment made is contract binding.** No backing out.
3. **Canaux don't take sides.** Serve all, ally with none.
4. **Water sources are sacred.** Never reveal, never pollute.
5. **Canaux protect Canaux.** Internal disputes stay internal.

**Violation:**
Anyone who breaks the code is expelled. Expulsion = no navigation songs. Without songs, they'll die in the tunnels.

## Relations with Other Factions

### The Universal Problem

Les Canaux have the same relationship with everyone: essential and resented.

**Every faction:**
- Needs Les Canaux for services
- Doesn't respect them
- Would prefer they didn't exist
- Can't function without them

### Le Carrefour (Grievance)

Les Canaux provide critical services to Le Carrefour. They move goods, carry messages, provide intelligence.

**The Seat Issue:**
Les Canaux don't have a seat on Le Tribunal du Quai. They've demanded one for 150 years. Every faction has blocked them.

**Argument for:** "We're essential. We should have representation."
**Argument against:** "You're not a faction. You're service providers."

This is the deepest grievance Les Canaux hold.

### La Dalle Éternelle (Useful)

La Dalle uses Les Canaux extensively for:
- Smuggling luxury goods
- Corporate espionage
- Moving goods around tariffs

They pay well. They don't respect.

### Le Grand Nord (Complicated)

Some Les Canaux workers tried to unionize. Le Grand Nord supported this. Other Canaux opposed it (unions = hierarchy = against the code).

Result: Ongoing internal debate. Le Grand Nord is sympathetic but confused.

### Les Ossuaires (Professional Respect)

Two groups who know tunnels. Mutual recognition.

**Status:** Good relations. They share some border areas. Trade happens. Professional respect.

### Le Palais Profond (Transactional)

Le Palais Profond uses Les Canaux for discreet services. They pay in francs (Les Canaux accept them as curiosities).

### L'Est Profond (Friendly)

L'Est Profond treats Canaux better than most. Some Les Canaux have adopted L'Est-Parlé. Cultural exchange happens.

## Key Locations

### Le Confluent

The main social hub. Where major collectors meet beneath central Paris.

**Features:**
- Widest open space in the sewers
- Informal market
- Meeting ground for negotiations
- The "Council of Anciens" convenes here

### La Cache

Main storage depot. Location closely guarded.

**Features:**
- Preserved goods
- Emergency supplies
- Faction secrets (documents, artifacts held for payment)
- The "Archive of Debts" — who owes what to whom

### Les Quais du Souterrain

Boat staging area along underground Seine connections.

**Features:**
- The only boats in Paris (small rafts)
- Used for bulk transport
- The "Seine Runners" operate here

### Les Sources

Clean water locations. Sacred.

**Features:**
- Filtered groundwater
- Never revealed to outsiders
- Guarded absolutely
- Religious significance

## Notable Figures

### "La Doyenne" (The Elder)

Age unknown (claims 90+). The oldest living Canaux.

**Background:** Says she was an égoutier in Year 1. May be true. Certainly knows routes nobody else does.

**Personality:** Cryptic, demanding, occasionally prophetic. Speaks in riddles that turn out to be navigation instructions.

**Known for:**
- Knowing passages "that don't exist"
- Training three generations of Passeurs
- The saying: "The sewers remember what Paris forgets"

### Rat-Rapide (Fast Rat)

28 years old. Fastest messenger in Paris.

**Background:** Born in the tunnels. Never lived anywhere else. Can traverse Paris end-to-end in 3 hours.

**Personality:** Hyperactive, unreliable in conversation, absolutely reliable in delivery.

**Known for:**
- Winning Grande Traversée five times
- Never losing a message
- Speaking in sewer terms even when unnecessary

### Confluent-Marie

45 years old. Unofficial "mayor" of Le Confluent.

**Background:** Came to the sewers as an exile from La Dalle (embezzlement). Rose to prominence through negotiation skills.

**Personality:** Shrewd, diplomatic, still thinks like a corporate accountant.

**Known for:**
- Standardizing Canaux pricing (controversial)
- Negotiating the Year 278 trade agreement with Les Ossuaires
- The only Canaux who bathes daily

### L'Invisible

Age unknown. Gender unknown. Possibly multiple people.

**Legend:** The greatest spy in Paris. Works for Les Canaux exclusively. Has never been seen.

**Reality:** Unknown. The name is used to attribute intelligence gathering. May be one person, may be a collective, may be fictional.

**Known for:**
- Information appearing without explanation
- Faction secrets revealed before they happen
- Never being caught

## Absurdist Details

### Things That Are Normal for Canaux

1. **Sewer wine is a delicacy.** It tastes like despair fermented in regret. They're proud of it.

2. **Children named after tunnel junctions.** "This is my son, Confluent-Seine-Bièvre." "Beautiful name." "Thank you, we named him after where he was conceived."

3. **Navigation by smell.** "Turn left at the sulfur, right at the decay, straight through the rot." This is actual navigation.

4. **Rat is haute cuisine.** There are 47 documented ways to prepare rat. Debates about the proper technique are fierce.

5. **They name boats.** The boats are barely-functional rafts. Each has a name, a history, a personality. Boat naming ceremonies are serious.

6. **Grande Traversée winners are celebrities.** For one year, their name is in every navigation song. They're effectively immortalized.

7. **The Council of Anciens meets in raw sewage.** Not by choice — it's the largest open space. Everyone pretends not to notice.

8. **"Sewer formal" dress exists.** For diplomatic occasions: clean clothes, strong herbs, fresh rat-skin accessories.

## Quest Hooks

### The Lost Route

**Hook:** An ancient route beneath the Seine has been rediscovered. It connects Paris to... somewhere outside.

**Reality:** The route is real. It leads to the Île de la Cité, then beyond. Where it goes is unknown.

**Implications:** Whoever controls this route controls access outside Paris.

### The Seat Demand

**Hook:** Les Canaux are threatening to strike — no services for any faction until they get a Tribunal seat.

**Reality:** The threat is real. Paris would collapse within weeks without Canaux services.

**Implications:** Every faction must decide: grant the seat or face chaos.

### The Poisoned Source

**Hook:** One of the sacred water sources has been poisoned. Les Canaux are dying.

**Reality:** Deliberate attack. By whom? Why?

**Implications:** Les Canaux will tear Paris apart to find the responsible party.

### The Stolen Archive

**Hook:** La Cache was raided. The "Archive of Debts" — who owes what to whom — was taken.

**Reality:** Whoever has it has leverage over everyone.

**Implications:** Blackmail, chaos, power shifts. The Canaux want it back.

### L'Invisible's Request

**Hook:** L'Invisible contacts the Hero directly. They want something. They're offering something.

**Reality:** Unknown. L'Invisible has never made direct contact before.

**Implications:** Whatever this is, it's important enough to break all precedent.
