# Les Montparnos ("The Montparnasse Ones")

> **Quick Reference:** Intellectuals, artists, and philosophers who preserved the Left Bank's café culture — in a metro station.
>
> **Territory:** South-Centre — Montparnasse station complex, connecting tunnels
> **Population:** ~8,000-10,000
> **Government:** L'Agora (assembly of intellectuals, theoretically)
> **Founded:** Year 6 AS (from Montparnasse survivors)
> **Year 300 Status:** Culturally pretentious, philosophically rich, practically chaotic

---

## Origin Story

### Before The Silence: The Left Bank

Montparnasse was legendary. Since the 1920s — Hemingway, Picasso, Sartre, de Beauvoir — it was Paris's intellectual heart. Cafés where ideas were born. Galleries where art was made. Streets where revolution was planned.

By 2034, the neighborhood had gentrified but retained its soul: universities, publishers, small theaters, philosophical cafés, artists too stubborn to move.

**Who was there at 5:34 AM:**
- Gare Montparnasse (mainline station): Early commuters, railway workers
- Metro Montparnasse-Bienvenüe: Intersection of lines 4, 6, 12, 13
- The neighborhood: Night owls from bars, philosophy students pulling all-nighters, artists in studios
- The cemeteries: Montparnasse Cemetery above, catacombs below

When The Silence hit, the intellectuals survived by accident and stayed by choice.

### Day 1: The Thinkers

**First hour:**
- Chaos in the station (like everywhere)
- But then: Someone started organizing, because that's what academics do

**First day:**
A philosophy professor named **Jean-Marc Legrand** — tenured at Paris-Sorbonne, specialist in existentialism — stood on a bench and declared:

> "We are confronted with the absurd. We must choose: despair or revolt. I choose revolt. Who joins me?"

About 200 people did. They were the nucleus of Les Montparnos.

### Year 1-10: The Philosophical Survival

Les Montparnos didn't just survive — they theorized survival.

**The early approach:**
- Form committees (naturally)
- Debate everything (excessively)
- Create frameworks (endlessly)
- Actually do things (eventually)

**The "Manifeste de la Station" (Year 3):**
A founding document combining:
- Existentialist philosophy (we create our own meaning)
- Anarchist organization (no permanent leaders)
- Artistic commitment (culture is survival)
- Intellectual rigor (everything must be debated)

It's 847 pages long. Nobody has read all of it. Everyone cites it.

### The Great War (Year 78-82)

Les Montparnos tried to stay neutral. They wrote manifestos against war. They published pamphlets.

**Year 79: The "Declaration of Intellectual Neutrality"**

Les Montparnos issued a formal statement declaring war "intellectually unjustifiable" and refused to participate.

Both sides ignored them. But they weren't attacked either — Les Montparnos had nothing anyone wanted, and their territory was hard to hold.

After the Treaty of Platform A, Les Montparnos were recognized as a faction mostly because nobody could figure out how to classify them otherwise.

## Year 300: The Café Republic

### What They Became

Les Montparnos preserved Left Bank café culture underground. They argue about everything, theorize constantly, produce art prolifically, and get things done only when absolutely necessary.

**The irony they recognize but can't escape:**
They became what they studied. Sartre wrote about bad faith — they live it. They debate authenticity — while performing inauthenticity. They know this. They talk about it constantly. They don't change.

### Governance: L'Agora

**Official structure:**
A direct democracy where all citizens can speak and vote.

**Actual structure:**
Whoever talks loudest and longest wins.

**How it works:**
1. Issue raised in L'Agora
2. Someone gives a speech (20 minutes minimum)
3. Someone else rebuts (20 minutes minimum)
4. Discussion opens (hours)
5. Meta-discussion about the discussion (more hours)
6. Vote called (eventually)
7. Vote challenged on procedural grounds (frequently)
8. Debate about whether the challenge is valid (endlessly)
9. Something gets decided (maybe)

**Average time to pass legislation:** Unknown. Some debates have been ongoing since Year 34.

**The Perpetual Debates:**
| Topic | Ongoing Since | Status |
|-------|---------------|--------|
| "What is authentic survival?" | Year 6 | Active |
| "Should we have leaders?" | Year 12 | Active |
| "Is mushroom farming labor or art?" | Year 45 | Active |
| "What constitutes 'good' music?" | Year 67 | Active |
| "Are we really better than La Dalle?" | Year 102 | Active |

### Social Structure

**Officially:** No hierarchy. Everyone is equal.

**Actually:** Prestige hierarchy based on intellectual reputation.

| Status | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| **Les Penseurs** | Recognized intellectuals (self-declared, but some are respected) |
| **Les Artistes** | Practicing artists (visual, performance, literary) |
| **Les Étudiants** | Students of penseurs (endless, never graduate) |
| **Les Cafetiers** | Those who maintain the spaces (secretly most powerful) |
| **Les Travailleurs** | Those who do actual work (ironically respected) |

## Daily Life

### The Café System

Les Montparnos organized around cafés. Each café is:
- A social space
- A political unit
- An intellectual faction
- A home

**Major cafés:**

| Café | Philosophy | Clientele |
|------|------------|-----------|
| **Le Deux Magots Souterrain** | Classical existentialism | Traditionalists |
| **La Rotonde Profonde** | Neo-absurdism | Avant-garde |
| **Le Dôme des Ombres** | Nihilism (ironic) | Youth |
| **Le Select du Métro** | Pragmatic philosophy | Workers |
| **La Coupole de la Dalle** | Post-structuralism | Nobody understands them |

**Café loyalty:**
Your café is your identity. Switching cafés is social death. Inter-café debates are fierce.

### The Daily Schedule

**Morning (theoretical):**
- Wake up
- Coffee substitute (mushroom-based, called "le faux noir")
- Morning debate at café (2 hours minimum)

**Midday:**
- Work (if you must)
- Lunch (arguments about food are philosophical)
- More debate

**Afternoon:**
- Art creation
- Art critique (more heated than creation)
- Reading, writing, theorizing

**Evening:**
- L'Agora sessions (if scheduled)
- Performance (theater, music, poetry)
- Night debates (the serious ones)

### Work

**Attitude toward work:**
Work is necessary but philosophically suspect. Manual labor is "authentic" (Marxist influence). Intellectual labor is "essential" (Montparno self-justification).

**Who actually works:**
- Mushroom farmers (respected as "authentic laborers")
- Craftspeople (respected as "artisan-philosophers")
- Teachers (respected as "transmitters of wisdom")
- Café workers (secretly the elite — they control space and coffee)

**Who avoids work:**
- Penseurs (too busy thinking)
- Some artists (too busy creating)
- Students (eternal)

### Education

**Philosophy:** Everyone should learn everything. Forever.

**Structure:**
- No formal schools
- Mentorship model (student attaches to penseur)
- Seminars held in cafés
- "Graduation" is impossible — you're always learning

**What's taught:**
- Philosophy (obviously)
- Art (all forms)
- History (pre-Silence)
- Science (what they remember)
- Debate technique (most important)

**Eternal students:**
Some "students" are 60 years old. This is normal.

## Culture & Customs

### The Arts

Art is survival. Survival is art. Everything is art.

**Literary production:**
- Pamphlets (constant)
- Manifestos (frequent)
- Poetry (ubiquitous)
- Novels (rare — finishing things is bourgeois)
- Philosophy texts (endless, unread)

**Visual art:**
- Murals on station walls
- Sculpture from found materials
- "Installations" (things arranged in ways that mean something)

**Performance:**
- Theater (existentialist plays mostly)
- Poetry readings (mandatory attendance)
- Music (contested — what is "real" music?)
- "Happenings" (spontaneous, bewildering)

### The Montparnasse Debates

Formalized debate as entertainment.

**Format:**
- Two speakers
- One topic
- No time limit (debates have lasted 72 hours)
- Audience votes winner
- Winner gets prestige; loser gets "development opportunity"

**Famous debate topics:**
- "Is survival itself bad faith?" (Year 67)
- "Does art require audience?" (Year 112)
- "Are Les Ossuaires more authentic than us?" (Year 203, caused scandal)
- "Is this debate itself meaningful?" (Year 287, ongoing)

### Fashion

**Philosophy:** Fashion is self-expression. But expressing too much care is bourgeois.

**Result:** Intentionally disheveled aesthetic.

**Standard look:**
- Worn clothing (new clothes hidden until appropriately aged)
- "Intellectual" accessories (glasses real or fake, scarves)
- Paint stains (for artists, deliberate)
- Coffee stains (for penseurs, authentic)

**Black:** Wearing all black is traditional. Wearing color is "making a statement."

### Language

Les Montparnos speak French elevated to incomprehensibility.

**Features:**
- Philosophical jargon (constant)
- German loanwords (Hegel's influence)
- Invented terms (each penseur creates their own)
- Ironic deployment of clichés

**Sample phrases:**
- "The ontological implications of your mushroom stew suggest a fundamental misunderstanding of being-in-the-world."
- "I find your assertion interesting but phenomenologically naive."
- "Let's not fall into the trap of binaries here."
- "Surely you're not suggesting a return to metaphysical essentialism?"

**Nobody knows what any of this means. Including Les Montparnos.**

## Military & Defense

### The Philosophical Objection to Violence

**Official position:** Violence is intellectually unjustifiable.

**Actual position:** Violence is intellectually unjustifiable but occasionally necessary.

**Defense structure:**
- "La Garde Philosophique" (~500 people)
- Nominally volunteers defending the community
- In practice, those who couldn't talk their way out of duty

### Historical Defense

**The Commune Raid (Year 156):**
Raiders from the surface (somewhere) attacked Les Montparnos.

**Response:**
- Penseurs debated appropriate response (3 hours)
- Raiders grew bored waiting
- Garde Philosophique finally engaged
- Raiders defeated through "disorganized but enthusiastic" resistance

**Casualties:** 47 Montparnos, ~60 raiders
**Lesson learned:** Debates must be suspended during active attack (controversial ruling)

### Weapons

Standard batons, improvised weapons, and "the word."

**"La Parole comme Arme" (The Word as Weapon):**
Les Montparnos believe in talking enemies into submission. This has worked exactly once (Year 201, when a confused raider was lectured for 6 hours and left voluntarily).

## Economy

### Production

| Product | Output | Notes |
|---------|--------|-------|
| Art | High | Mostly unsellable |
| Ideas | Unlimited | Mostly untradeable |
| Texts | Prolific | Mostly unread |
| Teachers | Available | Other factions hire them |
| Performers | Available | Popular at Le Carrefour |
| Coffee substitute | Decent | Their one practical achievement |

### Trade

**What they export:**
- Teachers (paid well by other factions)
- Performers (entertainment value)
- Art (prestige pieces)
- Written works (niche market)

**What they import:**
- Everything practical (food, materials, tools)

### Currency

They reject currency as "commodification of existence."

**Result:** They use Le Carrefour market tokens while complaining about it.

**Internal economy:** Prestige, favors, café credit

## Key Locations

### Gare Montparnasse-Bienvenüe

The main station complex. Central hub.

**Features:**
- L'Agora (main assembly hall)
- The Archives (collected texts, constantly expanding)
- "La Galerie" (permanent art exhibition)
- The Cafés (the real power centers)

### Le Cimetière Souterrain

Connection to catacombs near Montparnasse Cemetery.

**Features:**
- Burial spaces (some choose catacomb interment)
- "Le Jardin des Penseurs" — memorial to deceased intellectuals
- Philosophical significance (death studies are popular)

### Les Ateliers

Artist workshops in converted service tunnels.

**Features:**
- Studios (individual and collective)
- Performance spaces
- Experimental zones (where "happenings" happen)

### La Bibliothèque Profonde

The underground library.

**Features:**
- ~3,000 pre-Silence books (sacred)
- ~15,000 post-Silence texts (mostly unread)
- Reading rooms
- Debate halls

## Notable Figures

### "Le Doyen" Maurice Legrand

87 years old. Grandson of founder Jean-Marc Legrand.

**Background:** Born in the tunnels, never been anywhere else. Considers himself the inheritor of his grandfather's vision.

**Personality:** Pompous, brilliant, insufferable. Speaks in paragraphs. Has opinions about everything.

**Known for:**
- The "Legrand Synthesis" — attempt to combine all Montparno philosophies (failed, but impressive)
- Never losing a debate (people stop arguing with him out of exhaustion)
- Referring to other factions as "philosophically underdeveloped"

### Simone Chen (Lead Artist)

52 years old. Most celebrated visual artist.

**Background:** Third-generation Montparno-Chinese descent. Pioneered "Infrastructure Art" — using metro materials as medium.

**Personality:** Intense, perfectionist, surprisingly practical for an artist.

**Known for:**
- "The Great Mural of Station 6" — took 12 years to complete
- Refusing to explain her work ("If you understood it, I failed")
- Actually finishing projects (rare and respected)

### "Petit Jean" (The Revolutionary)

29 years old. Leader of the "young radicals."

**Background:** Impatient with Montparno debates. Wants action.

**Personality:** Charismatic, impractical, dangerous. Talks about "real change."

**Known for:**
- The "Year 296 Manifesto" — called for armed confrontation with La Dalle
- A following among frustrated youth
- Being watched nervously by L'Agora

### Madame Café (Anonymous)

Age unknown. Owner of Le Deux Magots Souterrain.

**Background:** Nobody knows her real name. She's always been there. She might be immortal.

**Personality:** Observant, cryptic, absolutely in control of her space.

**Known for:**
- Knowing everyone's secrets
- The best coffee substitute in Paris
- Never speaking in debates but deciding outcomes anyway

## Absurdist Details

### Things Only Les Montparnos Consider Normal

1. **72-hour debates about lunch.** "Is eating alone more authentic than eating communally?" This took three days. They still eat both ways.

2. **Artists refusing to explain their art.** A muralist was asked what her painting meant. She threw paint at the questioner. This is considered a valid response.

3. **The "Ongoing Referendum."** There's a vote that's been "in progress" since Year 89. Nobody remembers what it's about. Voting continues.

4. **Publishing unfinished works.** Finishing a book is bourgeois. Most texts end mid-sentence. This is intentional.

5. **Café warfare.** Le Deux Magots and La Rotonde haven't spoken since the "Sartre Interpretation Schism of Year 167." Nobody remembers what the schism was about.

6. **Fake glasses.** ~40% of glasses-wearers have perfect vision. The glasses signal intellectualism.

7. **"Spontaneous" performances.** Carefully rehearsed for weeks, then presented as improvised.

8. **Hating on La Dalle while secretly admiring their organization.** The most forbidden thought in Les Montparnos.

## Relations with Other Factions

### La Dalle Éternelle (Contempt)

Les Montparnos find La Dalle philosophically bankrupt. Corporate culture is "bad faith incarnate."

**Status:** Minimal direct contact. Trade through Le Carrefour.

### Le Grand Nord (Respect, But)

Les Montparnos appreciate worker solidarity. But they find unions "insufficiently theorized."

**Status:** Occasional collaboration. Le Grand Nord finds them annoying.

### Les Ossuaires (Fascination)

The bone-keepers represent "authentic being-toward-death." Les Montparnos write extensively about them.

**Status:** Good relations. Les Ossuaires find the attention baffling.

### Le Carrefour (Necessary)

Les Montparnos need Le Carrefour for trade. They have a Tribunal seat.

**Status:** Active participation, constant complaints about "commodification."

### L'Est Profond (Cultural Kinship)

Both factions value culture. They collaborate on festivals and exchanges.

**Status:** Good relations. L'Est finds them pretentious; Les Montparnos find L'Est "refreshingly untheorized."

### Le Palais Profond (Mixed)

Both value tradition and bureaucracy (in different ways).

**Status:** Polite distance. Occasional intellectual exchanges.

### Les Canaux (Useful)

Les Canaux provide services. Les Montparnos pay and philosophize about the transaction.

**Status:** Transactional, over-analyzed.

## Quest Hooks

### The Stolen Manuscript

**Hook:** A pre-Silence manuscript — possibly an unpublished Sartre work — was stolen from La Bibliothèque.

**Reality:** It's real and priceless (to Les Montparnos).

**Implications:** Every faction would pay for it. Who took it? Why?

### The Schism

**Hook:** Le Deux Magots and La Rotonde are on the verge of open conflict. The "New Authenticity Debate" has turned violent.

**Reality:** Two cafés about to go to war over philosophy.

**Implications:** Could split Les Montparnos entirely. Needs mediation.

### Petit Jean's Revolution

**Hook:** The young radicals are planning something. Arms are being gathered.

**Reality:** A genuine coup attempt against L'Agora is forming.

**Implications:** Could destabilize or transform Les Montparnos.

### The Unfinished Text

**Hook:** A dying penseur wants to finish their life's work before death. They need help from outside.

**Reality:** The work might actually be important. Or it might be 800 pages of nonsense.

**Implications:** Philosophical treasure or elaborate waste of time?

### The Café Spy

**Hook:** Someone in Les Montparnos is passing information to La Dalle.

**Reality:** Corporate infiltration for unknown purposes.

**Implications:** Trust breakdown. Investigation required.
