# Les Ossuaires ("The Bone-Keepers")

> **Quick Reference:** Catacombs dwellers who share their home with 6 million dead — living tenants of a skeletal landlord.
>
> **Territory:** South — The Catacombs and Grand Réseau Sud
> **Population:** ~10,000-14,000
> **Government:** The Warrens (decentralized clan system)
> **Founded:** Day 1 (evolved from cataphiles)
> **Year 300 Status:** Mysterious, feared, essential

---

## Origin Story

### Before The Silence: The Cataphiles

The catacombs were already inhabited before The Silence. Not officially, but continuously.

**Who was down there at 5:34 AM on October 14, 2034:**
- **Cataphiles:** Urban explorers who treated the catacombs as their playground. Illegal raves, art installations, underground parties. Maybe 200-500 at any given night.
- **Homeless:** Those who knew the access points used the tunnels for shelter. Warmer in winter, cooler in summer, invisible always.
- **The Curious:** Artists, photographers, filmmakers, seekers of the strange.
- **The Dead:** 6 million Parisians, stacked in neat walls since the 1780s.

The cataphiles knew every tunnel, every passage, every hidden water source. They had maps painted on walls with phosphorescent paint. They had secret entrances. They had community.

What they didn't have was food.

### Day 1: The Living Join the Dead

When The Silence hit, the cataphiles had a unique advantage: they were already underground, already organized, already used to darkness.

**First week:**
- Cataphiles sealed known entrances against panicked surface refugees
- Scouted for supplies in basements above
- Made contact with metro tunnel survivors
- Established "the First Covenant" — anyone who knows the tunnels can stay

**First month:**
- Food ran out
- First surface raids (dangerous, deadly)
- Discovery of mushroom cultivation possibility
- First deaths from silence (those who wandered too far alone)

**First year:**
- Population stabilized at ~2,000
- Warren system emerged (tunnel sections became clans)
- The dead became neighbors, not just scenery
- "Mort-parler" dialect began forming

### The Philosophy of Living with the Dead

When you share your home with 6 million skeletons, your relationship with death changes.

**The First Keeper, Marianne Duval**, was a 34-year-old artist who led illegal cataphile tours before The Silence. In Year 3, she articulated what became the Ossuaire philosophy:

> "We are not the owners here. The dead came first. We live in their house. We keep their bones. We are tenants, and we pay our rent in respect."

This wasn't mysticism — it was pragmatism. The bones couldn't be moved. The tunnels were cramped. You either made peace with the skulls watching you sleep, or you went mad.

By Year 50, it had become something like religion.

## Year 300: The Bone Culture

### What They Became

300 years of living with 6 million skeletons created something unique. Les Ossuaires don't worship the dead — they coexist with them. The bones are furniture, markers, company. Death isn't feared; it's accepted as a roommate.

This attitude terrifies other factions.

### Governance: The Warrens

There is no central government. Les Ossuaires organize by "warren" — a tunnel section controlled by an extended family group.

**Warren structure:**
- ~30-50 warrens total
- Each warren: 100-500 people
- Warren boundaries marked by bone arrangements
- Internal governance varies (some democratic, some patriarchal, some consensus)

**The Bone Council:**

For issues affecting all Ossuaires, the Bone Council meets. It's held in the largest ossuary chamber — walls of skulls surrounding a central space.

- One representative per warren
- Decisions require consensus (majority isn't enough)
- Meetings can last days
- The Council has no enforcement power — it's advisory

**Leadership inheritance:**

When a warren leader dies, their bones are placed in a prominent location. The new leader is chosen based on proximity — literally, whoever the old leader's bones are placed next to becomes the heir.

This sounds mystical. It's actually practical — the dying leader's family arranges the bones to indicate their preferred successor. But the ritual has taken on weight.

### Social Structure

There is no formal class system, but some roles carry weight:

| Role | Status | Responsibility |
|------|--------|----------------|
| **Cartographes** | Highest | Map the tunnels, maintain navigation |
| **Gardiens des Os** | High | Maintain the bone walls, arrange the dead |
| **Passeurs** | High | Guide outsiders, guard entrances |
| **Cultivateurs** | Middle | Mushroom farmers |
| **Artisans** | Middle | Craftspeople |
| **Veilleurs** | Middle | Sound-keepers, drummers |
| **Enfants des Os** | Everyone | Children are communal responsibility |

The Cartographes are the closest thing to aristocracy. Their knowledge is irreplaceable.

## Daily Life

### The Tunnels

The catacombs are not one thing — they're many:

**The Official Catacombs:** The tourist section (closed, of course) with its neat bone walls. Ossuaires live adjacent to this, using it as ceremonial space.

**The Grand Réseau Sud (GRS):** The southern tunnel network — old quarries, passages, crawlspaces. Hundreds of kilometers. This is where most Ossuaires live.

**The Connections:** Les Ossuaires have carved passages to metro tunnels, sewers, building basements. They can go almost anywhere unseen.

### Navigation

Navigating the catacombs without training is suicide. Les Ossuaires have developed methods:

**Maps:**
- Painted on walls with phosphorescent mushroom paste
- Coded — outsiders can't read them
- Each warren maintains its own section
- Cartographes oversee the master map (memorized, never written)

**Bone markers:**
- Skull arrangements indicate directions
- A skull facing left means "turn left"
- Specific patterns indicate danger, water, farms
- Outsiders see random bones; Ossuaires see highway signs

**Smell:**
- Different areas have different smells
- Water, farms, habitation, death
- Experienced Ossuaires can navigate by scent alone

### Living Spaces

| Space Type | Description |
|------------|-------------|
| **Warrens** | Carved chambers, 2-3m ceilings, bone-reinforced walls |
| **Galleries** | Larger communal spaces for gatherings |
| **Farms** | Chambers with controlled humidity for mushrooms |
| **Cisterns** | Water collection points (filtered groundwater) |
| **The Bone Halls** | Ceremonial spaces, walls of stacked dead |

**A typical warren home:**
- 15-30 square meters
- Carved stone shelves for sleeping
- Bone hooks for belongings
- A "sound corner" — small drum or chime to maintain noise
- Phosphorescent paint indicating exits

### Food

**Primary:** Mushrooms (many varieties cultivated)
**Secondary:** Rats (yes, they eat them)
**Tertiary:** Trade goods from surface/metro factions
**Luxury:** Anything else

Les Ossuaires are the best mushroom farmers in Paris. Their conditions are perfect — constant temperature, high humidity, no sunlight. They trade surplus for everything else they need.

### The Sound Problem

The catacombs are quiet. Dangerously quiet.

**Solutions:**
- **Water features:** Dripping water channeled to create constant sound
- **Wind chimes:** Bone chimes hung at air passages
- **Drums:** Rotating duty, never silent
- **Singing:** Humming, chanting, or talking expected always
- **The Whisperers:** Children trained to make constant quiet noise

### Language: Mort-Parler

After 300 years, Les Ossuaires speak a dialect other factions barely understand.

**Components:**
- Base French
- Verlan (French backslang, popular pre-Silence)
- Cataphile jargon
- Invented words for catacomb-specific concepts
- Bone metaphors for everything

**Sample vocabulary:**

| Mort-Parler | Standard French | English |
|-------------|-----------------|---------|
| "Les Crâniers" | Les ancêtres | The ancestors (lit. "the skull-folk") |
| "Faire la dalle" | Manger | To eat |
| "Aller à l'os" | Mourir | To die (lit. "to go to bone") |
| "Terrier" | Maison | Home (lit. "burrow") |
| "Lumière morte" | Phosphorescence | Glow-paint |
| "Raconter les os" | Expliquer | To explain (lit. "to tell the bones") |
| "Chair neuve" | Un étranger | A stranger (lit. "new flesh") |
| "Mâche-pierre" | Un idiot | An idiot (lit. "stone-chewer") |

**Common phrases:**

- "Excuse les vivants" — "Excuse the living" (excuse me)
- "Que tes os reposent bien" — "May your bones rest well" (goodbye)
- "Les Crâniers écoutent" — "The skulls are listening" (be careful what you say)
- "On naît chair, on meurt os" — "Born flesh, die bone" (philosophical acceptance)

## Culture & Customs

### Death Rituals

Death is treated differently here.

**When someone dies:**
1. The body is laid in a "transition chamber" for 3 days
2. Family and friends visit, speak to the deceased, say goodbye
3. On day 3, the "Gardiens des Os" arrive
4. The body is processed (details vary by warren, but the result is bones)
5. Bones are cleaned, blessed, and added to the walls
6. The deceased's skull is placed in a location of honor — near family, near home
7. A small marker indicates their name and dates

**The "Future Spot":**
Every Ossuaire knows where their bones will rest. This spot is chosen in adolescence during a coming-of-age ritual. Birthdays are celebrated at your future spot — a picnic with the dead, a reminder of mortality, a way to make death familiar.

### Nuit Blanche ("White Night")

Once a year, Les Ossuaires attempt to stay silent as long as possible.

**How it works:**
- All drums stop
- All chimes are stilled
- Everyone sits in absolute darkness and silence
- The goal is to experience what the dead experience

**Duration:**
- Record is 47 minutes (Year 186)
- Most years, someone breaks at 20-30 minutes
- Children are exempt (kept in sound-sealed chambers)

**Why:**
- To honor the dead
- To face fear
- To prove they can survive what others can't
- To "listen to what the bones hear"

**Other factions' reaction:** Absolute terror. The idea of voluntarily sitting in silence is insane. Ossuaire delegations to Le Carrefour are watched nervously.

### Art

Les Ossuaires are artists. Always have been.

**Bone art:**
- Sculptures made from bones (with consent — only using ancient bones or those of people who requested it)
- Arrangements — skulls positioned to create patterns, faces, scenes
- Jewelry — small bone fragments polished into beads

**Wall art:**
- Phosphorescent murals depicting catacomb life
- Maps that double as artwork
- Historical scenes painted across cavern walls

**Music:**
- Unique instruments made from bones and stone
- The "skull-drum" — a drum made with bone frame
- The "rib-harp" — stringed instrument using rib bones
- Haunting vocal harmonies that echo through tunnels

### Family Structure

Family is warren-based but flexible.

**Marriage:** Partnership declarations made before the Bone Council representative. No ceremony required — just public acknowledgment.

**Children:** Raised communally within the warren. "It takes a village" is literal — every adult is responsible for every child.

**Names:** Children receive three names:
1. Personal name (chosen by parents)
2. Warren name (which warren they're from)
3. Bone name (given at adolescence, based on which ancestor's bones they're placed near)

Example: **Lucien des Grands-Augustins, os-de-Marianne** — Lucien, from the Grands-Augustins warren, bones-of-Marianne (connected to First Keeper Marianne Duval's lineage).

## Military & Defense

### Strategy: Invisibility

Les Ossuaires don't fight wars. They disappear.

**If attacked:**
- Defenders melt into side tunnels
- Attackers get lost
- Attackers die of silence
- Les Ossuaires return when it's over

No faction has ever successfully invaded the catacombs. The few who tried during the Great War found themselves in an infinite maze with no sound, no light, and 6 million skulls watching them die.

### The Passeurs

The closest thing to a military. Passeurs guard entrances, guide authorized visitors, and eliminate threats.

**Skills:**
- Perfect navigation in darkness
- Silent movement
- Ambush tactics
- Knowledge of every entrance and exit

**Weapons:**
- Knives (essential, used constantly)
- Crossbows (rarely — sound is precious)
- Traps (the tunnels themselves are weaponized)

### Defensive Infrastructure

The catacombs are booby-trapped. Not everywhere, but in key locations:

- Collapsible ceilings (pull a rope, the tunnel caves in)
- Sound traps (chambers that absorb noise — death zones)
- Misdirection (false paths leading to dead ends)
- The "Bone Gates" — walls of skulls that can be collapsed to block passages

## Economy

### What They Have

| Resource | Status |
|----------|--------|
| Mushrooms | Abundant (best in Paris) |
| Water | Sufficient (filtered groundwater) |
| Space | Abundant (endless tunnels) |
| Knowledge | Irreplaceable (catacomb navigation) |

### What They Need

| Resource | Source |
|----------|--------|
| Protein | Trade, rats, occasional surface hunting |
| Metal | Trade |
| Textiles | Trade |
| Medicine | Trade, some cultivation |

### Trade

Les Ossuaires trade mushrooms and passage for everything else.

**Passage rights:** Les Canaux may control the sewers, but Les Ossuaires control the catacombs. Anyone wanting to move through southern Paris unseen pays them.

**Guide services:** Getting through the catacombs without an Ossuaire guide is suicide. They charge accordingly.

**Mushroom exports:** High-quality, consistent, diverse. The best in Paris.

### Currency

They use **bone tokens** — small, polished bone chips marked with warren symbols.

Each warren issues its own tokens, but all are accepted. The value is backed by mushroom production — each token is redeemable for a set amount of food.

## Key Locations

### La Grande Galerie

The main ceremonial space. A vast natural cavern reinforced with bone walls.

- Bone Council meets here
- Major celebrations held here
- The "Wall of Names" — every Ossuaire who has died inscribed on bone tablets

### Les Champignonnières

The mushroom farms. Spread across multiple chambers.

- Climate-controlled through ventilation
- Rotating crops
- The "Gardiens des Champignons" maintain them
- Outsiders rarely see these — too valuable

### L'Ossuaire Central

The original tourist catacombs. Now sacred space.

- The neatest bone walls
- Where Nuit Blanche is observed
- Where the First Keeper's bones rest
- Visitors from other factions are received here

### Les Passages Secrets

The hidden entrances. Dozens of them.

- To metro tunnels
- To building basements
- To the sewers
- Locations known only to Passeurs and Cartographes

### Le Puits des Morts ("The Well of the Dead")

A vertical shaft that connects to unknown depths.

- Used for disposing of enemies (rarely)
- Said to reach groundwater
- Considered haunted
- No one has explored it and returned

## Notable Figures

### Émile Duval (Elder of the Bone Council)

73 years old. Direct descendant of First Keeper Marianne Duval.

**Background:** Born in the catacombs, has never been to the surface. The oldest living Ossuaire. Knows more tunnel routes than anyone alive.

**Personality:** Serene, ancient, slightly unsettling. Speaks in bone metaphors constantly. May or may not be senile — hard to tell.

**Known for:**
- Survived Nuit Blanche for 42 minutes (personal record)
- Mediated the Warren Dispute of Year 267
- His "predictions" are suspiciously accurate
- May be slightly psychic (or just very observant)

### Sylvie des Catacombes (War Chief of the Passeurs)

38 years old. Leader of Passeur operations.

**Background:** Grew up in a border warren. Lost her brother to Le Grand Nord raiders in Year 289. Took leadership through sheer competence.

**Personality:** Cold, efficient, protective. Never smiles. Kills without hesitation. Loves children and mushroom soup.

**Known for:**
- The "Year 289 Retaliation" — 7 Le Grand Nord raiders entered the catacombs. None returned.
- Training regime that other factions consider torture
- The only person who's explored Le Puits des Morts and returned (won't discuss it)

### Père Thomas (The Heretic Priest)

61 years old. A curiosity — an actual Catholic priest who lives in the catacombs.

**Background:** His grandfather was a priest who survived Day 1. The family maintained the faith. Thomas was "ordained" by his dying father (validity: questionable).

**Personality:** Gentle, philosophical, deeply conflicted. Believes the dead deserve Christian burial, but respects Ossuaire tradition.

**Known for:**
- Performs Catholic rites for those who want them
- Argues theology with secular Ossuaires (they enjoy it)
- The only person who prays to God instead of talking to bones
- May be the last Catholic priest in France

## Absurdist Details

### Things That Are Perfectly Normal to Ossuaires

1. **Birthdays at your burial site.** You bring cake (mushroom cake). You sit next to where your skull will be. You contemplate mortality. Happy birthday.

2. **Bone furniture.** Chairs made of bones. Tables with femur legs. Chandeliers of vertebrae. It's not disrespectful — the dead aren't using them.

3. **Asking the skulls for directions.** "Which way to the farms?" *Skull faces left.* "Thanks, Grand-père." This is navigation, not superstition.

4. **Naming children after death.** "Meet my daughter, Petite Mort" (Little Death). "This is my son, Crâne-Fleuri" (Flowered Skull). These are compliments.

5. **Rating bones.** Connoisseurs of bone quality. "That's a fine femur — good density, nice patina." "Ah, but the clavicle is cracked." This is aesthetic appreciation.

6. **Polite conversation with the dead.** "Excuse me, Madame, just passing through." "Sorry for the noise, Monsieur." They're not crazy — it's manners.

7. **Competitive silence.** "I once stayed silent for 38 minutes." "Amateur. I did 41." This is bragging. Other factions find it horrifying.

## Relations with Other Factions

### Le Carrefour (Trade Partners)

Les Ossuaires need Le Carrefour for trade. Le Carrefour is nervous around them but likes their mushrooms.

**Status:** Stable trade relationship. Ossuaire merchants are recognizable by their pale skin and unsettling calm.

### Les Canaux (Complicated Colleagues)

Both factions move through underground Paris. Their territories connect.

**Status:** Mutual respect, occasional tension. Les Canaux handle sewers; Les Ossuaires handle catacombs. Border disputes happen.

### La Dalle Éternelle (Minimal Contact)

La Dalle finds them creepy. Les Ossuaires find La Dalle's corporate culture baffling.

**Status:** Rare interaction. When they do meet, Les Ossuaires are polite; La Dalle is uncomfortable.

### Le Grand Nord (Hostile)

Le Grand Nord raiders tried to expand into catacomb territory in Year 289. It didn't go well.

**Status:** Cold war. No open conflict but no trust.

### Le Palais Profond (Mutual Suspicion)

Le Palais Profond considers Ossuaire culture "un-Republican." Les Ossuaires don't care what Le Palais Profond thinks.

**Status:** Official non-recognition. Informal tolerance.

## Quest Hooks

### The Lost Warren

**Hook:** A warren stopped responding. Scouts found the tunnels empty — no bodies, no bones, just silence.

**Reality:** 300 people vanished. The tunnels show no signs of struggle.

**Implications:** Did they leave? Were they taken? Is something in the deep tunnels?

### The First Keeper's Skull

**Hook:** Marianne Duval's skull — the most sacred relic of Les Ossuaires — has been stolen.

**Reality:** It's just a skull. But to Les Ossuaires, it's priceless.

**Implications:** Who took it? Another faction? An internal rival? What do they want?

### Le Puits des Morts

**Hook:** Something is moving at the bottom of the Well of the Dead. Sounds echo up that weren't there before.

**Reality:** Unknown. Sylvie won't discuss what she saw down there.

**Implications:** Exploration opportunity. Could be pre-Silence bunker, could be something worse, could be nothing.

### The Bone Prophet

**Hook:** A young Ossuaire claims the dead speak to her. She's predicting things — and being right.

**Reality:** She might be mentally ill. She might be observant. She might be genuine.

**Implications:** Her followers are growing. The Bone Council is nervous. Is she a threat or a miracle?
