# Le Carrefour ("The Crossroads")

> **Quick Reference:** The free city of Châtelet-Les Halles — neutral ground where all factions trade, fight (verbally), and pretend to tolerate each other.
>
> **Territory:** Centre — Châtelet-Les Halles complex
> **Population:** ~18,000-22,000 (fluctuates with trade)
> **Government:** Le Tribunal du Quai (neutral council)
> **Founded:** Year 82 AS (Treaty of Platform A)
> **Year 300 Status:** Chaotic, essential, ungovernable

---

## Origin Story

### Day 1: The Chaos Junction

Châtelet-Les Halles is the heart of Paris's underground. Five metro lines, three RER lines, hundreds of corridors, the massive Forum des Halles shopping center — all converging at one point.

At 5:34 AM on October 14, 2034, thousands of people were there:
- Metro workers starting shifts
- Homeless sleeping in corners
- Night-shift workers going home
- Early commuters arriving
- Security guards patrolling
- Cleaning crews finishing up

When The Silence hit, Châtelet became a pressure cooker.

**Day 1:**
- ~8,000 people trapped in the complex
- No organization, no leadership
- Multiple groups claiming different sections
- First fights over vending machine food by Hour 6

**Week 1:**
- 4 distinct factions emerged, each controlling different platforms
- The "Platform Wars" began — skirmishes over territory
- ~2,000 dead from violence, silence, despair

**Year 1:**
- Population stabilized at ~3,000
- 4 factions in permanent cold war
- The complex became unusable for trade

### Years 1-78: The Deadlock

For 78 years, Châtelet was a war zone. Not constant fighting — more like Northern Ireland during the Troubles. Everyone hated everyone, territory was sacred, crossing the wrong corridor could get you killed.

**The Four Original Factions:**
1. **Les Quais du Nord** — Controlled RER platforms, connected to what became Le Grand Nord
2. **La Fosse** — Held the Forum des Halles underground mall
3. **Les Métropolitains** — Metro workers who controlled metro platforms
4. **Les Halles Basses** — Survivors in the deepest sub-basements

None could destroy the others. None could cooperate. Châtelet rotted.

### The Great War (Year 78-82)

In Year 78, La Dalle Éternelle decided to break the stalemate. They wanted to control Le Carrefour — and thus control all underground trade.

**What happened:**
- La Dalle allied with Les Quais du Nord
- Le Grand Nord (by then, consolidated) saw this as aggression
- Les Ossuaires stayed neutral but sold passage to both sides
- Everyone else chose sides

The war spread across Paris. ~5,000 dead over 4 years.

**Year 82: The Breaking Point**

By Year 82, everyone was exhausted. The war had settled nothing. Châtelet was rubble. Trade had collapsed.

A woman named **Colette Marchand** — a trader from Les Métropolitains — proposed something radical:

> "This place belongs to no one. Let it belong to everyone."

### The Treaty of Platform A

On Year 82, Day 147, representatives from all factions met on Platform A of the RER line. They signed the "Accords du Quai A" — the Treaty of Platform A.

**The Five Laws:**

1. **No weapons in Le Carrefour.** Violation = exile.
2. **No factional symbols displayed.** Violation = exile.
3. **No voice above "market volume."** Violation = fine, then exile.
4. **All disputes resolved by Le Tribunal du Quai.** No exceptions.
5. **The Rhythm never stops.** All factions contribute drummers.

Anyone who breaks the laws is "mis à quai" — put to platform, exiled to the tunnels without faction protection. This is usually death.

The Treaty has held for 218 years.

## Year 300: The Free City

### What It Became

Le Carrefour is the closest thing the Paris underground has to a city. It's loud, crowded, chaotic, commercial, frustrating, dirty, vibrant, and absolutely essential.

**Population:**
- ~12,000 permanent residents
- ~6,000-10,000 transients (traders, visitors, pilgrims)

**No faction controls it. Everyone uses it. Everyone hates it. Everyone needs it.**

### Geography

The Châtelet-Les Halles complex is massive. Year 300 divisions:

| Zone | Location | Function |
|------|----------|----------|
| **Le Grand Marché** | Former Forum des Halles | Main marketplace |
| **Les Quais** | Metro/RER platforms | Transport hub, borders |
| **La Place Souterraine** | Central atrium | Gathering space, performances |
| **Les Niveaux Bas** | Sub-basements | Cheap housing, black market |
| **Le Quartier Gastronomique** | Former food court | Restaurants, taverns |
| **Les Sons d'Avant** | Former FNAC | Museum of pre-Silence sound |

### Governance: Le Tribunal du Quai

**Structure:**
- 9 judges total
- 1 representative from each of 7 major factions
- 2 "neutral" judges elected by Le Carrefour residents

**The 7 Faction Seats:**
1. La Dalle Éternelle
2. Le Palais Profond
3. Les Ossuaires
4. Le Grand Nord
5. L'Est Profond
6. Les Montparnos
7. La Gare du Sud

(Les Canaux don't get a seat — they're considered "infrastructure" rather than a faction. They're bitter about this.)

**Powers:**
- Enforce the Five Laws
- Arbitrate disputes
- Manage common infrastructure
- Set market regulations
- Oversee the Rhythm

**Limitations:**
- Cannot make new laws without unanimous consent
- Cannot tax factions
- Cannot maintain an army
- Cannot expand territory

**The Gardiens du Quai:**
Neutral police force, recruited from all factions. ~200 strong.
- Must renounce faction loyalty to join
- Cannot return to faction service
- Wear distinctive gray uniforms
- Armed with batons only (no weapons rule applies to them too)

## Daily Life

### The Market Economy

Le Carrefour runs on trade. Everything is bought, sold, or bartered.

**Market Schedule:**
- **Dawn market (06:00-10:00):** Fresh goods, mushrooms, perishables
- **Day market (10:00-18:00):** General goods, services, craftwork
- **Night market (18:00-24:00):** Entertainment, luxury goods, black market (unofficially)

**Currencies accepted:**
- Labor credits (La Dalle)
- Francs souterrains (Le Palais Profond)
- Bone tokens (Les Ossuaires)
- Union scrip (Le Grand Nord)
- Barter (universal)

The most common currency is actually a standardized "market token" issued by Le Tribunal — accepted everywhere, convertible to anything.

### The Rhythm

The Rhythm is sacred. All factions contribute drummers.

**How it works:**
- 24/7 drumming in central locations
- Rotating shifts from each faction
- The schedule is negotiated annually
- Refusing drum duty is grounds for faction sanctions

**The Rhythm Station:**
La Place Souterraine has a permanent drum platform. 4 drummers minimum at all times.

**Cultural significance:**
The Rhythm is neutral ground in sound form. When you hear it, you know you're in Le Carrefour. When it stops — which has happened twice in 218 years — everyone panics.

### Housing

Space is precious and expensive.

**Price zones:**
| Zone | Price | Who Lives There |
|------|-------|-----------------|
| Near La Place | Highest | Wealthy merchants, Tribunal officials |
| Market-adjacent | High | Established traders, professionals |
| Residential corridors | Medium | Working residents |
| Les Niveaux Bas | Low | Poor, new arrivals, criminals |

**Property rights:**
Le Tribunal maintains a registry. Ownership disputes are common. The legal system is a nightmare.

### Crime

Le Carrefour has crime. The Gardiens handle petty theft, assault, fraud.

**Major crimes:**
- Bringing weapons (exile)
- Violence (exile)
- Breaking the Rhythm (execution — only crime with death penalty)
- Faction agitation (exile)

**The Black Market:**
Everyone knows it exists. Les Niveaux Bas has dealers, smugglers, fixers. The Gardiens tolerate it as long as it stays quiet.

## Culture & Customs

### The "Carrefour" Identity

Permanent residents have developed their own identity — neither fully factional nor fully neutral.

**Carrefouriens:**
- Consider themselves "citizens of the crossroads"
- Speak a neutral French (without faction dialect markers)
- Take pride in neutrality
- Somewhat look down on "factionnaires" (faction members)

**Status symbols:**
- Length of residence (generational residents have highest status)
- Property ownership
- Tribunal service
- Successful business

### Festivals

| Date | Festival | Description |
|------|----------|-------------|
| Day 147 | **Jour de la Paix** | Anniversary of Treaty of Platform A. Major celebration. |
| Quarterly | **La Grande Foire** | Major trade fair, special goods |
| Variable | **Les Nuits du Son** | Music festival, performances from all factions |
| Winter | **Le Marché Sombre** | The black market becomes semi-legal for one week |

### The FNAC Museum

**Les Sons d'Avant** (The Sounds of Before) occupies the former FNAC store.

**Collections:**
- Pre-Silence music players (non-functional, revered)
- CD collection (~3,000 discs, unplayable but preserved)
- Musical instrument fragments
- "The Archive" — written descriptions of pre-Silence music

**Significance:**
This is one of the largest collections of pre-Silence audio culture. Musicians from all factions pilgrimage here to see instruments their ancestors played.

### Religion

Le Carrefour is secular, but several faiths are practiced:

- **Catholicism:** Chapel in a former storage room
- **Islam:** Prayer space in eastern corridors
- **The Rhythm Faith:** Those who believe the Rhythm itself is divine
- **Ancestor Worship:** Ossuaire-influenced practices
- **Atheism/Agnosticism:** Majority

### Food

The "Quartier Gastronomique" has everything:

- **Mushroom everything:** Steaks, soups, stews, bread
- **Rat:** Grilled, stewed, jerked (don't ask)
- **Fish:** From water-connected tunnels (rare, expensive)
- **Fermented beverages:** Mushroom wine, tunnel beer
- **Luxury imports:** Occasionally, real vegetables from surface raids

Famous dishes:
- **Le Plateau du Carrefour:** Sampler of mushrooms from every faction
- **Soupe des Morts:** Recipe from Les Ossuaires, name intentionally creepy
- **Croissant Corporatif:** La Dalle's attempt at pastry, surprisingly good

## Economy

### Trade Volume

Le Carrefour handles ~80% of inter-faction trade in Paris.

**Major trade goods:**
| From | What |
|------|------|
| La Dalle | Processed goods, textiles, tools |
| Le Palais Profond | Documents, formal clothing |
| Les Ossuaires | Mushrooms (best quality), passage |
| Le Grand Nord | Labor, metal goods, transport |
| L'Est Profond | Music, cultural goods, spices |
| Les Canaux | Information, smuggled goods |
| Les Montparnos | Art, intellectual services |
| La Gare du Sud | Regional trade goods |

### Banking

**La Banque du Carrefour** — established Year 112.

Services:
- Currency exchange
- Deposits (secure storage)
- Loans (high interest)
- Letters of credit

The Bank is theoretically neutral. In practice, La Dalle has significant influence (they understand banking).

### Disputes

Trade disputes are constant. Le Tribunal handles dozens daily.

**Common issues:**
- Contract breaches
- Quality disagreements
- Property disputes
- Debt collection
- Fraud allegations

**Resolution process:**
1. File complaint with Le Tribunal (fee required)
2. Initial hearing (within 3 days)
3. Evidence presentation
4. Tribunal ruling
5. Enforcement by Gardiens

## Key Locations

### La Place Souterraine

The heart of Le Carrefour. Former atrium of Les Halles.

Features:
- The Rhythm Station (drums, always)
- Public announcements platform
- Meeting space
- Performances
- The "Forever Fountain" — still works, sound is sacred

### Le Grand Marché

The main market. Hundreds of stalls.

Sections:
- Food hall
- Craftwork corridor
- Services alley
- The Antique Quarter (pre-Silence goods)
- The Livestock Section (rats, mostly)

### Le Tribunal

Former administration building. Now courts and government.

Contains:
- Main courtroom
- Judges' chambers
- Records hall (extensive)
- Gardiens headquarters
- Prison cells (for temporary holding)

### Les Niveaux Bas

The deep sub-basements. Cheaper, darker, more dangerous.

Contains:
- Black market stalls
- Cheap flophouses
- Criminal hideouts
- The occasional hiding fugitive

### Le Quai A

Platform A. Where the Treaty was signed. Sacred ground.

- The original treaty is displayed here (behind glass)
- Annual ceremonies
- Considered neutral-within-neutral

### Level -7

**Rumored to exist. Never confirmed.**

According to legend:
- There's a Level -7 below everything
- Nobody who's found it has returned
- It may contain pre-Silence technology
- It may contain something else
- Le Tribunal denies it exists

## Notable Figures

### Présidente Margot Delacroix (Head Judge)

58 years old. Current chief of Le Tribunal.

**Background:** Third-generation Carrefourien. Rose through legal system. Elected Présidente in Year 294.

**Personality:** Fair, exhausted, cynical. Has seen every kind of dispute. Trusts no one, respects everyone's money.

**Known for:**
- The "Delacroix Precedent" — ruled that faction membership doesn't affect contract law
- Surviving two assassination attempts
- Never smiling during hearings

### Captain Louis Benoît (Gardiens Commander)

45 years old. Head of the Gardiens du Quai.

**Background:** Former Le Grand Nord union member. Left to join Gardiens at 20. Pure neutral now.

**Personality:** Grim, incorruptible, violent when necessary. Lives by the Five Laws absolutely.

**Known for:**
- The "Year 287 Purge" — expelled 12 corrupt Gardiens
- Never taking bribes (unique in his position)
- Personally enforcing the exile of a La Dalle executive

### "The Rat King" (Black Market Boss)

Age unknown. Name unknown. Maybe mythical.

**Background:** Allegedly controls the black market in Les Niveaux Bas. Nobody's seen them. Everyone's heard of them.

**Personality:** Unknown.

**Known for:**
- Everything illegal that happens in Le Carrefour, allegedly
- Never being caught
- Possibly being multiple people
- Possibly not existing at all

### Marie-Claire Fontaine (Museum Curator)

67 years old. Curator of Les Sons d'Avant.

**Background:** Dedicated her life to preserving pre-Silence music culture. Never married, no children, only the Archive.

**Personality:** Obsessive, knowledgeable, slightly unhinged. Will talk about pre-Silence music for hours.

**Known for:**
- The "Fontaine Collection" — 847 CDs, personally catalogued
- Claiming she once heard a working CD player (disputed)
- Crying when describing "orchestras"

## Absurdist Details

### Things Everyone Accepts as Normal

1. **The RER announcements are prayers.** "Le train à destination de..." hasn't meant anything in 300 years, but people recite the phrases as mantras. The "train to Saint-Germain-en-Laye" is a blessing for travelers.

2. **Property is measured in "proximity to the Rhythm."** Real estate values increase exponentially as you get closer to the drum platform. The richest spot in Paris is 3 meters from a drum.

3. **The fountains are holy.** Water disputes have caused three near-wars. There's a temple to the fountain. People make offerings (small coins, flowers, bones).

4. **Level -7 is definitely real.** Everyone knows someone who knows someone who found it. No one has ever actually been there. Claiming you have is grounds for mockery.

5. **The FNAC museum charges admission to see objects no one understands.** "This was a 'smartphone.' It showed pictures and played music." "What's music?" "...We think it was like drumming, but smaller."

6. **Fast food is fine dining.** The former McDonald's location serves "Le Royal avec Fromage" — mushroom patty, fermented cheese, artisanal bread. 47 market tokens. Reservations required.

7. **The escalators are monuments.** They haven't worked in 300 years. They're decorated with flowers. Children climb them as a rite of passage.

## Relations with Other Factions

### La Dalle Éternelle (Commercial)

La Dalle wants to dominate Le Carrefour economically. They've tried. They keep failing. The Tribunal blocks them.

**Status:** Major trading partner, constant political maneuvering.

### Le Grand Nord (Tense)

Le Grand Nord and La Dalle are rivals. Their tension plays out in Le Carrefour constantly.

**Status:** Trade happens, arguments happen more.

### Les Ossuaires (Necessary)

Les Ossuaires supply the best mushrooms. Everyone needs them. Everyone is slightly creeped out by them.

**Status:** Stable trade. Ossuaire merchants are tolerated but watched.

### Le Palais Profond (Bemused)

Le Palais Profond wants Le Carrefour to recognize them as the legitimate government. Le Carrefour smiles and nods.

**Status:** Polite non-engagement. Trade happens.

### L'Est Profond (Cultural)

L'Est Profond brings music, diversity, excitement. Le Carrefour appreciates this.

**Status:** Good relations. Cultural exchange.

### Les Canaux (Useful)

Les Canaux move goods, carry messages, provide intelligence. They're essential but not respected.

**Status:** Transactional. Les Canaux resent not having a Tribunal seat.

### Les Montparnos (Intellectual)

Les Montparnos bring ideas, art, pretension. Le Carrefour tolerates this.

**Status:** Good relations. They argue philosophically.

### La Gare du Sud (Growing)

Newer faction, regional trade. Growing relationship.

**Status:** Expanding trade ties.

## Quest Hooks

### The Tribunal Corruption

**Hook:** Someone is bribing Tribunal judges. Verdicts are going wrong. Innocent people are being exiled.

**Reality:** Multiple factions are involved. The corruption is systemic.

**Implications:** If Le Tribunal falls, Le Carrefour falls. The peace of 218 years could end.

### Level -7

**Hook:** Someone claims they found Level -7. They have proof — an artifact from below.

**Reality:** The artifact is real. It's pre-Silence technology. Working pre-Silence technology.

**Implications:** Everyone wants it. The finder is in danger. What else is down there?

### The Rhythm Theft

**Hook:** Someone stole one of the sacred drums. The Rhythm is wavering.

**Reality:** This is an attack on Le Carrefour's very identity.

**Implications:** Find the drum. Find who took it. Prevent war.

### The Rat King's Offer

**Hook:** A message arrives from the Rat King (if they exist). They want a meeting.

**Reality:** Unknown. This has never happened before.

**Implications:** What does the Rat King want? Is it a trap? An opportunity?

### The Water War

**Hook:** Two factions are disputing fountain access. Tensions are rising. Violence is imminent.

**Reality:** Both have legitimate claims. Someone needs to mediate.

**Implications:** If this becomes violent, it violates the Five Laws. Could trigger wider conflict.
