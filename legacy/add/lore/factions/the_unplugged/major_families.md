## Overview

The Unplugged comprise ~400 families descended from 102 original families who entered Les Grottes de la Bresme in Year 1 AS. Over 300 years, families have merged, branched, risen, and fallen.

### The Founding Culture

The original Unplugged were not random survivalists. They were a specific type of person:
- **Practical researchers** — Scientists, engineers, medical professionals
- **Manual workers** — Builders, craftspeople, farmers, mechanics
- **People who questioned everything** — Skeptics, preppers, those who didn't trust "the system"

This created a community that valued:
- **Intellectual AND manual work equally** — A geologist's opinion matters as much as a carpenter's
- **Practical knowledge** — Theory is worthless without application
- **Self-reliance** — If you can't do it yourself, learn

### The "Service Worker" Insult

The Unplugged founders noticed something: the consultants, managers, and "service workers" of the old world mocked them. "Preppers are crazy," they said. "Why would you live in a cave?"

Those people died.

Over 300 years, "service worker" became an insult in Unplugged culture. It means:
- Someone who talks but doesn't do
- Someone who manages but can't build
- Someone who consults but has no real skills
- Someone who would have laughed at the caves

**Example usage:**

> "He wants to lead the Council but he's never dug a meter of tunnel. Total service worker."

> "She has opinions about mushroom cultivation but has never touched soil. Service worker thinking."

> "Don't service-worker me — I built this chamber with my own hands."

> "The Delacroix used to work. Now they just talk. Three generations of service workers."

**The hierarchy of insults:**
- "Service worker" — mild insult, suggests laziness or incompetence
- "Pure service worker" — stronger, suggests complete uselessness
- "Pre-Silence service worker" — the ultimate form, suggests the person would have died on Day 1

**Political use:**
The insult is weaponized in Council debates. Calling someone a "service worker" questions their right to speak on practical matters. Families with strong manual labor traditions (Lefebvres, Ferrands, Moreaus) use it against families perceived as "soft" (sometimes the Delacroix, sometimes newcomers who haven't proven themselves).

**The defense:**
Some argue the insult has gone too far — that leadership and coordination are valuable skills, not "service work." The Democrats particularly push back: "Someone has to organize. That's not service work, that's governance."

The Traditionalists respond: "Governance that can't dig its own latrine is governance that dies."

**The irony:**
Marc Delacroix — second Dadi, hero of the community — was an architect. A planner. A coordinator. By strict definition, a "service worker." No one calls him that. His descendants benefit from his reputation while sometimes being called service workers themselves. The contradiction is never addressed directly.

## The Founding Families (Year 1 AS)

These families trace directly to the original Unplugged members. They carry historical prestige and often hold significant Council influence.

### The Marchands
**Origin:** Henri Marchand (@UndergroundDad) — civil engineer, founder, first Dadi

**Founding Role:**
- Henri led the Unplugged evacuation
- His grandparents Georges and Madeleine owned the caves
- Their vinyl collection became the community's sacred music
- Henri died in Month 3 of Year 1 AS (heart condition)

**Year 300 Status: The Quiet Power**

The Marchands deliberately stepped back from politics after Henri's death, believing leadership should be earned, not inherited. But they hold secrets — including oral traditions about why the caves were so well-prepared. They know @QuietDad_FR existed; they don't know who he was.

- **Role:** Secret keepers, behind-the-scenes influencers
- **Political alignment:** Neutral (officially)
- **Influence:** High (hidden)

**The Stolen Drive:**
The Marchands hold a secret even they don't fully understand: Marc Delacroix's encrypted hard drive. Around Year 180 AS, a Marchand ancestor — acting on a deathbed confession from an elderly Delacroix servant — located and quietly took the drive from its hiding place in the Delacroix family quarters.

The theft was never discovered. The Delacroix family believes the drive was lost during a cave expansion project. The Marchands have attempted to decrypt it several times over the generations, but the encryption has held. They know it contains something important about Marc and possibly about The Silence itself. They don't know what.

The current Marchand family head keeps the drive in a secure location. They've considered returning it, destroying it, or seeking outside help to crack it. For now, they wait — uncertain whether the truth would help or harm the community.

### The Delacroix
**Origin:** Marc Delacroix (@NapTimeNinja / @QuietDad_FR) — ESA architect, secret conspirator, second Dadi

**Founding Role:**
- Marc was a Sounding Five member who secretly funded and guided The Unplugged
- He carried the guilt of causing The Silence to his grave (Year 53 AS)
- His encrypted hard drive contains proof of the conspiracy
- His descendants don't know the truth

**Year 300 Status: The Honored Lineage**

The Delacroix are celebrated as the family of "the great Marc" who led the community for 53 years. They're politically powerful, often producing Dadis. Their status is built on Marc's legacy — the quiet leader who guided the Unplugged through their darkest years.

- **Role:** Political dynasty, Dadi family
- **Political alignment:** Traditionalist
- **Influence:** Very high

**The Lost Drive:**
The Delacroix family knows Marc kept an encrypted hard drive — a family heirloom passed down through generations. They believed it contained Marc's personal journals, perhaps records of the early years. Around Year 180 AS, the drive went missing during a cave expansion project that relocated several families' quarters.

The family searched for years. They blame the construction chaos, water damage, simple misplacement. Some Delacroix elders still organize occasional searches through storage areas, hoping to find it.

They don't know the Marchands took it. They don't know it contains proof of Marc's role in The Silence.

**The irony:**
The Delacroix family's political power rests on Marc's reputation as a hero. If the drive were decrypted and its contents revealed, that reputation would be destroyed. The Marchands, by stealing the drive, accidentally protected the Delacroix legacy — and the community's peace of mind.

Neither family understands the full truth. The drive remains encrypted. The secret endures.

### The Moreaus
**Origin:** Jean-Pierre Moreau (@DrumCircleDad) — retired military logistics officer

**Founding Role:**
- Created the activation protocol that saved everyone
- Organized the first drum rotation schedules
- First to start the drums in the caves
- Brought military discipline to civilian survival

**Year 300 Status:**
The Moreaus are the "soldier family" — known for discipline, organization, and leading dangerous expeditions. They've produced many expedition leaders and Council representatives.

**Political alignment:** Digger (expansion through systematic effort)

**Cultural trait:** The Moreaus still run the drum rotation schedules with military precision. Every drummer knows their shift, their backup, and their backup's backup.

### The Vidals
**Origin:** Dr. Claudine Vidal (@SporeMom) — mycologist from Bordeaux

**Founding Role:**
- Taught the community how to grow mushrooms underground
- Wrote the cultivation manual that's still used today
- Established the food production systems that made long-term survival possible

**Year 300 Status:**
The Vidals are the "farmer family" — dominant in food production. They control significant agricultural resources and have expertise others depend on.

**Political alignment:** Democrat (resources should be shared equally, not hoarded)

**Cultural trait:** The Vidals still use Claudine's original manual, hand-copied through generations. They consider mushroom cultivation a sacred duty.

## Emerged Families (Post-Year 1 AS)

These families were not among the original 102 but rose to prominence over three centuries through skill, politics, or circumstance.

---

### The Perezes
**Origin:** Marriage of Antonia Perez (Rousseau branch) to Lucien Favre (minor family) — Year 78 AS

**Emergence:**
The Perezes emerged when Antonia, a gifted midwife from the Rousseau medical tradition, married into the small Favre family. Her children inherited her skills but not the Rousseau name. By Year 150, the Perez-Favre branch had trained more midwives than the Rousseaus themselves. They eventually dropped "Favre" and claimed their own identity.

**Year 300 Status:**
The Perezes are the "birth family" — specialists in midwifery, childcare, and early education. While the Rousseaus handle medicine broadly, the Perezes focus on bringing new life into the caves.

- **Role:** Midwives, teachers, child welfare
- **Political alignment:** Democrat (every child matters equally)
- **Influence:** Medium (essential but specialized)

**Cultural trait:** The Perezes keep a "Book of Names" — every child born in the caves for the past 200 years is recorded there.

---

### The Girards
**Origin:** Guillaume Girard — cartographer who joined the Unplugged in Year 3 AS (rescued from a collapsed building near Tours)

**Emergence:**
Guillaume was not an original Unplugged member but was found alive during an early surface expedition. He carried mapping equipment and an obsessive attention to detail. His descendants became the official mapmakers of the cave system.

**Year 300 Status:**
The Girards are the "map family" — they document every tunnel, chamber, and passage. Where the Lefebvres dig, the Girards record.

- **Role:** Cartographers, record-keepers, archivists
- **Political alignment:** Opener (knowledge should be shared)
- **Influence:** Medium (everyone needs maps)

**Cultural trait:** The Girards maintain The Archive — the community's central repository of documents, maps, and records. They're obsessive about organization.

**Note:** Guillaume's rescue is sometimes cited by Openers as proof that surface expeditions save lives. Earthbound members point out he's the only one ever rescued.

### The Martinets
**Origin:** Consolidation of three smaller families (Martin, Martineau, Petit-Martin) around Year 120 AS

**Emergence:**
Three failing family lines — all coincidentally with "Martin" in their names — merged to survive. They pooled resources and skills, creating a new identity. The name "Martinet" was chosen partly as a joke (a "martinet" is a strict disciplinarian in French). The joke became prophecy.

**Year 300 Status:**
The Martinets are the community's historians, crime novel writers, and investigators. In a secluded underground community, this is as close as anyone gets to professional detectives — and they take it *extremely* seriously.

- **Role:** Historians, investigators, crime novelists, bet managers, Watch patrol
- **Political alignment:** Traditionalist (rules exist for reasons)
- **Influence:** Medium-High (everyone fears The Lecture)

**The Watch:**
The Martinets run "The Watch" — managing security, patrol schedules, and community defense. But their role has evolved far beyond simple guard duty.

**Historians and Investigators:**
The role began when Thomas Martinet (Year 45 AS) started documenting disputes for posterity. His meticulous records proved so useful that the family formalized the practice. Over generations, the Martinets developed an entire literary tradition around their work.

**The Crime Novels:**
The Martinets transform real cases into instructive literature. Names are changed. Drama is enhanced. A stolen turnip becomes a multi-chapter thriller. Every Martinet has a "signature style" in both investigation and prose.

**Notable Works:**
- *The Mushroom Conspiracy* (Year 89) — A classic of the genre
- *Death in the Deep Tunnels* (Year 156) — Despite the title, no one died; supply theft
- *The Yellow Ribbon Murders* (Year 201) — Again, no murders; dramatic license
- *One Thousand Days of Apology* (Year 245) — Based on a legendary Public Apology case

**The Martinet Character:**
- Very solemn. Always.
- Very calm. About everything.
- Over every. Single. Thing. A stolen turnip receives the same grave investigation as an accusation of sabotage.
- Parodies of police novel detectives: dramatic pauses, thoughtful chin-stroking, meaningful looks at evidence
- They narrate their own investigations, sometimes out loud
- Each has a "signature phrase" they use when concluding an investigation

**The Lecture:**
The Martinets' primary enforcement tool. No physical punishment. No raised voices. Just... the lecture.

A Martinet lecture involves:
- Extended eye contact
- Disappointed sighs
- References to historical precedent ("In Year 87, a similar case...")
- Quotations from their own crime novels
- Detailed explanations of exactly how the offender has failed the community
- Uncomfortable silences

*Very few can withstand a Martinet's lecture.* Strong men have been reduced to apologizing for crimes they didn't commit just to make it stop.

**Bet Management:**
The Martinets manage ALL community wagers:
- Outcome of ongoing disputes
- Duration of Public Apology sentences
- Whether the accused will cry during The Lecture
- How long until a "permanent" sentence gets commuted

They maintain scrupulous records and take a small administrative fee (2%) for community funds.

**Cultural trait:** Every Martinet child learns to write their first "case report" by age 10. Most are about siblings stealing food. The quality varies. The solemnity does not.

### The Beaumonts
**Origin:** André Beaumont — one of the original 102, a teacher who left no detailed records

**Emergence:**
The Beaumonts were unremarkable for generations — a quiet family of teachers and clerks. Around Year 200 AS, a Beaumont matriarch named Céleste organized the first formal school system in the caves. Her descendants have run education ever since.

**Year 300 Status:**
The Beaumonts are the "teacher family" — they run the community's schools and preserve oral traditions. They determine what children learn about history, the Before, and The Silence.

- **Role:** Education, oral history, cultural transmission
- **Political alignment:** Varies (teachers hold diverse views)
- **Influence:** High (they shape what people believe)

**Cultural trait:** Every Beaumont child memorizes "The First Year" — an oral history of the Unplugged's arrival at the caves. The version has drifted over time, but no one admits this.

**Note:** The Beaumonts control what gets taught about The Silence. They don't know the truth, but they shape the community's understanding of it.

### The Ferrands
**Origin:** Split from the Brenner family — Year 165 AS

**Emergence:**
A Brenner daughter, Ingrid, married Michel Ferrand (from a minor family of woodworkers). Their children combined electrical knowledge with carpentry skills, becoming specialists in building construction. They eventually broke from both families.

**Year 300 Status:**
The Ferrands are the "builder family" — they construct new chambers, reinforce tunnels, and maintain structures. Where the Lefebvres study rock, the Ferrands build with it.

- **Role:** Construction, structural engineering, carpentry
- **Political alignment:** Digger (more building = more space)
- **Influence:** High (essential for expansion)

**Cultural trait:** The Ferrands have a saying: "The Lefebvres tell us where; we tell them how." The relationship between the two families is competitive but respectful.

### The Bouches
**Origin:** Lucien Bouche (Year 89-167 AS) — a musician with an unusual talent

**Emergence:**
Lucien Bouche could imitate drum sounds with his mouth. It was a party trick — he'd beatbox during community gatherings, making children laugh. Nobody thought it was important.

Then the Third Silence happened (Year 134 AS). The primary speaker wiring flooded. The drums kept going, but music stopped. For 8 minutes, the community panicked.

Lucien grabbed the backup microphone and started beatboxing. He layered rhythms, added vocal bass, created something that wasn't quite music but wasn't silence either. It worked. People calmed. The engineers fixed the wiring.

After that, Lucien taught his children. They taught theirs. The Bouches became the emergency backup — human beings who could replace technology.

**Year 300 Status:**
The Bouches are the "sound family" — emergency responders who can produce continuous rhythm with nothing but their voices. They're also skilled Disciples under the Vinyl Guardian, maintaining the broadcast system.

- **Role:** Emergency sound production, broadcast system maintenance, Disciples
- **Political alignment:** Traditionalist (sound is life; don't change what works)
- **Influence:** Medium normally, CRITICAL in emergencies

**The Bouche Training:**
Every Bouche child learns to beatbox. It's not optional.
- Age 5: Basic rhythms
- Age 8: Sustained performance (10+ minutes)
- Age 12: Layered patterns, vocal bass
- Age 16: "The Endurance Test" — perform continuously for one hour

Family dinners involve practice sessions. Competitions are common. New sounds are celebrated.

**Famous Bouche Sounds:**
- **The Piaf Punch** — a beat mimicking *Non, Je Ne Regrette Rien*
- **The Brassens Roll** — gentler, based on guitar strumming
- **The Emergency Pulse** — standard crisis beat, maximum volume and sustainability
- **The Trenet Skip** — upbeat, used to calm children

**The Great Panic (Year 278 AS):**
The Fifth Silence. Unknown electrical failure knocked out all speakers. For 58 minutes, the caves had no broadcast music.

Jérôme "Beatbox" Bouche was eating dinner when it happened. He ran to La Salle Piaf, grabbed the backup microphone (still working on battery), and began performing. For 58 minutes straight. He switched styles. Added vocal melodies. Occasionally sang fragments of songs. His throat was destroyed for weeks afterward.

Jérôme is now a community hero. Children sing songs about him. The Bouches' status was elevated to "emergency essential" — they're exempt from certain duties so they're always available.

**The Five Silences:**
The Bouches remember all five (see [vinyl_guardian.md](vinyl_guardian.md) for details):
1. Year 3 AS — 47 minutes (pre-Bouche; drummers covered)
2. Year 67 AS — 12 minutes (needle accident; drums covered)
3. Year 134 AS — 8 minutes (flooding; Lucien's first performance)
4. Year 201 AS — 3 minutes (child's tantrum; resolved quickly)
5. Year 278 AS — 58 minutes (electrical failure; Jérôme's legendary performance)

**Cultural trait:** Bouches greet each other with rhythm — a quick beat-pattern instead of words. Outsiders find this strange. The Bouches consider speech inefficient.

### The Lindquists
**Origin:** Kaylee Jo Lindquist (The Raver) — American stalker who followed the evacuation by accident

**Founding Role:**
- Brought "unsanctioned items" (her own possessions)
- Introduced bizarre American traditions
- Created the "lectromusic" cult
- Was NOT part of the original Unplugged group

**Year 300 Status:**
See [faction_id_card.md](faction_id_card.md) for full details. The Lindquists are:
- ~40 members
- The "odd family"
- Politically neutral
- High Resilience rate
- Now leading the Studio Echo expansion

**Political alignment:** None (outside the political system)

**Vinyl creed:** The Lectromusic Cult (see below)

**The Ententernickname Exception:**
The Unplugged name children with both a legal name and a "nickname" — the name the community actually uses. This tradition comes from the original Telegram group, where everyone had handles (@UndergroundDad, @SporeMom, etc.).

The Lindquists were never part of the online community. Kaylee didn't have a handle. She didn't understand the culture. When her first child was born, she just used his first name as the nickname: "Thomas."

Other families found this baffling. "That's not how it works." But Kaylee shrugged. The tradition stuck — every Lindquist child's nickname is simply their first name. It marks them as outsiders, one more reason they're "the odd family."

**Except once.**

**The Pas-encore Exception (Year 112 AS):**
Margot Lindquist gave birth to twins. The first survived. The second lived only six hours.

In those six hours, Margot refused to name the dying baby — as if naming him would make the loss real. The Rousseau healer attending kept asking: "What do you want to call him?"

Margot, exhausted and grieving, finally whispered: "Pas-encore." *Not yet.*

The baby died. The Martinets recorded his nickname as requested: Pas-encore Lindquist. It was the only time a Lindquist broke from the first-name tradition.

The Girard archives record: "Pas-encore Lindquist. Born Year 112 AS. Died Year 112 AS. Lived 6 hours. Nickname chosen by Margot Lindquist. Reason: grief."

Every Lindquist since has chosen their first name anyway — but they all know the story of the one who was named for hope that never came.

### The Lectromusic Cult

The Lindquists don't follow any of the four major vinyl creeds. They worship something that doesn't exist in The Collection: *lectromusic*.

**Origin:**
Kaylee Jo Lindquist was a raver — an obsessive fan of electronic dance music who followed her favorite DJ across Europe. She ended up in France stalking him, got caught up in the Unplugged evacuation by accident, and brought her own possessions into the caves: a broken MP3 player, festival wristbands, glow sticks, and memories of music no one else had ever heard.

**The Distortion:**
Over 300 years, Kaylee's story transformed:
- Her "stalking" became a "holy pilgrimage"
- Her favorite DJ became "The Prophet of the Beat"
- The music festivals became "the sacred gatherings of the Before"
- Studio Echo — where electronic music was recorded — became the Holy Place
- The lectromusic she described became a lost paradise: music that makes you move, music that transcends thought, music that brings bliss

**What Lindquists believe:**
- The surface holds the Holy Place (Studio Echo) where lectromusic was created
- Returning to the surface is a sacred duty
- The bliss Kaylee described — dancing until dawn, unity with strangers, transcendence through rhythm — can be recovered
- The vinyl religions worship the past; lectromusic worships the future

**The Cult in practice:**
The Lectromusic Cult is secretive. Outsiders know the Lindquists are "weird about music" but few understand the depth of their beliefs.

- **Surface expeditions** are religious events. When one is planned, the entire family celebrates. Volunteers are honored as pilgrims.
- **Mushroom cultivation:** The Lindquists are the caves' best growers of hallucinogenic mushrooms ("chrooms"). Other families pretend not to know this. The Lindquists use them in rituals to "hear the beat" — to experience what Kaylee described.
- **Secret gatherings:** Late at night, in remote tunnels, Lindquists hold their own ceremonies: dancing in the dark, drumming in complex rhythms, eating chrooms, trying to recreate what Kaylee remembered. These gatherings are not advertised.
- **The Holy Items:** Kaylee's broken MP3 player is a sacred relic. Her festival wristbands are passed down. No one can play the MP3 player — it died centuries ago — but it represents the promise of lectromusic.

**Why it matters:**
The Lindquists' fanaticism about the surface made them perfect for the Studio Echo mission. They *wanted* to go. They believed they were on a holy pilgrimage. The Hero's success at Studio Echo isn't just an expansion — for the Lindquists, it's prophecy fulfilled.

**The C282Y Connection:**
The Lindquist family has an unusually high rate of Resilients — individuals who can survive 24 hours of silence instead of the standard 4. This is statistically improbable (baseline rate: 1 in 50,000). The explanation lies in their genetics.

Kaylee Jo Lindquist carried the C282Y variant of the HFE gene — a mutation associated with hereditary hemochromatosis, common in Scandinavian ancestry. While this gene typically affects iron metabolism, it also appears to interact with the Hush-2 virus in unexpected ways:
- The C282Y variant alters how cells process certain proteins
- This interaction slows the virus's progression from dormant to active state
- Carriers gain extended survival time in silence (24 hours instead of 4)

The Lindquist family, through 300 years of relative isolation (few outside marriages due to their "odd" status), has concentrated this genetic marker. Roughly 1 in 4 Lindquists carry C282Y — far higher than any other family.

**What this means:**
- The Lindquists are uniquely suited for surface operations
- Their genetic "defect" is now their greatest asset
- Other families may want to marry into the Lindquist line — a dramatic reversal of 300 years of social distance
- The Hero's Resilience is not luck; it's inheritance
