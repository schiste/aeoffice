# The Great Animal Ethics Debate

> **Three months of passionate discussion about protecting animals from harm.**
>
> **Date Range:** March 14 - June 22, 2031
>
> **Participants:** The Sounding Five
>
> **Outcome:** Hush calibrated to spare animal life
>
> **Irony Level:** Catastrophic

---

## Context

By March 2031, the Sounding Five had moved from theoretical discussion to practical planning. Marie Fontaine's Hush virus was entering final design stages. The question arose: what about animals?

What followed was **three months** of the most earnest, passionate, ethically rigorous debate in the group's history.

They were very concerned about the animals.

## Historical Assessment

The Sounding Five's commitment to animal welfare was genuine. They spent more time debating the ethics of animal impact than any other aspect of Operation Lullaby.

Marie Fontaine's calibration worked exactly as designed. Animals survived Hush exposure.

What no one anticipated was that "survival" and "unchanged" are not the same thing.

The neural disruption didn't kill animals. It destabilized their sensory processing, triggering cascading evolutionary adaptations compressed into generations instead of millennia.

Jonathan Reeves's conservation estate became one of the most dangerous mutation nests in the Loire Valley.

The irony is not lost on historians.
