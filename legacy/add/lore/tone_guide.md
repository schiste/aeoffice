# Tone Guide

> **Writing style reference for AD&D content — lore, dialog, and in-game text.**
>
> **Related documents:** [thesaurus.md](thesaurus.md) (terminology) • [naming_conventions.md](naming_conventions.md) (naming rules) • [the-truth.md](the-truth.md) (conspiracy details)

## Core Tone: Gallows Humor + Pragmatic Hope

The world ended because exhausted parents wanted quiet. That's absurd. Lean into it.

**The balance:**
- **Dark** enough to feel the stakes (silence = death)
- **Funny** enough to be bearable (survivors laugh to cope)
- **Hopeful** enough to motivate play (things can get better)

**Never:**
- Grimdark despair porn
- Cheap nihilism
- Preachiness about "the hubris of man"

## Voice by Context

### Lore Documents (Internal)

**Audience:** Writers, designers
**Voice:** Omniscient, detailed, occasionally wry
**Purpose:** Establish truth so content can hint at it

```
Example:
"[The Sounding Five](thesaurus.md#the-sounding-five) genuinely believed they were improving civilization.
This is important. They weren't cartoon villains. They were tired parents
who convinced themselves that making the world quieter was a gift.

They were wrong. But they weren't evil. That's worse."
```

### In-Game Dialog (Player-Facing)

**Audience:** Players
**Voice:** Character-appropriate, concise, implies more than states
**Purpose:** Build world without exposition dumps

```
Example ([Survivor Cave](thesaurus.md#the-survivor-cave) elder):
"Grandpa Henri always said the drums were for 'psychological well-being.'
Funny how he was right for the wrong reasons. Or wrong for the right ones.
I never could figure out which."
```

### Item Descriptions

**Audience:** Players
**Voice:** Terse, evocative, occasionally darkly funny
**Purpose:** Reward reading without requiring it

```
Example:
"Telegram Mug — Faded logo. '[Sleepless in Decibels](thesaurus.md#sleepless-in-decibels) - 5 Year Anniversary.'
Someone's grandmother must have loved this group."
```

### Tutorial / UI Text

**Audience:** Players (especially new)
**Voice:** Clear, direct, personality through word choice
**Purpose:** Teach without boring

```
Example:
"Keep the drums going. Always. If you're wondering why — don't.
Just trust us. Tap here to assign a Drummer."
```

## Humor Guidelines

### Do:
- **Irony:** The conspiracy was founded on [Fête de la Musique](thesaurus.md#fête-de-la-musique). The drums packed "for morale" turned out to be survival tools.
- **Understatement:** "The satellites worked better than expected. Much better."
- **Dark wordplay:** "Silent but deadly" → "[The Deadly Silent](thesaurus.md#the-silence--the-deadly-silent)"
- **Survivor gallows humor:** Characters joke about death because it's everywhere
- **Absurdist details:** A man in a gorilla suit at a noise complaint. Chloé's first word being "loud."

### Don't:
- **Punch down:** Don't mock victims of [The Silence](thesaurus.md#the-silence--the-deadly-silent)
- **Be flippant about current deaths:** Past deaths can be dark humor; imminent threat is serious
- **Overexplain jokes:** If it needs explanation, cut it
- **Force humor:** Some moments should be sincere

### Example Spectrum:

**Too dark:**
> "The library was full of corpses. They died reaching for books about meditation."

**Right balance:**
> "The library is quiet now. It always was, actually. That's the problem."

**Too light:**
> "LOL the library people died because they liked quiet! Ironic!"

## Exposition Rules

### The Iceberg Principle

Players see 10%. The other 90% exists and informs everything, but stays submerged.

**Show through:**
- Artifacts (items that imply history)
- Offhand NPC comments
- Environmental details
- Contradictions between sources

**Never:**
- Textwall lore dumps
- NPCs who explain things they'd have no reason to explain
- "As you know, Bob..." dialog

### Example: Revealing the Conspiracy

**Bad:**
> NPC: "Did you know that 300 years ago, a group of parents created a virus and launched satellites that caused the Silence? It's all in these Telegram logs I found."

**Good:**
> NPC: "Found this in the old Archive cache. Chat logs from before. They were complaining about... noise? Their neighbors' music? And then someone says 'what if we just... turned it all off.' Everyone laughed."
>
> [Player finds another fragment later]
>
> Different NPC: "That symbol on your mug — my grandmother had one. Said it was from some parents' group. Support community, she called it. Before everything."

## Emotional Beats

### When to be funny:
- Downtime / safe moments
- Describing the past (distance = humor)
- Item flavor text
- NPC small talk

### When to be serious:
- Immediate danger
- Character deaths
- Major revelations
- Player choices with consequences

### When to be ambiguous:
- The truth about the conspiracy
- Faction morality
- Whether hope is justified

## Faction Voices

Each faction has a distinct way of speaking:

| Faction | Voice | Sample |
|---------|-------|--------|
| **[Archive](thesaurus.md#the-archive)** | Measured, cryptic, transactional | "Information has a price. The truth costs more." |
| **[Choir](thesaurus.md#the-choir)** | Reverent, communal, hymn-like | "The Crystal sings. We sing back. That is the covenant." |
| **[Engineers](thesaurus.md#the-engineers)** | Technical, pragmatic, frustrated | "It's not magic. It's physics. Badly documented physics." |
| **[Silent](thesaurus.md#the-silent)** | Unsettling, calm, proselytizing | "You fear the quiet. We've learned to listen to it." |
| **[Resonants](thesaurus.md#the-resonants)** | Hopeful, ambitious, networked | "One Crystal saved a village. Imagine what a hundred could do." |
| **[Survivor Cave](thesaurus.md#the-survivor-cave)** | Folksy, traditional, myth-making | "The founders knew. They always knew. We just follow the rhythm." |

## Word Choice

### Prefer:
- **Active verbs:** "The drums pound" not "The drums are being played"
- **Concrete nouns:** "The Crystal hums" not "The resonance emanates"
- **Short sentences:** Mobile screens. Tap-through dialog. Brevity.

### Avoid:
- **Passive voice** (unless character-appropriate)
- **Jargon** (unless faction-specific flavor)
- **Modern slang** (300 years have passed)
- **Overly formal** (people are surviving, not writing theses)

### Sound words to use freely:
Drum, beat, rhythm, pulse, hum, ring, echo, resonate, vibrate, thunder, roar, crash, bang, clatter, clang

### Sound words to use carefully:
Silence, quiet, still, hush, mute, deaf, peace, rest (all have death associations)

## Length Guidelines

| Content Type | Target Length | Max Length |
|--------------|---------------|------------|
| Item description | 1-2 sentences | 3 sentences |
| NPC bark (ambient) | 1 sentence | 1 sentence |
| Dialog line | 1-2 sentences | 3 sentences |
| Dialog exchange | 3-5 lines | 8 lines |
| Quest description | 2-3 sentences | 5 sentences |
| Codex entry | 1-2 paragraphs | 3 paragraphs |
| Tutorial tooltip | 1 sentence | 2 sentences |

---

## The Golden Rule

**If the player is tapping "skip," you've failed.**

Every line should either:
1. Give useful information
2. Build character/world
3. Be entertaining enough to read for its own sake

Ideally, all three. At minimum, one.
