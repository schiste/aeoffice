**How a South Dakota Heiress Discovered French Electro:**

2028: Kaylee attended Coachella (VIP passes, father's corporate connections). Voltage Cathedral played a 2 AM set on a side stage. 47 people attended. Kaylee was in the front row.

> "It was like... they were speaking directly to my soul, you know? Like, the synthesizers were saying everything I'd ever felt but couldn't put into words. I literally cried. The guy next to me thought I was on drugs. I was just... *moved*."

She flew to Paris three weeks later to see them again. Then Berlin. Then Amsterdam. Her father assumed she was "finding herself." Her mother assumed she was on drugs. Her brother assumed she was an idiot.

They were all partially correct.

### The Stalking

**October 2034:**

Kaylee had discovered (through obsessive fan forums) that Voltage Cathedral would be recording at Studio Echo, a legendary recording studio in the Loire Valley.

**Her plan:**
1. Fly first-class to Paris (charged to father's corporate card)
2. Rent a car (premium SUV, obviously)
3. Drive to the Loire Valley
4. Camp near Studio Echo (in a $400 tent from REI)
5. Wait for the band to arrive
6. ??? (she hadn't figured out this part)
7. Meet her heroes, become their friend, maybe join them on tour

**The Date Confusion:**

| What she saw | What she thought | What it actually meant |
|--------------|------------------|------------------------|
| "Session: 10/14" | October 14th (American format) | Europeans write dd/mm — could have been 14 October or a different date entirely |
| She arrived October 11th | Three days early, perfect timing | She may have been months late, or right on time for the wrong reason |

**Her Setup:**
- $400 REI tent
- $200 sleeping bag (rated for 40°F, it was 55°F)
- Portable Bluetooth speaker
- Two bottles of wine (she didn't drink wine, but "it's France")
- Energy bars (Kind brand, variety pack)
- A homemade "I ❤️ Voltage Cathedral" sign (glitter, obviously)
- Complete inability to speak French beyond "merci" and "croissant"

**What actually happened:** It doesn't matter. The band never arrived. The Silence did.

## The Accident That Saved Her

### October 14, 2034

**Timeline:**

| Time | Kaylee's Experience | What Was Actually Happening |
|------|---------------------|----------------------------|
| 4:34 AM | Sleeping in tent near Studio Echo | The Filter activates globally |
| 6:00 AM | Wakes to strange silence, phone dead | Humanity beginning to die |
| 7:00 AM | Sees people moving through countryside | Unplugged evacuation in progress |
| 8:00 AM | Thinks "underground rave!" and follows | Following evacuees to caves |
| 10:00 AM | Arrives at cave entrance, confused | Enters Les Grottes de la Bresme |
| 11:00 AM | Realizes this isn't a party | Trapped underground with survivors |

**What she was wearing:**
- Festival outfit (crop top, high-waisted shorts)
- Glitter makeup (applied fresh that morning, "just in case")
- Platform sneakers ($280, white, now muddy)
- Vintage band t-shirt (Voltage Cathedral, obviously)
- A flower crown (left over from Coachella)

**What she said when she arrived:**
> "Oh my God, hi! Is this the party? Sorry I'm late — well, I'm not late, I'm actually super early, but like, I saw everyone heading this way and I was like, this must be it! Is this like, an underground rave? Because that's so cool. I'm Kaylee, by the way. Kaylee Jo? From South Dakota? In America? Do you guys have Red Bull?"

**French survivors' reaction:** Stunned silence. Then slow, horrified realization that this person was now trapped with them forever.

### The First Days

**Day 1-3:**
- Kept asking "when does the music start?"
- Tried to leave twice (stopped both times)
- Complained about the drumming being "too monotonous" and "not danceable"
- Other survivors viewed her as "the loud American in glitter"
- Kept saying "ope, sorry!" whenever she bumped into anyone in the dark

**Day 4-7:**
- Began to understand this wasn't temporary
- Assigned to drum shifts (proved surprisingly good at rhythm)
- Started asking real questions: "What happened? Why can't we leave?"
- Still wearing glitter; it was becoming a strange comfort
- Began teaching people American phrases: "You betcha," "Don't cha know," "Uff da"

**Week 2:**
- Discovered the Marchand family vinyl collection
- Proposed The DJ Incident

## The DJ Incident

### The Performance (Week 3, October 2034)

**Setup:**
- One turntable (found in Marchand storage)
- Hand-crank generator (unreliable power)
- Records: Jacques Brel, Claude François, Serge Gainsbourg, Édith Piaf
- Kaylee's confidence: "Oh my God, I've got this, you guys"
- Kaylee's skill: Absolutely not

**The Pitch:**

> "Okay so like, I know this is super stressful for everyone, but I was thinking — what if I DJ'd? Like, I go to raves all the time, and I've watched tons of DJs, and it's basically just putting on music and vibing, right? I can totally do that. Trust me, you guys are gonna love this."

Henri, exhausted and desperate for any morale boost, agreed.

**The Performance:**

> **From Henri Marchand's journal:**
>
> "The American woman asked to 'DJ' for the community. She explained it was 'mixing music' — combining sounds into something greater.
>
> I said yes. We needed morale. Music might help.
>
> I was wrong.
>
> She put on a Jacques Brel record. She didn't know who he was. She called him 'this sad French guy.' Then she tried to 'blend' it with Claude François. The tempos didn't match. She kept saying 'wait, wait, I can fix this' while making it worse.
>
> Then she tried to 'scratch' the record. She had seen this technique but never practiced. The sound was like a dying animal being fed through a wood chipper.
>
> Children cried. Adults covered their ears. Someone vomited. The drums stopped because everyone was too horrified to continue.
>
> We nearly died because she made us stop drumming.
>
> The American said: 'Okay so that wasn't great but like, I'm still learning? Give me another chance?'
>
> I banned 'DJing' on the spot. Forever."

**Kaylee's reaction:**

> "Okay, that's fine, I totally get it. You guys just aren't ready for this kind of music yet. It's very avant-garde. Don't worry about it. I'm not offended. I mean, I'm a little offended, but it's fine. It's totally fine."

(It was not fine. She cried in a corner for three hours.)

### The Ban

**Article 7 of the Bresme Cave Code:**

> "The practice known as 'DJing' — the combination of recorded sounds through mechanical manipulation — is hereby prohibited in all community spaces.
>
> This ban is not about the art form itself, but about the demonstrated risk of psychological harm when attempted by the untrained.
>
> Future generations may revisit this rule. Until then: no DJing."

**Kaylee's lifelong position:**
She accepted the ban. She never accepted that she was bad at it.

> "They just weren't ready. European audiences are different. Back home, people would have been way more into it."

## Post-Silence Life

### Years 1-10 AS

**Social Status:**
- Tolerated but not welcomed
- The only American in the caves (this was noted constantly)
- Assigned to undesirable tasks (night drum shifts, latrine duty)
- Never invited to community councils
- Became a minor cautionary tale: "Don't be like the DJ American"

**Cultural Adaptation:**
Kaylee never fully adapted to French cave culture, but the caves adapted to her:

| Kaylee's Habit | Community Reaction | Long-term Result |
|----------------|-------------------|------------------|
| Saying "ope" | Confusion, then adoption | "Ope" became standard exclamation |
| Apologizing constantly | Annoyed tolerance | Lindquist descendants over-apologize |
| "Hot dish" references | "What is hot dish?" | Mushroom casseroles called "hot dish" |
| "You betcha" | Mimicked mockingly | Now sincere expression |
| Weather small talk | Baffled (they're in a cave) | Became family ritual |

**Romantic Life:**
- Year 3 AS: Began relationship with **Théo Marchand**, Henri's nephew
- Year 4 AS: Married (small ceremony, grudging attendance)
- Year 5 AS: Son born (**Thomas Lindquist-Marchand**, later simplified to Lindquist)
- Théo died Year 12 AS (tunnel collapse during expansion)

**Her Response to Ostracism:**
- Embraced outsider status ("They're just jealous of my energy")
- Kept the vinyl records (no one wanted them)
- Created secret "listening sessions" with her son
- Developed the mythology that would become the Vinyl Cult
- Maintained her Midwestern mannerisms aggressively

### Years 10-40 AS

**The Mythology She Created:**

| What She Told Her Son | What Was Actually True |
|----------------------|------------------------|
| "I was guided to the caves by destiny" | She thought it was a rave |
| "The music saved us" | She nearly killed everyone with bad DJing |
| "Voltage Cathedral were prophets who spoke through synthesizers" | They were a mid-tier French electro duo |
| "The Studio is a sacred place of power" | It's a recording studio she was stalking |
| "Lectromusic holds ancient wisdom" | It's just electronic dance music |
| "Back in South Dakota, I was basically a DJ" | She attended raves; she never DJ'd |

**The Midwestern Gospel:**
Kaylee also passed down her South Dakota heritage as quasi-religious tradition:

| Teaching | Context |
|----------|---------|
| "Minnesota Nice" | Being polite even to people you hate |
| "Uff da" | Expression of exhaustion or dismay |
| "You betcha" | Enthusiastic agreement |
| "Could be worse" | Response to any tragedy |
| "That's different" | Passive-aggressive disapproval |
| "Oh, for cute!" | Genuine approval |
| Hot dish recipes | Mushroom casseroles with crushed cracker topping |

**Death (Year 40 AS):**

Kaylee died at age 63, from pneumonia in the caves. Her last words:

> "The Studio is still out there. The music is waiting. You'll find it. And when you do... you betcha it's gonna be amazing."

Her son Thomas was 35. He believed every word.

### The Lindquist Family Lingo (Year 300 — Evolved)

Kaylee's phrases drifted over 300 years of oral transmission into something barely recognizable:

| Original (2034) | Year 300 Form | Meaning | Usage |
|-----------------|---------------|---------|-------|
| "Ope" | **"Ope"** | Surprise/apology | Unchanged — too short to drift |
| "You betcha" | **"Yabetja"** | Strong agreement | Almost a blessing |
| "Uff da" | **"Oofda"** | Exhaustion/dismay | Also a minor curse |
| "Oh, for cute!" | **"Fercute"** | Genuine approval | High praise |
| "That's different" | **"S'diff"** | Disapproval | Deadly insult among Lindquists |
| "Could be worse" | **"Codworse"** | Acceptance of fate | Almost religious |
| "Don't cha know" | **"Doncha"** | Emphasis/certainty | Adds weight to claims |
| "Cold enough for ya?" | **"Coldenuff?"** | Ritual greeting | Expected response: "Yabetja" |
| "Hot dish" | **"Odiche"** | Sacred meal | Sunday mushroom casserole |
| "Let me just sneak past ya" | **"Sneepas"** | Excuse me | Very polite |
| "Oh geez" | **"Ojeez"** | Mild dismay | Less serious than "oofda" |
| "For cryin' out loud" | **"Forcry"** | Exasperation | Often precedes leaving |

**Example Family Conversation (Year 300):**

> **Elder Lindquist:** "Coldenuff?"
> **Young Lindquist:** "Yabetja. Oofda, that tunnel's drafty, doncha."
> **Elder:** "Codworse. Least we got odiche."
> **Young:** "Fercute! You make that?"
> **Elder:** "Sneepas—" *(squeezes by)* "—I'll get you a plate."
> **Young:** *(bumps shelf)* "Ope."

Outsiders find this incomprehensible. The Lindquists consider it the sacred tongue of The Raver.

## The Truth vs. The Legend

| Legend (Vinyl Cult belief) | Truth |
|---------------------------|-------|
| Kaylee was chosen by destiny to survive | She confused date formats and thought she was going to a rave |
| She brought sacred knowledge from America | She brought platform sneakers and a flower crown |
| Her DJ performance was misunderstood genius | It was objectively terrible |
| Voltage Cathedral were prophets | They were a decent French electro duo |
| The Studio contains ancient power | It's a recording studio with a Crystal |
| Lectromusic can save humanity | It's just EDM |
| The Lindquists are blessed | They're treated as expendable weirdos |
| The Midwestern phrases are ancient wisdom | They're just how people talk in South Dakota |

**The irony:** Everything the Hero believes about their sacred mission is built on a foundation of a rich American girl's terrible DJ set and refusal to accept she was bad at it.

But they found The Studio anyway. The Crystal is real. The records are playable.

Maybe Kaylee was right about *something*.
