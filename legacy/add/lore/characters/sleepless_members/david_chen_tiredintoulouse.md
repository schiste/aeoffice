## The Hidden Connection

**David is the younger brother of Sarah Chen (@DeciBella), one of the Sounding Five.**

This is one of the most important hidden connections in the lore:

| Fact | David Knew | The World Knows (Year 300) |
|------|------------|---------------------------|
| Sister's identity | Knew Sarah was @DeciBella | No one knows @DeciBella = Sarah Chen |
| Sister's conspiracy role | **No** — thought she was just an acoustics researcher | The conspiracy is mostly forgotten |
| His own contribution | Helped Sarah with acoustics research (legitimately) | His papers are religious texts (misunderstood) |
| Connection to The Silence | **None** — innocent bystander | Historians don't connect Chen-Vidal to Sounding Five |

**What David carried to the Tarn caves:**
- Audio engineering equipment (useful for survival)
- Annotated copies of Sarah's acoustics research papers
- Knowledge of sound propagation, frequency analysis
- No idea his sister helped end the world

### The Jean-Claude Saga

David's claim to fame in Sleepless in Decibels: a 2,000-word manifesto about his neighbor's rooster.

**The Story (June 23, 2026):**
- David's neighbor kept a rooster named Jean-Claude
- Not on a farm — in a studio apartment in central Toulouse
- Jean-Claude crowed at 4:30 AM daily
- David's manifesto was both furious and oddly respectful
- The phrase "At least it's not Jean-Claude" became a group inside joke
- When Jean-Claude escaped (September 2026), the group tracked his whereabouts for six days
- David's final post about Jean-Claude: "He's living his best life. I respect it."

## Survival & Post-Silence Life

### Reaching the Tarn Caves (Day 1)

When The Silence began (October 14, 2034), David was in Toulouse:

**Timeline:**
- 4:34 AM UTC: The Filter activates
- 5:00 AM: David wakes to chaos — no radio, phones glitching
- 6:00 AM: Realizes something is deeply wrong
- 7:00 AM: Grabs his hiking gear, audio equipment, Sarah's research papers
- 9:00 AM: Reaches Tarn cave entrance (knew it from hiking)
- By noon: 30+ others have gathered at the cave

**Why the Tarn caves?**
- David knew the cave system from years of hiking
- Natural limestone formation, deep enough to block The Filter
- He didn't know *why* depth mattered — just instinct
- The audio equipment he brought proved invaluable for understanding sound in the caves

### Founding the Tarn Settlement

**Year 1-10 AS:**
- David emerged as a natural leader (calm, practical, technically skilled)
- His audio engineering knowledge helped optimize cave acoustics
- Established the Tarn settlement's first "rhythm protocols"
- Population grew from ~30 to ~200 by Year 10

**Year 10-47 AS:**
- Led the Tarn community for nearly four decades
- Married a French woman named Isabelle Vidal (Year 6 AS)
- Had three children: Pierre, Mathilde, and Henri
- Passed down his audio equipment and Sarah's papers as "sacred knowledge"
- Never learned the truth about his sister

### Death (Year 47 AS)

David died at age 71, surrounded by his family in the Tarn caves:

> "His last words, according to his daughter Mathilde: 'The sound must continue. Don't let it stop.'
>
> We buried him in the deepest chamber, where the echoes last longest."
>
> — From the Tarn Settlement Chronicles
