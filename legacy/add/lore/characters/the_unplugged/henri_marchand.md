### The Marchand Family of Saint-Etienne-de-Chigny

Henri's family history is central to the survival of humanity.

**Paternal Grandparents:**

| Name | Born | Died | Occupation | Significance |
|------|------|------|------------|--------------|
| **Georges Marchand** | 1932 | 2015 | Stonemason / Quarryman | Knew every cave in the Bresme valley; taught Henri to navigate underground |
| **Madeleine Marchand** (nee Dubois) | 1935 | 2019 | Homemaker / Mushroom farmer | Raised family in troglodyte house; keeper of oral history |

**Family Home:**

*La Demeure aux Tilleuls* ("The Linden Tree Dwelling") — A traditional troglodyte house carved into the tuffeau limestone cliffs above the Bresme river in Saint-Etienne-de-Chigny. The Marchand family lived there for three generations.

- Built: ~1890 (expanded from older cave)
- Rooms carved directly into limestone
- Connected to deeper cave network (wine cellars, old quarries)
- Sold by Henri's parents in 2019 after Madeleine's death
- Current status: Unknown (post-Silence)

**Henri's Summers (1985-1998):**

Every summer from age 5 to 18, Henri stayed with his grandparents in Saint-Etienne-de-Chigny. During those summers:

- Georges taught him the cave systems ("Every boy should know where to hide")
- He learned to navigate by touch, by echo, by the feel of limestone
- He explored abandoned mushroom farms, quarry tunnels, forgotten passages
- He drew maps with his grandfather — some later proved critical to survival
- He learned the quarrymen's marks (medieval symbols indicating safe passages)
- He learned that caves are not dead space but living systems

**Why It Mattered:**

When The Unplugged needed a shelter, Henri knew exactly where to go. Not because of mystical fate — because his grandfather had patiently taught a curious child every summer for thirteen years.

## Psychology

### Core Motivation

Protecting his family. Everything else is secondary. The caves, the preparations, the leadership — all in service of keeping Nathalie, Lucas, and Emma safe.

### Greatest Fear

Being unable to protect them. Being above ground when something happens below. Being too slow, too weak, too unprepared.

### Secret/Shame

He suspected the truth about Marc and the Sounding Five. He saw the patterns. He chose not to ask because the answer might have obligated him to act — and acting might have endangered his family's place in the preparations.

He chose his family over the world.

He's not sure he was wrong.

### Moral Line

He won't sacrifice strangers to protect his own — but he'll prioritize his own when resources are limited. He won't lie to his children. He won't abandon people who trust him.

### Coping Mechanisms

- Physical work (excavation, construction)
- Mapping and planning (control through knowledge)
- Wine (moderate, social)
- Walking the tunnels alone

### Triggers

- Sudden loud noises (professional hazard)
- Being underground when someone above might need him
- Anyone questioning his preparations (defensive)
- Mentions of his grandparents (emotional)

## Background

### Life Before the Group

Henri lived a good life. Successful career, happy marriage, two children he was proud of. He'd achieved more than his parents expected — a university degree, professional recognition, a nice apartment in Lyon.

But he never forgot where he came from. Every year, he took his family to Touraine. He showed his children the caves, though they never loved them as he did. He maintained the family grave in Saint-Etienne-de-Chigny. He kept his grandfather's maps.

He joined Sleepless in Decibels because of banal apartment noise — nothing apocalyptic. The upstairs neighbors. Construction outside. The modern world's constant hum.

He stayed because he found people who understood exhaustion.

### The Noise Problem

Lyon apartment: thin walls, inconsiderate neighbors, endless urban construction. Nothing compared to some members' situations, but enough to make him appreciate the group's gallows humor.

His real noise problem was existential: the feeling that modern life was too loud, too fast, too disconnected from the quiet rhythms of his grandfather's world.

### Defining Moment

**Summer 1994, age 7.** Lost in the caves for three hours. His grandfather found him calm, sitting in the dark, listening to water drip.

"You're not scared?" Georges asked.

"I knew you'd come," Henri said. "And the caves aren't scary. They're just quiet."

Georges smiled. "Then you're a Marchand."

### What He Wanted

To retire to Touraine. Buy a house near his grandparents' old place. Grow a garden. Drink wine from caves he could walk to. Watch his grandchildren explore the same tunnels he explored.

He got the caves. He got the quiet.

Not the way he imagined.

## Story Function

### Role in Narrative

Henri is the bridge between the modern world and ancient survival knowledge. He represents:
- Practical competence over ideology
- Family loyalty over abstract ethics
- The value of inherited knowledge
- The tragedy of a good man who didn't ask hard questions

### Information They Hold

- Detailed knowledge of the cave systems
- His grandfather's maps (some passages unknown to anyone else)
- Suspicions about Marc that he never voiced
- The location of emergency caches he prepared alone

### Fate

**Year 1 AS:** Henri died approximately three months after The Silence. His heart condition, manageable with modern medication, became fatal without it. Stress and physical exertion accelerated the decline.

He died in The Sanctuary, surrounded by the people he'd saved, in caves his grandfather had shown him.

His last words to Marc: "You knew. I don't need to know why. Thank you."
