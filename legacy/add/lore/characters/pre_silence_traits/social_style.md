# Social Style

> **Category:** Personal Traits
>
> **Template Field:** `Social Style`
>
> **Dialog Impact:** Conversation patterns, group behavior, power dynamics

---

## Overview

Social style determines how someone behaves in group interactions—who talks first, who listens, who leads, who follows. It shapes conversation dynamics and relationships.

---

## Styles

### Dominant

**Definition:** Leads conversations, states opinions firmly, takes charge.

**Characteristics:**
- Speaks first and often
- Interrupts (intentionally or not)
- States opinions as facts
- Moves conversation toward action
- May not realize they're dominating

**Dialog Markers:**
```
"Here's what we're going to do."
"Let me stop you there."
"The bottom line is..."
"Anyone disagree? Good."
"I'll take the lead on this."
```

**Group Impact:**
- Sets agenda and pace
- May suppress quieter voices
- Gets things done
- Can alienate others

**Sleepless Context:**
- Natural leader in group
- May push toward action too fast
- Could be founding five material
- Frustrates consensus-seekers

---

### Influential

**Definition:** Persuades, enthuses, sells ideas, brings energy.

**Characteristics:**
- Tells stories to make points
- Enthusiastic pitch mode
- Wants buy-in from others
- Optimistic framing
- Builds excitement

**Dialog Markers:**
```
"Imagine if we could..."
"This is going to change everything."
"Let me tell you about a guy who..."
"Are you in? I'm in."
"This is bigger than we realize."
```

**Group Impact:**
- Rallies support
- May oversell
- Creates momentum
- Can manipulate

**Sleepless Context:**
- Recruiter energy
- Could bring people in
- May downplay risks
- Good at messaging, dangerous at execution

---

### Steady

**Definition:** Listens, supports, asks clarifying questions, maintains harmony.

**Characteristics:**
- Lets others speak first
- Validates contributions
- Asks "how does everyone feel?"
- Seeks common ground
- Reliable follow-through

**Dialog Markers:**
```
"I hear what you're saying."
"How does everyone else feel about this?"
"Let's make sure we're all comfortable."
"I'll support whatever we decide."
"What do you need from me?"
```

**Group Impact:**
- Stabilizing presence
- May not push own agenda
- Keeps group cohesive
- Can enable dysfunction

**Sleepless Context:**
- The glue person
- May not challenge bad ideas
- Trusted by all
- Could be exploited

---

### Conscientious

**Definition:** Careful, asks for details, wants to be correct, follows process.

**Characteristics:**
- Asks "how" and "why" questions
- Wants data and evidence
- Cautious about committing
- Notices what's missing
- Holds to standards

**Dialog Markers:**
```
"Wait, what's the evidence for that?"
"Let me understand the details first."
"Has anyone verified this?"
"I'm not comfortable until I know..."
"What does the process say?"
```

**Group Impact:**
- Quality control
- May slow momentum
- Prevents errors
- Can seem obstructionist

**Sleepless Context:**
- The fact-checker
- May catch problems early
- Could be dismissed as paranoid
- Valuable if heard

---

### Avoidant

**Definition:** Withdraws, short answers, deflects, stays peripheral.

**Characteristics:**
- Brief responses
- Doesn't volunteer
- Watches from edges
- Uncomfortable with attention
- May have valuable input never shared

**Dialog Markers:**
```
"I don't know."
"Whatever you think."
"I'm just here to listen."
"..."
[Long pauses, shrugs]
```

**Group Impact:**
- Easy to overlook
- Doesn't create conflict
- May disengage entirely
- Could be checked out or storing info

**Sleepless Context:**
- The lurker in person
- May know more than they share
- Could be silently dissenting
- Might suddenly become crucial

## Style Combinations

People often combine styles:

| Combination | Description |
|-------------|-------------|
| Dominant + Influential | Charismatic leader, may overwhelm |
| Influential + Steady | Warm persuader, builds consensus |
| Steady + Conscientious | Careful supporter, thorough |
| Dominant + Conscientious | Exacting leader, demanding |
| Avoidant + Conscientious | Silent expert, hard to access |

---

## Style Under Stress

Sleep deprivation and crisis affect social style:

| Style | Under Stress |
|-------|-------------|
| Dominant | More aggressive, less listening |
| Influential | More desperate selling |
| Steady | Withdraws or becomes doormat |
| Conscientious | Paralyzed by uncertainty |
| Avoidant | Disappears entirely |

---

## Style Dynamics in Groups

How different styles interact:

| Pairing | Dynamic |
|---------|---------|
| Dominant + Dominant | Power struggle |
| Dominant + Steady | Works if Steady is okay with it |
| Influential + Conscientious | Friction (hype vs. caution) |
| Steady + Steady | Pleasant but leaderless |
| Avoidant + Avoidant | Nothing gets said |
