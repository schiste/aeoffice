# Institution Prestige

> **Category:** Education & Credentials
>
> **Template Field:** `Institution Prestige`
>
> **Dialog Impact:** Confidence, networking, elitism

---

## Overview

Where someone went to school affects their networks, their confidence, and how others perceive them. It also shapes their relationship to credentialism and institutional authority.

---

## Levels

### Elite / Ivy League

**Definition:** Harvard, Stanford, MIT, Yale, Princeton, Oxbridge, Grandes Écoles, etc.

**Dialog Markers:**
- Doesn't mention school (doesn't need to)
- Or drops it subtly and constantly
- Network references ("my roommate from...")
- Confidence in own judgment
- May be unaware of privilege

**Speech Patterns:**
```
"Oh, you went to [state school]? That's great." [condescending without meaning to]
"A friend from college actually works on this exact thing."
"In my program, they taught us to think about..."
"It's not about the name—but also, doors open differently."
```

**Behavioral Notes:**
- Either humble about it or insufferable
- Assumes competence unless proven otherwise
- Networks are genuinely powerful
- May be isolated from "regular" experiences

---

### Highly Selective

**Definition:** Top 50 schools, flagship state universities, respected specialized institutions.

**Dialog Markers:**
- Proud of school
- Mentions rankings
- Good network but works harder for it
- May feel like "almost elite"
- Defensive about not being Ivy

**Speech Patterns:**
```
"[My school] is actually ranked higher than you'd think."
"We had a solid program in [field]."
"I didn't need to go to Harvard to learn that."
"The professors there were actually published, doing real research."
```

---

### State / Public University

**Definition:** Large public universities, varying quality, democratic access.

**Dialog Markers:**
- School pride (often sports-related)
- Practical education emphasis
- May be defensive or dismissive of prestige
- "Real world" orientation
- Debt and cost awareness

**Speech Patterns:**
```
"I got a great education without the debt."
"State schools produce most of the actual [profession]."
"Nobody cares where you went after five years."
"Go [mascot]! Sorry, had to."
```

---

### Community College / Local Institution

**Definition:** Two-year colleges, local institutions, accessible higher education.

**Dialog Markers:**
- Practical, vocational focus
- May downplay education
- "It was what I could afford"
- Transfer pathway stories
- No school network to speak of

**Speech Patterns:**
```
"I did my first two years at community college."
"It got me where I needed to go."
"Not everyone has parents who can pay for four years."
"I was working full-time. Community college made it possible."
```

---

### Online / Non-Traditional

**Definition:** University of Phoenix, online programs, boot camps, certificates.

**Dialog Markers:**
- Defensive about legitimacy
- Emphasizes practical skills
- "The degree is the same"
- May overcompensate with credentials
- Working while studying

**Speech Patterns:**
```
"The degree says the same thing on it."
"I learned more from the bootcamp than four years would have taught me."
"Try doing school while working full-time and raising kids."
"Employers care about skills, not where you got them."
```

---

### No Formal Institution

**Definition:** Self-taught, apprenticed, learned on the job.

**Dialog Markers:**
- Anti-credential stance
- "School of hard knocks"
- Suspicious of formal education
- Practical proof over papers
- May be brilliant or bluffing

**Speech Patterns:**
```
"Never went to college. Never needed to."
"I learned by doing. That's worth more."
"Show me what you can do, not where you went."
"Some of the smartest people I know dropped out."
```

---

### Foreign Institution

**Definition:** Educated abroad, credentials may not translate directly.

**Dialog Markers:**
- Translation and equivalency issues
- May be overqualified but underrecognized
- "In [country], I was..."
- Navigating credential recognition
- Cultural differences in education

**Speech Patterns:**
```
"In Germany, my degree was in [long specific title]."
"Here, nobody knows my university, but it's one of the best in [country]."
"I have to explain what my qualification means every time."
"The education was rigorous. Getting it recognized is the hard part."
```

## Prestige Dynamics

### Prestige Insecurity
- Went to good school, still feels inadequate
- "I only got in because..."

### Prestige Overconfidence
- Assumes elite degree = elite ability
- "I went to [school], I think I know..."

### Anti-Prestige
- Deliberately rejects prestige markers
- "I don't care where you went"

### Prestige Recovery
- Didn't go to good school, proves worth anyway
- "I had to work twice as hard to be taken seriously"

---

## Impact on Sleepless Dynamics

Prestige creates invisible hierarchies in the group:
- Whose expertise is trusted?
- Who assumes leadership?
- Who feels like an outsider?
- Whose ideas get airtime?
