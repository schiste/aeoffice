> **Category:** Parenting & Family Stress

> **Template Field:** `Noise Complaint History`

## Overview

Every Sleepless in Decibels member has a noise story. The specifics of their noise complaint shapes their desperation level, what solutions they've tried, and what they're willing to consider.

---

## Questions to Define

### What is the noise source?

**Residential:**
- Neighbor's music/bass
- Neighbor's parties
- Barking dogs
- Footsteps above
- Domestic disputes
- Children (other people's)
- Construction/renovation
- HVAC/mechanical systems

**Commercial/Industrial:**
- Nearby business
- Delivery trucks
- Industrial facility
- Bars/clubs
- Event venues
- Traffic

**Infrastructure:**
- Flight paths
- Train lines
- Highway
- Construction projects
- Emergency services route

**Other:**
- Unknown source
- Multiple sources
- Intermittent/unpredictable

---

### How long has it been going on?

| Duration | Psychological Effect |
|----------|---------------------|
| Days-Weeks | Acute frustration, still hopeful |
| Months | Wearing down, tried several things |
| 1-2 Years | Chronic stress, hope fading |
| Years+ | Permanent fixture, rebuilt life around it |

---

### What have they tried?

**Direct Approaches:**
- Talked to neighbor
- Written notes
- Mediation attempts
- Escalating confrontation

**Official Channels:**
- Called police/non-emergency
- Filed noise complaints
- Contacted landlord
- HOA involvement
- Code enforcement
- Legal action

**Physical Solutions:**
- Earplugs/noise-canceling
- White noise machines
- Soundproofing attempts
- Rearranging house
- Moving bedrooms

**Escape Attempts:**
- Staying elsewhere temporarily
- Looking for new housing
- Staying at work late
- Driving around at night

**Coping Mechanisms:**
- Medication (sleep aids, anxiety)
- Alcohol
- Therapy
- Support groups (hello, Sleepless)
- Acceptance attempts

---

### What's the current status?

| Status | Implications |
|--------|--------------|
| Ongoing, unchanged | Chronic stress, desperation |
| Ongoing, getting worse | Crisis building |
| Intermittent | Unpredictability adds anxiety |
| "Resolved" but trauma remains | May still participate |
| Moved, but problem followed | Feels inescapable |

## Sample Noise Histories

### The Upstairs Neighbor

```
Source: Neighbor's subwoofer
Duration: 8 months
Tried: Conversation (2x), notes, landlord complaints (3x), 311, earplugs, white noise, soundproofing ceiling
Status: Ongoing. Landlord won't act. Can't afford to break lease.
Effect: Baby wakes every time bass hits. Wife considering divorce.
```

**Character Implication:**
- Feels trapped
- Has tried "proper channels"
- Ready for improper channels

---

### The Construction Project

```
Source: High-rise construction, starts 6am
Duration: 18 months (projected 2 more years)
Tried: City permits (valid), noise ordinance review (compliant), soundproofing, white noise
Status: Legal. Ongoing. No recourse.
Effect: Twins sleep in shifts. Parent sleep destroyed.
```

**Character Implication:**
- Anger at powerlessness
- System is working but broken
- May radicalize against "progress"

---

### The Unknown Source

```
Source: Low-frequency hum, intermittent, unidentifiable
Duration: 6 months
Tried: Acoustic consultants, neighbor queries, utility companies, doctor (hearing checked)
Status: Unknown. Sometimes present, sometimes not.
Effect: Feels crazy. Nobody else hears it. Sleep anxiety.
```

**Character Implication:**
- May be dismissed by others
- Self-doubt
- Desperate for validation
- Could be most desperate

---

### The Resolution That Wasn't

```
Source: Neighbor's dog (barking at night)
Duration: 2 years
Resolution: Neighbor moved. Dog gone.
Status: Quiet now. 3 months.
Effect: Still wakes at phantom barks. PTSD. Can't trust silence.
```

**Character Implication:**
- Trauma persists
- May stay in group for community
- Understands others' desperation
- Warning about lasting damage
