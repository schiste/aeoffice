# Mental Health Status

> **Category:** Health & Physical
>
> **Template Field:** `Mental Health`
>
> **Dialog Impact:** Anxiety, depression, stress management, medication

---

## Overview

Mental health status affects how characters process stress, communicate, and cope. In the context of sleep deprivation and conspiracy involvement, mental health is both cause and effect.

---

## Statuses

### Stable

**Definition:** Well-adjusted, good coping mechanisms, generally healthy.

**Characteristics:**
- Handles stress well
- Maintains perspective
- Support systems work
- Bounces back
- Clear thinking under pressure

**Dialog Markers:**
```
"This is hard, but I'll get through it."
"I have people I can lean on."
"I've dealt with difficult things before."
"Let me take a breath and think."
"I know my limits."
```

**In Sleepless Context:**
- May be anchor for others
- Could be unaware of own decline
- Sleep deprivation will test stability
- "I thought I was fine until I wasn't"

---

### Anxious

**Definition:** Worry-prone, catastrophizes, needs reassurance, hypervigilant.

**Characteristics:**
- Anticipates worst case
- Physical symptoms (racing heart, tension)
- Seeks reassurance repeatedly
- Avoids triggering situations
- May be highly functional but suffering

**Dialog Markers:**
```
"What if it gets worse?"
"I can't stop thinking about it."
"Has anyone else worried about...?"
"I need to know the plan."
"Just tell me it's going to be okay."
```

**Under Sleep Deprivation:**
- Anxiety spirals
- Catastrophizing intensifies
- Physical symptoms worsen
- May become paralyzed

**In Sleepless Context:**
- May be most motivated by fear
- Could predict real dangers
- Exhausting for others
- Could drive or derail action

---

### Depressed

**Definition:** Low energy, pessimistic, withdrawn, possibly hopeless.

**Characteristics:**
- Low motivation
- Negative self-talk
- Withdraws from others
- May seem passive
- Difficulty with decisions

**Dialog Markers:**
```
"What's the point?"
"It doesn't matter anyway."
"I'm just so tired."
"Go ahead without me."
"I can't do this anymore."
```

**Under Sleep Deprivation:**
- Deepening depression
- Energy completely gone
- May become suicidal
- Withdrawal from group

**In Sleepless Context:**
- May be silent sufferer
- Could disappear from group
- Most vulnerable to crisis
- May not ask for help

---

### Stressed

**Definition:** Overwhelmed, short-tempered, coping poorly, at capacity.

**Characteristics:**
- Snapping at people
- Can't take one more thing
- Losing track of tasks
- Physical exhaustion
- "Running on empty"

**Dialog Markers:**
```
"I can't deal with this right now."
"Everything is too much."
"Don't add another thing."
"I just need everyone to leave me alone."
"I'm barely holding it together."
```

**Under Sleep Deprivation:**
- This is most common state
- Normal for sleep-deprived parents
- Can persist for months
- Becomes chronic

**In Sleepless Context:**
- Baseline for most members
- May make desperate decisions
- Capacity for ethics reduced
- "I'll do anything to make this stop"

---

### In Treatment

**Definition:** Seeing therapist, on medication, actively working on mental health.

**Characteristics:**
- Self-aware about issues
- Has professional support
- May be managing well
- Or may be struggling despite help
- Takes effort to maintain

**Dialog Markers:**
```
"My therapist says..."
"The medication helps, mostly."
"I'm working on it."
"I have an appointment tomorrow."
"I've learned some coping strategies."
```

**In Sleepless Context:**
- May have better insight
- Professional may notice changes
- Medication affected by sleep loss
- "Even my therapist doesn't know what to suggest"

---

### Undiagnosed

**Definition:** Symptoms present but not addressed. May be in denial or unaware.

**Characteristics:**
- Struggling but "fine"
- Symptoms visible to others
- May resist suggestion of help
- Doesn't have language for it
- Could be cultural, financial, or personal barriers

**Dialog Markers:**
```
"I'm fine. I'm just tired."
"Everyone deals with this."
"I don't need a therapist."
"It's just stress."
"I'll feel better when [condition] changes."
```

**In Sleepless Context:**
- Most common in group
- May attribute everything to noise/sleep
- Doesn't recognize own decline
- Could be most at risk

## Mental Health and Consent

Important for character ethics:

| Mental State | Consent Quality |
|--------------|-----------------|
| Stable | Can give informed consent |
| Anxious | May consent under fear |
| Depressed | May consent passively |
| Stressed | Consent to make it stop |
| In Treatment | Depends on treatment success |
| Undiagnosed | Impaired without knowing |

**Relevance to Operation Lullaby:**
Many members may have compromised capacity to consent to conspiracy participation due to mental health + sleep deprivation.

## Mental Health Trajectories

How mental health changes over story:

| Period | Common Trajectory |
|--------|-------------------|
| Joining group | "Finally, people who understand" |
| Active involvement | Stress increasing, purpose provides focus |
| Late conspiracy | Mental health declining, too invested to leave |
| Post-Silence | Survivors carry complex trauma |
