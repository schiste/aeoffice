# Technology View

> **Category:** Beliefs & Values
>
> **Template Field:** `View of Technology`
>
> **Dialog Impact:** How they relate to tech solutions, Operation Lullaby

---

## Overview

A character's view of technology shapes how they approach the Resonance solution—and how they rationalize (or resist) its deployment.

---

## Views

### Techno-Optimist

**Definition:** Technology solves problems. Progress is good. The future is better.

**Beliefs:**
- Every problem has a tech solution
- Innovation is inherently positive
- Early adoption is valuable
- "Move fast" is good
- Side effects are worth it

**Dialog Markers:**
```
"There's definitely a technical solution to this."
"We just need the right app/device/system."
"Technology has always improved life. This is no different."
"Let's disrupt the noise problem."
"I trust engineers more than politicians."
```

**Relationship to Operation Lullaby:**
- Natural supporters
- May be architects
- "We built something that works"
- Hardest to recognize harm

---

### Techno-Skeptic

**Definition:** Technology creates as many problems as it solves. Unintended consequences matter.

**Beliefs:**
- New tech has hidden costs
- "Smart" often means "surveillance"
- Old solutions are proven
- Beware the hype cycle
- Who profits from this?

**Dialog Markers:**
```
"What are the side effects we're not thinking about?"
"Just because we can doesn't mean we should."
"Remember when everyone said [past tech] was safe?"
"Who's getting rich off this?"
"I don't trust anything that 'just works.'"
```

**Relationship to Operation Lullaby:**
- Early warning voices
- May be Unplugged candidates
- "I told you something was wrong"
- May be dismissed until too late

---

### Pragmatist

**Definition:** Tools are neutral. Depends on use. Neither worship nor fear.

**Beliefs:**
- Technology is a tool
- Context matters
- Benefits and costs are case-by-case
- No inherent good or evil
- Judge by results

**Dialog Markers:**
```
"Does it work? That's what matters."
"Every tool can be misused."
"I'm neither for nor against—show me the data."
"Technology is what we make of it."
"Let's evaluate this objectively."
```

**Relationship to Operation Lullaby:**
- Could go either way
- May be convinced by results
- May be convinced by ethics
- Key swing group

---

### Luddite

**Definition:** Prefers analog. Overwhelmed by digital. Doesn't want to learn.

**Beliefs:**
- Things were better before
- Digital is alienating
- Don't need it
- Can't learn it (or won't)
- Simpler is better

**Dialog Markers:**
```
"I still have a flip phone. It works fine."
"Can someone explain this to me again?"
"I don't trust anything I can't fix myself."
"My parents didn't need all this."
"Whatever happened to talking to people?"
```

**Relationship to Operation Lullaby:**
- May not understand what's happening
- Could be left out of technical decisions
- Might see danger others miss
- "I don't understand it but it feels wrong"

---

### Industry Insider

**Definition:** Works in tech. Knows how it really works. Cynical or enthusiastic based on experience.

**Beliefs:**
- Knows the sausage-making
- Either "we're building the future" or "it's all smoke and mirrors"
- Understands technical limitations
- May be jaded or proud
- Knows what's possible

**Dialog Markers:**
```
"I've seen how these things actually get built."
"The demo never matches the product."
"Users don't understand what they're agreeing to."
"I know what's possible. This isn't."
[Or] "This is actually feasible. Here's how."
```

**Relationship to Operation Lullaby:**
- Knows if it's real
- May be builder or whistleblower
- "I worked on this"
- Heaviest moral weight if involved

## Technology View + Conspiracy Role

| Tech View | Likely Conspiracy Role |
|-----------|----------------------|
| Techno-Optimist | Architect, True Believer |
| Techno-Skeptic | Unplugged, Whistleblower |
| Pragmatist | Late Joiner, Conditional |
| Luddite | Unaware, Resistant |
| Industry Insider | Core Builder or Core Doubter |
