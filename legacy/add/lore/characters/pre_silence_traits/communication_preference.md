# Communication Preference

> **Category:** Personal Traits
>
> **Template Field:** `Communication Preference`
>
> **Dialog Impact:** How they prefer to exchange information

---

## Overview

Communication preference determines how someone likes to give and receive information—directly or indirectly, in stories or bullets, through questions or statements.

---

## Preferences

### Direct

**Definition:** Says what they mean, expects the same, impatient with hints.

**Characteristics:**
- States things plainly
- Wants straight answers
- May seem blunt or rude
- Efficient communication
- Frustrated by indirection

**Dialog Markers:**
```
"Just tell me what you need."
"What's the actual problem?"
"Skip to the point."
"I don't understand hints. Say it."
"Yes or no?"
```

**Group Dynamics:**
- Clear about positions
- May miss subtext
- Others may find harsh
- Good for action, bad for feelings

**Sleepless Context:**
- Gets to issues fast
- May push confrontation
- Good for decision-making
- Bad for diplomatic solutions

---

### Indirect

**Definition:** Implies, suggests, expects reading between lines.

**Characteristics:**
- Cushions statements
- Uses qualifiers
- May seem evasive
- Protects harmony
- Frustrated by bluntness

**Dialog Markers:**
```
"I was wondering if maybe..."
"Some people might think..."
"It's possible that..."
"Not that I'm saying anything, but..."
"You might want to consider..."
```

**Group Dynamics:**
- Diplomatic
- May not be understood
- Others may miss point
- Good for feelings, slow for action

**Sleepless Context:**
- Handles delicate issues
- May not convey urgency
- Good at maintaining relationships
- May not be heard when desperate

---

### Narrative

**Definition:** Tells stories to make points, roundabout, builds to conclusion.

**Characteristics:**
- Every point has a story
- Context is everything
- May take time to get there
- Engaging but slow
- Remembers through narrative

**Dialog Markers:**
```
"Let me tell you what happened..."
"This reminds me of..."
"So, picture this..."
"The backstory is important."
"...and that's why I think we should."
```

**Group Dynamics:**
- Engaging for some
- Frustrating for others
- Provides context
- May lose direct thinkers

**Sleepless Context:**
- Good at explaining situations
- May not get to the point fast enough
- Valuable for documentation
- Could be the group's chronicler

---

### Interrogative

**Definition:** Asks questions, draws information out, Socratic.

**Characteristics:**
- Questions more than states
- Leads others to conclusions
- May seem like not contributing
- Actually processing deeply
- Frustrates impatient types

**Dialog Markers:**
```
"What do you mean by that?"
"Have you considered...?"
"Why do you think that happened?"
"What would it take to...?"
"And then what?"
```

**Group Dynamics:**
- Facilitates discussion
- May not state own view
- Helps others think
- Can seem evasive

**Sleepless Context:**
- Good at understanding problems
- May not drive solutions
- Helps clarify group thinking
- Could be the therapist type

---

### Demonstrative

**Definition:** Shows rather than tells, actions over words.

**Characteristics:**
- "Let me show you"
- Actions speak louder
- May not verbalize well
- Proof through doing
- Impatient with talk

**Dialog Markers:**
```
"Watch this."
"I'll just do it."
"Here—like this."
"Actions matter. Words don't."
[Does something instead of explaining]
```

**Group Dynamics:**
- Produces results
- May not explain reasoning
- Others may not understand why
- Good for execution, gaps in communication

**Sleepless Context:**
- Takes action while others talk
- May act without group consensus
- Gets things done
- Could be dangerously unilateral

---

## Preference Conflicts

Different preferences create friction:

| Preference A | Preference B | Conflict |
|--------------|--------------|----------|
| Direct | Indirect | "Just say it" vs. "That was rude" |
| Direct | Narrative | "Get to the point" vs. "Context matters" |
| Narrative | Interrogative | Story vs. Questions |
| Demonstrative | Narrative | "Enough talk" vs. "Let me explain" |
| Indirect | Demonstrative | Hints vs. Action |

---

## Preference Under Stress

Sleep deprivation and crisis affect communication:

| Preference | Under Stress |
|------------|--------------|
| Direct | Even more blunt, possibly harsh |
| Indirect | May become confusing or silent |
| Narrative | Stories become rambling or stopped |
| Interrogative | Questions become anxious |
| Demonstrative | Acts without warning |
