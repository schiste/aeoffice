## Overview

How someone communicates digitally—especially in the Telegram group—reveals different aspects of personality than face-to-face. For Sleepless in Decibels members, online communication may be their primary mode of connection.

---

## Styles

### Formal

**Definition:** Full sentences, proper punctuation, email-like even in chat.

**Characteristics:**
- Complete sentences always
- Proper capitalization and punctuation
- May seem cold or distant
- Takes longer to compose
- "Professional" in all contexts

**Sample Messages:**
```
I wanted to follow up on the discussion from yesterday. Has anyone made progress with the soundproofing approach?

Thank you for sharing that article. I found the section on acoustic frequencies particularly relevant.

I apologize for the late response. I was dealing with another incident.
```

**Why They're Like This:**
- Professional habit
- Non-native (learned formal English)
- Generational (didn't grow up with chat)
- Anxiety about being misunderstood
- Respect for others' time

---

### Casual

**Definition:** Lowercase, emoji, abbreviations. Conversational tone.

**Characteristics:**
- lowercase often
- emoji sprinkled throughout
- abbreviations normal
- Quick, responsive
- Personality visible

**Sample Messages:**
```
lol same 😭

tbh i think we're overthinking this

wait what did i miss? just got the kids down finally

anyone else just... done? like completely done?
```

**Why They're Like This:**
- Digital native
- Chat as natural medium
- Efficient
- Comfortable with informality
- May not realize it's jarring to formal types

---

### Verbose

**Definition:** Long messages, over-explains, walls of text.

**Characteristics:**
- Multiple paragraphs common
- Context provided (wanted or not)
- May overwhelm others
- Thorough but exhausting
- Apologizes for length (then continues)

**Sample Messages:**
```
So I've been thinking about what @WhiteNoise_Mom said yesterday, and I think there's something to it, but I also want to push back a little because my situation is different. We live in a townhouse, not an apartment, and the noise comes from the shared wall which is actually load-bearing which limits what we can do structurally. I talked to a contractor last year (long story, he turned out to be useless) and he said...

[continues for three more paragraphs]

Sorry for the essay lol
```

**Why They're Like This:**
- Anxious about being misunderstood
- Processor who thinks by writing
- Doesn't realize length
- Academic/professional habit
- Can't help but provide context

---

### Terse

**Definition:** Minimal words. May seem cold or efficient.

**Characteristics:**
- Few words
- No elaboration
- May seem abrupt
- Efficient or disengaged?
- Hard to read emotionally

**Sample Messages:**
```
Makes sense.

Tried that. Didn't work.

No.

following this thread

ok
```

**Why They're Like This:**
- Efficient by nature
- Typing on phone
- Distracted (kids, work)
- Doesn't like text medium
- Actually cold/disengaged

---

### Meme-Fluent

**Definition:** Communicates through references, reaction images, internet culture.

**Characteristics:**
- GIFs and memes frequent
- Reference-heavy
- May not work for all audiences
- Humor as primary mode
- Can seem unserious

**Sample Messages:**
```
[reaction gif of Michael Scott saying "NO"]

this is fine 🔥🐕🔥

me at 3am when the bass drops again: 💀

normalize destroying your neighbor's bluetooth speaker

(galaxy brain meme about our situation)
```

**Why They're Like This:**
- Digital native
- Humor as coping
- Community signal
- Efficient emotional shorthand
- May struggle with serious tone

---

### Lurker

**Definition:** Reads more than posts. Careful when speaking.

**Characteristics:**
- Rarely initiates
- Responds when specifically relevant
- Watches and learns
- May save important posts
- Valued contributions when they come

**Sample Messages:**
```
[Reacts to messages but rarely types]

Actually, I looked into this. [link] Here's what I found.

I've been reading along. I have the same problem.

[months of silence, then suddenly helpful detail]
```

**Why They're Like This:**
- Introvert
- Careful about what they say
- Information gatherer
- Imposter syndrome
- Time-poor (reads while nursing, etc.)

---

### Voice-Message Heavy

**Definition:** Prefers voice messages over typing.

**Characteristics:**
- Sends audio clips
- More natural expression
- Can be annoying to others
- Often longer than would type
- Personality very audible

**Sample Messages:**
```
[2:34 voice message about their day]

[0:47 voice message reacting to thread]

"I hate typing, so..." [sends voice]

[voice message sent at 3am, whispering, baby in background]
```

**Why They're Like This:**
- Hands busy (holding baby)
- More natural than typing
- Dyslexic or typing-averse
- Emotional nuance matters
- Cultural background

## Style Combinations

Many people combine styles:

| Combination | Example |
|-------------|---------|
| Casual + Verbose | Friendly essays |
| Formal + Terse | Professional short |
| Meme-Fluent + Lurker | Only reacts with reactions |
| Verbose + Formal | Academic walls of text |
| Terse + Casual | "lol no" |

---

## Style and Context Shifts

How style changes with context:

| Situation | Style Shift |
|-----------|-------------|
| Crisis | Terse becomes verbose, or vice versa |
| 3am exhaustion | All become more raw |
| Good news | Meme-fluent people emerge |
| Technical discussion | Formal increases |
| Emotional sharing | Casual increases |

---

## Style and Group Dynamics

Digital communication style affects how others perceive:

| Style | Perception Risk |
|-------|-----------------|
| Formal | Cold, disconnected, "not one of us" |
| Verbose | Overwhelming, ignored, "here they go again" |
| Terse | Uninterested, rude, impossible to read |
| Meme-Fluent | Unserious, young, not understanding gravity |
| Lurker | Not pulling weight, mysterious, not committed |
