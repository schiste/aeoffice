# Register Range

> **Category:** Language & Communication
>
> **Template Field:** `Register Range`
>
> **Dialog Impact:** Formality flexibility, social fluency

---

## Overview

Register is the level of formality in speech. Range determines how well someone adapts their speech to different contexts—from boardroom to bar, from strangers to family.

---

## Range Types

### Fixed Formal

**Definition:** Always professional, regardless of context. Can't relax into casual.

**Characteristics:**
- Same tone everywhere
- Uncomfortable with slang
- May seem stiff or pretentious
- Struggles in casual settings
- "Proper" as default

**Dialog Markers:**
```
[At a party]: "It's a pleasure to meet you. What is your profession?"
[With friends]: "I find that amusing."
[Under stress]: "This is quite distressing."
[Online]: Full sentences, proper punctuation always
```

**Why They're Like This:**
- Trained for formality (law, medicine, academia)
- Social anxiety masked by rules
- Non-native speaker default
- Class/upbringing expectations
- Autism spectrum / preference for predictability

---

### Fixed Casual

**Definition:** Always relaxed, regardless of context. Struggles to formalize.

**Characteristics:**
- Same friendly tone everywhere
- May seem unprofessional
- Doesn't register formality cues
- Comfortable but sometimes inappropriate
- "Just be yourself" taken literally

**Dialog Markers:**
```
[In meeting with executives]: "Yeah, totally, we should do that thing."
[Job interview]: "So like, what's the vibe here?"
[With authority]: "Hey, no worries, we got this."
[Online]: "lol same tbh"
```

**Why They're Like This:**
- Never in formal environments growing up
- Rebellion against formality
- Genuinely doesn't see difference
- Privilege (never faced consequences)
- Industry culture (tech, creative)

---

### Adaptive

**Definition:** Matches context naturally. Socially fluent across situations.

**Characteristics:**
- Shifts smoothly
- Reads social cues well
- Comfortable in many settings
- May be seen as chameleon
- "Code-switching" natural

**Dialog Markers:**
```
[In boardroom]: "Per our discussion, I recommend..."
[At bar]: "Dude, that's insane. Tell me more."
[With kids]: "Hey buddy! What's up?"
[Online]: Matches the platform and audience
```

**Why They're Like This:**
- Exposed to many environments
- Natural social intelligence
- Trained (service industry, diplomacy)
- Cross-class experience
- Professional requirement

---

### Code-Switcher

**Definition:** Deliberately different person in different contexts. Intentional, sometimes exhausting.

**Characteristics:**
- Very aware of switching
- Different "selves" in different spaces
- May feel inauthentic
- Strategic about presentation
- Cultural navigation

**Dialog Markers:**
```
[Professional]: "I appreciate the opportunity to discuss this matter."
[With cultural in-group]: Completely different vocabulary, accent, references
[Alone]: "God, I'm tired of performing."
[With mixed group]: Visible calculation about which mode
```

**Why They're Like This:**
- Minority in professional spaces
- Immigrant or cross-cultural background
- Class mobility
- Protecting aspects of identity
- Survival strategy

---

### Awkward

**Definition:** Misreads social cues. Wrong register at wrong time. Uncomfortable.

**Characteristics:**
- Doesn't know what's expected
- Often too formal or too casual
- Others find them confusing
- May give up and just be themselves
- Social anxiety visible

**Dialog Markers:**
```
[At funeral]: "So, what do you do?" [too casual]
[Casual hangout]: "I must express my gratitude for the invitation." [too formal]
[Professional setting]: Random over-sharing
[Online]: Inconsistent, unsure of tone
```

**Why They're Like This:**
- Not enough social exposure
- Neurodiverse (may miss cues)
- Anxiety disrupts calibration
- New to culture/context
- Genuine preference for authenticity over fitting in

## Register and Sleepless Context

Register range affects group dynamics:

| Register Type | Group Dynamic |
|---------------|---------------|
| Fixed Formal | May seem out of place in casual venting; could be taken too seriously |
| Fixed Casual | May not convey gravity of situation; might offend formal members |
| Adaptive | Bridges different members; may take on mediator role |
| Code-Switcher | Understands outsider experience; may feel performative in group |
| Awkward | May be underestimated or dismissed; could be most authentic |

---

## Register and Sleep Deprivation

Exhaustion affects register control:

| Sleep Level | Register Effect |
|-------------|-----------------|
| Functional | Normal range maintained |
| Chronic | Range narrows; harder to adapt |
| Crisis | Stuck in one mode; may over-share or under-communicate |
| Complete | Social niceties gone; pure function |
