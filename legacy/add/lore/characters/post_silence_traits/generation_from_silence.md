# Generation from Silence

> **Category:** Origin & Lineage

> **Template Field:** `Generation from Silence`

---

## Overview

In ~2334 CE (Year 300), characters are roughly 10-14 generations from the Silence of 2034. Their generational distance shapes their relationship to "Before"—whether it feels like history, mythology, or irrelevance.

---

## Generations

### 9th-10th Generation (Elders)

**Definition:** Oldest living people. Grandparents knew people who remembered "Before."

**Connection to Before:**
- Two degrees of separation
- Stories from people who were there
- Oral history is personal, not abstract
- May have touched pre-Silence objects knowingly
- Carries weight of transmission

**Perspective:**
- "Before" was real, with real people
- Details matter, accuracy matters
- Responsibility to pass on correctly
- May correct younger generations
- "I heard this from my grandmother, who heard it from hers..."

**Dialog Markers:**
```
"My grandmother remembered the stories from her mother's mother. She was there."
"We're the last who know what the words really meant."
"The young ones don't understand. They think it's all legend."
"I've spent my life keeping the truth alive."
"When I die, something dies with me. That's the burden."
```

**Story Function:**
- Living memory
- Truth-keepers
- May know dangerous secrets
- Death = lost knowledge

---

### 11th-12th Generation (Established)

**Definition:** Middle-aged and mature adults. "Before" is ancient history but feels real.

**Connection to Before:**
- Stories from grandparents
- "Before" is history, not memory
- Respects the past, focused on present
- May have fragments, not full picture
- Accepts oral tradition

**Perspective:**
- "Before" matters as foundation
- Details may be fuzzy, meaning is clear
- Balance of past and present
- May question elders' accuracy
- "The stories say, but who really knows?"

**Dialog Markers:**
```
"I know the stories. I'm not sure about all the details."
"What matters is what we do now, but we shouldn't forget."
"The elders say one thing, common sense says another."
"Before was real. Just... very long ago."
"We honor the past by living well today."
```

**Story Function:**
- Bridge generation
- Practical focus
- May be leaders, workers
- Questions traditions productively

---

### 13th+ Generation (Young)

**Definition:** Young adults and children. "Before" is mythology—the bubble is the entire world.

**Connection to Before:**
- Stories are just stories
- World has always been this way
- May find "Before" absurd or fascinating
- No emotional connection
- Crystal life is normal

**Perspective:**
- "Before" is like fairy tales
- May romanticize or dismiss
- Present is real, past is story
- May be curious or indifferent
- "Why does it matter?"

**Dialog Markers:**
```
"They really flew in metal birds? Sounds made up."
"The bubble is the world. What else is there?"
"Elders love their stories. I respect that. I guess."
"Before people ruined everything. Why would I want to be like them?"
"Maybe things were different. Maybe not. We're here now."
```

**Story Function:**
- Fresh perspective
- May discover things anew
- Represents future
- Could repeat mistakes or break cycles

## Knowledge Degradation

What gets lost over generations:

| Generation | Lost Knowledge |
|------------|---------------|
| 9th-10th | Specific names, dates start fading |
| 11th-12th | Technical details, motivations obscured |
| 13th+ | Context, scale, cause-and-effect |

**What Survives:**
- Core moral lessons (often simplified)
- Dramatic moments (often exaggerated)
- Practical survival knowledge
- Emotional truths

**What Gets Distorted:**
- Numbers (millions become "countless")
- Technology (becomes magic-like)
- Motivation (becomes simpler, more villainous or heroic)
- Geography (shrinks to known world)

## Generational Memory and Resilients

Resilient status affects generational perspective:
- Elders may remember when Resilients were rarer
- Established may have normalized Resilients
- Young may not see what the fuss is about
