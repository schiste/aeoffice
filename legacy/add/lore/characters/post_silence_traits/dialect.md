# Dialect

> **Category:** Language & Speech
>
> **Template Field:** `Primary Dialect` / `Secondary Dialect(s)`

> **Dialog Impact:** Word choice, accent markers, cultural signals

## Overview

After 300 years of isolated communities, distinct dialects have emerged. Dialect marks origin, class, profession, and community membership.

---

## Dialects

### Settlement Common

**Definition:** The baseline lingua franca understood everywhere. Default for trade and inter-settlement communication.

**Characteristics:**
- No distinctive markers
- Neutral, practical vocabulary
- Understood by all
- May seem "flat" to locals
- Default for outsiders

**Dialog Markers:**
```
[Unremarkable speech—no distinctive features]
"I speak so everyone understands."
"Plain words. Plain meaning."
```

**Who Speaks It:**
- Traders
- Travelers
- Diplomats
- Those who've lived in multiple settlements

---

### Cave Speak

**Definition:** Dialect of underground settlements. Echo-awareness built into speech.

**Characteristics:**
- Volume control (naturally quiet)
- Echo-aware phrasing
- Resonance terms
- Direction words (up-shaft, down-tunnel)
- Darkness vocabulary

**Vocabulary Examples:**
- "Bright" = loud (sound carries like light)
- "Deep" = distant (further underground)
- "Echo-true" = honest
- "Stone-sure" = certain, reliable

**Dialog Markers:**
```
"Keep your voice down-shaft. Sound carries."
"That's echo-true."
"I know it stone-sure."
"The deep places remember."
```

**Who Speaks It:**
- Cave settlement natives
- Underground workers
- Some mushroom farmers

---

### Coastal Tongue

**Definition:** Dialect of coastal settlements. Wave rhythms influence speech patterns.

**Characteristics:**
- Rhythm like waves
- Tide-based time references
- Water vocabulary bleeds everywhere
- Patience built into cadence
- "Current" and "flow" metaphors

**Vocabulary Examples:**
- "High tide" = busy time
- "Low tide" = quiet time
- "Against current" = difficult
- "Drift" = wander, be aimless

**Dialog Markers:**
```
"Come back at high tide."
"You're swimming against the current there."
"Let it drift for now."
"The tide brings what it brings."
```

**Who Speaks It:**
- Coastal settlement natives
- Fishers anywhere
- Traders who work coastal routes

---

### Choir Liturgical

**Definition:** Formal, archaic dialect used by those close to Crystals and sound ceremonies.

**Characteristics:**
- Formal, archaic phrases
- Hymn-like cadence
- Communal "we" over "I"
- Reverent vocabulary
- Ritual phrases preserved

**Vocabulary Examples:**
- "The Resonance provides"
- "In harmony we..."
- "Sound be with you"
- "The Silence takes"

**Dialog Markers:**
```
"Sound be with you, traveler."
"The Resonance provides what we need."
"We give voice to the Crystal's will."
"In harmony we serve."
```

**Who Speaks It:**
- Crystal Tenders
- Singers and Chanters
- Religious/ceremonial leaders
- Formal occasions

---

### Engineer Technical

**Definition:** Dialect of those who work with old technology and practical systems.

**Characteristics:**
- Precise terms
- Frustrated by vagueness
- Jargon-heavy
- Systematic vocabulary
- "Works" vs. "doesn't work" thinking

**Vocabulary Examples:**
- "Functional" = good
- "Broken" = bad (morally too)
- "System" = any organized thing
- "Mechanism" = cause

**Dialog Markers:**
```
"What's the mechanism?"
"That's non-functional."
"The system is working. Leave it."
"Specify what you mean."
```

**Who Speaks It:**
- Tinkerers
- Smiths
- Builders
- Anyone technically minded

---

### Trade Pidgin

**Definition:** Simplified, practical dialect for trade between settlements.

**Characteristics:**
- Simplified grammar
- Gesture-supported
- Direct, efficient
- Minimal vocabulary
- Understood widely

**Vocabulary Examples:**
- "Good-good" = agreement
- "No-work" = broken, useless
- "How-many?" = price
- "Far-walk" = distance

**Dialog Markers:**
```
"Good-good. We trade."
"This? How-many?"
"Far-walk, bad-road."
"No-work. Different one."
```

**Who Speaks It:**
- Traders (with outsiders)
- Anyone in cross-settlement communication
- Nomads

---

### Founder's French

**Definition:** Old French words and phrases that persist from the original Founder, Marc Delacroix.

**Characteristics:**
- Certain phrases in French
- Marks founder lineage
- Prestige dialect
- Often misused by non-speakers
- Historical connection

**Vocabulary Examples:**
- "C'est la vie" = acceptance of fate
- "Mon ami" = friend (affectionate)
- Terms for certain objects/concepts

**Dialog Markers:**
```
"C'est la vie. What can you do?"
"Come, mon ami."
"My grandmother knew the old words."
"The Founders spoke this way."
```

**Who Speaks It:**
- Founder lineage families
- Those claiming prestige
- Often partially, with errors

---

### Frontier Sparse

**Definition:** Minimal, efficient dialect of frontier settlements.

**Characteristics:**
- Few words
- Efficient
- Silence-comfortable (social silence, not Silence)
- Actions over words
- Direct to point of bluntness

**Vocabulary Examples:**
- Single-word answers preferred
- Grunt acknowledgment acceptable
- Long speeches distrusted

**Dialog Markers:**
```
"Yep."
"Nope."
"Handled."
"Coming?"
[Long comfortable silences between exchanges]
```

**Who Speaks It:**
- Frontier settlement natives
- Scouts
- Those who spend time alone
- Guards on remote duty

## Code-Switching

Many characters speak multiple dialects:

| Situation | Likely Dialect |
|-----------|---------------|
| With strangers | Settlement Common |
| With own community | Native dialect |
| With authority | More formal |
| In trade | Trade Pidgin |
| In ceremony | Choir Liturgical |
| Under stress | Most comfortable dialect |
