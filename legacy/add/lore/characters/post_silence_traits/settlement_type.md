# Settlement Type

> **Category:** Origin & Lineage
>
> **Template Field:** `Settlement Type`
>
> **Dialog Impact:** Comfort with spaces, sounds, strangers

---

## Overview

Where someone grew up shapes their relationship to space, sound, community, and danger. Settlement type creates lifelong instincts and expectations.

---

## Types

### Cave Dweller

**Definition:** Grew up underground—in natural cave systems expanded for habitation.

**Environment:**
- Enclosed, echoing spaces
- Constant awareness of acoustics
- Darkness normal, light precious
- Tight community, limited escape
- Stone and water sounds

**Psychological Markers:**
- Claustrophilia—comfort in enclosed spaces
- Distrusts open sky
- Sensitive to echo and resonance
- May feel exposed outside
- Thinks in tunnels and chambers

**Dialog Markers:**
```
"The sky is... unsettling. Too much nothing."
"In the caves, you always know where you are."
"Echo tells you everything. I can hear the shape of a room."
"I don't trust horizons. What's behind them?"
"We lived by drip-time. The water never lied."
```

**Skills & Instincts:**
- Navigates by sound
- Comfortable in darkness
- Knows cave systems intimately
- May be skilled climber
- Water-finding instincts

---

### Crystal Settlement

**Definition:** Raised within the protective bubble of a Crystal—the most "civilized" option.

**Environment:**
- Constant resonance awareness
- Regular community sounds
- Protected, structured
- Crystal maintenance is life
- Sound is safety

**Psychological Markers:**
- Understands resonance intuitively
- Sound is comfort, not just survival
- May take protection for granted
- Community-oriented
- Structured thinking

**Dialog Markers:**
```
"I've never known life without the Crystal's hum."
"Resonance isn't magic. It's home."
"When the Crystal song changes, everyone notices."
"We're protected. That's just... how it is."
"Silence isn't nothing. It's death."
```

**Skills & Instincts:**
- Crystal sensitivity
- Community coordination
- Structured routine
- May be less hardy in wilderness
- Understands maintenance cycles

---

### Coastal

**Definition:** Grew up on coastline settlements—waves, tides, and sea sounds as constants.

**Environment:**
- Wave rhythms structure life
- Tidal schedules
- Open horizons
- Fish and sea resources
- Salt and storms

**Psychological Markers:**
- Different rhythm sense
- Thinks in cycles and tides
- Horizon-comfortable
- Weather-aware
- May seem distant to inland folk

**Dialog Markers:**
```
"The tide tells time. Everything else is just waiting."
"Waves are never silent. That's how we live."
"Storm coming. Can't you smell it?"
"Inland people don't understand rhythm. The sea teaches it."
"Low tide, high tide. That's all you need to know."
```

**Skills & Instincts:**
- Weather prediction
- Swimming, boat handling
- Tidal schedule intuition
- Fish preparation
- Patience with cycles

---

### Industrial Ruin

**Definition:** Grew up in repurposed pre-Silence industrial sites—factories, power plants, etc.

**Environment:**
- Machine sounds, pipes, metal
- Practical, functional spaces
- Old technology around (broken)
- Salvage-rich environment
- Echoes in metal chambers

**Psychological Markers:**
- Mechanical thinking
- Comfortable with machines
- "How does it work?" instinct
- Practical problem-solving
- May seem unsentimental

**Dialog Markers:**
```
"Everything was built for something. Figure out what."
"Machines have logic. They're easier than people."
"The old ones built well. Even broken, it works."
"I grew up surrounded by things I couldn't use. Made me curious."
"Metal sounds different than stone. Cleaner."
```

**Skills & Instincts:**
- Mechanical understanding
- Salvage and repair
- Metal identification
- Systematic thinking
- Comfort with industrial aesthetics

---

### Nomadic

**Definition:** No fixed home—traveled between settlements or lived in moving groups.

**Environment:**
- Constantly changing
- Adaptable shelters
- Multiple sound environments
- Paths and routes
- Temporary everything

**Psychological Markers:**
- Reads terrain quickly
- Trusts few, befriends many
- No attachment to place
- Adaptable survival
- Always ready to move

**Dialog Markers:**
```
"Home is where you stop. Then you move on."
"I know roads you've never heard of."
"Staying too long makes you soft. Or dead."
"Every settlement has rules. Learn them fast, leave faster."
"I don't get attached to places. People neither, mostly."
```

**Skills & Instincts:**
- Navigation
- Quick social reading
- Portable skills valued
- Scouting and pathfinding
- Danger assessment

---

### Frontier

**Definition:** Edge of known territory—outposts, watchtowers, new settlements.

**Environment:**
- Proximity to danger
- Self-reliance required
- Limited community
- Resource scarcity
- First line of defense

**Psychological Markers:**
- Self-reliance is survival
- Suspicious of strangers
- Resourceful improvisation
- Comfort with isolation
- Always alert

**Dialog Markers:**
```
"Out there, you handle things yourself or you don't handle them."
"Trust is earned slow. Suspicion is free."
"We see what comes before anyone else."
"Resources are thin. You learn to make do."
"Frontier folk don't complain. We solve."
```

**Skills & Instincts:**
- Improvised survival
- Danger awareness
- Self-sufficiency
- Fortification thinking
- Watchfulness

## Settlement Type and Sound Relationship

| Type | Sound Relationship |
|------|-------------------|
| Cave Dweller | Echo-aware, resonance intuitive |
| Crystal Settlement | Sound = safety, structured |
| Coastal | Rhythm-oriented, wave-timing |
| Industrial Ruin | Mechanical sounds, metallic echoes |
| Nomadic | Adapts to local soundscape |
| Frontier | Alert to warning sounds |
