# Lineage

> **Category:** Origin & Lineage
>
> **Template Field:** `Lineage`
>
> **Dialog Impact:** Pride, expectations, inherited knowledge

---

## Overview

In the post-Silence world (~2334 CE), lineage determines social status, inherited knowledge, and a character's relationship to "Before." After 300 years, bloodlines carry weight, stories, and baggage.

---

## Lineage Types

### Founding Line

**Definition:** Descended from the original Unplugged survivors who first established the settlements.

**Social Position:**
- Highest traditional status
- Custodians of origin stories
- Expected to lead, guide, know
- Carries weight of ancestors' choices

**Knowledge Inherited:**
- Why the Unplugged left
- Fragments of pre-Silence life
- Original settlement protocols
- Old secrets, old guilt

**Dialog Markers:**
```
"My family was there at the beginning."
"We've always known the truth about the Founding."
"It's not just history for us. It's family."
"My great-great-great-grandmother walked out of that cave."
"With the name comes... responsibility. And burden."
```

**Character Tensions:**
- Pride vs. humility
- Inherited expectations vs. individual identity
- Knowing family secrets vs. keeping them
- "We left them to die" guilt transmission

---

### First Generation

**Definition:** Ancestors joined the caves/settlements in the early years after the Founding. "Almost founders."

**Social Position:**
- High status, second-tier
- Proved worth by early contribution
- May have chips on shoulder about not being "true founders"
- Established families, not originals

**Knowledge Inherited:**
- Stories of early survival
- Adaptation challenges
- May have pieces founders don't
- "We were there when it mattered"

**Dialog Markers:**
```
"We weren't first, but we were early."
"Our people helped build this place."
"First Gen earned our place."
"Some Founding Lines forget—we saved them too."
"Almost doesn't count, they say. We say otherwise."
```

**Character Tensions:**
- Second-best resentment
- Proving worth still
- May be more capable, less honored
- Complicated relationship with Founding Lines

---

### Refugee Blood

**Definition:** Ancestors arrived seeking shelter—perhaps from other failed settlements or the wilderness. "Newcomers" even generations later.

**Social Position:**
- Outsider status persists
- Always proving worth
- May form sub-communities
- Gratitude expected, resentment possible

**Knowledge Inherited:**
- Stories from elsewhere
- Different survival techniques
- May know things locals don't
- Outside perspective

**Dialog Markers:**
```
"My family was taken in. We never forget that debt."
[Or] "My family was taken in. We've been repaying ever since."
"We came from [other settlement]. Or what was left of it."
"People still look at us different. After all these generations."
"We know what it's like out there. They don't."
```

**Character Tensions:**
- Gratitude vs. resentment
- Permanent outsider
- Different traditions vs. assimilation
- May know about other settlements

---

### Bubble-Born

**Definition:** Family has always lived within Crystal protection. No memory or story of any other existence.

**Social Position:**
- Neutral—neither privileged nor stigmatized
- May be seen as naive about danger
- The "normal" people
- No dramatic heritage to claim

**Knowledge Inherited:**
- Crystal living is all they know
- May not understand bubble-less life
- Settlement traditions, not survival
- Comfortable, possibly complacent

**Dialog Markers:**
```
"This is all we've known."
"The bubble is home. It's just... home."
"I can't imagine living without the Crystal."
"Some say we're soft. Maybe they're right."
"What's out there doesn't seem real to us."
```

**Character Tensions:**
- Naive or grounded?
- May underestimate danger
- Could be strongest in normal times
- May be first to panic in crisis

---

### Nomad Strain

**Definition:** Family traveled between settlements. Mixed traditions, adaptable, no single home.

**Social Position:**
- Respected for knowledge, distrusted for loyalty
- Know more settlements than most
- Never fully belong anywhere
- Valuable for connections

**Knowledge Inherited:**
- Multiple settlement traditions
- Trade routes and contacts
- Comparative knowledge
- Stories from everywhere

**Dialog Markers:**
```
"We've lived in half a dozen settlements."
"Nowhere is really home. Everywhere is."
"I know how they do things in [other place]. Different."
"People trust us with messages. Not with staying."
"My grandmother always said: roots are traps."
```

**Character Tensions:**
- Belonging vs. freedom
- Knowledge vs. depth
- Trusted for info, not for loyalty
- May be scouts, traders, messengers

---

### Outcast Redeemed

**Definition:** Ancestor was exiled, family worked back into community over generations.

**Social Position:**
- Complicated—redemption story
- Original crime may be remembered
- May overcompensate to belong
- Some suspicion persists

**Knowledge Inherited:**
- Why ancestor was exiled (or the official story)
- Life outside—briefly
- What it costs to be cast out
- Determination to never go back

**Dialog Markers:**
```
"My great-grandfather was exiled for [crime]. We've been making up for it since."
"Our family name used to be spit. Now it's not. Mostly."
"Some people still remember. We live with that."
"I will never give them reason to send us out again."
"Redemption isn't something you finish. You just keep doing it."
```

**Character Tensions:**
- Original sin haunts
- Overcompensation
- Fear of exile
- May be most loyal—or most resentful

---

### Unknown

**Definition:** Orphan, foundling, or deliberately obscured history.

**Social Position:**
- No inherited status—blank slate
- May be pitied or suspected
- Creates own identity
- Mystery can be advantage or liability

**Knowledge Inherited:**
- Nothing—or fragments
- May search for origins
- May have secrets planted
- Free from lineage burden

**Dialog Markers:**
```
"I don't know who my parents were."
"Some people think that makes me no one. I think it makes me anyone."
"I'm searching. Someday I'll know."
"My family is who I choose."
"The past doesn't define me. I define me."
```

**Character Tensions:**
- Identity quest
- Freedom vs. rootlessness
- May have dramatic origin reveal
- Suspicious to those who value lineage

## Lineage and Sound

Each lineage has a different relationship to the Silence and sound:

| Lineage | Sound Relationship |
|---------|-------------------|
| Founding Line | "Our ancestors knew why sound matters" |
| First Generation | "We helped build the traditions" |
| Refugee Blood | "We've heard the Silence. You haven't." |
| Bubble-Born | "Sound is what we've always had" |
| Nomad Strain | "Sound varies from place to place" |
| Outcast Redeemed | "We know what silence means—exile" |
| Unknown | "I don't have stories. I have now." |
