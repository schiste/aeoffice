# First Session Balance

This document defines the current tuning target for AD&D's first session.

It exists so early pacing is treated as a product contract, not a moving intuition.

This is not the full economy specification.
It is the practical target for the first 10-15 minutes of play.

## Purpose

The first session should teach the player:

- how to wake the base up
- what the three bands mean
- why staffing matters
- why spending is risky
- why the Fire Pit matters
- why recruitment matters

The first session should not feel:

- empty
- over-explained
- deadlocked
- or mathematically opaque

## Target Arc

The desired early arc is:

1. Investigate Base
2. Explore Base
3. start the first Crystal cleanup/upgrade loop
4. gather enough Stone to restore the Studio
5. bring Chorus online
6. gather enough Stone to build the Fire Pit
7. start the Vibes loop
8. open the recruitment gate
9. recruit the first survivor

## Timing Targets

These targets are intentionally approximate.
They are there to keep the first session coherent.

### Tutorial Actions

- `Investigate Base`: immediate availability
- `Explore Base`: immediately after Investigate completes

### Studio

- `Restore Studio` should become affordable quickly after Explore
- the player should not need to sit idle wondering how to get started
- Stone should feel like a short operational gate, not a wall

### Fire Pit

- `Build Fire Pit` should follow naturally after Studio, not long after it
- it should feel like the next obvious move once the player understands Vibes

### Recruitment

- the first recruit should become affordable inside the first real session window
- the player should have time to understand the Vibes loop before the recruit arrives
- the recruit should not feel instant, but it should not feel remote either

## Pressure Targets

### Bassline

- the player should understand that spending can threaten coverage
- the player should not suffer immediate confusing bubble collapse during the onboarding path

### Chorus

- Chorus pressure should matter shortly after the Studio comes online
- it should create understandable brownout risk
- it should not instantly punish the player for unlocking power

### Harmonics

- Harmonics must be worth staffing soon after it becomes available
- it must not be a mathematically dead assignment

### Housing / Vibes

- overcrowding pressure should be understandable
- bad Vibes should be visible before it becomes severe
- the player should see that recruitment and housing are linked

## Validation Standard

First-session tuning is acceptable when:

- the onboarding sequence has no deadlocks
- early resource bottlenecks are visible
- the first recruit reliably happens in a sensible first-session window
- Harmonics feels worth assigning after unlock
- Chorus pressure is real but not arbitrary
- the player can read what went wrong when pressure rises
