## YEAR 26 AS (October 2059 - October 2060)

### The Year of Expansion

**Global Population:** ~215,000 (underground-only, per LORE_REVISION_v2)

**Theme:** Underground communities expand their tunnel networks. Connection between settlements begins.

---

### Major Events

**The Safe Radius Theory:**

Engineer communities begin calculating maximum distance from noise sources before Hush exposure becomes dangerous. The answer varies by noise type:
- Ocean waves: 2-3km inland
- Factory machinery: 500m-1km
- Drum circles: 200-400m
- Underground echo: variable (depends on geology)

This becomes the foundation for territorial planning.

**The First "Border" Dispute:**

Two communities in former Bavaria discover they've been expanding toward the same valley. Neither wants to share. Neither wants to fight (drums would stop).

Resolution: They agree to share the valley in alternating weeks. This creates the first formal "treaty" of the post-Silence era.

"Bavarian rotation" enters common usage: sharing resources through scheduling.

**The Unplugged Year 26:**

Chloé Delacroix, now 28, formally marries Bernard Rousseau (no relation to Dr. Émile Rousseau of Lyon). They met through the courier network — he delivered mushroom trade contracts.

Marc gives his blessing but looks increasingly frail. He's 61 now, and the years underground are showing.

**Marc Delacroix's Journal (Year 26):**

> "Chloé is married. She's radiant. Bernard is a good man — steady, practical, makes her laugh.
>
> They want children. The thought terrifies me. Not because I don't want grandchildren. Because those children will be three generations removed from the world I helped destroy.
>
> They'll grow up thinking drums and caves are normal. They'll hear stories about 'before' — cars and cities and music that didn't keep you alive — and they'll think we're talking about mythology.
>
> Maybe that's better. Maybe they should never know what we had. What I took from them.
>
> Claire used to say I thought too much. She was right. I still think too much. And I still can't tell anyone why."

## YEARS 27-30 AS (2060-2064): The Last Human Era Peak

**Population:** ~215,000 → ~225,000

**Theme:** The final isolated survivors are discovered. The "Last Human" phenomenon reaches its absurd peak.

---

### Year 27 AS: The Twins Discovery

François and Francine Moreau — deaf since birth — are discovered living 4.7km apart, each unaware of the other's existence for 27 years.

Their father, a paranoid engineer who died in Year 3, had built them separate soundproofed bunkers with 30-year supply caches. The bunkers were identical. The twins were identical. Neither knew about the other.

François's bunker had a note: "You are my only child. Your mother and I loved you. Stay safe. — Papa"

Francine's bunker had an identical note.

When couriers finally introduced them, the exchange was conducted entirely in sign language:

- François: *[You're deaf too?]*
- Francine: *[Obviously. You?]*
- François: *[27 years.]*
- Francine: *[Same.]*
- François: *[Did Papa—]*
- Francine: *[Build you a bunker? Yes.]*
- François: *[Did your note—]*
- Francine: *[Say 'only child'? Yes.]*

They sat in silence (safe silence — they were in François's bunker with mechanical generators running) for 20 minutes.

Then François signed: *[I'm older. I was born at 11:47 PM.]*

Francine replied: *[11:51 PM. Twins, you idiot.]*

They've been inseparable ever since.

### Year 28 AS: The Bernard Watcher Problem

In the ruins of Avignon, a survivor known as "Bernard" has been living in a bell tower for 28 years. He has excellent visibility across the Rhône valley. He can see smoke signals, movement, even hear distant drums on clear days.

Problem: He never investigates. He just... watches.

Meanwhile, someone else is watching Bernard. An observer in a separate tower 800 meters away, who has been documenting Bernard's activities for 17 years.

This observer is known as "The Bernard Watcher."

The Bernard Watcher has been recording Bernard's daily routine:
- 6:00 AM: Bell ringing (keeping Hush at bay)
- 7:00 AM: Scavenging expedition
- 11:00 AM: Return, lunch
- 1:00 PM: Bell ringing
- 2:00 PM: Observation of valley (never investigating)
- 6:00 PM: Bell ringing
- 7:00 PM: Dinner, sleep

The Bernard Watcher's journal concludes: *"Bernard could walk to at least three survivor communities visible from his tower. He will not. I could walk to Bernard. I will not. We have reached equilibrium."*

A third party — a courier — discovers both of them in Year 28.

The courier's report: *"I found two men living 800 meters apart. One watches the valley. One watches the man watching the valley. Neither speaks to anyone. Neither investigates anything. Both claim to be 'The Last Human (Avignon Region).'"*

When asked why they never contacted each other:
- Bernard: "He could have visited me."
- The Bernard Watcher: "He could have visited anyone."

Both are technically correct.

### Year 29 AS: The Three Brothers Incident

Three brothers — Matthias, Laurent, and Claude Fontaine — survived the Silence separately. Each thought the others were dead. Each has been living within 12km of the others for 29 years.

Discovery timeline:
- Year 29, Day 47: Matthias finds Laurent's camp
- Year 29, Day 48: Matthias and Laurent agree they're "probably the last Fontaines"
- Year 29, Day 51: They find Claude's camp
- Year 29, Day 52: Claude sees them approaching, assumes they're raiders, flees
- Year 29, Day 53-89: Chase ensues
- Year 29, Day 90: Claude is cornered in a cave system
- Year 29, Day 90: Matthias shouts "CLAUDE IT'S YOUR BROTHERS"
- Year 29, Day 90: Claude shouts back "PROVE IT"
- Year 29, Day 90: Laurent yells "YOU STILL OWE ME 30 EUROS FROM 2033"
- Year 29, Day 90: Claude: "...Matthias?"
- Year 29, Day 90: Reunion

Their mother had taught them a family whistle. All three had been using it for 29 years as a "check if anyone survived" signal.

None of them ever heard the others because they were each whistling during their scavenging hours — different times of day.

The whistle worked. The timing didn't.

### Year 33 AS: The Hermit-Musician Gap Closes

Céline the Musician and The Hermit of Lyon have lived 3km apart for 8 years. Both make noise constantly — Céline plays flute, the Hermit drums.

Each can hear the other's music. Neither investigates because:
- Céline: "I assumed it was an echo effect."
- Hermit: "I assumed it was auditory hallucination."

A courier finally introduces them. They become immediate friends.

The Hermit's first words: "You're real?"
Céline's response: "You're disappointed, aren't you."
The Hermit: "Relieved, actually. I was starting to think I'd invented you."

They collaborate on musical compositions for 6 months. Then the Hermit dies of old age in Year 34.

Céline writes 47 songs about him over the next 20 years. Every performance begins: "This is for the man who took 8 years to walk 3 kilometers. I miss him every day."

"The Musician's Gap" enters common usage: being separated from someone by a distance easily crossed but never traversed.

---

### Year 37 AS: Bernard Finally Moves

After 37 years in his bell tower, Bernard of Avignon descends. Not because he's found anyone. Because his tower is collapsing.

He walks 800 meters to The Bernard Watcher's tower.

The exchange:
- Bernard: "My tower is falling down."
- The Bernard Watcher: "I noticed. Seventeen days ago."
- Bernard: "You could have mentioned it."
- The Bernard Watcher: "I wrote it in my journal."
- Bernard: "I can't read your journal from 800 meters away."
- The Bernard Watcher: "That's fair."

They live together for 2 years. The Bernard Watcher continues documenting Bernard's activities, now from close range.

Bernard's routine doesn't change. The Bernard Watcher's conclusion: *"Proximity changes nothing. Bernard remains Bernard. This is somehow comforting."*

### Year 39 AS: The Corsican Dies

Simone of Corsica — the longest-surviving isolated immune human — dies of natural causes at approximately age 78.

She is found 6 months later by a fishing expedition. Her shelter contains:
- 39 years of journals (all entries: "Still here. Still alone. Still fine.")
- A garden (thriving)
- A note addressed "To whoever finds this":

> "I am Simone Leclerc. I was a schoolteacher in Ajaccio. When the Silence fell, I was on a hiking trip alone. I survived.
>
> I have seen smoke signals. I have heard distant sounds. I never investigated because I assumed I was hallucinating.

> If you're reading this, I was wrong. There were others.
>
> I hope you're doing better than I did. I hope you found each other sooner.
>
> I was never the last human. But I was the last one here.
>
> That's close enough.
>
> — Simone, Year 39 (probably)"

The journals are preserved in The Archive. Her garden is maintained by the fishing community as a memorial.

### Year 41 AS: THE CRYSTAL DISCOVERY

**Location:** Mponeng Mine, South Africa (formerly world's deepest gold mine, 3.16km underground)

**Date:** Approximately March, Year 41 AS (2075 CE)

**Discoverer:** Thabo Ndlovu, mining engineer (survivor since Day 1)

---

### The Discovery

The Mponeng survivor community had been living underground since Year 1. At 3+ kilometers depth, The Filter's effects were completely blocked. They didn't need drums — the earth itself insulated them.

But they wanted to expand upward. Closer to the surface meant easier scavenging, better air circulation, potential for surface expeditions.

Problem: The higher they went, the more danger from Hush exposure during quiet moments.

Thabo Ndlovu was surveying a tunnel at 2.2km depth when he noticed something strange.

The air felt different. Not temperature — vibration. A subtle resonance, barely perceptible.

He called his team. "Do you feel that?"

They did.

**The Investigation:**

Following the sensation led them to a quartz formation in the tunnel wall. Unremarkable at first glance — miners had passed it thousands of times before The Silence.

But when someone accidentally struck it with a pick, it rang. Not like normal quartz. This kept resonating for 47 minutes.

And during those 47 minutes, the "safe feeling" expanded measurably.

**The Test:**

Thabo proposed an experiment: Set up continuous sound near the crystal. Measure the safe radius.

Results:
- Without crystal: drums kept Hush at bay within ~150m
- With crystal + drums: safe radius expanded to ~800m
- Effect increased proportionally to sound input

The crystal was amplifying and propagating sound somehow. Creating a bubble where The Filter's effects were weakened.

**Thabo's Journal (Year 41, Day 89):**

> "We don't understand what this is. The crystal shouldn't do this. No mineral formation should be able to weaken The Filter. But it does.
>
> We've been testing for six weeks. The effect is consistent. Reproducible. Real.
>
> If this exists here, it might exist elsewhere. If we can find more of these crystals, we could expand our safe zones dramatically.
>
> This could change everything.
>
> We're calling it 'The Resonance Anomaly.' But everyone just calls it 'the Crystal.'"

---

### The Implications

Word spreads slowly through courier networks. By Year 42, survivor communities across southern Africa know about Mponeng's discovery.

Initial reactions:
- **Engineers:** "Impossible. We need to study this."
- **Desperate communities:** "Where can we find more?"
- **Religious groups:** "This is divine providence."
- **The cynical:** "Great. Now we have something else to fight over."

All of them were right.

---

### Year 42 AS: Bernard and The Bernard Watcher Die

Both men die within days of each other in their shared tower in Avignon.

The Bernard Watcher's final journal entry:

> "Bernard died yesterday. Heart failure, I think. He was old. We're all old now.
>
> I've been watching him for 31 years. Writing about him. Documenting him. I never asked why he didn't investigate the valley. I just recorded that he didn't.
>
> Now I know I'll never know.
>
> I could walk down to the valley myself. There are people there. I can see their fires.
>
> I won't. Bernard was right — whatever's down there can exist without me. And I can exist without it.
>
> We weren't the last humans. But we were the last ones who chose isolation.
>
> That's close enough.
>
> — The Bernard Watcher, Year 42, Day 112"

He dies 3 days later. A courier finds them both the following week.

The journal is preserved. The tower is now a memorial called "The Two Bernards." Guides explain the story to visitors who always ask the same question: "Why didn't they just walk down to the valley?"

The answer is carved on a plaque: *"They could have. They didn't want to. The apocalypse doesn't erase personality. It clarifies it."*

## YEARS 43-50 AS (2077-2084): The Crystal Rush Begins

**Population:** ~850 million (stable)

**Theme:** The race to find more Crystals transforms post-Silence society.

---

### Year 43 AS: The Second Crystal

A community in the Ural Mountains reports finding a similar formation. Tests confirm: it has the same resonance properties as the Mponeng Crystal.

Pattern discovered: Both crystals are at specific depths, in quartz-rich geology, at locations with unusual acoustic properties.

The Engineers begin theorizing: Crystals might form at standing wave nodes in The Filter's interference pattern. The Filter itself may have created them as an emergent property.

"The Filter's greatest side effect becomes humanity's only salvation" — First Engineer Guild paper on Crystals

---

### Year 44 AS: The First Crystal Expedition Disaster

A 30-person expedition sets out from former Germany to search for Crystals in the Alps.

Only 3 return. The rest died attempting to cross quiet valleys.

Lesson learned: Crystal hunting is deadly. The promise of salvation doesn't protect from Hush.

"Crystal fever" enters common usage: risking death for potential salvation.

---

### Year 45 AS: Five More Crystals Found

Success rate improves as expeditions learn from failures. By Year 45, seven confirmed Crystals exist worldwide.

Communities form around each discovery. Population migrates toward Crystal sites — larger safe radius = better survival = more prosperity.

The post-Silence economic hierarchy begins:
1. Crystal settlements (safest, richest)
2. Coastal/industrial settlements (reliable noise)
3. Cave/metro settlements (underground safety)
4. Nomadic drum tribes (most dangerous)

---

### Year 47 AS: The Crystal Wars Begin

Two communities claim the same newly-discovered Crystal in former Poland. Negotiations fail.

The First Crystal War lasts 3 months. Deaths: ~400 combatants, ~1,200 civilians (supply lines cut, drums stopped, Hush claimed them).

Result: Both communities are devastated. A third community moves in and claims the Crystal while the first two are burying their dead.

Lesson: Crystal conflicts have no winners. Only survivors.

---

### Year 48 AS: Marc Delacroix's Decline

Marc is now 73. His journal entries become infrequent, repetitive. He's developing what The Unplugged healers call "cave fog" — memory loss, confusion, withdrawn behavior.

Chloé, now 40, has effectively led the community for years. Marc is "Elder Emeritus" — honored but not in power.

He spends his days in the mushroom chambers, helping cultivate. The repetitive work seems to comfort him.

**Marc's Journal (Year 48, Day 203):**

> "I forgot Chloé's name today. Just for a moment. She was standing there, speaking to me, and I couldn't remember.
>
> Then it came back. Chloé. My daughter. Of course.
>
> Claire would know what to say. Claire always knew. But Claire is gone.
>
> The drums keep beating. I've heard them for 48 years. Constant. Unchanging. The sound of survival.
>
> Sometimes I think: If I had known the drums would save them, would I have still done it? Caused all this for a chance at quiet?
>
> I don't know. I can't remember who I was anymore. I can barely remember who I am.
>
> The mushrooms are growing well this season."

### Year 50 AS: The Fifty-Year Mark

Half a century since The Silence.

**Global Population:** ~850 million (stable)
**Known Crystals:** 14 worldwide
**Crystal settlements:** ~25 (some shared, some contested)
**Deaths from Crystal conflicts:** ~8,000 (so far)

**The Unplugged Status:**
- Population: ~4,500
- Cave systems: 5 (interconnected network)
- External contacts: 200+ communities
- Trade network: Major hub for mushroom cultivation expertise
- Marc Delacroix: increasingly confused, still tending mushrooms

**Chloé Delacroix-Rousseau's Half-Century Speech:**

> "Fifty years ago, the world ended. My father was 25. I was 2.
>
> He tells me stories — when he can remember. About cities with lights that never dimmed. Machines that moved faster than horses. Music you didn't have to make yourself.
>
> Fifty years ago, all that ended. Not slowly. Instantly. In a single morning, everything humanity had built became useless.
>
> But we didn't end. We're still here.
>
> My father saved us. Him and the other founders. They prepared when no one else did. They brought us underground. They packed the drums.
>
> I asked him once: 'How did you know?' He said: 'I didn't. I just... hoped I was wrong and prepared like I was right.'
>
> That's all survival is. Hoping you're wrong. Preparing like you're right. Making it to tomorrow.
>
> The drums keep beating. Year 51 begins. We continue."

What Chloé doesn't know: Her father didn't hope he was wrong. He knew exactly what was coming. Because he helped create it.

Marc, standing at the back of the gathering, hears the speech. His eyes focus briefly. He whispers to himself: "I'm sorry. I'm so sorry."

No one hears him. The drums are too loud.

## YEAR 51 AS (October 2085 - October 2086)

### The Year of Archives

**Theme:** The formalization of knowledge preservation. The Archive emerges.

---

### The Archive Founded

**Location:** A hardened bunker in former Switzerland, originally built to house Telegram's backup servers

**Founding members:** 23 former librarians, museum curators, data archivists, and "people who couldn't stop collecting things"

**Key discovery:** The complete Telegram chat logs, including "Sleepless in Decibels" — both public and private channels

---

### The Founding Meeting

The group had been informally collaborating for years, trading documents, preserving artifacts, copying journals. In Year 51, they formalized.

**First debate:** Should they preserve everything, or curate?

Vote: 19 for "everything," 4 for "curate"

The majority argued: "We don't know what future generations will need. Let them decide what matters."

**Second debate:** Should they share freely, or control access?

This debate lasted three weeks.

Result: "Knowledge is the only currency that appreciates. Free information has no value. We preserve everything. We share nothing freely. Information for trade only."

**Third debate:** What about the Sleepless in Decibels logs?

The room went quiet.

They'd all read them. They all knew what they meant. The conspiracy. The Sounding Five. The Unplugged connection. The truth about why the world ended.

**The Keeper's Decision:**

The first Keeper — a former chief librarian named Adelina Corvus — made the ruling:

> "The truth would cause wars. Between The Unplugged descendants and everyone else. Between those who knew and those who didn't. Between those who have and those who don't.
>
> The Unplugged saved themselves through preparation. Their descendants believe it was foresight. That belief is what holds their communities together.
>
> If we reveal that their ancestors caused the apocalypse they prepared for? Their societies collapse. Their descendants are exiled or killed. The knowledge that saved thousands becomes the sword that kills them.
>
> We keep the logs. We study them. We never share the truth freely.
>
> When the world is ready — if it's ever ready — we'll decide then.
>
> Until then: we are Archivists. We preserve. We don't judge. We don't interfere.
>
> This is the first rule of The Archive."

**Vote:** Unanimous approval

The secret is kept.

---

### What The Archive Preserves

Within the first year, The Archive catalogs:
- 2.3 million pages of pre-Silence documents
- 400+ personal journals from Years 1-50
- The complete Telegram logs (12GB of chat history)
- Maps, photographs, technical manuals, recipes, music sheets
- Artifacts: pre-Silence technology, clothing, media, tools

The rule: Everything is valuable. Everything is preserved. Nothing is discarded.

**The Archive motto:** "Memory fades. Paper doesn't. Neither do our prices."

---

### Year 51 Impact

By the end of Year 51, The Archive has:
- Established trade agreements with 40 communities
- Set prices for information (paid in resources, labor, or other information)
- Begun training apprentice Archivists
- Created the first comprehensive post-Silence library catalog

They are not popular. Knowledge-hoarding in a time of rebuilding offends many.

But they're effective. And in a world where information is scarce, effectiveness matters more than popularity.

## YEAR 53 AS (October 2087 - October 2088)

### The Year of Endings

**Theme:** The original survivors are dying. The first generation passes.

---

### Marc Delacroix Dies (Year 53 AS)

**Date:** February 8, Year 53 AS (2088 CE)
**Age:** 82 years old
**Location:** The Ardèche Caves, mushroom cultivation chamber

---

### The Final Day

Marc woke at dawn. He'd been doing that for 53 years — the habit never left him.

He went to the mushroom chambers. He'd been tending them daily for 5 years, ever since his memory started failing. The work was simple, repetitive, soothing.

Chloé found him there at noon, sitting against the wall, eyes closed.

She thought he was sleeping. Then she realized he wasn't breathing.

---

### The Memorial

Three thousand people attended. The entire Unplugged community, plus representatives from 40 allied settlements.

**Chloé's Eulogy:**

> "My father was a hero. That's what everyone says. He prepared for disaster. He built these caves. He saved us.
>
> But he was also just a man. A tired man who wanted his daughter to sleep through the night. A grieving man who lost his wife too soon. A confused man who forgot his own daughter's name.
>
> He carried something heavy his entire life. I never knew what. He never said. He just... carried it.
>
> Near the end, when his mind was mostly gone, he'd say things. 'I'm sorry.' 'I didn't know.' 'I just wanted quiet.'
>
> I don't know what he was apologizing for. Maybe nothing. Maybe everything.
>
> But I know this: He saved us. Whatever he was sorry for, he spent 53 years making sure we survived to hear his apology.
>
> That's enough."

Marc was buried in the deepest chamber, where the drums echo loudest. His headstone reads:

**MARC DELACROIX**
**2005-2088**
**FOUNDER • ELDER • FATHER**
**HE KEPT THE RHYTHM**

What it doesn't say: *He also broke the world. He never told anyone. He died with that secret.*

---

### The Encrypted Hard Drive

In Marc's personal effects, Chloé finds:
- His journals (fragmentary, confused in later years)
- Claire's wedding ring
- A child's drawing (Chloé's, age 5)
- An encrypted hard drive labeled "For Chloé — Only If Necessary"

She tries to access it. The encryption is pre-Silence military grade. Unbreakable with current technology.

She stores it in The Unplugged archives. "Only if necessary" means she'll decide when that time comes.

It never does. Not in her lifetime.

The drive is passed down through the Delacroix line for 247 years. No one ever cracks it.

Until Year 300, when a curious Engineer does.

---

### Year 53 Summary

Marc Delacroix dies taking his secret to the grave. The Unplugged descendants continue believing their ancestors were simply prepared. The Archive knows the truth but keeps it locked away.

The conspiracy remains buried.

For now.

## YEARS 54-70 AS (2088-2104): The Faction Era Begins

**Population:** 850M → 800M (slow decline from conflicts)

**Theme:** Ideological groups form around different interpretations of survival and Crystals.

---

### Year 56 AS: The Choir Emerges (Early Form)

Religious communities that interpreted The Silence as divine punishment begin coalescing. They're not yet organized, but the pattern is clear:
- Regular prayer schedules (which also maintain constant sound)
- Crystal tending as sacred duty
- Belief that Crystals are "gifts from God"

The first "Singing Settlement" is established in former Slovenia. Population: ~300.

They call themselves "The Voices." Later, others call them "The Choir."

---

### Year 59 AS: The Seventeen Crystal Rush

In a single year, 17 new Crystals are discovered. The Crystal Rush is now in full swing.

Expeditions set out weekly. Mortality rate: ~40%.

"Going crystal hunting" becomes euphemism for suicide missions.

But the rewards justify the risk. A settlement with a Crystal can support 10x the population of a settlement without one.

---

### Year 63 AS: The Engineer Guilds Form (Proto-Engineers)

Technical communities begin sharing knowledge systematically. They form "guilds" around specializations:
- Acoustic Engineers (study Crystals and The Filter)
- Structural Engineers (build around Crystals)
- Agricultural Engineers (optimize mushroom farms and surface gardens)

They're not yet a unified faction, but the foundation is laid.

---

### Year 65 AS: The Toulouse Census

Dr. Rousseau's descendant (Henri Rousseau III) completes a comprehensive census of former France:
- 23,000 survivors in 147 communities
- 7 known Crystals (3 controlled, 4 contested)
- Population growth rate: 1.2% annually

Extrapolating globally: ~800 million humans remain.

The die-off has stopped. Population is stable, possibly growing.

---

### Year 67 AS: The Silent Founded

**Founder:** Erasmus Vane, former philosophy professor

**Founding myth:** Vane survived 6 hours in total silence during a cave collapse. When rescuers found him, he claimed to have "transcended" and gained new understanding.

Whether this was true, delusion, or con artistry remains debated.

**Key belief:** The Hush is not a disease but an evolution. Humanity must adapt to silence, not fight it. Crystals are "crutches" preventing necessary adaptation.

**Practices:**
- Deliberate exposure therapy (highly dangerous)
- Crystal destruction when possible (extremely controversial)
- Recruiting the desperate and disillusioned

**First settlement:** Hidden location, ~40 members

By Year 70: ~200 members across multiple secret sites

The Silent are feared and hated. They're also growing.

---

### Year 70 AS: The Seventy-Year Mark

**Global Population:** ~800 million (declining slightly from conflicts)
**Known Crystals:** 41 worldwide
**Crystal settlements:** ~60
**Deaths from Crystal conflicts:** ~45,000 (cumulative)

The post-Silence world has stabilized into rough patterns:
- Crystal settlements are wealthy and powerful
- The Archive controls most pre-Silence knowledge
- Religious communities are organizing
- Technical communities are collaborating
- The Silent are recruiting

The faction era has begun.

---

## YEAR 71 AS (October 2105 - October 2106)

### The Choir Formally Organizes

The various "Singing Settlements" meet at a great gathering in former Slovenia. 800 people attend.

**The First Conductor:** Sister Magdalene Voix (distant relation to Philippe the Broadcaster)

**Founding principles:**
1. Crystals are divine gifts
2. Song is prayer
3. Constant devotion keeps Hush at bay (both practically and spiritually)
4. Communities should share resources (radical idea)

**The Choir anthem:** A hymn in rotating three-part harmony. Someone is always singing. Always.

By year's end: 12 settlements formally join. Population: ~4,000.

---

## YEARS 72-75 AS (2106-2109): The First Conflicts

**Theme:** Organized factions mean organized conflicts.

---

### Year 72 AS: The First Archive-Choir Dispute

The Choir wants access to pre-Silence religious texts. The Archive demands payment (food, labor, materials).

The Choir argues knowledge of God should be free. The Archive counters that storage and preservation aren't free.

Standoff lasts 3 months. Resolution: The Choir provides 50 people for 1 year of archive labor in exchange for unrestricted access to religious texts.

Both sides declare victory. Neither is satisfied.

"Archive bargaining" enters common usage: negotiations where both sides feel cheated.

---

### Year 73 AS: The First Silent Crystal Attack

A small Crystal in former Poland goes dark. Investigation reveals deliberate sabotage — the Crystal was cracked with steel tools.

400 people die before survivors evacuate.

Evidence points to The Silent. They don't deny it.

Erasmus Vane's statement: "Every Crystal destroyed is a step toward adaptation. We don't enjoy the deaths. But evolution is never comfortable."

The Silent are declared enemies by 30+ communities. They go fully underground.

Crystal hunts begin. Not for new Crystals — for Silent members.

---

### Year 74 AS: The Engineers Unify

The scattered engineering guilds meet in former Germany. 200+ members attend.

**Decision:** Form a single unified faction dedicated to understanding and eventually reversing The Silence.

**First Chief Engineer:** Klaus Hartmann, acoustic physicist

**Mission statement:** "The Filter is artificial. The Crystals are emergent phenomena. Therefore: The Filter can be understood. What can be understood can be controlled. What can be controlled can be defeated. We will end The Silence. Or die trying."

Ambitious. Probably impossible.

But they have to try.

---

### Year 75 AS: The Seventy-Fifth Year

**Global Population:** ~780 million (decline from conflicts)
**Known Crystals:** 48 worldwide
**Major Factions:** Archive, Choir, Engineers, Silent

The post-Silence world has entered a new phase. Not survival — organization. Not random communities — factions with beliefs, resources, conflicts.

The Quiet Centuries are far from over. But they've changed character.

**Chloé Delacroix-Rousseau's Speech (Age 67):**

> "Seventy-five years. Three quarters of a century.
>
> My father died 22 years ago. My mother died 60 years ago. The world they knew is gone.
>
> But we're still here. Not just surviving. Building. Fighting. Believing. Disagreeing.
>
> We have factions now. The Archive hoards knowledge. The Choir sings to God. The Engineers dream of fixing what's broken. The Silent want to destroy what keeps us alive.
>
> We're not united. We never will be. That's what it means to be human.
>
> The drums keep beating. The Crystals keep humming. We keep living.
>
> Year 76 begins. We continue. Divided, arguing, surviving.
>
> Just like always."
