## Introduction: When "Last" Gets Complicated

The standard Last Human debates focus on who died when and where. These cases explore a more interesting question: **What counts as "last"?**

This section contains the disputed, the fraudulent, the technical, the regional, the collective, the ironic, the meta, the premature, the retroactive, and the philosophical.

In other words: the fun ones.

---

## 1. THE DISPUTED: The Ghost of Carcassonne

**Year:** Allegedly 29 AS
**Location:** Carcassonne fortress, former France
**Claimed status:** "The Last Human (Possibly Fictional)"

### The Evidence

Multiple sources reference "The Ghost" — an immune survivor who allegedly lived in Carcassonne's medieval fortress until Year 29 AS. The problem: no body, no journals, no artifacts.

**Sources citing The Ghost:**
- A second-hand account from a scavenger in Year 51 AS who "heard about him from someone"
- A note found in Year 73 AS referencing "the hermit in the old castle"
- Three separate oral histories collected between Years 120-180
- Zero physical evidence whatsoever

### The Scholarly Debate

**Pro-Ghost faction:** "Absence of evidence isn't evidence of absence. Multiple independent sources confirm the Ghost existed."

**Anti-Ghost faction:** "All your sources are people who heard about a guy from someone else. That's called a legend, not history."

**Moderate position (Acoustic Engineers, Year 295 AS):** "The Ghost is either the best-documented person with no physical evidence, or the worst-documented person with abundant secondary sources. We're calling it 'plausible but unconfirmed.'"

### The Carcassonne Tourism Board Position

The local settlement around the old fortress officially recognizes The Ghost as real and celebrates "Ghost Day" annually. When pressed about the lack of evidence, the festival organizer shrugged and said: "It's good for business."

**Current status:** Disputed. Historians fight about this one at conferences.

## 2. THE FRAUDULENT: The Forged Testament of "Auguste the Eternal"

**Year:** Claimed Year 67 AS
**Location:** Near Avignon
**Claimed status:** "The Last Human Who Lived Until Year 67"

### The Claim

In Year 112 AS, a descendant community near Avignon produced a "newly discovered" journal claiming their ancestor Auguste survived until Year 67 AS — 25 years after Simone, the previous record-holder.

The journal was remarkably well-preserved. Suspiciously well-preserved.

### The Problems

**Carbon dating:** Showed the paper was manufactured around Year 90 AS (30+ years after the claimed date)

**Linguistic analysis:** Auguste's writing contained three loan-words from post-Silence Resonant dialect that didn't exist until Year 50+

**Historical inconsistencies:** Auguste describes eating "Crystal-grown tomatoes" in Year 64 AS. The first Crystal wasn't discovered until Year 41 AS, and Crystal agriculture wasn't developed until Year 70+.

**The smoking gun:** Auguste references "the ongoing Bernard-Simone debate" in Year 65 AS. That debate started in Year 89 AS.

### The Defense

The Avignon community insisted the journal was authentic. When confronted with the anachronisms, they revised their position to: "Maybe Auguste was a time traveler?"

This did not help their case.

### The Aftermath

**Year 118 AS:** The Archive officially declared the journal a forgery
**Year 119 AS:** The forger confessed (it was Auguste's great-great-grandson, trying to "honor the family legacy")
**Year 120 AS:** The Avignon community held a "Worst Forgery Festival" and now celebrates their ancestor's descendant's terrible lying skills

**Current status:** Definitively fake. Celebrated anyway.

## 3. THE TECHNICALITY: The Woman Who Died Twice

**Year:** 16 AS (first death), 18 AS (second death)
**Location:** Marseille
**Claimed status:** "The Last Human (After Being Dead Already)"

### The Story

**Marguerite Blanc** was found unconscious in Year 16 AS by a small survivor group. No pulse. No breathing. They performed funeral rites and left her in a sealed room, as was custom (you don't bury bodies in a post-apocalypse without certainty).

**Year 18 AS:** A scavenger team returns to the same building. Marguerite is sitting up, alive, confused, and very hungry.

### The Explanation (Probably)

Medical analysis of the remains (Year 201 AS) suggests:
- Severe hypothermia induced temporary metabolic shutdown
- The sealed room's temperature stayed low enough to prevent decay
- Marguerite woke up after approximately 18-22 months in a coma-like state
- She survived another 3-4 weeks before dying of starvation (couldn't find food in time)

### The Debate

**Question:** If you were clinically dead for 18 months, then came back to life, are you still eligible for "Last Human" status?

**The "Yes" faction:** She died in Year 18 AS, which would make her the last human in the Marseille region.

**The "No" faction:** She died in Year 16 AS. The fact that her body restarted doesn't change her original death date.

**The "She Died Twice" faction:** She should count as two separate Last Humans.

**Marguerite's position (from her final note):** "I don't care. I'm starving. If someone finds this: I died twice and I'm still hungry. What a life."

**Current status:** Technically last? Philosophically disputed.

## 4. THE REGIONAL CHAMPION: The Last Human of the 5th Arrondissement

**Year:** 22 AS
**Location:** 5th Arrondissement, Paris
**Claimed status:** "The Last Human in a Very Specific Part of Paris"

### The Claim

**François Montand** left a note declaring himself "The Last Human of the 5th Arrondissement" in Year 22 AS.

Not the last human in Paris. Not the last human in France. The last human in a specific 2.5 square kilometer administrative district of a city that no longer had administrative districts.

### The Note

> "I am François Montand, and I am the last living human in the 5th Arrondissement of Paris. There may be others in the 6th. I saw someone in the 13th last year. But in the 5th? I am alone. I am last. This counts."

### The Debate

**Purists:** "This is absurd. You can't claim Last Human status for a neighborhood."

**François supporters:** "He never claimed to be THE Last Human. He claimed to be the last human *in the 5th Arrondissement.* That's technically accurate."

**Counter-argument:** "By that logic, everyone is the last human of their specific GPS coordinates."

**François supporter rebuttal:** "Correct. Everyone is last somewhere."

This is when the debate got philosophical and people stopped listening.

### Similar Claims

Once the "Last Human of [Specific Region]" precedent was established (or not established, depending on who you ask), others followed:

- **The Last Human of the Left Bank** (Year 27 AS)
- **The Last Human of the Métro Line 4 Stations** (Year 31 AS)
- **The Last Human Within Sight of the Eiffel Tower** (Year 33 AS)
- **The Last Human Who Lived Above a Bakery** (Year 34 AS, very specific)

**Current status:** Technically accurate. Completely meaningless. Beloved by pedants.

## 5. THE COLLECTIVE: The Mutual Last Humans Society

**Year:** 37-39 AS
**Location:** Brittany coast
**Claimed status:** "We Are All the Last Humans (Together)"

### The Formation

In Year 37 AS, six immune survivors in Brittany discovered each other. Instead of each claiming to be "the last," they formed **The Mutual Last Humans Society** and declared themselves collectively the last.

### The Charter (carved into driftwood)

> "We, the undersigned, being the last humans we have encountered in three years of searching, do hereby declare ourselves MUTUALLY the Last Humans. We shall share this title equally. If one of us dies, the title redistributes among survivors. No one person shall claim superiority."
>
> Signed:
> - Amélie (fisher)
> - Jacques (farmer)
> - Séverine (doctor)
> - Thomas (engineer)
> - Céleste (teacher)
> - Paul (former chef, current complainer)

### The Dissolution

The Society lasted two years.

**Year 38 AS:** Jacques died. The remaining five renewed the charter.

**Year 39 AS:** Thomas and Séverine died within weeks of each other. The remaining three (Amélie, Céleste, Paul) met to renew the charter.

Paul said: "I'm going to be honest. I think I'm going to outlive you both."

Amélie: "That's not the spirit of the Society."

Paul: "The Society is down to three people. The spirit is dying faster than we are."

Céleste: "Should we vote?"

Paul: "On what? Whether we're allowed to admit this is pointless?"

**The final vote:** 2-1 to disband the Society. Paul voted to keep it going.

### The Aftermath

**Year 39 AS, three weeks later:** Céleste died.

Amélie left a note: "Paul was right. I hate that he was right. — Amélie, probably second-to-last"

**Year 40 AS:** Paul died. His final note: "I knew it. — Paul, ACTUAL last"

### The Legacy

The Mutual Last Humans Society is commemorated in Brittany settlements as an example of "trying to be wholesome during the apocalypse."

Their driftwood charter is preserved in the Archive and occasionally loaned out for exhibitions.

The exhibition is titled: "We Tried: A History of Failed Optimism"

**Current status:** Collectively last. Sequentially disputed.

## 6. THE IRONIC: The Man Who Refused

**Year:** 28 AS
**Location:** Toulouse region
**Claimed status:** None (aggressively)

### The Story

**Étienne Roux** survived until approximately Year 28 AS. He was aware of other immune survivors. He met several. He outlived most of them.

He never once claimed to be the last human.

### The Evidence

Extensive journals (14 volumes) documenting his survival, his encounters with others, and his growing frustration with the "Last Human" phenomenon.

**Selected quotes:**

**Year 19 AS:**
> "Met a man today who introduced himself as 'probably the last human.' I said, 'I'm standing right here.' He said, 'Well, the last IMPORTANT human.' I left."

**Year 23 AS:**
> "Another Last Human. That's the fifth one. Do people not understand what 'last' means?"

**Year 26 AS:**
> "I've outlived three people who called themselves the Last Human. The irony is lost on them. Because they're dead."

**Year 28 AS (final entry):**
> "I'm dying. I know I'm dying. And I know what people will say: 'Oh, Étienne thought he was the last human.' NO. I never thought that. I REFUSED to think that. I saw how stupid it made everyone else. I will die with dignity, which means dying without making grand claims about my place in history. I am not the last human. I am just... last. There's a difference. — Étienne Roux, NOT the Last Human, and don't you dare say otherwise"

### The Posthumous Irony

**Year 89 AS:** Scholars studying the Last Human phenomenon discover Étienne's journals.

**Year 90 AS:** A faction forms arguing that Étienne's refusal to claim the title makes him "the most legitimate Last Human."

**Year 91 AS:** A different faction argues that Étienne should be honored by NOT calling him the Last Human, as per his wishes.

**Year 92 AS:** The factions fight. Actual fight. Fists.

**Year 94 AS:** A compromise is reached: Étienne Roux is officially designated "The Last Human (Reluctant)" in Archive records.

### Étienne's Theoretical Position

If Étienne were alive to hear this, his position would probably be: "I hate all of you."

**Current status:** Last Human against his will. The irony would kill him if he weren't already dead.

## 7. THE META: The Last Human Historian

**Year:** 35-40 AS
**Location:** Loire Valley
**Claimed status:** "Documenter of Last Humans (Not One Myself)"

### The Project

**Isabelle Moreau** was an immune survivor and former museum curator. Instead of claiming to be the last human, she spent her final years documenting everyone else who did.

### The Archive

Isabelle's cave contained:
- A hand-drawn map of France with marked locations of "Last Humans"
- 23 documented cases with dates, locations, and evidence assessment
- A rating system (1-5 stars) for "claim credibility"
- Extensive commentary

**Sample entry:**

> **Bernard of Périgord (Year 41 AS)**
> Claimed status: "The Last Human, Don't Let Anyone Tell You Otherwise"
> Evidence: Note found in cave
> Credibility: ★★☆☆☆
> Assessment: Met another survivor (Simone) and dismissed her as "weird." Self-disqualified by own logic. Not a serious person.

### The Irony

Isabelle died in Year 40 AS.

**Her final journal entry:**

> "I have documented 23 people who claimed to be the last human. I have outlived all of them. The irony is not lost on me. But I refuse to claim the title. I am a DOCUMENTARIAN, not a subject. If someone finds this and tries to add me to the list, I will haunt them. — Isabelle, Year 40"

### The Aftermath

**Year 87 AS:** Scholars discover Isabelle's archive.

**Year 88 AS:** Scholars add Isabelle to the official list of Last Humans.

**Year 89 AS:** Several scholars report "feeling watched" while working on the Last Human project.

**Year 90 AS:** One scholar claims to have seen "a disapproving woman in curator clothes" in the Archive stacks. This is dismissed as stress.

**Year 91 AS:** The same scholar finds their notes on Isabelle mysteriously reorganized with corrections in the margins. The handwriting matches Isabelle's journal.

**Year 92 AS:** The Archive officially designates Isabelle as "The Last Human (Documentarian)" and adds a footnote: "We apologize for including you in the list. Please stop reorganizing our notes."

The mysterious corrections stop.

**Current status:** Last Human documentarian. Possibly haunting the Archive. Definitely not happy about it.

## 8. THE PREMATURE: The Man Who Predicted Wrong

**Year:** 13 AS (prediction), 26 AS (actual death)
**Location:** Normandy
**Claimed status:** "Future Last Human (Prediction Failed)"

### The Prediction

**Luc Fontaine** wrote extensive journals throughout the early post-Silence years. In Year 13 AS, he made a prediction:

> "I have calculated my remaining resources. I have examined my health. I have assessed the probability of encountering other survivors. Based on this analysis, I will be the last human. I will die in approximately Year 15 AS. This is my legacy: the last man who saw it coming."

### The Problem

Luc was good at survival. Very good.

**Year 15 AS:** Luc is still alive. He writes: "Calculations were off. Revised estimate: Year 17 AS."

**Year 17 AS:** Still alive. "Further revision: Year 19 AS. The rabbits are more abundant than expected."

**Year 19 AS:** Still alive. "I am... surprisingly healthy? Year 21 AS, surely."

**Year 21 AS:** "This is getting embarrassing."

**Year 23 AS:** "I am the longest-surviving person who predicted their own death incorrectly. This is not the legacy I wanted."

**Year 25 AS:** "I met another survivor. Her name is Geneviève. She's been here the whole time. Three kilometers away. I am not the last human. I am not even the last human in my immediate area. I am a failure."

**Year 26 AS (final entry):** "I'm dying now. Finally. Geneviève says she'll outlive me. She's probably right. She's been right about everything else. Tell my story as a warning: don't predict the future. You'll just be wrong and live long enough to regret it. — Luc, not the last human, not even close"

### Geneviève's Addendum

Found on the same page, different handwriting:

> "He died yesterday. He was wrong about being last. He was right about me outliving him. I'm going to miss him. He was an idiot, but he was MY idiot. — Geneviève, Year 26"

**Geneviève's death:** Year 28 AS. She never claimed to be the last human. Just the last person who knew Luc.

### The Legacy

Luc and Geneviève are commemorated together in Normandy settlements. Their story is used to teach children:
1. Don't make predictions you can't control
2. Check if your neighbors are alive before declaring yourself alone
3. Being wrong and living is better than being right and dead

**Current status:** Prematurely last. Correctly not-last. Remembered fondly.

## 9. THE RETROACTIVE: The Posthumous Last Human

**Year:** 21 AS (death), 156 AS (designation)
**Location:** Pyrenees mountains
**Claimed status:** None (declared last by committee 135 years later)

### The Original Story

**Matthieu Arnaud** died in Year 21 AS in a mountain cave in the Pyrenees. He left no note. He made no claim. He was found by explorers in Year 154 AS, mummified by the dry mountain air.

### The Discovery

**Year 154 AS:** Explorers find Matthieu's body and journals. The journals are detailed survival logs with zero mention of being "the last human."

**Sample entry (Year 18 AS):**
> "Killed a mountain goat today. Made stew. It was fine. Tomorrow I'll check the traps."

That's it. Three years of survival. Zero philosophical reflection on humanity's fate.

### The Designation

**Year 156 AS:** The Pyrenean Council of Historians meets to discuss regional Last Humans. They have no good candidates.

**The debate:**

Scholar 1: "What about Old Marie?"
Scholar 2: "Alsace region. Different committee."
Scholar 1: "The Three Brothers?"
Scholar 2: "Normandy. Also, they're disputed."
Scholar 1: "Do we... have anyone?"
Scholar 2: "There's Matthieu."
Scholar 1: "Matthieu who?"
Scholar 2: "Matthieu Arnaud. Died Year 21. Mountain cave. Made no claims."
Scholar 1: "Perfect. He's our Last Human."
Scholar 2: "Should we ask his descendants?"
Scholar 1: "He died alone in a cave. No descendants."
Scholar 2: "Even better. No one to object."

**Vote:** Unanimous (4-0, two scholars were absent)

### The Controversy

**Year 157 AS:** A different historical society discovers the Pyrenean designation and objects.

"You can't just ASSIGN someone Last Human status 135 years after they die!"

**Pyrenean response:** "We just did."

**Counter:** "It doesn't work that way!"

**Pyrenean response:** "Matthieu isn't here to object, and frankly, neither are you. This is our region. He's our Last Human."

### The Compromise

**Year 158 AS:** Matthieu is officially designated "The Last Human of the Pyrenees (Retroactive)" with a footnote: "Did not claim this title. Did not want this title. We gave it to him anyway."

A monument is erected at his cave with the inscription:
> "Matthieu Arnaud
> Year 21 AS
> Last Human (Allegedly)
> He Never Asked For This"

**Current status:** Retroactively last. Posthumously annoyed (probably).

## 10. THE PHILOSOPHICAL: The Last Human Who Wasn't

**Year:** 44 AS
**Location:** Bordeaux region
**Claimed status:** "The Last Human Because Humanity Ended Already"

### The Philosophy

**Renée Beaumont** survived until Year 44 AS — one of the longest-surviving immune humans on record. But her claim to "Last Human" status wasn't chronological. It was philosophical.

### The Argument (from her journals)

**Year 38 AS:**
> "People keep asking if I'm the last human. Wrong question. Humanity ended in Year 0. What we are now — survivors, scavengers, cave-dwellers — we're not humans anymore. We're remnants. Echoes. The species died. We're just the body twitching."

**Year 40 AS:**
> "I am the last human because I remember what humans were. I remember cities, governments, art, music you could hear without drums. I remember concerts where the sound traveled freely. I remember humanity. When I die, that memory dies. The others? They're survivors. I'm the last human."

**Year 42 AS:**
> "Met a young survivor today. Born after the Silence. She asked me what 'radio' was. I tried to explain. She didn't understand. She couldn't understand. Her world is drums and caves and Crystals. She's not human. She's something new. Something next. I'm the last of the old species. She's the first of the new one."

**Year 44 AS (final entry):**
> "I'm dying. The last human is dying. Not the last survivor — there are thousands of those. But the last person who remembers humanity as it was? That's me. When I close my eyes, civilization closes with me. I am the last human because I am the last who remembers humans. — Renée, Year 44"

### The Scholarly Response

**The Existentialist Faction:** "She's right. Humanity as a category ended with the Silence. We're post-human now."

**The Pragmatist Faction:** "This is ridiculous. Humans are humans regardless of circumstances."

**The Linguistic Faction:** "She raises interesting questions about the definition of 'human' as both biology and culture."

**The Annoyed Faction:** "This is why philosophers shouldn't be allowed near apocalypses."

### The Cultural Impact

Renée's journals became foundational texts for several philosophical movements:

**The Echoes:** A movement believing current survivors are "echoes of humanity" rather than humans themselves

**The Continuum:** A counter-movement arguing humanity is a continuum and declarations of "end dates" are arbitrary

**The Memory-Keepers:** A group dedicated to preserving pre-Silence culture, considering themselves "custodians of the last humanity"

**The Next:** A group embracing post-human identity and rejecting "last human" debates entirely

### The Current Debate

**Year 300 AS:** Renée's philosophy is taught in Archive schools. The debate continues:

"Are we humans? Or are we something else?"

"Does it matter?"

"If we don't remember what humans were, are we still them?"

"Renée died 256 years ago and she's still causing arguments. Is THAT human?"

### The Irony

Renée claimed to be the last human because she was the last to remember humanity.

But her journals ensured that memory would survive her.

Which means, by her own definition, she failed.

Or succeeded in a way she didn't anticipate.

The philosophers are still arguing about it.

**Current status:** Philosophically last. Definitely human. Probably would find this whole debate exhausting.

## Conclusion: What We've Learned

After reviewing the disputed, fraudulent, technical, regional, collective, ironic, meta, premature, retroactive, and philosophical cases, we can conclude:

1. "Last Human" is a meaningless designation that everyone cares about anyway
2. Humans will debate anything, even after humanity ends
3. The more specific your claim, the harder it is to dispute and the less it matters
4. You can be wrong about being last and still die eventually
5. Philosophy makes everything worse
6. Ghosts might be real (the Archive is still investigating)
7. Never trust a forgery that mentions time travel
8. Being declared something posthumously means you can't object
9. Regional pride survives longer than the regions themselves
10. If you refuse to claim a title, people will give it to you out of spite

The "Last Human" debate will continue until the last person who cares about it dies.

That person will probably claim to be the Last Last-Human-Debate-Haver.

We're pre-annoyed about it already.
