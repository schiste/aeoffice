# Doué-la-Fontaine Mutation Nest

> **The troglodyte zoo — where animals already lived in caves.**
>
> **Location:** ~80km southwest of Tours
>
> **Pre-Silence Population:** 1,000 animals, 130 species
>
> **Current Status:** Active mutation nest, HIGH DANGER
>
> **Related:** [README.md](../README.md) | [beauval.md](beauval.md)

---

## Overview

**Bioparc de Doué-la-Fontaine** was unique among European zoos: it was built into **troglodyte caves**. The same tuffeau limestone that created Les Grottes de la Bresme also created Doué-la-Fontaine — but here, someone decided to put animals in the caves.

Before The Silence, this was charming. A "natural habitat" experience. Animals living in caves carved over centuries.

After The Silence, it became a problem.

The animals were already adapted to underground life. When civilization fell, they didn't need to learn. They just... stayed.

And bred.

And changed.

---

## Why Doué-la-Fontaine Is Different

Most zoo animals had to escape first, then adapt to local conditions. Doué-la-Fontaine's animals were pre-adapted to cave living:

- Constant 12°C temperature (already acclimated)
- Low light conditions (already adjusted)
- Underground water sources (already known)
- Tunneled architecture (already navigating)

They didn't escape into the world. They expanded through it.

The troglodyte cave system around Doué-la-Fontaine is extensive. Some tunnels connect to other underground networks. Some connect to... we don't know what.

**The concern:** Doué-la-Fontaine creatures may have spread further underground than any surface expedition can track.

## Known Mutations (Year 10 AS)

### The Lemur Colonies

Lemurs were always vocal, social, and adaptable. Post-Silence lemurs are:

- **Larger** (1.5x original size)
- **Quieter** (adapted to sound-sensitive environment)
- **Nocturnal to troglodytic** (now live entirely underground)
- **Cooperative hunters** (no longer purely fruit-eating)

**The Echo Adaptation:**

Doué-la-Fontaine lemurs have developed rudimentary echolocation. Not as sophisticated as bats, but enough to navigate in total darkness.

They click. They listen. They know where you are.

**Threat Level:** MEDIUM — Avoid cave systems near Doué-la-Fontaine.

---

### The Underground Leopards

Snow leopards and clouded leopards were already adapted to low-light hunting. Post-Silence, they've become something else entirely.

**Les Léopards de l'Ombre** (The Shadow Leopards):

| Trait | Pre-Silence | Year 10 AS |
|-------|-------------|------------|
| Habitat | Surface, low light | Entirely underground |
| Coloration | Spotted grey/white | Solid dark grey, almost black |
| Eyes | Normal feline | Enlarged, reflective, low-light specialized |
| Size | 30-50kg | 40-60kg |
| Hunting style | Ambush | Underground ambush |

They've colonized cave systems up to 20km from the original zoo.

**The Problem:** They hunt in caves. Our caves. The Bresme caves.

Year 7 AS saw the first confirmed encounter between a Shadow Leopard and Les Grottes de la Bresme perimeter patrols. Two guards injured. The creature was driven off with drums.

They've been seen twice since.

**Threat Level:** HIGH — Cave travel requires extreme caution.

---

### The Burrowing Problem

Meerkats and prairie dogs are burrowing specialists. They dig. It's what they do.

Post-Silence meerkats dig *better*.

**Current Status:**
- Colony size: Unknown (underground)
- Tunnel network: Expanding constantly
- Depth: Some tunnels exceed 30 meters
- Diet: Adapted to underground insects, fungi, possibly other things

**The Concern:**

Meerkat tunnels have been found connecting to existing cave systems. In Year 6 AS, a patrol in a "secure" tunnel found meerkat scratches on the walls — scratches that weren't there the week before.

They're digging toward us.

We don't know if this is coincidence or intention.

**Threat Level:** LOW (individually) / HIGH (structurally) — Monitor cave integrity.

---

### The Vulture Question

Doué-la-Fontaine had a significant vulture collection. Vultures are scavengers. They find the dead.

Post-Silence vultures have become... efficient.

They roost in the cave entrances. They watch. When something dies — anything, anywhere within their range — they know. They circle.

Expedition teams have learned: if vultures are circling, something is dead.

If vultures are circling YOU, something is about to be dead.

**Current Behavior:**
- Patience has increased dramatically
- Will follow injured creatures for days
- Have developed darker plumage (cave adaptation)
- Make almost no sound (no warning)

**Threat Level:** LOW (direct) / MEDIUM (indicator of other dangers)

---

### The Reptile Situation

Nobody knows what happened to the reptiles.

The reptile exhibits were underground. The heating systems failed. By all reasonable prediction, they should have frozen in the first winter.

But every few years, someone reports seeing something scaled in the cave systems.

**Probably nothing.**

**Definitely nothing.**

**Let's move on.**

## Territorial Range

Unlike Beauval (contained to former zoo + immediate area), Doué-la-Fontaine creatures have spread **underground**:

| Direction | Known Extent | Notes |
|-----------|--------------|-------|
| North | 15km | Toward Loire valley |
| South | 8km | Into forest caves |
| East | 20km+ | Toward Saumur wine caves |
| West | Unknown | Deep cave systems, unexplored |

**The Network Problem:**

Loire Valley cave systems connect. Doué-la-Fontaine caves connect to wine caves connect to quarry caves connect to... other caves.

Creatures that learned to live underground can travel underground.

This is why perimeter patrols exist at Les Grottes de la Bresme.

---

## Implications for Survivors Cave

Distance from Les Grottes de la Bresme: ~68km surface, **unknown underground**.

**Direct Threat Assessment:** MODERATE

**Reasons for Concern:**
1. Shadow Leopards hunt caves
2. Lemur colonies expanding through tunnel networks
3. Meerkat tunneling may create new connections
4. Underground routes are difficult to patrol

**Current Protocols:**
- Weekly tunnel inspections
- Sound discipline in peripheral caves
- Emergency drums positioned at all major junctions
- Cave-in protocols for compromised tunnels

## The Irony

Doué-la-Fontaine was designed to showcase how animals could live in harmony with cave environments.

They can.

They do.

They're very good at it now.

## Expedition Recommendations

**Surface approach:** Relatively safe. Standard wildlife precautions.

**Cave approach:** NOT RECOMMENDED. Shadow Leopard territory.

**Resource value:** LOW — Most supplies looted or degraded. Veterinary materials potentially useful but location-specific.

**Intelligence value:** HIGH — Understanding underground creature spread is strategically important.
