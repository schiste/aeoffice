## Overview

The Marchand Collection contains vinyl records from **23 French artists**, all released before May 1968. This is the only recorded music in Les Grottes de la Bresme, and over 300 years, these artists have become the foundation of the Unplugged's spiritual life.

## The Vinyl Religions

Over 300 years, musical preference has evolved into quasi-religious affiliation. The theological debates are deeply serious. The subject matter is completely absurd. This is intentional.

### Major Denominations

| Religion | Prophet | Core Belief | Adherents (est.) |
|----------|---------|-------------|------------------|
| **Piafism** | Edith Piaf | Emotional intensity is the highest truth | ~30% |
| **Brassensisme** | Georges Brassens | Philosophy and wit lead to wisdom | ~25% |
| **Trenétisme** | Charles Trenet | Optimism conquers all darkness | ~20% |
| **Brelisme** | Jacques Brel | Passion and poetry define humanity | ~15% |
| **The Undecided** | (Various) | All prophets have merit | ~10% |

### Sample Theological Disputes

**"The Brel Intensity Question" (Year 187 AS):**
A three-day Council debate over whether *Ne me quitte pas* should be played at weddings. Brelistes argued it expressed true love. Trenétistes called it "emotional terrorism." Piafistes abstained because they were busy crying. The compromise: allowed, but only after the third course.

**"The Gainsbourg Heresy" (Year 203 AS):**
A young scholar argued that Gainsbourg's *Le Poinçonneur des Lilas* was actually the most profound philosophical work in The Collection. He was not exiled, but people stopped sitting next to him at meals for two years.

**"The Great Tino Rossi Rationing" (Year 156 AS):**
After a particularly difficult winter, someone played *Petit Papa Noël* in July "to lift spirits." The resulting theological crisis led to the Tino Rossi Protocols: Christmas music in December only, enforced by the Vinyl Guardian.

### The Johnny Schism

**The eternal question:** Is Johnny Hallyday acceptable?

**Pro-Johnny faction:**
- Madeleine liked him (legitimacy through matriarch)
- Early yé-yé is technically pre-1968
- Children deserve to know rock exists
- "He makes people dance. Dancing is good."

**Anti-Johnny faction:**
- Georges merely tolerated him
- American-influenced corruption
- Slippery slope to Claude François
- "First Johnny, then what? Disco?"

**Status:** Unresolved for 290 years. Both sides have developed sophisticated theological arguments. Neither side will admit the debate is silly. This makes it funnier.

### The Great Hallyday Feud (Year 8-10 AS)

The Johnny question wasn't always civilized.

**What happened:**
In Year 8 AS, a young mother named Céline Dubois played *Souvenirs, souvenirs* during a children's gathering. She argued the song was "fun" and "made the children smile." An elder named Gustave Renard — a hardline Brassensiste — called her "a corruptor of youth" and demanded the records be destroyed.

The argument escalated. Céline's family (the Dubois, later absorbed into the Ferrands) and Gustave's family (the Renards, later merged with the Martinets) began avoiding each other. Then insulting each other. Then, in Year 9 AS, physically fighting each other.

**The violence:**
For two years, any encounter between Dubois and Renard family members could turn violent:
- A shared meal ended with thrown bowls
- A tunnel expansion project was abandoned when workers from both families refused to cooperate
- A wedding between unrelated families nearly became a brawl when both sides were invited
- Three broken noses, two broken arms, one concussion, countless bruises

The community was horrified. Not because of the violence itself — cave life is hard — but because people were fighting over *Johnny Hallyday*. The absurdity was unbearable.

**The Resolution — Year 10 AS:**
Marc Delacroix, then Elder, proposed a radical solution: formalize the debate. Give both sides a day to argue their case, loudly and publicly. Let everyone watch. And — crucially — let everyone *participate*.

The first **Hallyday Debating Day** was held on June 24, Year 10 AS (the anniversary of Fête de la Musique, chosen deliberately).

**The Rules:**

1. **Anyone may speak.** Pro-Johnny or Anti-Johnny, declare your position and take the platform.
2. **Anyone may slap the speaker.** If you disagree strongly enough, walk up and deliver one open-palm slap. The speaker must accept it and continue speaking.
3. **No punching.** Slaps only. Closed fists result in immediate expulsion.
4. **The speaker may not slap back.** They must maintain dignity (such as it is).
5. **At day's end, a vote is taken.** The vote is purely symbolic — it changes nothing about Johnny's status.

**Why slapping?**
Marc reasoned: if people wanted to fight about Johnny, let them fight about Johnny — but in a controlled way. A slap is humiliating but rarely injurious. It lets anger out without causing real harm. And it forces the speaker to stand behind their words.

**The tradition persists:**
Hallyday Debating Day is now an annual event, held every June 24. It has evolved:

- **Opening ceremony:** Both Johnny records are played in full. Everyone listens in silence (or visible displeasure).
- **The debates:** Hours of impassioned speeches, interrupted by slaps. Some speakers collect dozens; others speak so well that no one dares approach.
- **The counting:** At day's end, slaps are counted. The side that delivered more slaps "wins" (bragging rights only).
- **The feast:** Afterward, everyone shares a meal. The Dubois and Renard descendants (now Ferrands and Martinets) traditionally sit together, honoring their ancestors' ridiculous feud.

**Notable moments:**
- **Year 87 AS:** A record 47 slaps delivered to a single speaker (Margot Renard, who argued Johnny was "better than Brassens at rhythm"). She finished her speech.
- **Year 156 AS:** The same day as the Tino Rossi incident. Double crisis.
- **Year 234 AS:** A speaker argued Johnny was actually good. She received no slaps — everyone was too stunned to move. She is now considered either a prophet or a lunatic.
- **Year 299 AS:** The current record for fewest total slaps: 3. Both sides gave excellent speeches. People called it "the boring year."

**The absurdity:**
After 290 years, the theological positions are sophisticated:
- Pro-Johnny scholars cite the "Madeleine Precedent" and "the Joy Doctrine"
- Anti-Johnny scholars invoke "the Purity Principle" and "the Slippery Slope Theorem"
- Both sides have written multi-generational treatises
- Neither side has changed anyone's mind since Year 50 AS
- Everyone knows this. No one cares. The debate is the point.

**The Johnny Underground:** Rumor persists that someone has hidden a post-1968 Johnny record somewhere in the caves. Expeditions have been mounted to find it. Nothing has been discovered. The search continues. Some say it's a myth to keep the controversy alive. Others say that's exactly what someone hiding a forbidden record would want you to think.

**The ultimate irony:** Georges Marchand, whose collection started all this, thought Johnny was "fine." Not good. Not bad. Just fine. He included the records because Madeleine asked. 290 years of theological warfare over "fine."

**The Petit Papa Noël Incident (Year 178 AS):**

Rémi Duval chose *Petit Papa Noël* as his song. His reasoning: "Everyone takes this too seriously."

Consequences:
- His wedding featured *Petit Papa Noël* in July
- His children's births were marked with *Petit Papa Noël*
- His future funeral will feature *Petit Papa Noël*
- Every birthday, the community sings *Petit Papa Noël* to a 138-year-old man
- His wife has never fully forgiven him

Rémi insists he has no regrets. He is lying, but he's committed now.

No one else has chosen *Petit Papa Noël* since. Whether out of respect for Rémi's commitment or fear of the consequences, the record shows: one choice, ever.

### The Final Song and The Return (Death)

When someone dies, the community gathers within 24 hours. Their chosen song plays one final time. The Vinyl Guardian announces: "[Nickname] chose [song]. The song returns to The Collection."

Then the body is burned and the ashes are scattered on the surface.

**Why burning and surface scattering:**
- **Practical:** Space is limited underground. Disease risk from burial. Cremation is safer.
- **Symbolic:** All survivors return to the surface eventually. The living stay below. The dead go above.

The world above killed humanity — but it's still the world. The dead return to sun, wind, and silence.
