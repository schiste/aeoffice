# La Dalle Éternelle ("The Eternal Slab")

> **Quick Reference:** Prepper faction controlling La Défense — corporate survivalists who became what they parodied.
>
> **Territory:** West — La Défense complex (parking levels P-1 through P-7)
> **Population:** ~20,000-24,000
> **Government:** Conseil d'Administration (Board of Directors)
> **Founded:** Day 1 (prepared since 2031)
> **Year 300 Status:** Wealthy, organized, insufferable

---

## Origin Story

### Before The Silence (2031-2034)

In 2031, a group of French survivalists rented parking spaces on levels P-4 through P-7 of the La Défense underground complex. Unlike their American counterparts who stockpiled guns and machismo, these preppers stockpiled spreadsheets, freeze-dried couscous, and PowerPoint presentations.

They called themselves "La Dalle" — a triple pun:
- "La dalle" = the massive concrete slab on which La Défense was built
- "Avoir la dalle" = French slang for "being starving hungry"
- "Dalle" sounds like "deal" — they were making the ultimate deal

Their founder, **Bertrand Vauclair**, was a middle manager at BNP Paribas who became convinced that society would collapse. He was right, but for the wrong reasons (he feared nuclear war, not a sound-killing virus).

**What they stockpiled:**
- 3 years of freeze-dried food (military surplus, camping rations)
- Water filtration systems
- Solar-charged batteries (useless underground, but they didn't know)
- An entire archive of "survival guides" (mostly American, mostly useless)
- Detailed inventory spreadsheets
- A projector and screen (for "survival briefings")
- Business casual clothing (surprisingly practical)

### Day 1: The Preparation Pays Off

At 5:34 AM, October 14, 2034, The Silence hit.

La Défense was just waking up — early workers, cleaning crews, security guards, and a few hundred souls who lived in the towers above. Within hours, ~5,000 people flooded into the underground levels, desperate for shelter from a world gone silent.

The preppers were ready.

While chaos erupted in the metro tunnels across Paris, La Dalle had:
- Locked doors to protect supply rooms
- Posted guards with the only functioning flashlights
- Activated their "Emergency Governance Protocol" (yes, they had one)

By Week 1, Bertrand Vauclair had declared himself "Directeur Général Provisoire" (Interim CEO). The "provisional" part was dropped by Year 2.

### Years 1-10: Consolidation

The preppers couldn't save everyone, but they saved more than most. Their organizational mania — the spreadsheets, the protocols, the obsessive inventory management — proved invaluable.

**Year 1 survival tactics:**
- Strict rationing (ration cards issued, tracked in spreadsheets)
- Work-for-food system (everyone contributed or starved)
- Scouting parties to nearby buildings (found more supplies)
- "HR Department" assigned roles based on skills

**What they learned:**
- Underground farming was possible (mushrooms first, then other fungi)
- The parking ventilation system could be modified for agriculture
- The corporate towers above were death traps (too quiet), but their basements had resources
- Organization beat chaos every time

By Year 10, La Dalle controlled the entire La Défense underground complex — the largest prepared space in Paris.

## Year 300: The Corporation

### What They Became

They became what they parodied.

The original preppers were mostly tech workers, middle managers, and accountants who saw corporate culture as a dark joke. Their "Board of Directors" was ironic. Their "quarterly reports" were parody. Their dress code was a bit.

300 years later, it's all real. The irony evaporated; the structure remained.

### Governance: The Conseil d'Administration

**Le PDG (Président-Directeur Général):** The CEO. Elected every 5 years by shareholder vote.

**Shareholders:** Those who hold "parts" (shares) in La Dalle. Shares are:
- Inherited from founding families
- Earned through exceptional service
- Bought (in exchange for labor credits)
- Never, ever sold

**The Board:**
| Position | Role | Current Holder |
|----------|------|----------------|
| PDG | Chief Executive | Antoine Vauclair IV |
| Directeur des Ressources | Resource management | Marianne Leclerc |
| Directeur de la Sécurité | Security & defense | Jean-Pierre "JP" Morel |
| Directeur de l'Infrastructure | Construction & maintenance | Sylvie Chen |
| Directeur du Capital Humain | HR, population, breeding programs | François Dubois |

**Departments:**
Every citizen belongs to a department. Your department is your identity.

- **Ressources:** Farming, food production, water management
- **Sécurité:** Guards, scouts, border patrol
- **Infrastructure:** Construction, repairs, engineering
- **Capital Humain:** Education, healthcare, population management
- **Commerce Extérieur:** Trade with other factions
- **Archives:** Records, history, data preservation

### Social Structure: The Levels

La Défense has 7 underground parking levels. Your level is your class.

| Level | Class | Who Lives Here |
|-------|-------|----------------|
| P-1 | Elite | Board members, major shareholders, founding families |
| P-2 | Upper management | Department heads, senior specialists |
| P-3 | Middle management | Team leads, skilled workers |
| P-4 | Workers | General labor, junior positions |
| P-5 | Apprentices | Youth, trainees, newcomers |
| P-6 | Probationary | Recent arrivals, those demoted |
| P-7 | The Reserves | Storage, agricultural workers, "special projects" |

**Mobility:** You can move up through performance reviews, exceptional service, or marriage. You can move down through failure, crime, or "restructuring."

## Daily Life

### The Work Week

The 35-hour work week is sacred (inherited from pre-Silence France). Everyone works their scheduled hours — no more, no less. Overtime exists but requires paperwork.

**Daily schedule:**
- 07:00 — Wake announcement (drumbeats through corridors)
- 07:30 — Morning briefing (department meetings)
- 08:00 — Work begins
- 10:00 — Coffee break (15 minutes, mandatory)
- 12:00-14:00 — Déjeuner (lunch, sacred, no work)
- 14:00 — Afternoon shift
- 16:00 — Coffee break (15 minutes, mandatory)
- 18:00 — Work ends
- 19:00 — Evening meal (cafeteria or home)
- 22:00 — Quiet hours begin (lower volume, not silence)

### Performance Reviews

Every citizen receives quarterly performance reviews. Yes, even children.

**Metrics tracked:**
- Work output (productivity)
- Collaboration (teamwork)
- Initiative (proactivity)
- Rhythm contribution (drumming shifts)
- "Synergy" (nobody knows what this means, but it's measured)

**Consequences:**
- Excellent review → Bonus ration cards, potential promotion
- Good review → Standard allocation
- Needs improvement → Warning, mandatory training
- Poor review → Demotion (move down a level)
- Three poor reviews → Exile consideration

### Team Building Exercises

Mandatory. Annual. Universally despised.

**Examples:**
- "Trust falls" into mushroom-fiber nets
- Group problem-solving in escape-room-style bunkers
- Departmental singing competitions (all songs about productivity)
- The annual "Corporate Retreat" — a weekend on P-7 where executives pretend to understand manual labor

### The Break Room

Every level has a break room. Break room hierarchy is sacred.

- The best seats (near the rhythm pipes) belong to senior staff
- Juniors stand or sit on the floor
- Stealing someone's seat is a fireable offense
- The coffee substitute (mushroom-based, called "noir") is distributed by seniority

## Culture & Customs

### Language

La Dalle speaks French, but with heavy corporate jargon that's become literal:

| Phrase | Original Meaning | Year 300 Meaning |
|--------|-----------------|------------------|
| "Faisons le point" | "Let's touch base" | Formal greeting |
| "On se fait un call" | "Let's have a call" | "Let's talk privately" |
| "C'est dans ma pipeline" | "It's in my pipeline" | "I'm working on it" |
| "Synergie" | Corporate buzzword | Actual religious concept |
| "Restructuration" | Layoffs | Exile or execution |
| "Optimiser" | Optimize | To improve anything |
| "Livrable" | Deliverable | Any product or result |

### Dress Code

Business casual. 300 years later.

They've reconstructed it from memory and fragmentary images. It's... close.

**Standard attire:**
- Collared shirts (handwoven mushroom fiber)
- Slacks or knee-length skirts
- "Closed-toe shoes" (reinforced leather)
- Name badges (required, always visible)

**Formal occasions:**
- Suits (for men and women)
- Ties (they figured out how to make these)
- The mysterious "blazer" — they're not sure what it was, but they made something

### Religion: The Synergy

La Dalle developed a quasi-religious belief system around corporate concepts:

**Core beliefs:**
- The Founders had a Vision (the survival plan)
- That Vision was preserved in the Archives (the spreadsheets)
- By following the System, we optimize ourselves
- Optimization leads to Synergy
- Synergy is transcendence

**Rituals:**
- The Quarterly Report (public reading of faction achievements)
- The Performance Review (personal reflection and judgment)
- The Merger (marriage ceremony)
- The Restructuring (funeral — "we're letting them go")

### Holidays

| Day | Name | What Happens |
|-----|------|--------------|
| Jan 1 | Fiscal New Year | New department goals announced |
| Apr 15 | Founder's Day | Bertrand Vauclair's birthday, major celebration |
| Jul 14 | Bastille Day | Ironically preserved, celebrated with "fireworks" (drums) |
| Oct 14 | The Day of Preparation | Anniversary of The Silence, somber reflection |
| Dec 25 | Year-End Bonus Day | Bonus rations distributed |

## Military & Security

### La Sécurité

The security department doubles as the military. All citizens serve a 2-year mandatory rotation in Sécurité between ages 18-25.

**Structure:**
- **Gardiens:** Interior guards, patrol the levels
- **Éclaireurs:** Scouts, explore the surface and other territories
- **Sentinelles:** Border guards, watch the approaches
- **Interventionnistes:** Rapid response, handle emergencies

**Weapons:**
- Batons (standard issue)
- Crossbows (for Éclaireurs)
- "Percussive devices" — improvised weapons that make noise
- Shields (with La Dalle logo)

**Defense strategy:**
La Défense was built like a fortress. The underground levels have limited access points. La Dalle's strategy is simple: hold the chokepoints, use noise discipline, never engage on unfavorable terrain.

## Economy

### Internal Currency

**Labor Credits:** The primary currency. Earned by working, spent on goods and services.

**Ration Cards:** Distributed weekly based on family size and performance. Cover basics: food, water, clothing.

**Shares:** The real wealth. Inherited or earned. Shareholders receive dividends (extra rations, better housing, voting rights).

### What They Produce

| Product | Description | Trade Value |
|---------|-------------|-------------|

| Processed water | Filtered, clean | High |
| Textiles | Mushroom fiber, decent quality | Medium |
| Metalwork | Repurposed car parts, skilled | Medium |

## Key Locations

### Le Siège (The Headquarters)

P-1, the original prepper stronghold. Now the administrative center.

- Board meeting room (the "Salle du Conseil")
- Archives (climate-controlled, sacred)
- The CEO's quarters
- The original stockpile (museum, now empty)

### La Grande Ferme (The Great Farm)

P-6 and P-7, converted to massive mushroom farms.

- Kilometers of growing beds
- Climate control through ventilation
- The "agronomes" (farmers) are surprisingly respected

### Le Centre Commercial (The Shopping Center)

P-3, where commerce happens.

- Stalls selling goods
- The "Marché du Mardi" (Tuesday market)
- Cafeterias and eating halls

### L'École de la Dalle (The School)

P-4, education from age 5 to 18.

- Curriculum: literacy, mathematics, history (corporate focus), trade skills
- Graduation = departmental assignment
- The "valedictorian" gets automatic P-3 placement

## Notable Figures

### Antoine Vauclair IV (Current PDG)

Great-great-grandson of Bertrand Vauclair. Elected PDG at age 32, now 45.

**Personality:** Competent but arrogant. Genuinely believes La Dalle is the greatest society ever built. Has never left the complex.

**Known for:**
- Expanding trade with L'Est Profond
- Survived two assassination attempts (blamed on Le Grand Nord)
- The "Vauclair Efficiency Initiative" (popular with management, hated by workers)

### Marianne Leclerc (Directrice des Ressources)

60 years old. Runs the food supply. The most powerful person who isn't the PDG.

**Personality:** Practical, apolitical, terrifying. Everyone knows that if Marianne is unhappy, rations get... adjusted.

**Known for:**
- The "Leclerc Rationing Protocol" (harsh but effective)
- Personally inspects farms weekly
- Has never smiled in a meeting

### Jean-Pierre "JP" Morel (Directeur de la Sécurité)

45 years old. Former Éclaireur. Rose through ranks.

**Personality:** Gruff, loyal, secretly romantic. Writes poetry about the surface he's never seen.

**Known for:**
- Led the defense during the Year 267 border incident
- The only Board member who regularly eats in the P-5 cafeteria
- Rumored relationship with a woman from Les Canaux

## Absurdist Details

### Things That Make No Sense But Are Very Serious

1. **The parking ticket machines dispense ration cards.** They've never been reprogrammed. You insert a labor credit token, press "2 hours," and get a ration card.

2. **Quarterly earnings reports** are published on carved stone tablets. They track mushroom yields, population growth, "synergy metrics," and "stakeholder satisfaction."

3. **The break room coffee machine** is an altar. The original machine broke in Year 43. A replica was carved from stone. People still "get coffee" there — it's a meditation ritual.

4. **Business casual violations are crimes.** In Year 189, a man was demoted to P-7 for wearing shorts to a meeting.

5. **They say "let's circle back" unironically.** It means "we'll discuss this later." "Per my last message" is a formal rebuke.

6. **The elevator music still plays.** They've recreated it. Nobody remembers what it was. It's haunting.

7. **Team-building exercises include trust falls.** Into mushroom-fiber nets. People have died. It's still mandatory.

## Relations with Other Factions

### Le Grand Nord (Hostile)

Historical rivals. Both claim to be the "working" faction. La Dalle thinks Le Grand Nord are lazy unionists. Le Grand Nord thinks La Dalle are corporate parasites.

**Key tensions:**
- Territorial disputes at boundaries
- Trade embargoes (current: lifted in Year 289, reinstated Year 294)
- The "Strike of Year 156" — Le Grand Nord refused to trade; La Dalle starved for months

### Le Palais Profond (Diplomatic)

Two elites recognizing each other. La Dalle finds Le Palais Profond's bureaucracy absurd (which is saying something). Le Palais Profond finds La Dalle's corporate structure "charmingly provincial."

**Current status:** Formal alliance since Year 201. Ambassadors exchange quarterly.

### Le Carrefour (Commercial)

Le Carrefour is neutral, and La Dalle respects neutrality (it's good for business). Major trade partner.

**Key interest:** La Dalle wants economic dominance of Le Carrefour markets. They've tried. They've been rebuffed. They keep trying.

### Les Canaux (Pragmatic)

Les Canaux are useful. La Dalle doesn't respect them, but pays for their services.

**Key interest:** Passage rights, intelligence, smuggled luxury goods (real coffee was smuggled in Year 278; still talked about).

## Quest Hooks

### The Hostile Takeover

**Hook:** A faction within La Dalle's Board wants to "merge" with Le Carrefour — by force.

**Reality:** They're preparing a corporate coup to seize Le Carrefour's markets.

**Implications:** Could restart the Great War. The Hero might need to stop it — or profit from it.

### The Lost Spreadsheet

**Hook:** The original survival spreadsheet — Bertrand Vauclair's "Tableau Fondateur" — has been stolen.

**Reality:** It's just a spreadsheet. But to La Dalle, it's their constitution.

**Implications:** Whoever has it has leverage. Who took it? Le Grand Nord? Les Canaux? An internal rival?

### The P-8 Myth

**Hook:** Rumors persist of a P-8 — a level below P-7 that the Board has sealed.

**Reality:** There IS a P-8. It was sealed in Year 23 after "an incident." What's down there?

**Implications:** Could contain pre-Silence technology, forgotten supplies, or something worse.
