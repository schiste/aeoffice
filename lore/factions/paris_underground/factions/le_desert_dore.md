# Le Désert Doré ("The Golden Desert")

> **Quick Reference:** The wasteland west of La Défense — where the wealthy died and their ghosts remain. Not a faction, but a place all factions fear.
>
> **Territory:** West — Neuilly-sur-Seine, parts of western suburbs
> **Population:** ~200-500 (scavengers, hermits, the lost)
> **Government:** None
> **Founded:** N/A (never organized)
> **Year 300 Status:** Abandoned wasteland, resource-rich, extremely dangerous

---

## What It Is

Le Désert Doré is not a faction. It's a warning.

The wealthy western suburbs of Paris — Neuilly-sur-Seine, parts of the 16th and 17th arrondissements, areas around the Bois de Boulogne — were surface neighborhoods. No metro depth. No catacomb access. Minimal underground infrastructure.

When The Silence hit, almost everyone there died.

300 years later, it's a haunted no-man's-land between La Dalle Éternelle and the rest of Paris. Scavengers pick through the bones of the old world. Nobody lives there. Almost nobody.

---

## The Dying (Year 1)

### Why It Failed

**Geography:**
- Neuilly-sur-Seine: Wealthy residential, minimal underground
- 16th Arrondissement: Embassies, mansions, no metro depth
- Bois de Boulogne: Park — completely exposed
- Western suburbs: Spread out, car-dependent, no shelter

**Day 1:**
At 5:34 AM, the residents of Neuilly were:
- Asleep in their expensive apartments
- Driving to early appointments
- Walking dogs in the Bois de Boulogne
- Far from any underground shelter

**The Math:**
- Population of Neuilly (2034): ~62,000
- Population underground at 5:34 AM: ~500 (metro workers, basement dwellers)
- Population dead by Day 3: ~61,000

### The Exodus

Those who survived Day 1 had two choices:
1. Run east toward La Défense (some made it)
2. Run toward central Paris (most didn't)

**The Problem:**
- La Défense survivors sealed their territory quickly
- The journey east took hours through silent streets
- Most died in transit

**Year 1 survivors:** ~200-500 scattered through basements, parking garages, and the few metro stations.

**Year 3 survivors:** ~100 (most had fled to La Défense or central Paris)

### Why It Stayed Empty

By Year 3, the surviving factions had organized. They could have expanded into Neuilly. They didn't.

**Reasons:**
1. **No infrastructure:** Above-ground neighborhoods need constant noise. Impractical.
2. **Distance:** Too far from existing safe zones.
3. **Trauma:** Everyone knew people who died there. It felt cursed.
4. **La Défense:** The closest faction (La Dalle) had no interest — they had what they needed.

So Neuilly became Le Désert Doré — the Golden Desert.

## Year 300: The Wasteland

### What's There

**Above ground (lethal):**
- Silent streets
- Decaying buildings
- Overgrown vegetation (300 years of growth)
- The bones

**Underground (survivable, barely):**
- Scattered basements
- Parking garages (some with noise-making potential)
- Metro Line 1 western terminus (Pont de Neuilly station)
- Service tunnels

### Who's There

**Scavengers:**
~200-300 people who venture into Le Désert Doré temporarily.

They come from:
- La Dalle (authorized expeditions)
- Les Canaux (smuggling routes)
- Independents (treasure hunters, desperate)

They don't live there. They loot and leave.

**Hermits:**
~50-100 people who actually live in Le Désert Doré.

Types:
- **The Exiled:** Cast out from other factions, nowhere else to go
- **The Mad:** Those who broke and found peace in emptiness
- **The Seekers:** Treasure hunters who went too far
- **The Ghosts:** Nobody knows where they came from

**Les Gardiens du Silence:**
A small cult (~30-50 people) who worship The Silence.

They believe:
- The Silence is purification
- The dead of Neuilly achieved enlightenment
- Living in Le Désert Doré is spiritual discipline
- They "guard" the dead's eternal rest

They're non-violent but deeply unsettling.

## Why It Matters

### Resources

Le Désert Doré is a treasure trove.

**What's there:**
| Resource | Status | Value |
|----------|--------|-------|
| Pre-Silence technology | Abundant | Extremely high |
| Luxury goods | Rotted but some intact | High |
| Metal | Unlimited (cars, buildings) | Medium |
| Clothing (preserved) | Some | Medium |
| Food | None | N/A |
| Books/Documents | Weathered but some readable | High |

**The Mansions:**
Wealthy families had collections. Art, jewelry, technology. Most has been picked over. Some remains.

**The Embassies:**
The 16th had embassies. Diplomatic residences with vaults. Most have been opened. Some haven't.

**The Bois de Boulogne:**
The park is completely overgrown. Also completely silent. Nobody goes there. Something might be worth finding. Nobody knows.

### Strategic Position

Le Désert Doré sits between:
- La Dalle Éternelle (east)
- The Seine (south)
- The outer suburbs (west, north)

**Control implications:**
If anyone could hold it, they could:
- Control western approaches to La Défense
- Access resources indefinitely
- Expand territory massively

**Why nobody tries:**
Maintaining noise over that area is impossible with current technology. You'd need either a Crystal or an army of drummers.

## The Dangers

### The Silence

The primary danger is simply being above ground.

**Above-ground rules:**
- Continuous noise generation required
- Solo travelers die
- Groups must maintain sound discipline
- Night is worse (colder, quieter)

**Typical expedition:**
- Minimum 4 people
- Continuous drumming rotation
- Maximum 12 hours above ground
- Emergency retreat plan

### The Wildlife

300 years of nature reclaiming.

**What's there:**
- Feral dogs (packs, dangerous)
- Rats (everywhere)
- Birds (lots — indifferent to Silence)
- Wild boars (from Bois de Boulogne)
- Unknown things (rumors only)

**The boar problem:**
Wild boars have colonized the Bois. They're aggressive, large, and smart. Expeditions avoid the park.

### The Architecture

300 years of decay.

**Dangers:**
- Collapsed buildings
- Unstable floors
- Falling debris
- Glass (broken windows everywhere)
- Flooding (basements)

**Year 245: The Neuilly Collapse**
An expedition of 12 entered a mansion. The floor collapsed. 8 died in the basement before the silence could. After that, La Dalle restricted expeditions.

### The Cult

Les Gardiens du Silence don't attack expeditions. But they follow them. Watch them. Sometimes appear in doorways, just standing there.

**What they want:**
- For you to leave
- For the dead to be respected
- To convert you (gentle proselytizing)

**What they do:**
- Shadow expeditions
- Leave messages ("THE SILENCE IS PEACE")
- Occasionally help injured scavengers (to deliver their message)
- Perform rituals over the dead

They're not dangerous. They're just deeply weird.

### The Hermits

Some hermits are harmless. Some are not.

**Types:**
- Peaceful: Leave them alone, they leave you alone
- Territorial: Will fight for their basement
- Predatory: Hunt scavengers for supplies

Most are avoidable. Some aren't.

## Expeditions

### La Dalle Authorized Expeditions

La Dalle runs official salvage missions.

**Rules:**
- Teams of 6-12
- Professional drummers included
- 8-hour time limit
- Specific targets only
- All salvage belongs to La Dalle

**Success rate:** ~80% return safely

**Frequency:** Monthly

### Independent Expeditions

Unauthorized salvage. Illegal according to La Dalle (they claim Le Désert Doré). Happens anyway.

**Who does it:**
- Les Canaux guides (paid handsomely)
- Desperate individuals
- Treasure hunters
- Criminals fleeing other factions

**Success rate:** ~60% return

### Les Canaux Routes

Les Canaux know sewer paths through Le Désert Doré.

**Services offered:**
- Guide through underground sections
- Surface crossings at "safe" points
- Extraction if things go wrong

**Price:** Extremely high

### Pont de Neuilly Station

Metro Line 1 western terminus. The only "safe" base in Le Désert Doré.

**Status:**
- Partially maintained by La Dalle
- Used as staging point for expeditions
- Small garrison (rotates monthly)
- Connected to La Dalle by tunnel

### The Ambassador's Row

Former embassy district. Diplomatic residences.

**Status:**
- Mostly looted
- Some vaults remain sealed
- The American Embassy vault is legendary (never opened)

### The Bois de Boulogne

Former park. Now wilderness.

**Status:**
- Completely overgrown
- Wild boar territory
- Nobody enters
- Rumors of pre-Silence structures in the center

### The Rothschild Mansion

Former estate of banking family.

**Status:**

### The Neuilly Cemetery

Pre-Silence cemetery.

**Status:**

- They maintain it
- Non-violent but will protect

## Les Gardiens du Silence

The cult that calls Le Désert Doré home.

### Beliefs

**Core doctrine:**
- The Silence is divine
- The virus was cosmic correction
- The dead of Neuilly were first to achieve peace
- Living in silence is spiritual practice

**Practices:**
- Extended silence exposure (they've developed tolerance)
- Meditation among the bones
- Care of the dead (they bury unburied remains)
- Minimal noise (they drum only when necessary)

### Organization

- ~30-50 members
- No formal hierarchy
- Decisions by consensus
- New members welcome (rare, converts usually die)

### Interactions

**With expeditions:**
- Watch from distance
- Occasional approach to preach
- Will provide information (for converts)
- Will rescue injured (to preach to)

**With other factions:**
- No official relations
- Occasionally trade at Le Carrefour
- Considered harmless crazy people

### The Truth About Their Survival

**How they survive silence:**
They don't. They're not immune. They've developed rotation systems:
- Small bells worn constantly
- Humming as meditation
- Wind chimes throughout their territory
- The "Prayer of Breath" — continuous vocalization

They live at the edge of silence, not in it.

## Rumors and Legends

### The Vault

**Rumor:** Somewhere in Neuilly is a vault that was never opened. It contains pre-Silence technology that still works.

**Evidence:** None confirmed. But La Dalle keeps sending expeditions.

### The Silent Ones

**Rumor:** There are people living in Le Désert Doré who don't need sound. They've evolved or changed somehow.

**Evidence:** Sightings by unreliable witnesses. Probably hermits. Probably.

### The Bois Beast

**Rumor:** Something lives in the center of Bois de Boulogne. Larger than boars. Not natural.

**Evidence:** None. The boars are real and dangerous enough.

### The Underground Mansion

**Rumor:** One wealthy family built an entire underground complex before The Silence. Self-sufficient. Still inhabited.

**Evidence:** Old documents reference "Project Archimedes." Location unknown.

### The Embassy Codes

**Rumor:** The American Embassy vault requires a code. The code is on a body somewhere in the city.

**Evidence:** A cryptic note found in Year 156. May be fake.

## Quest Hooks

### The Rothschild Descent

**Hook:** Someone claims to have found a route into the Rothschild basement. They need a team.

**Reality:** The route exists. What's down there is unknown.

**Implications:** Could be legendary treasure. Could be a trap. Could be empty.

### The Embassy Key

**Hook:** A dying hermit has information about the American Embassy vault code.

**Reality:** He knows something. He wants something in return.

**Implications:** Faction competition. The vault may contain pre-Silence weapons.

### The Gardiens Request

**Hook:** Les Gardiens du Silence want help. Someone is desecrating their cemetery.

**Reality:** Raiders from outside Paris. First outside contact in decades.

**Implications:** Where did they come from? What else is out there?

### The Bois Expedition

**Hook:** La Dalle wants to know what's in the center of Bois de Boulogne. Major reward offered.

**Reality:** Nobody has returned from the center.

**Implications:** Discovery could be valuable. Survival is uncertain.

### The Lost Expedition

**Hook:** A La Dalle expedition didn't return. Important people aboard. Retrieval mission.

**Reality:** They found something and can't leave. Or they're dead.

**Implications:** Rescue or recovery. What did they find?
