# La Gare du Sud ("The Southern Station")

> **Quick Reference:** The traders and travelers of Gare de Lyon — gateway to the south, master negotiators, and the only faction with regular outside contact.
>
> **Territory:** South-East — Gare de Lyon complex, surrounding metro stations
> **Population:** ~10,000-12,000
> **Government:** Le Conseil des Quais (Council of Platforms)
> **Founded:** Year 9 AS (from station survivors and trade coalitions)
> **Year 300 Status:** Wealthy from trade, cosmopolitan, secretly terrified of the outside

---

## Origin Story

### Before The Silence: The Gateway

Gare de Lyon was Paris's gateway to the south. High-speed trains to Lyon, Marseille, the Riviera, Italy, Switzerland. Busy, international, commercial.

**The Station (2034):**
- Major terminus for TGV Sud-Est
- Metro lines 1 and 14
- RER lines A and D
- The famous Le Train Bleu restaurant
- Constant flow of travelers

**At 5:34 AM, October 14:**
- Early morning travelers
- Night train arrivals from the south
- Railway workers (SNCF)
- Station businesses preparing to open
- The usual homeless population

**Estimated underground:** ~3,000-5,000 people

### Day 1-Year 3: Survival and Split

Gare de Lyon survivors had a choice: join the northern railway workers or stay independent.

They stayed independent.

**Why:**
- Geography: Gare de Lyon is south of the Seine, separated from Gare du Nord
- Identity: Different travelers, different culture
- Opportunity: They could see the tracks heading south

**The Big Question (Year 2):**

Someone asked: "The trains went to Lyon, Marseille, everywhere. Are there survivors there?"

Nobody knew. The tracks led into silence.

### Year 3: The First Expedition

A group of 12 survivors set out along the tracks toward Lyon.

**Conditions:**
- Constant drumming required
- Limited supplies
- No map of safe points
- No way to communicate back

**Result:**
- 9 died
- 3 reached a settlement 200km south (what would become a Burgundy cave community)
- They came back with news: others survived

This changed everything.

### Year 5-50: The Southern Network

Gare de Lyon survivors systematically established contact along the old rail lines.

**What they built:**
- Rest stations (points where noise could be maintained)
- Trade routes
- A network of contacts stretching hundreds of kilometers

**What they learned:**
- Other survivor communities existed
- They were isolated, desperate
- They would pay for contact

Gare de Lyon became a trading hub connecting Paris to the south.

## Year 300: The Gateway

### What They Are

La Gare du Sud is Paris's window to the outside world. They're the only faction with regular contact beyond the city. This gives them:
- Wealth (trade monopoly on southern goods)
- Knowledge (news from elsewhere)
- Fear (they know what's out there)

### The Southern Network (Year 300)

| Distance | Settlement | Connection |
|----------|------------|------------|
| 50km | Fontainebleau caves | Strong |
| 120km | Burgundy tunnels | Strong |
| 200km | Lyon underground | Medium |
| 350km | Rhône valley settlements | Weak |
| 400km+ | Mediterranean contacts | Rare |

**Trade routes:**
- 3 established "safe paths" to Burgundy
- Quarterly caravans to Lyon
- Annual expeditions further south

**The Math:**
Each expedition requires:
- 20-30 people minimum (drumming rotation)
- 2-4 weeks travel time
- Substantial supplies
- High mortality (~10-15% per major expedition)

### Governance: Le Conseil des Quais

**Structure:**
Gare de Lyon has multiple platform areas. Each is represented.

| Platform/Area | Representative | Specialty |
|---------------|----------------|-----------|
| **Quai A** | Trade affairs | Commerce, external relations |
| **Quai B** | Security | Defense, expeditions |
| **Quai C** | Internal affairs | Domestic governance |
| **Le Train Bleu** | Culture, hospitality | Tourism, visitors |
| **Les Souterrains** | Infrastructure | Maintenance, expansion |
| **La Voie** | The Routes | Expedition planning, network |

**Le Président du Conseil:**
Rotating position among representatives. Changes yearly.

**Decision-making:**
Majority vote on most issues. Trade agreements require unanimous consent.

### Social Structure

Wealth-based hierarchy, trade-oriented.

| Class | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| **Les Voyageurs** | Expedition veterans, highest status |
| **Les Marchands** | Successful traders |
| **Les Quaîtres** | Platform/station managers |
| **Les Travailleurs** | General workers |
| **Les Arrivants** | Recent arrivals from outside |

**Mobility:** Based on wealth and expedition service.

## Daily Life

### Trade Culture

Everything revolves around trade.

**The Market:**
Gare de Lyon has the best market in Paris for outside goods.

**What they sell:**
| Good | Source | Price |
|------|--------|-------|
| Southern wine | Burgundy/Rhône | Very high |
| Preserved fruits | Mediterranean | Extremely high |
| Salt | Southern mines | Medium |
| Textiles (different styles) | Various | Medium |
| News/Information | Everywhere | Varies |
| Spices | Mediterranean | Extremely high |

**What they buy:**
- Paris-produced goods (to sell south)
- Mushrooms (the south has less)
- Metal goods (La Dalle quality)
- Culture (L'Est musicians, Montparno art)

### Le Train Bleu

The famous restaurant is now a cultural institution.

**Pre-Silence:** Ornate Belle Époque restaurant in the station.

**Year 300:**
- Preserved decor (carefully maintained)
- High-end dining (for traders, dignitaries)
- Diplomatic meetings
- The best food in Paris (they have spices)

**Menu includes:**
- Mushroom dishes with southern herbs
- Preserved fruit desserts
- Actual wine (the real thing, from the south)
- "Les Plats du Voyage" — meals recreating pre-Silence cuisine

### The Expeditions

**Joining an expedition:**
- Physical fitness required
- Drumming proficiency required
- Training (4 weeks minimum)
- Contract signed (estate provisions if you die)

**What expeditions do:**
- Trade caravans (most common)
- Exploration (new routes)
- Rescue (retrieve lost caravans)
- Diplomacy (rare, high-level)

**Expedition roles:**
| Role | Function |
|------|----------|
| **Chef d'Expédition** | Leader |
| **Batteurs** | Drummers (most important) |
| **Porteurs** | Cargo carriers |
| **Éclaireurs** | Scouts |
| **Marchands** | Trade negotiators |
| **Gardes** | Security |

**Success rates:**
- Burgundy route: 95% return
- Lyon route: 85% return
- Further south: 70-80% return

### Outside News

La Gare du Sud is Paris's connection to the outside world.

**What they know (Year 300):**

| Region | Status |
|--------|--------|
| Loire Valley | Survivor settlements exist (including the player's area) |
| Burgundy | Moderate population, cave-based |
| Lyon | Large underground community, comparable to Paris |
| Marseille | Rumored to exist, no confirmed contact in 50 years |
| Alpine regions | Isolated communities, minimal contact |
| Italy | Unknown, trade contacts lost Year 234 |
| Spain | Complete unknown |
| Northern France | Some contact via Le Grand Nord routes |
| Britain | Unknown |

**What they won't say:**
They're terrified. The outside world is getting quieter. Contact with Lyon has become harder. The Mediterranean route died. They wonder if Paris is the last large community.

## Culture & Customs

### Cosmopolitanism

La Gare du Sud has absorbed influences from everywhere.

**Languages:**
- French (base)
- Southern French dialects (from contacts)
- Trade pidgin (for negotiations)
- Some Italian, Occitan influences

**Customs:**
- Southern hospitality (guests are sacred)
- Mediterranean food culture (preserved)
- French formality (station tradition)
- Merchant pragmatism (everything is negotiable)

### Le Grand Départ

Coming-of-age ritual. Every citizen makes at least one expedition before age 25.

**Format:**
- Training period (6 weeks)
- First expedition (usually to Fontainebleau — "safe" route)
- Ceremonial return
- Status as "Voyageur" earned

**Those who refuse:**
- Socially marked
- Cannot hold Council positions
- Called "Les Quais-Bloqués" (Platform-Stuck)
- Rare, but it happens

### The Memorial Wall

A wall in the station listing every expedition member who never returned.

**Current count:** 4,847 names (Year 300)

**Ceremony:** Monthly reading of recent additions

### Hospitality

Guests are sacred. This is business.

**Guest rights:**
- Accommodation provided
- Food and water
- Safety guaranteed
- Trade negotiations facilitated

**Guest obligations:**
- Payment expected (eventually)
- Information shared (news is currency)
- Respect for local customs
- No faction politics in neutral spaces

### Wine

Real wine exists. From the south.

**Status:**
- Extremely expensive
- Served at Le Train Bleu
- Gift for diplomats
- Currency for major deals

**Types available:**
- Burgundy reds (most common)
- Rhône varieties (medium)
- Mediterranean (rare, expensive)
- Pre-Silence bottles (priceless, ceremonial only)

## Military & Defense

### Strategic Position

Gare de Lyon has defensible geography:
- Limited access points
- Well-known territory
- Distance from major rivals
- Les Ossuaires as southern buffer

### Forces

**Les Gardes de la Voie (Route Guards):** ~600 personnel

- Station guards (interior)
- Expedition security
- Border patrol
- Emergency response

**Expedition combat training:**
All expedition members can fight. They have to.

### Weapons

Standard Paris arsenal plus:
- More crossbows (expedition experience)
- Some southern weapons (different styles)
- Better armor (southern metalwork)

### Defense History

**Year 156: The Southern Raid**

A group from outside Paris — origin unknown — followed a returning expedition and attacked Gare de Lyon.

**Result:**
- 3-day siege
- 124 defenders dead
- Raiders defeated
- Increased security ever since

**Lesson learned:** The outside world can follow them back.

## Economy

### The Trade Monopoly

La Gare du Sud controls southern trade. This makes them wealthy.

**Import value:**
| Good | Margin |
|------|--------|
| Wine | 500%+ |
| Southern spices | 300%+ |
| Mediterranean goods | 200%+ |
| Information | Priceless |

**Export value:**
They sell Paris goods (mushrooms, metalwork, culture) at similar markups in the south.

### Currency

They use all currencies plus their own: **Jetons de Voyage** (Travel Tokens)

- Based on expedition shares
- Widely accepted in Paris
- Only currency accepted in southern settlements

### Wealth Distribution

La Gare du Sud is the most unequal faction:
- Wealthy trader families
- Middle-class workers
- Poor recent arrivals

**The "Marchand Dynasties":**
~20 families control most trade routes. Inherited wealth. Old money (by post-Silence standards).

## Notable Figures

### Présidente (Current) Marguerite Vaillant

58 years old. Current Council President. Quai A representative.

**Background:** Third-generation merchant family. Made her fortune on the Lyon route. Survived 12 expeditions.

**Personality:** Sharp, calculating, paranoid about the outside world. Knows more than she says.

**Known for:**
- Negotiating the "Year 289 Lyon Accords" (major trade deal)
- Never drinking wine in public (superstition)
- The saying: "The south sends goods. The south sends news. The south sends nothing else anymore."

### "Le Vieux Voyageur" (The Old Traveler)

91 years old. Oldest living expedition veteran.

**Background:** Made 43 expeditions. Reached the Mediterranean twice (Year 234 was the last successful trip). Living legend.

**Personality:** Nostalgic, sad, wise. Knows the south is dying.

**Known for:**
- The only person who's seen Marseille (claims it existed in Year 210)
- Training three generations of expedition leaders
- Refusing to discuss what he saw in Year 234

### Captain Elise Moreau (Expedition Chief)

42 years old. Head of all expedition operations.

**Background:** Rose from porter to chief through merit. Known for never losing a caravan.

**Personality:** Professional, protective, driven. Considers every lost expedition member a personal failure.

**Known for:**
- The "Moreau Protocol" (standardized expedition safety procedures)
- 23 successful expeditions
- Personally rescuing the Year 291 lost caravan

### Chef Laurent (Le Train Bleu)

55 years old. Head chef. Cultural institution.

**Background:** Preserves pre-Silence recipes using available ingredients. Artist of food.

**Personality:** Perfectionist, temperamental, secretly very kind.

**Known for:**
- "The Feast of Year 275" (recreated 12-course pre-Silence menu)
- Having tasted real olive oil (once, Year 267)
- Crying when discussing Mediterranean cuisine

## Absurdist Details

### Things Only La Gare du Sud Does

1. **Train schedules still posted.** Destinations: Lyon, Marseille, Geneva, Milan. All marked "Delayed — 300 Years." The schedule is maintained meticulously.

2. **Wine hoarding is serious crime.** Finding private wine stocks triggers investigations. Wine is shared (for a price) or else.

3. **Expedition contracts specify where to bury you.** Before departure, you choose: home station, route where you fell, or leave remains for the travelers. This is normal paperwork.

4. **The clock must never stop.** The famous Gare de Lyon clock is maintained by a dedicated team. If it stops, expeditions are cancelled until it's fixed. Superstition? Tradition? Both.

5. **Southern accents are prestigious.** Expedition veterans adopt southern speech patterns. Fake southern accents are mocked brutally.

6. **Le Train Bleu dress code.** The restaurant enforces pre-Silence formal dress. They have a collection of preserved suits and dresses for guests who arrive "improperly attired."

7. **"News from the south" is its own currency.** Information about what's happening in Lyon or beyond is traded, bought, and sold like material goods.

8. **Nobody talks about Year 234.** The last successful Mediterranean expedition. What they found is classified. The Vieux Voyageur won't say.

## The Outside World

### What They Know

**Loire Valley (Hero's region):**
- Settler communities exist
- ~200km from Paris
- Occasional contact through intermediaries
- Potential trade route (not established)

**Burgundy:**
- Stable communities
- Cave systems inhabited
- Regular trade
- Wine source

**Lyon:**
- Large underground community
- Second largest known settlement
- Trade partnership
- Contact becoming harder (they won't say why)

**Mediterranean:**
- Last contact Year 234
- Marseille may exist
- Routes considered dead
- What happened is not discussed

### What They Fear

They won't say it publicly, but:
- Outside contact is decreasing
- Lyon expeditions are harder each year
- Something is changing in the south
- They may be the last major community

## Relations with Other Factions

### Le Carrefour (Commercial Partners)

Southern goods flow through Le Carrefour. La Gare du Sud has a Tribunal seat.

**Status:** Close commercial ties. Competition for market control.

### Les Ossuaires (Southern Neighbors)

Territory borders. Neutral to positive.

**Status:** Trade, mutual respect. Les Ossuaires provide passage through catacombs.

### La Dalle Éternelle (Rivals)

Both factions are wealthy. Both want more.

**Status:** Competition for market share. Tension over luxury goods.

### Le Grand Nord (Neutral)

Different territories. Limited interaction.

**Status:** Occasional trade. No strong relationship.

### L'Est Profond (Cultural Exchange)

L'Est musicians sometimes join expeditions. Cultural trade.

**Status:** Positive. Growing ties.

### Les Montparnos (Philosophical Distance)

Les Montparnos theorize about the outside world. La Gare du Sud has actually been there.

**Status:** Polite mutual incomprehension.

### Les Canaux (Essential)

Les Canaux know routes everywhere. Expeditions sometimes use sewer paths.

**Status:** Close working relationship. High payment for services.

## Quest Hooks

### The Lyon Problem

**Hook:** The last Lyon expedition returned with survivors from Lyon. They're scared. They won't talk about what's happening.

**Reality:** Something is wrong in Lyon. Population declining. Unknown cause.

**Implications:** If Lyon falls, the southern network collapses. Investigation needed.

### The Mediterranean Quest

**Hook:** Someone claims to have a route to Marseille. They need backing.

**Reality:** The route may be real. Or it may be suicide.

**Implications:** If Marseille exists, it changes everything. If it doesn't, people will die finding out.

### The Wine Heist

**Hook:** A shipment of Burgundy's finest was stolen en route. Inside job suspected.

**Reality:** Major merchant families are fighting. Trade war brewing.

**Implications:** Could destabilize La Gare du Sud internally.

### The Year 234 Truth

**Hook:** The Vieux Voyageur is dying. He wants to tell someone what happened in Year 234.

**Reality:** Unknown. But it scared him for 66 years.

**Implications:** Knowledge that changes understanding of the outside world.

### The Loire Connection

**Hook:** La Gare du Sud wants to establish a route to the Loire Valley. They've heard about settlements there.

**Reality:** The Hero's region. First contact opportunity.

**Implications:** Could connect the Hero's community to Paris trade network.
