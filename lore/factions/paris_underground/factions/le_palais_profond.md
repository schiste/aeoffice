# Le Palais Profond ("The Deep Palace")

> **Quick Reference:** The French Republic that never ended — government survivors who stayed sealed for 3 years debating door procedures.
>
> **Territory:** Centre-Nord — Élysée → Concorde → Grand Palais underground
> **Population:** ~5,000-7,000
> **Government:** Constitutional Republic (43rd President)
> **Founded:** Day 1 (Protocol Jupiter activation)
> **Year 300 Status:** Small, prestigious, bureaucratically insane

---

## Origin Story

### Day 1: Protocol Jupiter

At 5:34 AM on October 14, 2034, The Silence hit Paris. At 5:36 AM, a Secret Service agent activated **Protocol Jupiter** — the Élysée Palace's emergency evacuation plan.

Within 12 minutes, the following were evacuated to the presidential bunker:
- **President Jean-Marc Delacourt** (58, in pajamas)
- **First Lady Isabelle Delacourt** (54, also in pajamas)
- **8 Cabinet Ministers** (whoever was in Paris)
- **47 Secret Service agents**
- **23 Élysée staff** (night shift)
- **47 confused tourists** who happened to be on the 6 AM "early bird" palace tour

Total: **126 people**, crammed into a bunker designed for 80.

The bunker was connected to underground service tunnels beneath Place de la Concorde and the Grand Palais — a network built to move VIPs discreetly. They had supplies for 30 days.

They made them last **3 years**.

### The Door Problem (Years 1-3)

Le Palais Profond remained sealed for 3 years. Not because they couldn't open the door. Because they couldn't agree on the **procedure**.

**Timeline of the Door Crisis:**

| Date | Event |
|------|-------|
| **Day 1** | Protocol Jupiter activated. Door sealed. |
| **Day 3** | Scouts report chaos outside. President orders "assessment period." |
| **Week 2** | Assessment Committee formed (7 members). |
| **Month 1** | Committee recommends "further study before action." |
| **Month 3** | President Delacourt demands action. Committee requests Environmental Impact Assessment. |
| **Month 4** | EIA subcommittee formed (5 members). |
| **Month 8** | EIA completed: "Opening door may have environmental consequences." |
| **Month 10** | President Delacourt dies of heart attack (stress, age, frustration). |
| **Month 11** | Emergency election held underground. **Prime Minister René Beaumont** becomes President. |
| **Year 1, Day 1** | Beaumont demands door opened. Legal Affairs notes constitutional ambiguity. |
| **Year 1, Month 2** | Constitutional Committee formed to determine door-opening authority. |
| **Year 1, Month 6** | Committee rules: requires 2/3 cabinet vote AND presidential decree. |
| **Year 1, Month 7** | Cabinet votes 5-3 in favor. Two ministers are sick. Vote invalid (quorum). |
| **Year 1, Month 9** | Revote: 6-2 in favor. Legal Affairs notes procedural error in notification. |
| **Year 2, Month 1** | Third vote: 7-1 in favor. President signs decree. |
| **Year 2, Month 2** | Security Assessment Committee activated to determine safe opening procedure. |
| **Year 2, Month 6** | SAC recommends "phased opening" — crack door 2cm, observe for 30 days. |
| **Year 2, Month 8** | Door cracked 2cm. Nothing happens. 30-day observation begins. |
| **Year 2, Month 9** | Observation extended to 60 days (request from Health Ministry). |
| **Year 3, Month 1** | Full opening approved. Implementation Committee formed. |
| **Year 3, Month 2** | Door fully opened. Le Palais Profond emerges into a Paris that organized without them. |

### What They Found (Year 3)

When the door finally opened, they discovered:
- Paris had factions, territories, and treaties
- Nobody recognized their authority
- The world had moved on
- 47 tourists wanted to go home (home no longer existed)

President Beaumont declared: "The Republic endures. We shall adapt."

They've been "adapting" ever since.

## Year 300: The Republic Eternal

### Governance: Constitutional Continuity

Le Palais Profond maintains that they ARE the French Republic. The Fifth Republic. The one de Gaulle founded. Continuous from 1958 to 2334.

**The Logic:**
1. President Delacourt was the legitimate President
2. Constitutional succession occurred underground
3. Elections have been held every 7 years since
4. Therefore, the Republic never ended

**The Reality:**
The "elections" have... evolved.

### The Adoption Protocol

Elections are held every 7 years, as the Constitution requires. But there's a catch.

**Who can run for President?**
- Only the eldest child of the current President

**But what if the President has no children?**
- They must adopt one

**This has created:**
- A "dynasty by bureaucratic necessity"
- The current President, **Édouard XVII**, was adopted at age 45 by his predecessor
- He has no biological children
- He's currently "interviewing candidates" for adoption
- This is considered normal

### The Line of Succession (Selected)

| 2 | René Beaumont | Year 1 - Year 8 | Prime Minister succession |
| 5 | Marie Delacourt-Beaumont | Year 22 - Year 36 | Daughter of Beaumont's adopted son |

### The Full Title

The President's full ceremonial title takes approximately 4 minutes to recite:

> "Édouard XVII, Président de la République Française, Gardien de la Constitution du 4 Octobre 1958, Protecteur du Palais Profond, Héritier du Fondateur-Martyr Jean-Marc Delacourt Premier de ce Nom, Détenteur des Clés du Grand Bunker, Maître des Galeries de Concorde, Grand Officier de la Légion d'Honneur de l'Ère Souterraine, Commandeur Suprême des Forces Armées de la République en Exil, Garant de l'Héritage Républicain, Défenseur du Peuple Français Résiduel, Président de Tous les Français Survivants Reconnus et Non-Reconnus, Père Adoptif de la Nation..."

It continues for three more minutes.

## Government Structure

### The Ministries

Every ministry from the Fifth Republic still exists. Most are pointless.

| Ministry | Current Staff | What They Actually Do |
|----------|---------------|----------------------|
| **Affaires Étrangères** (Foreign Affairs) | 12 | Issue visas that nobody uses |
| **Intérieur** (Interior) | 45 | Actual governance, police, records |
| **Économie** (Economy) | 8 | Track internal trades, argue about currency |
| **Culture** | 15 | Preserve pre-Silence artifacts, education |
| **Défense** | 89 | Guards, border patrol, the only useful ministry |
| **Justice** | 23 | Courts, legal proceedings, document authentication |
| **Éducation** | 31 | Schools, training, history preservation |
| **Santé** (Health) | 18 | Medical care, fairly competent |
| **Agriculture** | 7 | Mushroom coordination |
| **Environnement** | 3 | Assess environmental impact of everything |
| **Sports et Jeunesse** | 2 | Organize annual games |
| **Outre-Mer** (Overseas Territories) | 1 | Claims jurisdiction over all of France (has never been there) |

### The National Assembly

100 elected representatives. Elections held every 5 years.

**How it works:**
- Universal suffrage (everyone over 18)
- Proportional representation by "arrondissement" (district)
- Actually fairly democratic
- Also completely powerless (the President decides everything)

**The Opposition:**
There IS an opposition party. It's called "Les Réformistes" (The Reformers). They've never won. They've never been allowed to win. But they exist, and that's constitutional.

### The Courts

The judiciary is independent. Theoretically.

**Courts:**
- **Tribunal de Première Instance:** Minor disputes
- **Cour d'Appel:** Appeals
- **Conseil Constitutionnel:** Constitutional review
- **Haute Cour de Justice:** Crimes against the Republic (treason, mostly)

**Notable case law:**
- *Touraine v. République (Year 47)*: Ruled that tourists are citizens
- *In re Door Committee (Year 4)*: Retroactively approved door-opening procedure
- *Delacourt-Beaumont v. Adoption Board (Year 23)*: Established adoption succession rules

## Daily Life

### The Bureaucratic Life

Everything requires paperwork.

**To get food:**
- File Form A-12 (Ration Request)
- Have it stamped by your Ministry supervisor
- Take it to Distribution Office
- Wait in line
- Receive rations
- Sign Form A-12-B (Ration Receipt)

**To move apartments:**
- File Form H-7 (Residence Transfer Request)
- Attach justification letter
- Submit to Housing Commission
- Wait 6-18 months
- Receive approval or denial
- If approved, file Form H-8 (Move Authorization)
- Execute move during approved window

**To get married:**
- File Form F-1 (Union Declaration)
- Both parties must submit Form F-2 (Consent Verification)
- Medical examination (Form F-3)
- Background check (Form F-4)
- Ministry approval (Form F-5)
- Public announcement (30-day waiting period)
- Ceremony (must include reading of Civil Code Article 212-215)

### The Diplomatic Pass

Other factions find Le Palais Profond bewildering, but they play along.

**To visit Le Palais Profond:**
- Request visa at border (Form V-1)
- State purpose of visit
- Provide "credentials" (any documentation works)
- Receive stamped visa
- Keep visa visible at all times
- Check in with local authorities daily
- Apply for extension if staying more than 7 days

**Nobody uses the visa.** But Le Palais Profond issues them anyway, and some factions accept them as curiosities.

### Holidays

| Date | Holiday | Observance |
|------|---------|------------|
| Jan 1 | Nouvel An | New Year; no work, ceremonial address |
| May 8 | Victoire 1945 | Still commemorated; they have no idea about WW3 |
| Jul 14 | Bastille Day | Major celebration, "fireworks" = drums |
| Oct 4 | Constitution Day | Constitution read aloud (takes hours) |
| Oct 14 | Jour du Fondateur-Martyr | Somber; candles lit for President Delacourt |
| Nov 11 | Armistice | WW1 commemoration still observed |
| Dec 25 | Noël | Religious/cultural; gift-giving (small, practical) |

### The 35-Hour Week

The 35-hour work week is constitutional law.

**No exceptions.**

When the Great War reached Le Palais Profond's borders in Year 79, the guards worked their 35 hours and went home. They scheduled the defense in shifts.

"The Constitution must be respected, even in war." — *Minister of Defense, Year 79*

## Culture & Customs

### Protocol and Etiquette

Le Palais Profond takes etiquette seriously.

**Greetings:**
- Formal: "Citoyen/Citoyenne [Name], au nom de la République" (Citizen [Name], in the name of the Republic)
- Informal: "Citoyen/Citoyenne" followed by a nod
- Between officials: Full title required

**Forms of address:**
- President: "Monsieur le Président" (always)
- Ministers: "Monsieur/Madame le/la Ministre"
- Citizens: "Citoyen/Citoyenne"
- Foreigners: "L'invité/L'invitée" (The guest)

### The Touraine Question

47 tourists were trapped in the bunker on Day 1.

By Year 10, they were citizens. By Year 50, their children intermarried with others. By Year 300, the "Tourist Families" are a distinct social group.

**Tourist Family surnames:**
- The Petersons (American)
- The Yamamotos (Japanese)
- The Schmidts (German)
- The Changs (Chinese)
- The Sanchezes (Spanish)

They're fully integrated but still called "Les Touristes" — sometimes fondly, sometimes not.

### Religion

The Republic is officially secular (laïcité), but practical accommodations exist.

**Religious groups:**
- Catholics (~40% — largest group)
- Atheists (~30%)
- Muslims (~15% — descendants of staff and tourists)
- Others (~15% — Jewish, Buddhist, new beliefs)

**The Chapel:**
A multi-faith space in the Grand Palais tunnels. Services rotate. Everyone gets their day.

### Fashion

Formal. Always formal.

Le Palais Profond preserved pre-Silence French fashion better than most. They have:
- Suits (proper ones, not the La Dalle approximations)
- Dresses (formal occasions)
- The tricolor (worn as sashes, pins, ribbons)

**The Dress Code:**
- Officials: Formal wear at all times
- Citizens: "Respectable" clothing
- Visitors: Whatever, but they'll judge you

## Military & Defense

### Les Forces Armées de la République

89 soldiers. The smallest military in Paris. But well-trained and fanatically loyal.

**Structure:**
- **La Garde Républicaine:** Presidential guard (ceremonial + real)
- **La Gendarmerie:** Police and border control
- **Les Chasseurs:** Scouts and reconnaissance

**Weapons:**
- Ceremonial swords (real, used in parades)
- Batons (standard issue)
- Crossbows (for Chasseurs)
- The "Légion" — 12 elite guards with pre-Silence firearms (7 guns still work)

**Defense strategy:**
The tunnels are narrow. Defenders have massive advantages. Le Palais Profond has never been successfully invaded — though it's also never been seriously attacked. Other factions find them more amusing than threatening.

## Economy

### Internal Currency

**Le Franc Souterrain** — The Underground Franc

They recreated the franc. Paper money, printed on mushroom-fiber paper.

**Denominations:** 1F, 5F, 10F, 20F, 50F, 100F

**Backing:** Faith in the Republic (nothing, basically)

**Exchange rate with Le Carrefour:** Wildly variable; officially 1F = 0.3 labor credits, but nobody really trades francs

### What They Produce

| Product | Description | Trade Value |
|---------|-------------|-------------|
| Documents | Beautifully calligraphed official papers | Medium (prestige) |
| Textiles | Formal clothing, high quality | High |
| Legal services | Contract authentication | High |
| Education | Tutors, teachers for hire | Medium |
| Art | Preserved and created | Prestige value |

### The Ministry of Economy's Great Project

They're trying to establish the franc as a standard currency across Paris.

**Progress after 250 years:** Le Carrefour accepts francs at some stalls. As curiosities.

## Notable Figures

### Édouard XVII (Current President)

Adopted at 45, now 57. The 43rd President.

**Background:** Was a Ministry of Culture archivist. Quietly competent. Chosen for adoption because he was "safe" — no ambition, no enemies.

**Personality:** Surprisingly good at the job. Navigates bureaucracy like water. Genuinely believes in the Republic. Still confused about why he's President.

**Known for:**
- First President to visit Le Carrefour (Year 291)
- Reformed adoption process (slightly)
- Actually reads the laws he signs

### Marguerite Delacourt-Yamamoto (Opposition Leader)

52 years old. Head of Les Réformistes. Descendant of both original Delacourt family and a Japanese tourist.

**Personality:** Fiery, idealistic, perpetually frustrated. Wants to abolish the adoption requirement and have "real" elections.

**Known for:**
- "The Speech of Year 295" — called for democratic reform, was ignored
- Writes pamphlets (hand-copied, distributed illegally)
- Still loses every election

### Colonel Henri Marchande (Commander, Forces Armées)

63 years old. Career military. Ancestor was a Secret Service agent on Day 1.

**Personality:** Gruff, traditional, secretly believes the Republic is doomed but will die defending it.

**Known for:**
- Held the eastern border during the Year 267 incident
- Personally knows every soldier by name
- Has killed exactly one person (a raider in Year 245)

## Absurdist Details

### Things That Make No Sense But Are Very Serious

1. **The Ministry of Foreign Affairs** has never had foreign affairs. They issue visas. They prepare "diplomatic pouches." They have a "Foreign Service Exam." They've trained for 300 years for a job that doesn't exist.

2. **Bastille Day fireworks** are rhythmic clapping. A specific pattern, passed down, that "represents" fireworks. It's beautiful. It makes no sense.

3. **Form V-27** is the "Request for Exception to Form V-26, Which is the Request for Exception to Form V-25." This chain goes back 15 forms.

4. **The Ministre de l'Outre-Mer** (Overseas Territories) has jurisdiction over "all French territory beyond the Paris underground." He has a staff of one (himself). He occasionally issues decrees about Martinique.

5. **Presidential portraits** are painted by the same family (the Dubois). They've painted 43 presidents. The style is inherited. Édouard XVII's portrait looks exactly like Édouard XII's.

6. **The Constitution has been amended 847 times.** Most amendments clarify previous amendments. Amendment 512 clarifies the clarification of Amendment 489.

7. **The official language is French.** They have an Academy. It has 6 members. They meet weekly to protect French from "foreign contamination." There are no foreigners.

## Relations with Other Factions

### La Dalle Éternelle (Diplomatic)

Two elites, mutually bewildered.

La Dalle finds Le Palais Profond's bureaucracy insane (which is saying something). Le Palais Profond finds La Dalle's corporate structure "charmingly provincial" but "fundamentally legitimate."

**Status:** Formal alliance, ambassadors exchanged.

### Le Grand Nord (Cool)

Le Grand Nord's union culture clashes with Le Palais Profond's hierarchical structure. Also, Le Grand Nord doesn't recognize the Republic's authority.

**Status:** Polite non-engagement. Trade happens through Le Carrefour.

### Le Carrefour (Pragmatic)

Le Palais Profond wants to be recognized as the legitimate government. Le Carrefour smiles, nods, and ignores them.

**Status:** Trade and cooperation. Le Palais Profond accepts Le Carrefour's neutrality (reluctantly).

### Les Ossuaires (Suspicious)

Le Palais Profond finds them unsettling. The bone-worship, the strange dialect, the Nuit Blanche silence ritual — it's all very un-Republican.

**Status:** Minimal contact. Official position: "They are citizens, but misguided."

### Les Canaux (Useful)

Les Canaux carry messages and goods. Le Palais Profond pays for services. Neither respects the other.

**Status:** Transactional. Les Canaux take francs (for the novelty).

## Quest Hooks

### The Lost Constitution

**Hook:** The original Constitution (the one brought from the Élysée) has been stolen.

**Reality:** It's a photocopy of a photocopy. But it's THEIR photocopy.

**Implications:** Constitutional crisis. If the Constitution is gone, is the Republic still valid? Who took it? Why?

### The True Heir

**Hook:** A claimant emerges — someone who claims to be the biological descendant of President Delacourt (through an affair, supposedly).

**Reality:** The claim is impossible to verify. But the chaos it causes is real.

**Implications:** If true, the entire adoption succession is illegitimate. If false, someone is trying to destabilize the Republic.

### The Ministry of Outre-Mer's Expedition

**Hook:** The Minister of Overseas Territories wants to send an expedition outside Paris. To reclaim French territory.

**Reality:** He's delusional but has convinced 12 volunteers. They want to reach the Loire Valley.

**Implications:** They'd have to pass through the Hero's territory (near Tours). This could be first contact.

### The Diplomatic Incident

**Hook:** A Le Palais Profond citizen was murdered in Le Carrefour. The President demands the killer be extradited.

**Reality:** Le Carrefour has no extradition treaty with Le Palais Profond. Le Palais Profond believes they do (they drafted one 200 years ago; Le Carrefour never signed it).

**Implications:** Potential conflict between Le Palais Profond and Le Carrefour. Might need mediation.
