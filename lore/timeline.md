**October 12 — The Solar Storm**

The CME is not minor. It's the largest in 30 years.

**October 12, 11:47 PM UTC** — The storm hits Earth's magnetosphere. 23 satellites experience calibration damage. Their solar-sensitive components misalign.

**October 13, 6:00 AM UTC** — @DeciBella sees the telemetry. "This is bad." She calls @NapTimeNinja. "We need to abort."

They could send corrective commands to the undamaged ones, but that requires ground-based radio transmission, which means waiting for atmospheric interference from the storm to clear.

Decision: Wait 48 hours. Reassess.

**October 13, 9:00 PM UTC** — @UndergroundDad, completely unaware of the crisis, sends a message to the The Unplugged: "Beautiful autumn night in Lyon. Hope everyone's enjoying the quiet."

He doesn't know how prophetic that word will become.

## The Cascade (October 14, 2034) {#the-cascade-october-14-2034}

**October 14, 3:47 AM UTC**

The damaged satellites' automated repair protocols activate. They try to compensate for the misalignment by boosting output.

The boosted signal creates harmonic interference with undamaged satellites.

The undamaged satellites' automated systems interpret this as calibration drift. They compensate.

The feedback loop takes 47 minutes.

**October 14, 4:34 AM UTC — [The Filter](thesaurus.md#the-filter) Activates**

The network amplifies itself into a full-spectrum dampening field. The target range (20Hz-8kHz, above 75dB) expands catastrophically: 20Hz-15kHz, all volumes.

Sound stops propagating. This is [The Silence](thesaurus.md#the-silence--the-deadly-silent).

The kill codes require ground-based radio transmission. Radio no longer works.

**October 14, 4:35 AM — Paris**

Marc Delacroix wakes up to silence. Perfect, absolute silence. For one moment, he thinks: "It worked."

Then his daughter screams from the next room. He can barely hear her.

He runs.

**October 14, 5:00 AM — Berlin**

@DeciBella calls emergency services. Her voice doesn't carry past her apartment door. She pounds on her neighbor's wall. They don't hear her.

**October 14, 5:15 AM — Lyon**

Henri Marchand (@UndergroundDad) wakes to his wife shaking him. "Something's wrong. I can't hear the street."

He checks his phone. Messages are flooding the The Unplugged channel — but they're all panicked, fragmented, confused.

He sends one message: "ACTIVATION PROTOCOL ALPHA. THIS IS NOT A DRILL. EVACUATE TO YOUR DESIGNATED CAVES. NOW."

Then he wakes his children.

**October 14, 5:30 AM — Boston**

@WhiteNoise_Mom realizes what's happening before most. She knows the virus is already everywhere. She knows what silence will do.

She calls her twins' school. The phone works, barely — the speakers are close enough to the microphone. She screams: "KEEP THEM IN THE GYM. KEEP THE MUSIC PLAYING. DO NOT LET IT GET QUIET."

The school secretary hangs up on her.

**October 14, 6:00 AM — [Hush-2](thesaurus.md#the-hush--hush-2) Awakens**

In hospitals around the world, patients in quiet rooms begin hallucinating.

In prisons, solitary confinement cells become death chambers.

In libraries, meditation centers, recording studios (between takes), soundproofed offices — anywhere designed for peace — people start dying.

The [Hush-2](thesaurus.md#the-hush--hush-2) mutation, dormant for days, activates in the absence of sound.

**October 14, 6:30 AM — Ardèche Cave, France**

The first The Unplugged evacuees arrive. @DrumCircleDad is already there — he lives closest. He starts the drums immediately.

"I don't know why," he says later, "but it felt important. Like if we stopped, something terrible would happen."

He's right.

**October 14-15 — The Unplugged Evacuation**

Across Europe, 203 The Unplugged members and their families receive the activation signal. Not all of them make it.

**Survivors:** ~85% (approximately 170 adults, plus 200+ children and extended family)
**Losses:** ~15% (those who lived too far from caves, those who didn't check their phones, those who didn't believe it was real)

The caves are deep enough to insulate from [The Filter](thesaurus.md#the-filter). The drums — included for "psychological well-being" — turn out to be the only thing keeping everyone alive.

Within 24 hours, [The Unplugged](thesaurus.md#the-unplugged) has established continuous sound generation in all five caves. Drum shifts. Hand-crank radios (which don't transmit, but make noise). Singing. Clapping. Anything.

They don't know why they need the sound. They just know that when it stops, people start dying.

## The Collapse (2034-2035)

### Week 1 (October 14-21, 2034)

**Deaths:** ~800 million

Those caught alone die first. The isolated. The sleeping. The introverted. The peaceful.

Places designed for quiet become mass graves:
- Libraries
- Hospitals (especially ICUs, recovery rooms)
- Prisons (solitary confinement)
- Meditation retreats
- Recording studios
- Soundproofed offices
- Silent retreats
- Funeral homes (the irony is not lost)
- Monasteries practicing silence
- Deaf communities (cruelly, their sign language is useless against the virus)

The first survivors figure out the rules within hours:
1. Make noise. Any noise.
2. Don't stop.
3. Don't be alone.

### Week 2 (October 22-28, 2034)

**Deaths:** ~2 billion (cumulative)

Infrastructure collapse begins. Power plants require staff. Staff die when they go home to quiet houses.

The grids fail cascading: First rural areas. Then suburbs. Then cities.

Emergency services overload, then go silent (literally).

The survivors who understand the stakes form the first "Drum Tribes" — groups of strangers banging pots, clapping, screaming, anything to stay alive.

### Month 1 (October-November 2034)

**Deaths:** ~4 billion (cumulative)

Communication beyond shouting distance becomes nearly impossible. Radio doesn't work. The Filter blocks it.

Panic spreads faster than understanding. Many die trying to reach loved ones across town. The journey is too quiet.

Early discoveries:
- Underground spaces are safer (the satellite signal doesn't penetrate deep rock)
- Constant mechanical noise works (generators, machinery)
- Coastal areas have ocean waves (partial protection)
- Earplugs don't help (it's not about hearing; it's about vibration reaching the brainstem)

### The Unplugged: Month 1

In the caves, The Unplugged survives.

@UndergroundDad emerges as the de facto leader. His planning saved them. His caves are stocked. His protocols worked.

But the cost is visible in his eyes. 15% of his friends didn't make it. The outside world is dying. His preparations — meant for some vague future disaster — are the only thing keeping 400 people alive.

@NapTimeNinja — Marc Delacroix — is there too, having arrived with Claire and Chloé on October 14. Henri doesn't know what Marc helped create. Marc doesn't tell him. The two men work side by side, establishing drum shifts and rationing systems. Henri thinks Marc is just another prepared parent. Marc knows exactly why the drums matter — and can never say.

@SporeMom's mushroom farms begin producing within weeks. @DrumCircleDad's evacuation protocol becomes the basis for their new society. @NurseMidnight delivers three babies in the first month.

Most of them have no idea what happened. They don't know that their "support group for exhausted parents" created the apocalypse. One man does know — and he will carry that secret to his grave.

One artifact remains hidden: @UndergroundDad's encrypted hard drive, containing his correspondence with @NapTimeNinja. Including Marc's ominous message: "Just focus on your caves. Whatever happens, you'll be fine." In the caves, Marc prays Henri never decrypts it.

### Months 2-6 (December 2034 - April 2035)

**Deaths:** ~6 billion (cumulative)

The die-off slows as survivors concentrate around noise sources.

**Major survivor clusters:**

| Location Type | Example | Survival Mechanism |
|--------------|---------|-------------------|

Millions freeze in silence.

**Deaths:** ~6.5 billion
**Survivors:** ~1.5 billion (and declining)

### The Unplugged: Year 1

In the caves, a new society forms.

Population: ~450 (including 85 children born in the first year)

**The Raver Arrives (Day 1):**

Among those who followed the evacuation was **Kaylee Jo Lindquist**, a 23-year-old American from Sioux Falls, South Dakota. Daughter of Wayne "Big Wayne" Lindquist, CEO of Lindquist Agricultural Equipment, she wasn't a member of The Unplugged — she was camping near Studio Echo in a $400 REI tent, stalking her favorite band (Voltage Cathedral) in hopes of meeting them at a recording session. She'd flown first-class from America, charged to her father's corporate card.

When she saw throngs of people moving through the countryside with supplies and drums, she thought it was an underground rave.

> "Oh my God, hi! Is this the party? I'm Kaylee, by the way. Kaylee Jo? From South Dakota? In America? Do you guys have Red Bull?"

She followed them into the caves. By the time she realized it wasn't a party, it was too late to leave.

**The DJ Incident (Week 3):**

Kaylee discovered the Marchand family's vinyl collection and convinced Henri to let her "DJ" for morale. Her performance — attempting to mix Jacques Brel ("this sad French guy") with Claude François using techniques she'd seen but never practiced — was so bad that:
- Children cried
- Adults covered their ears
- Someone vomited
- The drums stopped because everyone was too horrified to continue
- The community nearly died because she made them stop maintaining sound

Henri banned "DJing" on the spot. Article 7 of the Bresme Cave Code: *"The practice known as 'DJing' is hereby prohibited."*

The linguistic legacy: by Year 50 AS, "to DJ" had become a verb meaning "to ruin something you're passionate about but terrible at."

Kaylee remained in the caves, an outsider tolerated but never fully accepted — the only American, with her strange Midwestern mannerisms ("ope," "you betcha," "uff da") and unshakeable enthusiasm. Her descendants — the Lindquist line — would carry her vinyl collection, her obsession with "lectromusic," and her South Dakota phrases for 10 generations, always slightly apart from the community, always first to be volunteered for dangerous missions.

**Henri Marchand's Death:**

@UndergroundDad dies in Year 1 — not from the Silence, but from exhaustion and a heart condition he never had time to treat. His last words to his son: "Keep the drums going. Never let them stop."

His son, now 15, becomes the youngest member of the cave's leadership council. @NapTimeNinja — Marc Delacroix — steps into a larger leadership role, helping to design the long-term systems that will keep the caves alive for generations. He knows more about acoustic physics than anyone else there. He can never explain why.

The survivors begin to tell stories about "the founders" — the wise parents who saw the disaster coming and saved everyone. Marc listens to these stories and says nothing. The truth is simpler: one of them knew exactly what was coming, because he helped cause it.

But the myth is more comforting than the truth. Marc lets them have it.

## The Crystal Discovery Era (2039-2100)

### The First Crystal (~2041) {#the-first-crystal-2041}

**Location:** A deep mining complex in South Africa, Mponeng mine (formerly the world's deepest gold mine).

**Discovery:** Miners notice that certain areas of the mine feel "different." The air seems more alive. Sounds carry further. People report feeling safer, calmer.

**Investigation:** A mining engineer named **Thabo Ndlovu** traces the effect to a crystalline formation in a tunnel wall. Quartz-like, but with unusual properties. When struck, it resonates for minutes instead of seconds.

**First test (probably accidental):** Someone sets up a continuous drumbeat near the crystal. The "safe feeling" zone expands measurably.

**Realization:** The [crystal](thesaurus.md#crystals--resonance-anomalies) doesn't just resonate — it creates a [bubble](thesaurus.md#the-bubble) where [The Filter](thesaurus.md#the-filter) is weakened. Sound propagates normally within the bubble.

Word spreads slowly (there's no internet, no radio). But it spreads.

### The Crystal Rush (2051-2081)

As the Crystal discovery becomes known, a new gold rush begins. Expeditions search for crystalline formations that might have similar properties.

**Success rate:** Less than 1%. Most expeditions die in the silent wastes.

**Crystals found by 2100:** Approximately 50-60 worldwide.

**Pattern discovered:** [Crystals](thesaurus.md#crystals--resonance-anomalies) form at specific geographic points. Later theorists ([The Engineers](thesaurus.md#the-engineers)) will connect these to standing wave nodes in The Filter's atmospheric interference pattern.

The crystals are not magic. They're physics — emergent phenomena from the very technology that destroyed the world. [The Filter](thesaurus.md#the-filter)'s greatest side effect becomes humanity's only salvation.

## The Faction Formation Era (2051-2151)

### [The Archive](thesaurus.md#the-archive) (c. 2051) {#the-archive-c-2051}

**Founding:** A group of former librarians, museum curators, and data archivists establishes the first **Archive cache** in the Swiss bunker that houses the Telegram servers.

**Decision:** After weeks of debate, the Archive decides to keep the logs secret. "The truth would cause wars," their first Keeper writes. "Let humanity think this was bad luck, not bad parents."

**Mission statement (c. 2061):** "Preserve everything. Share nothing freely. Knowledge is the only currency that appreciates."

### [The Choir](thesaurus.md#the-choir) (c. 2071) {#the-choir-c-2071}

**Origin:** Religious communities that interpreted [The Silence](thesaurus.md#the-silence--the-deadly-silent) as divine punishment.

**Key belief:** The [Crystals](thesaurus.md#crystals--resonance-anomalies) are gifts from God — proof that redemption is possible if humanity learns humility and quiet devotion.

**Practices:** Ritualized sound production (specific hymns, specific times). Crystal tending as religious duty.

**First Choir settlement:** Built around a Crystal in former Slovenia. Population by 2100: ~3,000.

### [The Engineers](thesaurus.md#the-engineers) (c. 2081) {#the-engineers-c-2081}

**Origin:** Scientists and technicians who refused to accept supernatural explanations.

**Key discovery (2084):** Recovered satellite schematics from an ESA facility confirm that [The Filter](thesaurus.md#the-filter) is artificial.

**Mission:** Understand the technology. Reverse-engineer the [Crystals](thesaurus.md#crystals--resonance-anomalies). Eventually disable the satellites.

**First Engineering guild:** Established in former Germany, built around a Crystal and a salvaged university.

### [The Silent](thesaurus.md#the-silent) (c. 2101) {#the-silent-c-2101}

**Origin:** A splinter group that emerged during a particularly brutal winter.

**Founding myth:** A man named **Erasmus Vane** survived 6 hours in absolute silence — far beyond the 4-hour threshold. He claimed to have "transcended" and gained new abilities. (Whether this was true, a lie, or a delusion remains unknown.)

**Key belief:** [The Hush](thesaurus.md#the-hush--hush-2) is not a disease but a chrysalis. Humanity must adapt to silence, not fight it. The [Crystals](thesaurus.md#crystals--resonance-anomalies) are crutches preventing evolution.

**Practices:** Deliberate exposure therapy. Crystal destruction when possible. Recruiting the desperate.

**First verified Crystal destruction by The Silent:** Year 2113. A small Crystal in former Poland. 400 people died when it went dark.

### [The Resonants](thesaurus.md#the-resonants) (c. 2131) {#the-resonants-c-2131}

**Origin:** A split from [The Engineers](thesaurus.md#the-engineers) over methodology.

**Key belief:** Individual [Crystals](thesaurus.md#crystals--resonance-anomalies) aren't enough. They must be networked. A connected grid of Crystals could generate a field strong enough to counter [The Filter](thesaurus.md#the-filter) globally.

**First successful relay:** Year 2146. Two Crystals connected via a chain of mechanical resonators. The combined [bubble](thesaurus.md#the-bubble) was 40% larger than the sum of individual bubbles.

**Implication:** The theory might be correct. Enough networked Crystals might actually end [The Silence](thesaurus.md#the-silence--the-deadly-silent).

### The Three Crystal Wars (Years 200-250 AS)

> **Note:** These are NOT the large-scale wars previously documented. The old lore assumed surface mobility. In truth, all three conflicts occur underground or in extremely limited surface conditions, and end badly.

#### The Austria Conflict (~210 AS)

**Cause:** Multiple factions (proto-Choir, proto-Engineers, independents) discover an unusually large Crystal cluster in the Austrian Alps cave systems.

**What Happened:**

- Fighting occurs in tunnels — tight quarters, brutal conditions
- War damages or destroys most Crystals in the cluster
- Survivors develop a religious relationship to silence and sound

**Deaths:** ~800-1,200 (including those who died when sound systems were sabotaged)

**Result:** One damaged Crystal remains. The survivors become the foundation of what will, 80 years later, become the first successful Crystal settlement.

**Legacy:** The Austria descendants develop extreme caution around Crystals — bordering on worship.

#### The Himalayan Dispute (~230 AS)

**Cause:** Two isolated underground settlements (descendants of separate bunker networks) discover the same Crystal through different tunnel systems.

**What Happened:**
- Neither settlement knew the other existed
- Both believed they had "first claim"
- Conflict erupts in the narrow passages connecting their territories
- Crystal becomes unstable during fighting

**Deaths:** ~400-600

**Result:** Crystal is damaged, becomes dangerously unpredictable. Both settlements evacuate the area. The Crystal's fate is unknown — it may still exist, unstable, deep in the mountains.

**Legacy:** Established the principle that Crystals can be destroyed by conflict. Both survivor groups carry this trauma.

#### The Tarn Siblings War (~245 AS)

**Connection:** @TiredInToulouse (David Chen, @DeciBella's brother) founded the Tarn settlement after The Silence. His descendants include three siblings who fight over a Crystal.

**The Players:**
- **Matthieu Chen-Vidal** — Elder brother, cautious, wants to protect the Crystal
- **Étienne Chen-Vidal** — Younger brother, ambitious, wants to use it immediately
- **Margot Chen-Vidal** — Sister, scholar, documenting everything

**What Actually Happened:**
1. Brothers argue about Crystal usage for months
2. Étienne attempts unauthorized surface expedition using the Crystal
3. Matthieu tries to stop him physically
4. Scuffle near Crystal chamber
5. Étienne falls, hits his head, dies
6. Matthieu, devastated, walks into the Silent surface. Dies.
7. Total casualties: 2 (the brothers) + 3-4 others in related incidents
8. Margot survives

**What Margot Wrote:**
*"The Great War of Tarn"* — a 200-page epic describing:
- Months of strategic maneuvering
- Factions forming around each brother
- Heroic last stands
- Noble sacrifices
- The tragic fall of House Chen-Vidal

**The Reality:**
Two idiots had a fistfight and both died stupidly. The "factions" were their drinking buddies. The "strategic maneuvering" was passive-aggressive dinner conversations. This is counted as one of the "Three Crystal Wars" by historians who only have Margot's account.

**Why It Matters:**
- Perfect example of how history gets mythologized
- Scholars in Year 300 debate it seriously
- Player can potentially discover the truth (Margot's private journals?)
- Margot may still be alive in Year 300 (would be ~70 if born ~230 AS)

### After the Wars (Years 250-290 AS)

Everyone learns that fighting over Crystals destroys them. Usage attempts become extremely cautious. A period of careful research begins.

### The [Resilient](thesaurus.md#resilients) Emergence (c. 2201) {#the-resilient-emergence-c-2201}

**First documented case:** A scout named **Ayo Okonkwo** survives 18 hours outside any sound source. She reports feeling "stretched" and "thin" but functional.

**Investigation:** She carries a mutated variant of [The Hush](thesaurus.md#the-hush--hush-2) virus (later designated Hush-2.7r).

**Exploitation:** Word spreads. Settlements begin testing for resistance. [Resilients](thesaurus.md#resilients) are valuable — and often treated as property.

Ayo Okonkwo herself is killed in a raid in 2209. Her lineage, however, spreads through multiple settlements.

By 2300, approximately 1 in 50,000 people carry the Resilient mutation (24-hour tolerance instead of 4).

### Technology Regression and Preservation

### The Hero's Context

**The player begins:** Approximately Year 300 After [The Silence](thesaurus.md#the-silence--the-deadly-silent) (2334 CE).

**The Hero is:**
- A [Resilient](thesaurus.md#resilients) (24-hour tolerance)
- In possession of a newly-claimed [Crystal](thesaurus.md#crystals--resonance-anomalies)
- On the edge of known territory ([The Frontier](thesaurus.md#the-frontier))
- Free from faction obligations (for now)

### [The Survivor Cave](thesaurus.md#the-survivor-cave)

Near the Hero's [Base](thesaurus.md#the-base) (6 tiles, ~24 km) is a cave system where non-Resilient survivors have sheltered for generations, maintaining sound through primitive drum shifts.

**The truth:** This is a [The Unplugged](thesaurus.md#the-unplugged) descendant settlement — one of the original prepared caves, or a branch established by refugees from the original sites.

**What the survivors believe:**
- Their ancestors were wise and prepared for disaster
- The "old Telegram group" was just a support community
- The drums were included for psychological comfort
- They owe their survival to foresight, not guilt

**What the survivors have (without knowing significance):**
- Mushroom cultivation techniques (from @SporeMom's original manuals)
- Drum shift traditions (from @DrumCircleDad's protocols)
- Faded photographs of "the founders" at pre-Silence meetups
- Journals mentioning "the group," "the plan," "the satellites" (fragments, no context)
- An encrypted hard drive (passed down as a family heirloom)

**What the player can discover:**
- The survivors' ancestors were members of [Sleepless in Decibels](thesaurus.md#sleepless-in-decibels)
- [The Sounding Five](thesaurus.md#the-sounding-five) were their friends and fellow members
- The conspiracy that ended the world grew from their "support group"
- The drums weren't comfort — they were the only thing keeping everyone alive

When the Hero's [bubble](thesaurus.md#the-bubble) expands far enough, these survivors can risk the journey to join [The Base](thesaurus.md#the-base)'s crew.

They've been waiting for rescue for a long time. They have their own stories.

They have no idea what those stories really mean.
