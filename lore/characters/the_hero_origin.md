# The Hero's Origin

> **Quick Reference:** The player character — descended from a Raver who accidentally survived The Silence by stalking her favorite band. The family became outcasts, cult-like keepers of "lectromusic" traditions with bizarre Midwestern mannerisms, always first to volunteer for dangerous missions. The Hero was discovered to be a Resilient when their cousins died on a surface test mission.

## The Founding Ancestor: The Raver

### The Date Mix-Up That Saved Her Life

**October 14, 2034 — The Silence begins.**

In the hills outside Tours, a 23-year-old American woman named **Kaylee Jo Lindquist** is camping near Studio Echo in a $400 REI tent, wearing glitter makeup and a flower crown. She's been staking out the location for three days.

**Who she was:**
- Daughter of Wayne "Big Wayne" Lindquist, CEO of Lindquist Agricultural Equipment (Sioux Falls, South Dakota)
- Net worth: $2.3 million personal trust fund
- Job title: "Social Media Coordinator" (fake job her father made up)
- Actual occupation: Professional superfan of **Voltage Cathedral**, an obscure French electro duo

**Why she was there:**
- Voltage Cathedral was rumored to be recording at Studio Echo
- She'd flown first-class from South Dakota, charged to her father's corporate card
- She was waiting for the right moment to approach and... she wasn't sure. Get an autograph? Become their friend? Join them on tour?
- **The problem:** She'd mixed up the date format
  - The band's session was listed as **10/14**
  - She read it as October 14th (American mm/dd format)
  - Europeans write dd/mm — it could have meant something else entirely
  - The records don't survive. The point is: she was there for the wrong reason.

**What she saw (morning of October 14):**
- Throngs of people moving through the countryside, all heading the same direction
- Families with supplies, camping gear, drums
- They looked like they were heading to a festival

**What she thought:** "Oh my God, is this like, an underground rave? That's so cool!"

**What was actually happening:** The Unplugged evacuation. Henri Marchand's activation signal had gone out. 200+ families were converging on Les Grottes de la Bresme.

**Her decision:** She followed them.

---

### Arriving at the Caves

**October 14, 2034, 11:00 AM:**

Kaylee follows the crowd into the cave system. She's confused — this doesn't look like a rave venue. No sound systems, no lights, no DJ booth. Just... caves. And drums. And terrified families.

**What she said when she arrived:**
> "Oh my God, hi! Is this the party? Sorry I'm late — well, I'm not late, I'm actually super early, but like, I saw everyone heading this way and I was like, this must be it! Is this like, an underground rave? Because that's so cool. I'm Kaylee, by the way. Kaylee Jo? From South Dakota? In America? Do you guys have Red Bull?"

**French survivors' reaction:** Stunned silence. Then slow, horrified realization that this person was now trapped with them forever.

**First hours:**
- She tries to leave. Someone stops her. "You can't go outside. It's not safe."
- She asks when the music starts. People look at her like she's insane.
- She realizes this isn't a party. Something terrible is happening.
- She stays.

**First days:**
- The drums never stop. She doesn't understand why, but she helps.
- She's young, single, no family to protect — she's useful for drum shifts.
- She asks questions. "Why can't we go outside?" "What's happening?" "When does this end?"
- No one knows. They just know: drums = life.
- She keeps saying "ope, sorry!" whenever she bumps into anyone in the dark

**First week:**
- Kaylee discovers Henri's grandparents' collection: **vinyl records**
- She's the only person in the caves who recognizes them
- She proposes: "I can DJ! Like, I go to raves all the time. I can totally do this."
- Henri, desperate for morale: "...what's a DJ?"

## The DJ Incident (Year 1 AS)

### The Performance

**Week 3 in the caves:**

Kaylee has convinced Henri to let her try. She's rigged up a hand-crank generator, found a working turntable in the Marchand family's storage, and selected records from Georges Marchand's collection.

**The setup:**
- One turntable (barely functional)
- Hand-crank power (inconsistent)
- Records from the 1960s-80s (Georges Marchand was not an electro fan)
- Kaylee's confidence: "Oh my God, I've got this, you guys"
- Kaylee's skill: Absolutely not

**The pitch:**
> "Okay so like, I know this is super stressful for everyone, but I was thinking — what if I DJ'd? Like, I go to raves all the time, and I've watched tons of DJs, and it's basically just putting on music and vibing, right? I can totally do that. Trust me, you guys are gonna love this."

**The performance:**
- Kaylee announces: "Okay so this is called DJing. You guys are gonna love it."
- She starts with a scratchy Jacques Brel record. She doesn't know who he is. She calls him "this sad French guy."
- She attempts to beatmatch it with... Claude François
- The tempo is wrong. The key is wrong. The vibe is wrong.
- She keeps saying "wait, wait, I can fix this" while making it worse
- She tries to "scratch" — the needle skips, creating a sound like a dying animal being fed through a wood chipper
- Children start crying. Adults cover their ears. Someone vomits.
- The drums stop. People realize they've stopped drumming because the noise is so bad.
- Someone screams: "START THE DRUMS AGAIN!"
- Kaylee is physically removed from the turntable

**Her response:**
> "Okay so that wasn't great but like, I'm still learning? Give me another chance?"

**Henri's verdict:**

> "That was... I don't have words for what that was. We're banning this. No more 'DJing.' Whatever that was, it never happens again."

### The Ban

**From the Bresme Cave Code (established Year 1 AS):**

> **Article 7: On Unauthorized Sound Production**
>
> The practice known as "DJing" — the combination of recorded sounds through mechanical manipulation — is prohibited within community spaces.
>
> This prohibition is not about the *concept* but about the *execution*. The original practitioner demonstrated that enthusiasm does not equal ability. The risk of psychological damage to community members outweighs any potential benefit.
>
> Drumming remains encouraged. Singing remains encouraged. "DJing" does not.

**Kaylee's response:**
> "Okay, that's fine, I totally get it. You guys just aren't ready for this kind of music yet. It's very avant-garde. Don't worry about it. I'm not offended. I mean, I'm a little offended, but it's fine. It's totally fine."

(It was not fine. She cried in a corner for three hours.)

**The linguistic legacy:**

By Year 50 AS, "to DJ" had become a verb meaning:
- "To attempt something you're passionate about but terrible at"
- "To ruin something that was working fine"
- "To volunteer for a task you're unqualified for"

Example usage (Year 100 AS):
> "Don't let Pierre fix the water pump. He'll DJ the whole system."

## The Lindquist Line: Outcasts by Choice

### Kaylee's Life in the Caves

**Year 1-40 AS:**

After The DJ Incident, Kaylee was... tolerated. Not banned. Not feared. Just slightly apart. The only American in the caves, with her strange Midwestern mannerisms and unshakeable enthusiasm.

**Her role:**
- Always given the night drum shift (least desirable)
- Always assigned to dangerous supply runs
- Always seated at the edge of communal meals
- Never invited to planning councils
- Referred to as "the DJ American" long after anyone remembered what that meant

**Her response:**
- She embraced the outsider status ("They're just jealous of my energy")
- She kept the vinyl records (no one else wanted them)
- She created private listening sessions with hand-crank power
- She taught her children about "lectromusic" (her word for electronic music)
- She insisted the records were sacred — messages from before The Silence
- She aggressively maintained her Midwestern mannerisms

**Her marriage:**
- Year 3 AS: Began relationship with **Théo Marchand**, Henri's nephew
- Year 4 AS: Married (small ceremony, grudging attendance)
- Year 5 AS: Son born (**Thomas Lindquist-Marchand**, later simplified to Lindquist)
- Théo died Year 12 AS (tunnel collapse)

**Her children:**
- Born Year 5 AS: **Thomas Lindquist** (father of the next generation)
- Raised on stories of Voltage Cathedral, Studio Echo, and "the music that was"
- Taught to revere the records even though they couldn't play them
- Taught the "ancient wisdom" of South Dakota (Midwestern phrases)

**Kaylee's death (Year 40 AS):**
- Died at age 63
- Last words: "The Studio is still out there. The music is waiting. You'll find it. And when you do... you betcha it's gonna be amazing."
- Her vinyl collection passed to Thomas

### The Vinyl Cult

**Generations 2-8 (Year 40 AS - Year 300 AS):**

The Lindquist line developed increasingly cult-like behaviors:

**Core Beliefs:**

| Belief | Origin | Year 300 Understanding |
|--------|--------|------------------------|
| **"Lectromusic"** | Electronic music (Kaylee's passion) | A lost sacred art form; no one remembers what it sounded like |
| **The Studio** | Studio Echo (where Kaylee was stalking) | A holy site; must be found and restored |
| **The Records** | Georges Marchand's vinyl collection | Sacred texts; playing them would release ancient power |
| **The DJ** | Kaylee's failed performance | A forbidden technique; only the worthy can attempt it |
| **Voltage Cathedral** | Kaylee's favorite band | Mythological figures; possibly gods |

**Practices:**

- **Record Meditation:** Holding vinyl records and "feeling the grooves"
- **Studio Pilgrimage:** Every generation sends someone to search for Studio Echo
- **The Mix:** A theoretical future event where someone will successfully DJ
- **Secret Hand Signals:** Based on scratching motions Kaylee once made
- **The Greeting Ritual:** Always ask "Cold enough for ya?" regardless of temperature
- **Hot Dish Sundays:** Weekly mushroom casserole, served with crushed crackers

**The Lindquist Family Lingo (Evolved):**

Kaylee's Midwestern mannerisms became sacred family traditions — but after 300 years of oral transmission, they've drifted into something barely recognizable:

| Original (2034) | Year 300 Form | Meaning | Usage |
|-----------------|---------------|---------|-------|
| "Ope" | **"Ope"** | Surprise/apology | Said when bumping into anything (unchanged — too short to drift) |
| "You betcha" | **"Yabetja"** | Strong agreement | Enthusiastic affirmation, almost a blessing |
| "Uff da" | **"Oofda"** | Exhaustion/dismay | After any hard task; also a minor curse |
| "Oh, for cute!" | **"Fercute"** | Genuine approval | High praise; "that's fercute" = excellent |
| "That's different" | **"S'diff"** | Disapproval | Passive-aggressive criticism; deadly insult among Lindquists |
| "Could be worse" | **"Codworse"** | Acceptance of fate | Response to any tragedy; almost religious |
| "Don't cha know" | **"Doncha"** | Emphasis/certainty | End of statements; adds weight to claims |
| "Cold enough for ya?" | **"Coldenuff?"** | Ritual greeting | Asked regardless of temperature; expected response is "Yabetja" |
| "Hot dish" | **"Odiche"** | Sacred meal | Any mushroom casserole; Sunday tradition |
| "Let me just sneak past ya" | **"Sneepas"** | Excuse me | Moving through crowds; very polite |
| "Oh geez" | **"Ojeez"** | Mild dismay | Minor problems; not as serious as "oofda" |
| "For cryin' out loud" | **"Forcry"** | Exasperation | Frustration; often precedes leaving a room |

**Example Lindquist conversation (Year 300):**

> **Elder:** "Coldenuff?"
> **Young:** "Yabetja. Oofda, that tunnel's drafty, doncha."
> **Elder:** "Codworse. Least we got odiche."
> **Young:** "Fercute! You make that?"
> **Elder:** "Sneepas—" *(squeezes by)* "—I'll get you a plate."
> **Young:** *(bumps shelf)* "Ope."

Outsiders find this incomprehensible. The Lindquists consider it the sacred tongue of The Raver.

**Linguistic Note:** Scholars outside the family have tried to document "Lindquist-speak" but struggle because:
1. Pronunciation varies by generation
2. Context changes meaning dramatically
3. Some phrases are considered too sacred to explain
4. The Lindquists enjoy confusing outsiders

**Community Perception:**

| Era | View of the Lindquists |
|-----|---------------------|
| Year 1-50 | "Kaylee's weird family" |
| Year 50-100 | "The DJ people — keep them away from equipment" |
| Year 100-200 | "The Vinyl Cult — harmless but strange" |
| Year 200-300 | "The Lindquists — useful for dangerous missions" |

### "Volunteered" for Danger

**The Pattern:**

Whenever the community needed someone for a dangerous mission, the Lindquists were "suggested":

> "We need someone to scout the collapsed tunnel."
> "What about one of the Lindquist kids? They're always talking about exploring anyway."

**Why this worked:**

1. The Lindquists *wanted* to explore — they were looking for The Studio
2. The community didn't value them highly — acceptable losses
3. Each generation had members willing to take risks for the cult's goals
4. It became tradition: dangerous mission = ask the Lindquists first

**Lindquist Mission Casualties (partial list):**

| Year | Mission | Lindquist Member | Outcome |
|------|---------|---------------|---------|
| 87 AS | Tunnel scout | Claude Lindquist | Died (collapse) |
| 124 AS | Surface probe | Hélène Lindquist | Died (Hush exposure) |
| 156 AS | Crystal retrieval | André Lindquist | Died (unknown) |
| 189 AS | Tunnel expansion | Sophie Lindquist | Survived (injured) |
| 234 AS | Long-range scout | Jean Lindquist | Died (Hush exposure) |
| 267 AS | Studio search | Pierre Lindquist | Missing (presumed dead) |

**The irony:** The community treated them as expendable. But the Lindquists saw each death as martyrdom for the cause.

## The Hero's Generation

### The Test Mission (Year 299 AS)

**Background:**

The community has heard rumors of something unusual on the surface — a location matching descriptions from Kaylee's stories. A "studio" in the hills. After 265 years, someone finally has a lead on The Studio.

**The Mission:**
- Send a small team to investigate
- Test whether the location is viable
- Report back within 4 hours (standard Hush exposure limit)

**Who was sent:**
- **The Hero** (age 22) — current generation Lindquist
- **Cousin Julien** (age 25) — enthusiastic cult member
- **Cousin Marie** (age 20) — the "sensible" one who came to protect them

**What happened:**

| Time | Event |
|------|-------|
| Hour 0 | Team exits caves, begins surface journey |
| Hour 1 | Reach halfway point; all three feel fine |
| Hour 2 | Julien starts showing symptoms — dizziness, nausea |
| Hour 2.5 | Marie shows symptoms — confusion, motor issues |
| Hour 3 | Both cousins collapse. The Hero feels... fine. |
| Hour 3.5 | The Hero must choose: drag them back or continue |
| Hour 3.75 | Julien dies. Marie dies minutes later. |
| Hour 4 | The Hero, alone, reaches Studio Echo |

**What the Hero found:**
- An abandoned building, covered in vegetation
- A Crystal — one nobody knew existed
- Vinyl records (confirming the legends)
- Silence that should have killed them... but didn't

---

### Discovery: The Hero is Resilient

**The Return (Hour 8):**

The Hero returns to the caves. They've been on the surface for 8 hours. They should be dead.

**Community reaction:**
- Horror (the cousins are dead)
- Confusion (why is this one alive?)
- Fear (is this a monster wearing their face?)
- Realization (they've heard of this — Resilients)

**Medical examination:**
- Extended exposure test: 12 hours on surface, no symptoms
- Further test: 18 hours, mild discomfort but functional
- Diagnosis: Resilient — 24-hour tolerance instead of 4

**Community decision:**

> "The Lindquist found something. A Crystal. A 'studio.' They survived because they're Resilient — or cursed, depending on who you ask.
>
> Send them back. Send them alone. Let them claim whatever's out there.
>
> If they die, we lose one Lindquist. If they succeed, we gain a new settlement.
>
> Either way, the Vinyl Cult finally gets what they wanted."

## The Family Name

### Lindquist

**Origin:** Kaylee Lindquist — the Raver
**Meaning:** Common French surname (from "dark-skinned" or "Moorish")
**Year 300 Status:** ~40 living Lindquists in the Bresme caves

**The irony:** "Lindquist" sounds like "mort" (death) in French. The family that's always sent to die has death in their name. Scholars in Year 300 assume this is intentional. It's not — Kaylee just happened to have that surname.

## The Hero's Emotional Arc

**Starting state:**
- Carries family shame (the DJ ban)
- Carries family pride (the sacred mission)
- Carries survivor's guilt (the cousins)
- Carries exile's loneliness (sent away alone)
- Carries strange hope (they found The Studio)

**Central question:**
The Hero has spent their life believing in a mythology built on a stalker's date confusion. Now they have the power to build something real.

**Do they:**
- Continue the cult's traditions (even knowing they're absurd)?
- Reject their heritage and build something new?
- Try to actually learn to DJ (risking failure like Kaylee)?
- Share the Studio with the community that rejected them?
- Keep it for themselves?

The Hero is an outsider who's been given the keys to a kingdom. What they build is up to the player.
