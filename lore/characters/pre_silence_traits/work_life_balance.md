# Work-Life Balance

> **Category:** Profession & Career
>
> **Template Field:** `Work-Life Balance`
>
> **Dialog Impact:** Availability, stress, priorities

---

## Overview

Work-life balance (or its absence) determines how much of a character is consumed by work versus available for family, self, and community. For Sleepless members, poor balance compounds sleep deprivation.

---

## Balance Types

### Consumed by Work

**Definition:** Work dominates. 60+ hour weeks, always on call, identity = job.

**Dialog Markers:**
- Work always comes up
- "I'll sleep when I'm dead"
- Missed family events
- Phone checking constantly
- Guilt about not being present

**Speech Patterns:**
```
"I missed the recital. Again."
"My boss texted at 2am. While the baby was screaming."
"I can't take time off for this."
"Work doesn't care that I have kids."
"I'm in back-to-back meetings until 7."
```

**In Sleepless Context:**
- Least available for group activities
- Most desperate when they do show up
- May throw money at problems
- Work stress + home stress = breaking point

---

### Work-Dominant

**Definition:** Work comes first, but there's some boundary. Standard professional overwork.

**Dialog Markers:**
- Tries to protect weekends
- Fails regularly
- Feels guilty both ways
- "Just this once" (repeatedly)
- Career advancement pressure

**Speech Patterns:**
```
"I try to leave by 6, but it rarely happens."
"Weekends are supposed to be family time."
"I'm on the partner track. I can't ease up now."
"They say flexible, but they don't mean it."
"I'll make it up to the kids somehow."
```

---

### Struggling Balance

**Definition:** Actively trying to balance. Some wins, some losses. The fight is visible.

**Dialog Markers:**
- Calendar management references
- "I have to..."
- Negotiations with self and others
- Small victories celebrated
- Constant adjustment

**Speech Patterns:**
```
"I blocked off 5-7 for family. Non-negotiable. Mostly."
"We have a system. It falls apart sometimes."
"I'm learning to say no."
"This week was better. Last week was not."
"It's a juggling act. I drop things."
```

---

### Achieved Balance

**Definition:** Rare. Has boundaries that work. May have privilege, power, or priorities aligned.

**Dialog Markers:**
- Clear boundaries
- Leaves on time
- Others may resent them
- "My family comes first"
- May seem unrealistic to others

**Speech Patterns:**
```
"I don't check email after 6."
"I took the less prestigious position for the hours."
"My kids won't remember my title. They'll remember if I was there."
"It cost me promotions. Worth it."
"I'm ruthless about my time."
```

**In Sleepless Context:**
- Noise problem is extra frustrating (they did everything right)
- May have more energy for group
- Could be resented by more stressed members
- "I optimized everything. Except the neighbor."

---

### Life-Dominant

**Definition:** Work is minimal. Stay-at-home parent, part-time, or disengaged from career.

**Dialog Markers:**
- Home is the work
- May feel invisible/undervalued
- 24/7 availability for family
- Career identity minimized
- "I used to be..."

**Speech Patterns:**
```
"My job is the kids. It doesn't pay, but it's full-time."
"I gave up my career for this."
"Nobody asks what I do. Or cares."
"I'm always home. That's the problem—I can't escape the noise."
"I used to work in [field]. Now I work here."
```

**In Sleepless Context:**
- Can't escape during work hours
- May feel noise problem is their problem to solve
- More time for group, less energy
- Identity tied to home that's under siege

---

### Gig/Unstable

**Definition:** No traditional balance because no traditional job. Gig economy, freelance, between jobs.

**Dialog Markers:**
- Hustle vocabulary
- Multiple income streams
- No separation between work/home
- "I make my own hours" (meaning all of them)
- Financial stress

**Speech Patterns:**
```
"I'm always working. Or looking for work."
"There's no such thing as time off."
"I work from home. My home is a nightmare."
"Flexibility sounds nice until you realize it means no stability."
"I can't afford to not take the gig."
```

## Balance and Partnership

How balance interacts with relationship:

| Partner A | Partner B | Dynamic |
|-----------|-----------|---------|
| Consumed | Life-Dominant | One career, one home |
| Both Work-Dominant | Either | Scrambling together |
| Both Balanced | Either | Rare, resented by others |
| Work-Dominant | Gig/Unstable | Financial stress |
| Single parent | N/A | Everything alone |

---

## Balance and Sleep Deprivation

Work-life balance directly impacts ability to handle sleep-deprived parenting:

| Balance Type | Sleep Deprivation Effect |
|--------------|-------------------------|
| Consumed by Work | Catastrophic - no recovery time |
| Work-Dominant | Severe - weekends aren't enough |
| Struggling | Serious - but fighting |
| Achieved Balance | Manageable - if noise were the only issue |
| Life-Dominant | Constant exposure - can't leave |
