# Fields of Study

> **Category:** Education & Credentials
>
> **Template Field:** `Field of Study`
>
> **Dialog Impact:** Technical vocabulary, problem-solving frame, reference patterns

## Overview

What someone studied shapes how they think about problems, what analogies they reach for, and what jargon peppers their speech. Even if they don't work in their field, education leaves permanent marks on thinking patterns.

---

## Fields

### Engineering

**Thinking Pattern:** Systems, specifications, cause-and-effect, failure modes

**Dialog Markers:**
- "That won't work because..."
- Specifications and tolerances
- Failure mode analysis
- "What are the requirements?"
- Frustrated by vague goals

**Sample Phrases:**
```
"What's the actual specification here? What are we optimizing for?"
"That's a single point of failure."
"Let me draw a diagram."
"You can't just... there are constraints."
```

**In Sleepless Context:**
- Wants to solve the problem technically
- May design soundproofing solutions
- Frustrated when problem isn't solvable by engineering
- Could be drawn to Resonance technology development

---

### Medicine / Biology

**Thinking Pattern:** Diagnosis, symptoms, worst-case, bodily awareness

**Dialog Markers:**
- Clinical precision
- Body and health references
- Worst-case scenario thinking
- "What does the research say?"
- Comfortable with mortality

**Sample Phrases:**
```
"Sleep deprivation has measurable cognitive effects. This isn't theoretical."
"What are the symptoms? How long has it been going on?"
"At this rate, we're looking at long-term health consequences."
"I've seen what chronic stress does to people."
```

**In Sleepless Context:**
- Acutely aware of health impacts
- Can articulate medical consequences
- May provide credibility to health claims
- Ethical concerns about experimental solutions

---

### Law

**Thinking Pattern:** Precedent, technicality, liability, documentation

**Dialog Markers:**
- Qualifications and exceptions
- "Technically speaking..."
- Documentation instincts
- Contract and agreement language
- Liability awareness

**Sample Phrases:**
```
"We need to document everything. Times, dates, decibel levels."
"Technically, they're not violating any ordinance."
"I'd need to review the relevant statutes, but..."
"What's our exposure here? Worst case legally?"
```

**In Sleepless Context:**
- Knows what won't hold up legally
- Can navigate regulatory frameworks
- Documentation habits valuable
- May see legal limitations early

---

### Business / Finance

**Thinking Pattern:** ROI, value proposition, negotiation, stakeholder management

**Dialog Markers:**
- ROI framing
- "What's the value proposition?"
- Negotiation and deal language
- Stakeholder thinking
- Cost-benefit analysis

**Sample Phrases:**
```
"What's the cost-benefit here?"
"We need buy-in from all stakeholders."
"Let's look at this as an investment."
"What's our leverage? What do they want?"
```

**In Sleepless Context:**
- Approaches problem as transaction
- Thinks about incentives
- May try to "buy" solutions
- Useful for organizing resources

---

### Computer Science

**Thinking Pattern:** Debugging, abstraction, algorithms, edge cases

**Dialog Markers:**
- "It's basically just..."
- Debugging mindset
- Abstraction and patterns
- Edge cases and exceptions
- "What's the algorithm?"

**Sample Phrases:**
```
"This is basically a routing problem."
"What's the edge case we're not thinking about?"
"Let me walk through the logic."
"We need to isolate the variable here."
```

**In Sleepless Context:**
- May want to automate tracking
- Thinks about sound patterns systematically
- Could contribute to tech solutions
- Comfortable with complex systems

---

### Humanities (Philosophy, History, Literature)

**Thinking Pattern:** Context, narrative, precedent, meaning-making

**Dialog Markers:**
- "This reminds me of..."
- Historical parallels
- Philosophical framing
- Story and narrative focus
- "What does this mean?"

**Sample Phrases:**
```
"There's a historical precedent for this kind of collective action."
"Philosophically, this raises interesting questions about..."
"It's like [literary reference]. The parallels are striking."
"What's the story we're telling ourselves here?"
```

**In Sleepless Context:**
- Provides context and meaning
- May recognize patterns others miss
- Could rationalize or warn about ethical slopes
- Useful for group narrative and cohesion

---

### Social Sciences (Psychology, Sociology, Anthropology)

**Thinking Pattern:** Human systems, behavior patterns, group dynamics

**Dialog Markers:**
- "Research shows..."
- Studies and statistics
- Group behavior analysis
- "From a psychological perspective..."
- Systems + people

**Sample Phrases:**
```
"This is classic in-group/out-group dynamics."
"The research on sleep deprivation effects is clear."
"We're seeing collective behavior patterns here."
"What's the underlying need being expressed?"
```

**In Sleepless Context:**
- Understands group dynamics
- Can articulate psychological impacts
- May see manipulation tactics
- Valuable for understanding why people participate

---

### Arts (Fine Arts, Design, Music)

**Thinking Pattern:** Aesthetic, emotional, experiential, pattern-based

**Dialog Markers:**
- Aesthetic vocabulary
- Emotional expression
- "It feels like..."
- Pattern and form awareness
- Sound and rhythm sensitivity

**Sample Phrases:**
```
"There's something almost beautiful about how awful this is."
"The rhythm of it is what's unbearable."
"I experience it differently—it's not just volume, it's texture."
"Let me try to express what this feels like."
```

**In Sleepless Context:**
- Articulates sensory experience well
- May be more sensitive to sound
- Can communicate emotional impact
- Thinks about solutions aesthetically

---

### Natural Sciences (Physics, Chemistry, Biology)

**Thinking Pattern:** Empirical, measurable, experimental, natural laws

**Dialog Markers:**
- Measurement and precision
- "Let's test this"
- Natural phenomena explanations
- Skeptical of non-empirical claims
- "What's the mechanism?"

**Sample Phrases:**
```
"Sound propagation follows known principles."
"What's the actual decibel measurement?"
"Have we controlled for other variables?"
"The physics here is straightforward."
```

**In Sleepless Context:**
- Wants data and measurements
- Can explain acoustic principles
- May be dismissive of "feelings"
- Valuable for technical understanding

## Multiple Fields

Characters with interdisciplinary backgrounds:
- **Engineering + Business:** "What's the scalable solution?"
- **Psychology + Law:** "This is coercive, and I can prove it."
- **Medicine + Ethics:** "Just because we can doesn't mean we should."

---

## Field Limitations

Every field has blind spots:
- **Engineers:** May miss human factors
- **Lawyers:** May see only legal dimensions
- **Psychologists:** May over-psychologize
- **Business:** May miss non-transactional solutions
