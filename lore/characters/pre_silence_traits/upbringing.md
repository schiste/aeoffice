# Upbringing

> **Category:** Background & Origin
>
> **Template Field:** `Upbringing`
>
> **Dialog Impact:** Comfort zones, spatial preferences, crowd tolerance

---

## Overview

Where someone grew up shapes their relationship to space, noise, neighbors, and community. This directly affects how they experience noise problems and what solutions feel acceptable.

---

## Options

### Urban

**Definition:** Grew up in a city center. Dense housing, constant ambient noise, diverse population.

**Dialog Markers:**
- High baseline noise tolerance (usually)
- Comfortable with crowds, strangers
- Values convenience, walkability
- May see suburbs as "boring"
- References public transit, apartments, block life

**Speech Patterns:**
```
"I grew up in Brooklyn. Noise was just... the city."
"In my building, you knew everyone's business."
"I thought I was used to it. This is different."
"Suburbs feel dead to me. Too quiet."
```

**Noise Problem Context:**
- Higher tolerance means something really wrong
- May have tried urban solutions (311, doorman, landlord)
- Apartment living = limited escape options
- Neighbors above, below, beside

---

### Suburban

**Definition:** Single-family homes, car-dependent, planned communities. "Good schools" and "safe neighborhoods."

**Dialog Markers:**
- Privacy expectations
- Lawn/yard references
- "We moved here for the schools"
- HOA concerns
- Neighbor relations important

**Speech Patterns:**
```
"We bought in this neighborhood specifically for peace and quiet."
"The HOA is useless. They won't do anything."
"Our neighbors' parties go until 2am."
"This isn't what we signed up for."
```

**Noise Problem Context:**
- Expected quiet—feels betrayed
- May have invested heavily in the house
- "Not supposed to happen here" mentality
- Trapped by mortgage

---

### Rural

**Definition:** Countryside, farms, small towns. Space between neighbors, nature sounds, isolation.

**Dialog Markers:**
- Comfort with solitude
- May be overwhelmed by city noise
- Self-reliance emphasized
- Nature references
- "Back home..." nostalgia

**Speech Patterns:**
```
"I never heard another person unless I wanted to."
"City noise is constant. It never stops."
"Back home, the loudest thing was cicadas."
"I don't know how anyone lives like this."
```

**Noise Problem Context:**
- Lower tolerance for urban noise
- May have moved to city for work
- Feels trapped in unnatural environment
- Dreams of escaping back

---

### International

**Definition:** Grew up in another country. Different noise norms, cultural expectations, housing styles.

**Dialog Markers:**
- References "back in [country]"
- Different social norms around noise
- May feel isolated or misunderstood
- Cultural comparison points
- Language/accent markers

**Speech Patterns:**
```
"In Germany, this would never be tolerated."
"Where I'm from, we don't complain to authorities."
"Americans are so loud. I mean—present company excepted."
"The building standards here are terrible compared to [country]."
```

**Noise Problem Context:**
- Different baseline expectations
- May not know local channels
- Cultural isolation compounds problem
- Language barriers with neighbors

---

### Military/Mobile

**Definition:** Moved frequently growing up. Military family, diplomatic, or corporate relocations.

**Dialog Markers:**
- Adaptability
- Rootlessness
- Makes friends quickly (or doesn't bother)
- References many places
- "Home" is complicated

**Speech Patterns:**
```
"I've lived in twelve cities before I was eighteen."
"I can adapt to anything. Or I thought I could."
"Home is wherever the lease is."
"We moved every two years. Noise was temporary."
```

**Noise Problem Context:**
- Accustomed to tolerating temporary problems
- May struggle when they can't "just move"
- Less attached to specific place
- Network is scattered, not local

---

### Institutional

**Definition:** Grew up in boarding school, group home, foster care, or similar institutional setting.

**Dialog Markers:**
- Shared-space expertise
- Rules and structure orientation
- May be private about background
- Independence hard-won
- "Home" never quite right

**Speech Patterns:**
```
"I've never really had... quiet was never an option."
"I shared a room with five other kids."
"You learn to tune things out. Or you don't survive."
"Having my own space was the dream. Now this."
```

**Noise Problem Context:**
- May have higher tolerance
- First owned home = first real expectation of control
- Feels robbed of finally having peace
- May not recognize own coping mechanisms

---

## Hybrid Experiences

Many characters have mixed upbringings:

- **Suburban to Urban:** "I moved to the city for the energy. Now I miss the quiet."
- **Rural to Suburban:** "Suburbs felt like a compromise. Not wild, not cultured."
- **Urban to Suburban:** "We moved out for the kids. I miss the city."
- **International to American:** Navigating different noise cultures.
