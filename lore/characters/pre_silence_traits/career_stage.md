# Career Stage

> **Category:** Profession & Career
>
> **Template Field:** `Career Stage`
>
> **Dialog Impact:** Ambition, exhaustion, expertise, authority

---

## Overview

Where someone is in their career affects their energy, their priorities, and their relationship to work. It shapes how much they have to prove, how much power they wield, and how much they have left to give.

---

## Stages

### Entry Level

**Definition:** 0-3 years in field. Still learning, still proving, still eager.

**Dialog Markers:**
- Eager to contribute
- Learning jargon (sometimes misusing it)
- Proving self constantly
- Long hours normalized
- Looking up to seniors

**Speech Patterns:**
```
"I'm still learning, but I think..."
"At my level, we don't usually..."
"I want to be helpful here."
"Is this how it's supposed to work?"
"I'll stay late. It's fine."
```

**Behavioral Notes:**
- May not realize how tired they are
- Boundaries not yet established
- Identity wrapped in career progress
- Vulnerable to overwork

---

### Mid-Career

**Definition:** 5-15 years in field. Competent, plateau or climbing, aware of trade-offs.

**Dialog Markers:**
- Competent and frustrated
- Knows how things work (and don't)
- Seeking advancement or questioning it
- Work-life tension at peak
- "Is this it?"

**Speech Patterns:**
```
"I've been doing this long enough to know..."
"The system is broken, but we work within it."
"I thought I'd be further along by now."
"This is when the burnout hits."
"I can't keep doing this forever."
```

**Behavioral Notes:**
- Sleep deprivation at home + work pressure = crisis
- May be reconsidering everything
- Kids often arrive at this stage
- Most likely to be desperate

---

### Senior / Expert

**Definition:** 15+ years, recognized expertise, established position.

**Dialog Markers:**
- Confident in domain
- Mentoring others
- Protective of area/reputation
- "In my experience..."
- Seen it all before

**Speech Patterns:**
```
"I've seen this pattern before."
"Let me explain how this actually works."
"Young people don't understand..."
"I've earned the right to say this."
"Trust me on this one."
```

**Behavioral Notes:**
- Influence and resources
- May feel stuck or coasting
- Kids may be older (or second family)
- More likely to take action, less to vent

---

### Executive / Leadership

**Definition:** Decision-making authority. Manages teams, budgets, strategy.

**Dialog Markers:**
- Big picture focus
- Delegation language
- "My team will..."
- Meeting-heavy references
- Strategic vocabulary

**Speech Patterns:**
```
"I'll have my people look into this."
"What's the 30,000 foot view?"
"We need to align on priorities."
"I don't have time for details."
"Let me make some calls."
```

**Behavioral Notes:**
- Resources but no time
- May be disconnected from daily problems
- Used to problems being solved by others
- Frustrated when money/power don't fix it

---

### Burned Out

**Definition:** Any stage, but checked out. Going through motions.

**Dialog Markers:**
- Cynical about work
- Disengaged vocabulary
- "What's the point?"
- Doing minimum required
- Waiting for something

**Speech Patterns:**
```
"I stopped caring about performance reviews."
"They don't pay me enough to care about this."
"I'm just riding this out."
"My job is not my identity anymore."
"I show up. That's it."
```

**Behavioral Notes:**
- May have energy for other things
- Work problems feel meaningless
- Could be motivated by different cause
- Sleepless group might become new purpose

---

### Transitioning

**Definition:** Between roles. Laid off, career change, returning from leave.

**Dialog Markers:**
- Uncertain about identity
- "I used to be..."
- Exploring options
- Time flexibility (or desperation)
- Defensive about status

**Speech Patterns:**
```
"I'm between things right now."
"My background is in [field], but I'm exploring..."
"It's actually a good time to reassess."
"I have more time than I used to."
"This wasn't the plan."
```

**Behavioral Notes:**
- May have more time for group activities
- Identity in flux
- Financial stress possible
- Could throw self into cause

---

### Retired / Post-Career

**Definition:** No longer working full-time. By choice or not.

**Dialog Markers:**
- "When I was working..."
- Time wealth, sometimes energy poverty
- Advice-giving mode
- Legacy thinking
- May feel useless or liberated

**Speech Patterns:**
```
"In my day, we handled things differently."
"I have the time now, at least."
"Nobody listens to old people anymore."
"I've seen enough to know where this leads."
"What do I have to lose?"
```

**Behavioral Notes:**
- May not have young children (grandchildren?)
- Different noise problems (or helping adult children)
- Could be valuable for time/experience
- May be radicalized differently

## Career-Sleep Interactions

Career stage affects sleep deprivation experience:

| Stage | Work Stress | Home Stress | Total Load |
|-------|-------------|-------------|------------|
| Entry Level | High | Variable | Overwhelming |
| Mid-Career | High | High (kids) | Crisis point |
| Senior | Medium | Medium | Manageable |
| Executive | High (different) | Variable | Outsourced |
| Burned Out | Low (disengaged) | Variable | Depends |
| Transitioning | Variable | Variable | Uncertainty |
