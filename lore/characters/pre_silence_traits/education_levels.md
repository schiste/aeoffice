# Education Levels

> **Category:** Education & Credentials
>
> **Template Field:** `Highest Degree`
>
> **Dialog Impact:** Vocabulary complexity, citation habits, credentialism

---

## Overview

Educational attainment shapes vocabulary, reasoning patterns, and how characters engage with information. It affects their relationship to expertise, authority, and "official" knowledge.

---

## Levels

### None / Some High School

**Definition:** Didn't complete high school. May have dropped out, been expelled, or never had access.

**Dialog Markers:**
- Practical, experiential vocabulary
- Learns by doing, not reading
- May be defensive about education
- Distrusts "book smart" people
- Street smart, life smart

**Speech Patterns:**
```
"I didn't need a degree to figure that out."
"You learn more in a year of work than four years of school."
"Don't lecture me. I know what I know."
"All those experts and nobody can fix anything."
```

**Problem-Solving Approach:**
- Trial and error
- Asks people, not books
- Practical solutions over theoretical
- May dismiss research as "overthinking"

---

### High School / GED

**Definition:** Completed secondary education or equivalent. Standard baseline.

**Dialog Markers:**
- Functional vocabulary
- May overcompensate or dismiss higher education
- "I don't need college to..."
- Practical focus
- May have career-specific training

**Speech Patterns:**
```
"I did fine without college, thanks."
"Common sense is worth more than a degree."
"My parents didn't go to college either."
"Some of the dumbest people I know have degrees."
```

**Problem-Solving Approach:**
- Google and YouTube
- Asks friends and family
- Handles practical problems well
- May avoid complex paperwork

---

### Trade / Vocational

**Definition:** Specialized training in a trade: plumber, electrician, mechanic, medical tech, etc.

**Dialog Markers:**
- Expert vocabulary in their trade
- Proud of practical skills
- May look down on "useless" degrees
- Problem-solving is hands-on
- "Do it right or don't do it"

**Speech Patterns:**
```
"I can fix anything. Can your MBA do that?"
"That's not how [electricity/plumbing/engines] work."
"Whoever installed this didn't know what they were doing."
"I went straight to work. Best decision I made."
```

**Problem-Solving Approach:**
- Hands-on diagnosis
- Systematic troubleshooting
- Knows when to call another specialist
- Documents own work properly

---

### Some College

**Definition:** Attended but didn't complete degree. May have dropped out, paused, or be still working on it.

**Dialog Markers:**
- Exposed to academic thinking
- May have regrets or ongoing plans
- "I was going to..."
- Comfortable with some jargon
- Unfinished business energy

**Speech Patterns:**
```
"I had two years at State before... life happened."
"I've been meaning to go back."
"I know enough to follow along, at least."
"College wasn't for me. Or I wasn't for college."
```

**Problem-Solving Approach:**
- Mix of academic and practical
- Can research but may not trust it fully
- Defers to those who finished
- May underestimate own knowledge

---

### Bachelor's Degree

**Definition:** Completed four-year university degree. Baseline for many professional careers.

**Dialog Markers:**
- Field-specific jargon
- Comfortable with abstractions
- References college experience
- Credentials matter
- "Research shows..."

**Speech Patterns:**
```
"In my program, we learned..."
"I majored in [field], so I know something about this."
"The studies are pretty clear on this."
"College taught me how to learn, mostly."
```

**Problem-Solving Approach:**
- Research first, then act
- Looks for expert opinions
- Comfortable with complexity
- May overvalue credentials

---

### Master's Degree

**Definition:** Graduate-level specialized education. Often professional (MBA, MA, MS).

**Dialog Markers:**
- Deep specialization
- Comfortable with complexity
- May correct others' terminology
- Network from grad school
- "Well, technically..."

**Speech Patterns:**
```
"My thesis was actually on a related topic."
"The nuance here is important."
"At [grad school], we approached this differently."
"I've published in this area." [if academic]
```

**Problem-Solving Approach:**
- Methodical, structured
- Seeks primary sources
- Comfortable with ambiguity
- May overcomplicate simple problems

---

### PhD / Professional Degree

**Definition:** Doctoral or terminal professional degree (PhD, MD, JD, etc.). Highest formal education.

**Dialog Markers:**
- Expert vocabulary
- Citation habits ("Studies show...")
- May lecture unintentionally
- "Well, actually..."
- Credential-conscious

**Speech Patterns:**
```
"Actually, the literature is quite clear on this."
"Speaking as someone who's studied this..."
"In my dissertation, I found..."
"The methodology matters. You can't just..."
```

**Problem-Solving Approach:**
- Rigorous, evidence-based
- May be slow to act (needs more data)
- Defers to expertise (including own)
- Can miss forest for trees

---

### Multiple Degrees

**Definition:** Has degrees across different fields. Polymathic or career-changer.

**Dialog Markers:**
- Draws connections across fields
- May seem scattered or brilliant
- "From a [field] perspective..."
- Wide reference range
- "My first degree was in..."

**Speech Patterns:**
```
"This is actually where psychology and economics intersect."
"I did engineering before switching to law."
"Each field thinks differently about this."
"I've approached this from multiple angles."
```

## Education vs. Intelligence

Important distinctions:
- **Educated but not smart:** Has credentials, lacks judgment
- **Smart but not educated:** Has intelligence, lacks formal training
- **Both:** Credentials match ability
- **Neither self-perception:** Imposter syndrome or Dunning-Kruger
