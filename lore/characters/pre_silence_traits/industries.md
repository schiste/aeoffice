# Industries

> **Category:** Profession & Career
>
> **Template Field:** `Industry`
>
> **Dialog Impact:** Jargon, concerns, references, worldview

---

## Overview

The industry someone works in provides daily vocabulary, shapes their concerns, and determines what problems they find familiar. In the 2026-2034 era, industry also affects relationship to technology and Operation Lullaby.

---

## Industries

### Tech / Software

**Context (2026-2034):** Still the dominant industry. Startup culture, AI hype, remote work normalization.

**Dialog Markers:**
- Startup vocabulary (pivot, scale, disrupt)
- Solution-oriented thinking
- "Move fast and break things"
- Remote work references
- Product and feature mindset

**Vocabulary:**
```
"Let's iterate on this."
"What's the MVP here?"
"We need to scale this."
"That's a feature, not a bug."
"Let's async on this."
```

**In Sleepless Context:**
- May see noise problem as solvable with tech
- Comfortable with experimental solutions
- Could be involved in building Resonance tech
- Thinks in terms of platforms and networks

---

### Aerospace / Space

**Context (2026-2034):** Private space race, satellite networks, Mars missions.

**Dialog Markers:**
- Long timelines normal
- Orbital mechanics references
- "Launch windows"
- Precision and redundancy
- Scale of cosmic perspective

**Vocabulary:**
```
"That's not how orbital mechanics works."
"We're in a holding pattern until the next window."
"This needs triple redundancy."
"In space, there's no 'good enough.'"
"Earth problems seem small from orbit. But they're not."
```

**In Sleepless Context:**
- Satellite knowledge relevant to plot
- Understands global systems
- May have clearances, access
- "We put things in orbit. What could we put there?"

### Finance / VC

**Context (2026-2034):** Post-pandemic market volatility, crypto evolution, ESG debates.

**Dialog Markers:**
- Investment and returns language
- "Due diligence"
- Portfolio thinking
- Risk assessment
- "What's the play here?"

**Vocabulary:**
```
"What's our exposure on this?"
"This needs more due diligence."
"I'm bullish on a solution existing."
"Let me check my portfolio of options."
"Risk-adjusted, this makes sense."
```

**In Sleepless Context:**
- Could fund operations
- Thinks about incentives and returns
- May see conspiracy as "investment"
- Network of wealthy contacts

### Healthcare / Research

**Context (2026-2034):** Post-COVID healthcare system, biotech advances, research funding.

**Dialog Markers:**
- Clinical precision
- Ethics board references
- Grant and funding concerns
- Publication pressure
- Patient outcomes focus

**Vocabulary:**
```
"The IRB would never approve this."
"What does the peer-reviewed literature say?"
"We need a control group."
"Anecdotal evidence isn't data."
"First, do no harm. But also, do something."
```

**In Sleepless Context:**
- Understands health impacts of sleep deprivation
- Knows research processes
- May have ethical red lines
- Could validate or challenge claims

### Academia

**Context (2026-2034):** Tenure crises, adjunctification, publish or perish.

**Dialog Markers:**
- Publication references
- Tenure anxiety
- Teaching vs. research balance
- Sabbatical as goal
- Citation and credit concerns

**Vocabulary:**
```
"I'm up for tenure next year."
"The dean won't like this."
"My research touches on this."
"Publish or perish. Mostly perish."
"Let me cite my sources."
```

**In Sleepless Context:**
- Research skills valuable
- May have access to studies
- Publication could expose or legitimate
- Cautious about reputation

### Government / Policy

**Context (2026-2034):** Regulatory complexity, political polarization, bureaucratic inertia.

**Dialog Markers:**
- Regulatory and compliance language
- Stakeholder management
- "Through proper channels"
- Bureaucratic process awareness
- Political sensitivity

**Vocabulary:**
```
"There's a process for this."
"We need to loop in the relevant agencies."
"This crosses jurisdictional lines."
"The committee would need to review..."
"Off the record?"
```

**In Sleepless Context:**
- Knows regulatory landscape
- May know loopholes
- Could be obstacle or enabler
- "Proper channels" have failed them

### Service Industry

**Context (2026-2034):** Gig economy, automation threats, essential worker recognition.

**Dialog Markers:**
- Shift and schedule focus
- Customer service awareness
- Tips and wages
- Physical exhaustion normal
- "The customer" as presence

**Vocabulary:**
```
"I'm working doubles all week."
"Customers don't care about our problems."
"You learn to smile through it."
"My schedule changes every week."
"I can't call in sick. There's no one to cover."
```

**In Sleepless Context:**
- May have worst sleep deprivation (shifts + kids)
- Less flexibility to escape noise
- Different relationship to "expert" solutions
- Practical, immediate needs

### Legal Services

**Context (2026-2034):** Complex litigation, corporate law dominance.

**Dialog Markers:**
- Documentation emphasis
- Liability awareness
- Contract language
- Precedent references
- "I can't advise you to..."

**Vocabulary:**
```
"Document everything. Times, dates, witnesses."
"What's our legal exposure?"
"I'd need to review the relevant case law."
"This creates liability."
"I'm not saying do it. I'm saying hypothetically..."
```

**In Sleepless Context:**
- Knows legal limitations of noise complaints
- Can draft documents, letters
- Understands what won't hold up
- May know how to structure "plausible deniability"
