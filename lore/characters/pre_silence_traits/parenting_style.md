# Parenting Style

> **Category:** Parenting & Family Stress
>
> **Template Field:** `Parenting Style`
>
> **Dialog Impact:** How they talk about/to kids, patience, expectations

---

## Overview

Parenting style shapes how characters discuss their children, what solutions they'll accept, and how they respond to parenting advice (wanted or not).

---

## Styles

### Authoritative

**Definition:** High expectations + high responsiveness. Rules with reasoning. "Democratic" parenting.

**Characteristics:**
- Explains rules and reasons
- Listens to child's perspective
- Consistent boundaries
- Warm but firm
- Goals: independence, self-regulation

**Dialog Markers:**
```
"We've talked about why bedtime matters."
"I hear that you're upset. The answer is still no."
"Let's problem-solve this together."
"I believe in natural consequences."
"We're a team."
```

**Under Sleep Deprivation:**
- Explanations become shorter
- May snap then apologize
- Structure starts to slip
- "I'm too tired for democratic parenting tonight"

**In Sleepless Context:**
- Wants solutions that work with their approach
- May judge other parenting styles
- Concerned about long-term effects on kids
- "I refuse to let this make me a bad parent"

---

### Authoritarian

**Definition:** High expectations + low responsiveness. "Because I said so." Rules without discussion.

**Characteristics:**
- Clear rules, strict enforcement
- Limited explanation
- Obedience valued
- May seem harsh
- Goals: discipline, respect for authority

**Dialog Markers:**
```
"Because I said so."
"My house, my rules."
"When I was a kid, we didn't argue."
"The answer is no. Discussion over."
"Discipline is love."
```

**Under Sleep Deprivation:**
- Rules become harsher
- Patience for explanation vanishes
- May feel justified ("at least there's structure")
- Or may crack and regret

**In Sleepless Context:**
- May want forceful solutions
- Less concerned about gentleness
- "Whatever stops the noise"
- Could be driving toward action

---

### Permissive

**Definition:** Low expectations + high responsiveness. "Let kids be kids." Few rules, lots of love.

**Characteristics:**
- Few rules or boundaries
- Responsive to child's wants
- Avoids conflict
- May seem like friend more than parent
- Goals: happiness, freedom

**Dialog Markers:**
```
"I can't say no to that face."
"They'll grow out of it."
"I want them to have a happy childhood."
"I don't believe in forcing things."
"They're only young once."
```

**Under Sleep Deprivation:**
- Even fewer boundaries
- May give in to anything for quiet
- Guilt about inconsistency
- "I'm failing but I'm too tired to fix it"

**In Sleepless Context:**
- May be desperate—permissive isn't working
- Harder to change patterns
- "Nothing I try works"
- Could be most susceptible to any promise

---

### Uninvolved

**Definition:** Low expectations + low responsiveness. Hands-off, disengaged, minimal intervention.

**Characteristics:**
- Little oversight
- Basic needs met, emotional needs less so
- Disconnected from child's life
- May be overwhelmed or depressed
- Goals: survival

**Dialog Markers:**
```
"They're fine. Kids are resilient."
"I can't be everything."
"They figure it out."
"I don't have the energy for..."
[Doesn't mention kids much]
```

**Under Sleep Deprivation:**
- Even more withdrawn
- Kids may fend for themselves
- Guilt buried or absent
- Survival mode permanent

**In Sleepless Context:**
- May be silent about parenting issues
- "Not a parenting problem, a noise problem"
- Could be most desperate underneath
- May not engage with parenting discussions

---

### Helicopter

**Definition:** Overinvolved, anxious, controlling. Manages child's every experience.

**Characteristics:**
- Constant monitoring
- Intervenes quickly
- Anxious about outcomes
- May struggle to let go
- Goals: protection, success

**Dialog Markers:**
```
"I need to know where they are at all times."
"I've researched everything about this."
"I can't risk anything happening to them."
"I check on them every hour."
"What if something goes wrong?"
```

**Under Sleep Deprivation:**
- Anxiety spikes
- Control attempts intensify
- May burn out entirely
- "I can't protect them from this"

**In Sleepless Context:**
- Most anxious about children's sleep effects
- Researches obsessively
- May have most data on harm
- Could drive action through fear

---

### Attachment

**Definition:** Child-led, responsive, physically close. Emphasizes bonding and emotional security.

**Characteristics:**
- Co-sleeping, babywearing
- Responds to all needs immediately
- Child-led schedule
- Extended breastfeeding possible
- Goals: secure attachment

**Dialog Markers:**
```
"We co-sleep, so when they don't sleep, I don't sleep."
"I follow their cues."
"The bond is everything."
"I can't let them cry. I won't."
"They need to feel safe."
```

**Under Sleep Deprivation:**
- Most directly affected
- No separation between child sleep and own
- May be most exhausted
- "My body isn't mine"

**In Sleepless Context:**
- Especially tortured by noise
- Can't use some solutions (separate rooms)
- May feel judged by other styles
- "You don't understand our approach"

## Parenting Style Conflicts

Different styles create judgment and friction:

| Style A | Style B | Tension |
|---------|---------|---------|
| Authoritative | Authoritarian | "Too harsh" vs. "Too soft" |
| Authoritative | Permissive | "Set boundaries" vs. "Let them be" |
| Helicopter | Uninvolved | "You don't care" vs. "You're suffocating" |
| Attachment | Any | "You don't understand attachment theory" |

---

## Style and Noise Solutions

Different parenting styles prefer different solutions:

| Style | Solution Preference |
|-------|---------------------|
| Authoritative | Structured, explained, sustainable |
| Authoritarian | Whatever works, decisively |
| Permissive | Non-disruptive, gentle |
| Helicopter | Thoroughly researched, risk-free |
| Attachment | Child-centered, no separation |
