# Intelligence Types

> **Category:** Personal Traits
>
> **Template Field:** `Intelligence Type`
>
> **Dialog Impact:** How they solve problems, what they notice, how they explain

---

## Overview

Based loosely on Gardner's multiple intelligences, this trait identifies how a character thinks, learns, and approaches problems. It shapes their vocabulary, metaphors, and what they pay attention to.

---

## Types

### Logical-Mathematical

**Definition:** Systems thinking, patterns, logic, if-then reasoning.

**Thinking Pattern:**
- Cause and effect
- Categorization
- Pattern recognition
- Deductive reasoning
- Optimization

**Dialog Markers:**
```
"If we do X, then Y will happen."
"What's the variable here?"
"There's a pattern if you look."
"That doesn't logically follow."
"Let me walk through this systematically."
```

**Problem-Solving Approach:**
- Break into components
- Test hypotheses
- Eliminate variables
- Seek optimal solution

**Blind Spots:**
- May miss emotional factors
- Can over-systematize human problems
- Frustrated by illogical behavior

---

### Linguistic

**Definition:** Word-focused, enjoys language, persuasive, reads and writes easily.

**Thinking Pattern:**
- Thinks in words and narratives
- Sensitive to nuance
- Remembers through language
- Persuasion through rhetoric
- Enjoys wordplay

**Dialog Markers:**
```
"The word for this is..."
"Let me put it this way."
"Listen to how they phrase it."
"There's a story here."
"That's not quite what I meant."
```

**Problem-Solving Approach:**
- Talks through problems
- Persuades others to help
- Finds the right framing
- Documents and articulates

**Blind Spots:**
- May substitute talking for doing
- Can over-explain
- Frustrated by inarticulate situations

---

### Spatial

**Definition:** Thinks in maps, images, space, mental visualization.

**Thinking Pattern:**
- Pictures instead of words
- Mental models
- Spatial relationships
- Pattern in space
- Design-oriented

**Dialog Markers:**
```
"Picture this..."
"I can see it in my head."
"How does this fit together?"
"The layout matters."
"Let me draw it for you."
```

**Problem-Solving Approach:**
- Visualizes solutions
- Diagrams and maps
- Notices spatial patterns
- Thinks about positioning

**Blind Spots:**
- May struggle to verbalize thoughts
- Can miss non-visual information
- Frustrated by abstract concepts

---

### Musical

**Definition:** Rhythm-aware, pattern-sensitive through sound, thinks in beats and tones.

**Thinking Pattern:**
- Rhythm and timing
- Tonal patterns
- Sound sensitivity
- Temporal awareness
- Pattern through repetition

**Dialog Markers:**
```
"There's a rhythm to this."
"Something sounds off."
"It's like a pattern, a beat."
"Timing matters."
"I need to tune into this."
```

**Problem-Solving Approach:**
- Notices timing and rhythm
- Seeks harmony and resonance
- Sensitive to discordance
- Learns through repetition

**Blind Spots:**
- May be oversensitive to sounds
- Can miss visual or logical patterns
- Frustrated by arrhythmic situations

**In Sleepless Context:**
- Uniquely tortured by noise
- May be especially motivated
- Could contribute to sound-based solutions

---

### Bodily-Kinesthetic

**Definition:** Physical intelligence, learns by doing, body awareness.

**Thinking Pattern:**
- Physical metaphors
- Learning through action
- Body as knowledge
- Hands-on preference
- Movement-oriented

**Dialog Markers:**
```
"Show me, don't tell me."
"I need to try it."
"My gut says..."
"It feels wrong."
"Let me handle this."
```

**Problem-Solving Approach:**
- Trial and error
- Physical testing
- Trusts body knowledge
- Prefers doing over planning

**Blind Spots:**
- May act before thinking
- Can dismiss theoretical solutions
- Frustrated by passive situations

---

### Interpersonal

**Definition:** Reads people, understands social dynamics, empathic.

**Thinking Pattern:**
- Asks about feelings
- Notices social dynamics
- Predicts behavior
- Mediates conflicts
- Relationship-focused

**Dialog Markers:**
```
"How is everyone feeling about this?"
"I think [person] needs..."
"Let me talk to them."
"The real issue is between..."
"We need everyone on the same page."
```

**Problem-Solving Approach:**
- Works through people
- Builds coalitions
- Addresses emotions
- Seeks consensus

**Blind Spots:**
- May over-prioritize feelings
- Can neglect technical solutions
- Frustrated when people don't cooperate

---

### Intrapersonal

**Definition:** Self-reflective, philosophical, introspective, self-aware.

**Thinking Pattern:**
- Internal processing
- Self-knowledge
- Philosophical framing
- Meaning-seeking
- Internal dialogue

**Dialog Markers:**
```
"I've been thinking about how I feel..."
"What does this mean for us?"
"I know myself well enough to..."
"The deeper question is..."
"I need time to reflect."
```

**Problem-Solving Approach:**
- Internal processing first
- Seeks meaning
- Self-aware decision-making
- May work alone

**Blind Spots:**
- May be too internal
- Can seem disconnected
- Frustrated by surface-level engagement

---

### Naturalistic

**Definition:** Categorizes, notices environmental details, systems in nature.

**Thinking Pattern:**
- Classification
- Environmental awareness
- Pattern in nature
- "Types" of things
- Sorting and organizing

**Dialog Markers:**
```
"There are different types of this."
"It's like in nature when..."
"The environment matters."
"I notice when things are off."
"These belong together."
```

**Problem-Solving Approach:**
- Categorizes the problem
- Looks at environment
- Finds natural patterns
- Organizes information

**Blind Spots:**
- May over-categorize
- Can miss individual uniqueness
- Frustrated by things that don't fit categories

## Multiple Intelligences in Characters

Most characters have 2-3 dominant types:

| Combination | Character Type |
|-------------|---------------|
| Logical + Spatial | Engineer, architect |
| Linguistic + Interpersonal | Politician, therapist |
| Musical + Bodily | Performer, athlete |
| Intrapersonal + Linguistic | Writer, philosopher |
| Naturalistic + Spatial | Biologist, environmental designer |
