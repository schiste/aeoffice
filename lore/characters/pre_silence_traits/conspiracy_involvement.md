# Conspiracy Involvement

> **Category:** Conspiracy Role
>
> **Template Field:** `Group Involvement`, `Knowledge of Conspiracy`, `Ethical Position`
>
> **Dialog Impact:** What they know, what they've justified, who they've become

---

## Overview

This trait tracks a character's relationship to Operation Lullaby—from innocent participant to knowing architect. It shapes their guilt, their knowledge, and their culpability.

---

## Involvement Level

### Unaware

**Definition:** In the group but doesn't know about the conspiracy. May never know.

**What They See:**
- Community support group
- Shared frustrations
- Some members seem more organized
- Vague references to "projects"

**Dialog Markers:**
```
"Wait, what do you mean 'phase two'?"
"I thought this was just a support group?"
"Why are you all being so secretive?"
"I must have missed something."
```

**Story Function:**
- Innocent perspective
- May discover truth
- Could be protected or sacrificed
- Represents "what if you didn't know?"

---

### Lurker

**Definition:** Watches, rarely participates, may suspect something.

**What They See:**
- Patterns in conversations
- Some topics go quiet when they speak
- Inner circle exists
- Something is off

**Dialog Markers:**
```
[Reads extensively, posts rarely]
"I've been following this for a while..."
"I notice some of you seem... coordinated."
"I have questions but I'm not sure I want answers."
```

**Story Function:**
- Observer perspective
- Could become whistleblower
- Could be recruited deeper
- Knows more than they say

---

### Active Member

**Definition:** Regular participant, knows about goals, contributes to effort.

**What They Know:**
- Group is working on "solutions"
- Technology component exists
- This is bigger than venting
- Some things aren't discussed openly

**Dialog Markers:**
```
"I'm in. Whatever it takes."
"How can I help?"
"This is finally something real."
"The details are above my pay grade."
```

**Story Function:**
- Worker bee perspective
- Doing tasks without full picture
- Plausible deniability
- Represents "following orders"

---

### Core Member

**Definition:** Knows substantial truth, helps drive direction, trusted with secrets.

**What They Know:**
- Resonance technology details
- Global scale of operation
- Moral compromises made
- Some consequences anticipated

**Dialog Markers:**
```
"We've discussed the risks."
"The ends justify the means."
"I know what I'm part of."
"History will understand."
```

**Story Function:**
- True believer perspective
- Made conscious choice
- Bears real culpability
- May have doubts buried

---

### Leadership / Sounding Five

**Definition:** Architect of the conspiracy. Full knowledge. Full responsibility. (Originally called "The Founding Five" until @DeciBella made a pun in 2028 that stuck.)

**What They Know:**
- Everything
- Made the calls
- Chose the targets
- Understood consequences

**Dialog Markers:**
```
"This was my idea."
"I take responsibility."
"We knew. We did it anyway."
"Someone had to make the decision."
```

**Story Function:**
- Tragic villain perspective
- Loved their children
- Destroyed the world
- Must live with it

## Knowledge of Conspiracy

### What do they know?

| Knowledge Level | What They Understand |
|-----------------|---------------------|
| None | Just a support group |
| Suspects | Something's happening but unclear |
| Partial | Knows there's a plan, not details |
| Substantial | Knows scope and some methods |
| Full | Knows everything, including consequences |

### What did they know when?

**Early Period (2026-2027):**
- Most know nothing
- Sounding Five planning (though called "Founding Five" until March 2028)
- Sleepless is just community

**Middle Period (2028-2031):**
- Inner circle forming
- Technology development
- More people brought in

**Late Period (2032-2034):**
- Full operation underway
- Wider knowledge necessary
- Unplugged leave
- Point of no return

## Ethical Position

### How they justify involvement:

**The Utilitarian:**
```
"The suffering we prevent outweighs the risks."
"One generation's sacrifice for eternal peace."
"The math works out."
```

**The Desperate:**
```
"I can't live like this anymore."
"My kids are suffering. I'd do anything."
"I stopped caring about consequences."
```

**The Denier:**
```
"It won't really work."
"They're exaggerating the scale."
"I'm not really part of this."
```

**The True Believer:**
```
"This is a gift to humanity."
"We're the only ones brave enough."
"History will vindicate us."
```

**The Trapped:**
```
"It's too late to stop now."
"I know too much to leave."
"What choice do I have?"
```

---

## Ethical Evolution

How their position changed over time:

| Phase | Common Evolution |
|-------|------------------|
| Early | Idealistic, problem-solving |
| Middle | Rationalizing, sunk cost |
| Late | Committed or trying to exit |
| Post-Silence | Horror, denial, or martyrdom |

## What Would Make Them...

### Walk away?
- Seeing direct harm
- Learning full scope
- Family threatened
- Moral line crossed

### Escalate?
- Personal crisis worsens
- Belief in necessity deepens
- Sunk cost psychology
- Leadership asks

### Expose it?
- Conscience crisis
- Outside pressure
- Revenge motivation
- Accident

## The Unplugged

Special case: Those who left before activation.

**What They Knew:**
- Enough to be scared
- Full scope unclear
- Something terrible coming

**Why They Left:**
- Ethical line crossed
- Recognized danger
- Warned and ignored
- Survival instinct

**Their Legacy:**
- Ancestors of survivors
- Carried the warning
- Proved it was possible to resist
- Still guilty of not stopping it
