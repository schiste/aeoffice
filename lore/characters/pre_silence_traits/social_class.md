# Social Class

> **Category:** Background & Origin
>
> **Template Field:** `Social Class`
>
> **Dialog Impact:** How they view money, status, institutions

---

## Overview

Social class in the pre-Silence era (2026-2034) shapes vocabulary, expectations, problem-solving approaches, and interpersonal dynamics. A character's class background affects everything from how they talk about money to their comfort with institutional systems.

---

## Options

### Working Class

**Definition:** Manual labor, service industry, trades. Income from hourly wages. Often lacks generational wealth or safety net.

**Dialog Markers:**
- Practical, concrete vocabulary
- Skepticism toward "elites" and "experts"
- Values hard work, loyalty, self-reliance
- May be defensive about education
- Comfort with physical solutions

**Speech Patterns:**
- Direct, no-nonsense
- May use regional dialect more strongly
- Less likely to use corporate jargon
- "I work for my money"
- "These people don't know what real work is"

**Relationship to Sleepless in Decibels:**
- May have joined for practical solutions, not community
- Less likely to have resources for lawyers, moving, etc.
- Noise problems may stem from cramped housing
- May feel like an outsider if group skews professional

**Example Phrases:**
```
"Look, I don't need a study to tell me my kid can't sleep."
"Must be nice to have options."
"I've been up since five, so spare me the theories."
"You try working doubles and then dealing with this."
```

---

### Middle Class

**Definition:** White-collar professionals, small business owners, teachers, nurses. Stable income, some savings, education-focused.

**Dialog Markers:**
- Education is pathway to success
- Worried about "falling behind"
- Invests in children's futures
- Values stability, planning, credentials
- Comfortable with institutions (mostly)

**Speech Patterns:**
- Generally "correct" grammar
- Uses professional terminology
- References studies, experts, articles
- "We need to research this"
- "What do the professionals say?"

**Relationship to Sleepless in Decibels:**
- The core demographic of the group
- Believes in solutions, systems, proper channels
- Has tried "doing everything right"
- Frustrated that normal channels haven't worked

**Example Phrases:**
```
"We've tried everything the pediatrician suggested."
"I read an article about this exact thing."
"There has to be a process for this, right?"
"We moved to a good neighborhood specifically for this."
```

---

### Upper Middle

**Definition:** Senior professionals, executives, successful entrepreneurs. High income, investment portfolios, private education for kids.

**Dialog Markers:**
- Expects competence from others
- Impatient with inefficiency
- Has professional networks
- Thinks in terms of solutions and resources
- May be unaware of own privilege

**Speech Patterns:**
- Confident, commanding
- Business vocabulary bleeds into personal life
- "Let's solve this" mentality
- References to "my lawyer," "my advisor"
- "This shouldn't be that hard"

**Relationship to Sleepless in Decibels:**
- May have tried to throw money at the problem first
- Brings resources, connections, strategy
- Can be frustrated when money doesn't fix it
- May naturally assume leadership roles

**Example Phrases:**
```
"I've already had my attorney send a letter."
"This is a solvable problem. We just need the right approach."
"I know people who know people."
"Let me make some calls."
```

---

### Wealthy

**Definition:** Generational wealth, significant assets, multiple properties. Money is not a daily concern.

**Dialog Markers:**
- Thinks in investments, assets, long-term
- Expects service and responsiveness
- May be removed from everyday concerns
- Comfortable with power, influence
- Can be either generous or oblivious

**Speech Patterns:**
- Understated about money (doesn't brag)
- References travel, properties, staff
- "I'll just have [person] handle it"
- May not understand others' constraints
- Uses passive constructions ("It was arranged")

**Relationship to Sleepless in Decibels:**
- Rare in the group—can usually escape problems
- If present, the noise problem must be truly inescapable
- May fund group activities without realizing impact
- Could be target for resentment or seen as savior

**Example Phrases:**
```
"We could just summer elsewhere, but the children's school..."
"I've had people look into this."
"Money isn't the issue. It's the principle."
"We'll host at our place—there's plenty of room."
```

---

### Academic

**Definition:** University professors, researchers, intellectuals. May not be wealthy but exists in ideas-focused environment.

**Dialog Markers:**
- Ideas and concepts over practical concerns
- Credential-conscious (who has what degree)
- Heavy use of jargon from their field
- May be impractical about implementation
- Values rigor, evidence, methodology

**Speech Patterns:**
- Complex sentence structures
- Citations and references
- "The literature suggests..."
- "From a [field] perspective..."
- May lecture unintentionally

**Relationship to Sleepless in Decibels:**
- Brings research skills to the problem
- May have access to unpublished studies
- Can be invaluable or frustrating
- May theorize when action is needed

**Example Phrases:**
```
"Actually, the research on sleep disruption is quite clear about this."
"I wrote my dissertation on a related topic."
"The methodology here is important."
"Has anyone considered the sociological implications?"
```

---

### Tech Elite

**Definition:** Startup founders, successful tech executives, venture capitalists, senior engineers at major companies. High income combined with "disruption" mindset.

**Dialog Markers:**
- Believes technology solves problems
- Startup vocabulary: scaling, iterating, pivoting
- "Move fast and break things" mentality
- May underestimate social/regulatory barriers
- Comfortable with risk and experimentation

**Speech Patterns:**
- Jargon-heavy (MVP, disrupt, synergy)
- Data-driven arguments
- "Let's just build something"
- References to Silicon Valley culture
- May speak in pitches

**Relationship to Sleepless in Decibels:**
- Likely architects of technological solutions
- Sees the group as a "community" to scale
- May have resources to fund development
- Could be the ones who suggest Resonance tech

**Example Phrases:**
```
"What if we just build an app for this?"
"We need to think bigger. This isn't scalable."
"I know some engineers who might be interested."
"Let's iterate on this. What's the MVP?"
```

## Mixing Classes

Characters may have:
- **Class mobility:** Grew up working class, now upper middle
- **Class disconnect:** Academic background, broke from student loans
- **Class anxiety:** Middle class, terrified of falling
- **Class denial:** Wealthy but identifies as "regular people"

These tensions create interesting dialog:
```
"I know I sound like one of those people, but I worked for this."
"My parents would laugh at me complaining about this."
"I'm not rich—we just have a comfortable life."
```
