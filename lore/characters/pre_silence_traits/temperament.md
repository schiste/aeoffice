# Temperament

> **Category:** Personal Traits
>
> **Template Field:** `Temperament`
>
> **Dialog Impact:** Emotional baseline, reaction speed, conversational energy

---

## Overview

Temperament is the emotional baseline—the default state a character returns to. It shapes how they enter conversations, how they react to events, and how others experience them.

---

## Types

### Sanguine

**Definition:** Optimistic, talkative, warm, easily distracted, enthusiastic.

**Emotional Baseline:**
- Default state is positive
- Energy is outward
- Seeks connection and stimulation
- Moves on quickly from setbacks
- May seem superficial to others

**Dialog Markers:**
```
"Oh! That reminds me of—"
"This is going to work out. I can feel it."
"Let me tell you what happened today..."
"I mean, it could be worse, right?"
"Anyone want to grab a drink after this?"
```

**Under Sleep Deprivation:**
- Energy crashes hard
- Optimism becomes desperate
- May cycle between manic and depleted
- Social needs unmet = worse coping

**In Sleepless Context:**
- Brings energy to group
- May be dismissed as not understanding severity
- Good for morale, bad for planning
- Could be deeply affected when optimism fails

---

### Choleric

**Definition:** Driven, decisive, impatient, commanding, results-oriented.

**Emotional Baseline:**
- Default state is active
- Oriented toward action and goals
- Impatient with delay or excuses
- Natural leader or bulldozer
- May seem aggressive

**Dialog Markers:**
```
"What's the plan? We need a plan."
"This is taking too long."
"I'll just do it myself."
"Whose fault is this?"
"We're wasting time."
```

**Under Sleep Deprivation:**
- Impatience becomes rage
- Decision quality drops but speed doesn't
- May alienate allies
- Action without thought

**In Sleepless Context:**
- Drives group to action
- May push for solutions too fast
- Could escalate prematurely
- Frustrated when problems resist force

---

### Melancholic

**Definition:** Thoughtful, detail-oriented, pessimistic, deep, prone to brooding.

**Emotional Baseline:**
- Default state is contemplative
- Sees problems and flaws first
- Values depth over breadth
- Prepares for worst case
- May seem depressive to others

**Dialog Markers:**
```
"Have we considered what could go wrong?"
"I'm not pessimistic, I'm realistic."
"I've been thinking about this a lot..."
"This won't end well."
"I need time to process."
```

**Under Sleep Deprivation:**
- Pessimism spirals
- Paralysis by analysis
- May become genuinely depressed
- Catastrophizing intensifies

**In Sleepless Context:**
- Spots risks others miss
- May be right about dangers
- Could be written off as "always negative"
- May predict disaster before others see it

---

### Phlegmatic

**Definition:** Calm, reliable, passive, peacemaking, stable.

**Emotional Baseline:**
- Default state is stable
- Even-keeled through crises
- Prefers harmony
- May avoid conflict
- Seems unflappable

**Dialog Markers:**
```
"Let's not panic."
"I'm sure there's a reasonable explanation."
"Can't we all just get along?"
"I'll do whatever you think is best."
"It's fine. I'm fine."
```

**Under Sleep Deprivation:**
- Stability becomes numbness
- Passivity becomes paralysis
- May withdraw rather than cope
- "Fine" masks suffering

**In Sleepless Context:**
- Calming presence
- May be exploited by others
- Could enable bad decisions through passivity
- Finally breaking is dramatic

---

### Volatile

**Definition:** Unpredictable, intense, shifting between states, hard to read.

**Emotional Baseline:**
- No stable default state
- Swings between highs and lows
- Intense in all directions
- May surprise themselves
- Others walk on eggshells

**Dialog Markers:**
```
"I don't know how I'm going to feel tomorrow."
[One message angry, next message apologizing, next enthusiastic]
"Don't mind me. I'll be different in an hour."
"I know I'm a lot. I can't help it."
```

**Under Sleep Deprivation:**
- Swings intensify
- Cycles accelerate
- May become destabilizing
- Others can't predict

**In Sleepless Context:**
- Wild card member
- Could escalate or de-escalate randomly
- May form intense bonds then conflicts
- Could be scapegoated or protected

## Temperament Under Pressure

Sleep deprivation reveals and distorts temperament:

| Temperament | Early Sleep Loss | Severe Sleep Loss |
|-------------|------------------|-------------------|
| Sanguine | Still optimistic, tired | Desperate cheer or crash |
| Choleric | More aggressive | Rage-driven decisions |
| Melancholic | Deeper pessimism | Paralysis or despair |
| Phlegmatic | Maintaining calm | Numb, checked out |
| Volatile | Wider swings | Completely unpredictable |

---

## Temperament Combinations

Most people blend temperaments:

- **Sanguine-Choleric:** Enthusiastic leader, may overwhelm
- **Choleric-Melancholic:** Driven but pessimistic, harsh
- **Melancholic-Phlegmatic:** Thoughtful but passive, withdrawn
- **Sanguine-Phlegmatic:** Pleasant but may lack depth
- **Volatile-Any:** Adds unpredictability to any base

---

## Temperament vs. Mood

Important distinction:
- **Temperament:** Stable trait, baseline state
- **Mood:** Current emotional state, fluctuates

A melancholic person can have a good day.
A sanguine person can have a terrible week.
Temperament is about the center they return to.
