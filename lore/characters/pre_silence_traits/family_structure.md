## Overview

Family structure shapes how characters talk about relationships, handle conflict, and approach community. For Sleepless in Decibels members, family structure directly impacts their noise problems and available support.

---

## Options

### Nuclear

**Definition:** Traditional two-parent household with biological or adopted children. Both parents present and involved.

**Dialog Markers:**
- References "my wife/husband" or "my partner"
- "We" decisions with spouse
- Shared parenting language
- May take support system for granted
- Traditional holiday/milestone references

**Speech Patterns:**
```
"We talked about it and decided..."
"My husband and I are both at our limit."
"We take turns with the night shifts."
"Let me check with [spouse] first."
```

**In Sleepless Context:**
- Can share burden of sleep deprivation
- May disagree about solutions (source of tension)
- Often one partner more active in group than other
- "We need this to work for both of us"

---

### Single Parent

**Definition:** One parent raising children alone. May be divorced, widowed, or never partnered. Sole decision-maker and caregiver.

**Dialog Markers:**
- "I" not "we" for decisions
- References schedule juggling
- Underlying guilt and exhaustion
- Fiercely independent but stretched thin
- May resent couples' complaints

**Speech Patterns:**
```
"I don't have backup. When she doesn't sleep, I don't sleep."
"Must be nice to have someone to take the 3am shift."
"I make all the decisions because I have to."
"Daycare closes at 6. I have no margin for error."
```

**In Sleepless Context:**
- Most desperate members
- Time-poor, can't attend everything
- May be most motivated for radical solutions
- Isolation makes online community especially valuable

---

### Extended

**Definition:** Multigenerational or large family network. Grandparents, aunts, uncles, cousins involved in daily life.

**Dialog Markers:**
- References relatives frequently
- Family obligations and expectations
- "My mother thinks..." "My uncle says..."
- Built-in advice (wanted or not)
- Family gatherings, traditions, drama

**Speech Patterns:**
```
"My mother watches the kids on Wednesdays."
"Half my family has opinions about this."
"We can't move—we're two blocks from my parents."
"Family dinner is non-negotiable, even when I'm dead on my feet."
```

**In Sleepless Context:**
- Has support network (but also pressure)
- Family opinions may conflict with group
- Privacy concerns about involvement
- "What would my family think if they knew..."

---

### Blended

**Definition:** Step-parents, step-children, half-siblings. Formed from previous relationships combining.

**Dialog Markers:**
- Navigates multiple parenting styles
- References "his kids," "my kids," "our kids"
- Diplomatic about ex-partners
- Complex scheduling
- Loyalty tensions

**Speech Patterns:**
```
"On the weeks we have all four kids, it's chaos."
"My stepson's mother has different rules."
"We're still figuring out what works for everyone."
"It's complicated—whose kid is whose noise problem?"
```

**In Sleepless Context:**
- Complex household dynamics
- Noise problems may only exist part-time
- Solutions need to work for multiple configurations
- May feel judged for "non-traditional" family

---

### Estranged

**Definition:** Has family but doesn't speak to them. Cut off by choice or circumstance. Often undisclosed trauma.

**Dialog Markers:**
- Avoids family topics
- Deflects personal questions
- Self-reliance to the extreme
- May be defensive when family mentioned
- "Chosen family" language

**Speech Patterns:**
```
"I don't really do family stuff."
"Long story. Let's just say I handle things myself."
"Holidays? I work through them."
"My parents aren't in the picture. Haven't been for years."
```

**In Sleepless Context:**
- The group may become surrogate family
- No fallback support—high stakes
- May be wary of getting too close
- Could be especially loyal once trusted

---

### Childless

**Definition:** No children by choice or circumstance. May have strong opinions about parenting from outside.

**Dialog Markers:**
- Different life priorities
- May not understand parent exhaustion
- Could be sympathetic or judgmental
- "When I was a kid..." references
- Free time that parents envy

**Speech Patterns:**
```
"I mean, I don't have kids, so maybe I don't get it..."
"Couldn't you just...?" [oversimplified solution]
"My neighbor's kids are the problem. I didn't sign up for this."
"I value my sleep. That's why I made certain choices."
```

**In Sleepless Context:**
- May be in group for noise complaints, not parenting
- Could be target for "you don't understand" resentment
- Different motivation but shared problem
- Valuable outside perspective or friction point

## Family Dynamics Subtypes

### Supportive Partner
- "My partner is 100% on board."
- "We're a team. Whatever it takes."

### Resistant Partner
- "They think I'm overreacting."
- "We fight about this constantly."

### Absent Partner
- "Work takes them away a lot."
- "I'm basically a single parent half the time."

### Interfering In-Laws
- "His mother has opinions about everything."
- "They mean well but they're making it worse."

### Helpful Extended Family
- "My sister is a godsend."
- "We couldn't manage without my parents."
