# Political Leaning

> **Category:** Beliefs & Values
>
> **Template Field:** `Political Leaning`
>
> **Dialog Impact:** Triggers, assumptions, vocabulary, worldview

---

## Overview

Political leaning shapes vocabulary, triggers, assumptions about institutions, and how characters interpret events. It affects who they trust and how they frame problems.

---

## Leanings

### Progressive / Left

**Beliefs:**
- Systemic change needed
- Social justice priority
- Corporate skepticism
- Government can help (if reformed)
- Inequality is the problem

**Vocabulary:**
- "Systems of oppression"
- "Structural issues"
- "Intersectionality"
- "Corporate greed"
- "Late-stage capitalism"

**Dialog Markers:**
```
"This is a systemic issue, not individual."
"Of course corporations are behind this."
"The system is designed this way."
"Check your privilege."
"Who's being harmed that we're not seeing?"
```

**Triggers:**
- Dismissing systemic analysis
- "Both sides" framing
- Corporate apologetics
- Individual responsibility framing

**Relationship to Operation Lullaby:**
- May see it as justified against capitalism
- Or may see it as authoritarian overreach
- Complicated relationship

---

### Liberal

**Beliefs:**
- Moderate reform works
- Institutions can improve
- Compromise is valuable
- Progress is gradual
- Rules and norms matter

**Vocabulary:**
- "Incremental change"
- "Working within the system"
- "Reasonable compromise"
- "Both sides have points"
- "The process"

**Dialog Markers:**
```
"Let's work through proper channels."
"There's got to be a middle ground."
"Change takes time."
"We need to bring people together."
"The institutions exist for a reason."
```

**Triggers:**
- Extremism (either direction)
- Abandoning process
- "Burn it down" rhetoric
- Cynicism about democracy

**Relationship to Operation Lullaby:**
- Process-oriented concern
- "Did we try everything else first?"
- May be last to join, first to regret

---

### Centrist

**Beliefs:**
- "Both sides" have valid points
- Pragmatic above ideology
- Avoids political conflict
- "Common sense" over theory
- System basically works

**Vocabulary:**
- "Both sides"
- "Let's not get political"
- "Common sense"
- "Extremes are the problem"
- "I'm socially liberal, fiscally conservative"

**Dialog Markers:**
```
"Can we keep politics out of this?"
"Both parties are equally bad."
"I just want what works."
"The truth is somewhere in the middle."
"Let's focus on practical solutions."
```

**Triggers:**
- Being labeled politically
- Partisan rhetoric
- Being forced to choose sides
- Ideological arguments

**Relationship to Operation Lullaby:**
- "As long as it works"
- May not engage with ethics
- "I'm not political, I just want to sleep"

---

### Conservative

**Beliefs:**
- Traditional values matter
- Individual responsibility
- Skeptical of change
- Government should be limited
- Order and stability

**Vocabulary:**
- "Personal responsibility"
- "Traditional values"
- "Law and order"
- "Fiscal responsibility"
- "Family values"

**Dialog Markers:**
```
"People need to take responsibility."
"We've always done it this way for a reason."
"Government can't fix everything."
"What about the family?"
"Respect for authority matters."
```

**Triggers:**
- Dismissing tradition
- Expanding government
- Moral relativism
- Disrespect for authority

**Relationship to Operation Lullaby:**
- Law and order concerns
- But also: protecting family
- Complex relationship with tech solution

---

### Libertarian

**Beliefs:**
- Individual freedom above all
- Government is the problem
- Market solutions preferred
- Property rights sacred
- Leave people alone

**Vocabulary:**
- "Freedom"
- "Government overreach"
- "Free market"
- "My property, my rules"
- "Don't tread on me"

**Dialog Markers:**
```
"The government has no business..."
"It's my property, my decision."
"The market will sort it out."
"Regulation is the problem."
"Personal liberty is non-negotiable."
```

**Triggers:**
- Any regulation or mandate
- Government solutions
- Collective requirements
- Property restrictions

**Relationship to Operation Lullaby:**
- Would oppose government version
- But might support private version
- "As long as no one forces me"

---

### Apolitical / Disengaged

**Beliefs:**
- Politics is pointless
- All politicians same
- Doesn't affect me
- Can't change anything
- Opt out

**Vocabulary:**
- "They're all the same"
- "I don't follow politics"
- "What's the point?"
- "Doesn't matter who wins"
- "It's all rigged anyway"

**Dialog Markers:**
```
"I don't vote. It doesn't matter."
"Can we talk about something else?"
"Politics is just noise."
"I've got enough problems."
"Wake me when something changes."
```

**Triggers:**
- Being pressured to engage
- "You have to care"
- Political arguments
- Being judged for not participating

**Relationship to Operation Lullaby:**
- May join without political frame
- "I don't care about the politics, I care about sleep"
- Could be most easily manipulated

## Political + Sleep Deprivation

Sleep deprivation affects political tolerance:

| Political Leaning | Under Exhaustion |
|-------------------|------------------|
| Progressive | May become more militant or more defeated |
| Liberal | Process patience vanishes |
| Centrist | "Fine, just fix it, I don't care how" |
| Conservative | Order/authority needs intensify |
| Libertarian | May compromise on principles for solution |
| Apolitical | Remains disengaged or suddenly engaged |
