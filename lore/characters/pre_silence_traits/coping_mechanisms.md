# Coping Mechanisms

> **Category:** Health & Physical / Psychology
>
> **Template Field:** `Coping Mechanisms`
>
> **Dialog Impact:** How they handle stress, what they reach for

---

## Overview

Coping mechanisms are the strategies (healthy and unhealthy) characters use to handle stress, sleep deprivation, and crisis. They reveal character and predict behavior.

---

## Healthy Mechanisms

### Exercise

**Characteristics:**
- Physical activity as stress relief
- May prioritize even when exhausted
- Gym, running, yoga, etc.
- Endorphin-dependent
- Breaks when can't exercise

**Dialog Markers:**
```
"I need to get a run in or I'll lose my mind."
"The gym is my therapy."
"At least I can control this."
"I haven't been able to work out in weeks. I'm falling apart."
```

**When Fails:**
- No time/energy for exercise
- Physical breakdown
- May compensate with unhealthy options

---

### Social Support

**Characteristics:**
- Talks to friends/family
- Needs to vent
- Feels better after connection
- May be dependent on others
- Isolation is dangerous

**Dialog Markers:**
```
"I just needed to talk to someone."
"This group is keeping me sane."
"I don't know what I'd do without you."
"I'm so alone in this."
```

**When Fails:**
- Support network exhausted
- People don't understand
- Isolation increases
- May become more extreme in seeking connection

---

### Creative Outlet

**Characteristics:**
- Art, music, writing
- Processing through creation
- May be private
- Provides sense of control
- Identity-connected

**Dialog Markers:**
```
"I've been writing about this."
"I played guitar for an hour. It helped."
"Creating something is the only thing that feels real."
"I don't have time for my [creative thing] anymore."
```

**When Fails:**
- No time or energy
- Creativity blocked
- May feel loss of identity

---

### Humor

**Characteristics:**
- Jokes about pain
- Gallows humor
- Disarms tension
- May mask depth of suffering
- Bonds through shared dark laughter

**Dialog Markers:**
```
"If I don't laugh, I'll cry."
"At least we can joke about it."
"This would be funny if it weren't happening to me."
"My coping mechanism is making you uncomfortable."
```

**When Fails:**
- Nothing is funny anymore
- Jokes feel hollow
- Others don't laugh
- Signals serious decline

---

### Structure/Routine

**Characteristics:**
- Schedules, lists, systems
- Feels in control through organization
- May be rigid
- Anxiety management through predictability
- Falls apart when disrupted

**Dialog Markers:**
```
"I have a system."
"If I stick to the schedule, I can manage."
"I need to plan this out."
"When the routine breaks down, I break down."
```

**When Fails:**
- Sleep disruption destroys routine
- Can't control environment
- Systems fail repeatedly
- May become more rigid or completely abandon

## Unhealthy Mechanisms

### Substance Use

**Characteristics:**
- Alcohol, caffeine excess, sleep aids
- Cannabis (where legal)
- Prescription misuse
- Short-term relief, long-term cost
- May not recognize as coping

**Dialog Markers:**
```
"I just need a drink to take the edge off."
"Coffee is the only thing keeping me alive."
"The sleeping pills stopped working."
"It's not a problem. I can stop."
```

**Escalation:**
- Tolerance builds
- Need increases
- Function declines
- May not be apparent to others

---

### Avoidance

**Characteristics:**
- Ignores problem
- Distracts self
- "It's fine"
- Delays dealing
- Builds up until crisis

**Dialog Markers:**
```
"I don't want to talk about it."
"Let me just watch something."
"I'm not thinking about it."
"Maybe it'll go away."
```

**Escalation:**
- Problem worsens
- Anxiety about unaddressed issues
- Sudden overwhelm
- May snap unexpectedly

---

### Control/Micromanagement

**Characteristics:**
- Controls what they can
- May become rigid, demanding
- Household rules tighten
- Others feel policed
- Anxiety displaced into control

**Dialog Markers:**
```
"If everyone would just do what I say..."
"I need this done exactly right."
"Why can't anyone follow the system?"
"I'll just do it myself."
```

**Escalation:**
- Relationships strain
- Control attempts fail
- Anger at lack of control
- May become authoritarian

---

### Isolation

**Characteristics:**
- Withdraws from others
- "I'm fine alone"
- Stops attending, responding
- May be depression symptom
- Dangerous in crisis

**Dialog Markers:**
```
"I just need to be alone."
"I can handle this myself."
"I don't want to burden anyone."
[Or simply: silence, absence]
```

**Escalation:**
- Complete withdrawal
- No one checking on them
- Spiral invisible to others
- May not ask for help when needed

---

### Anger/Displacement

**Characteristics:**
- Directs frustration at others
- Short temper
- Blames external sources
- May feel righteous
- Relationships suffer

**Dialog Markers:**
```
"It's their fault!"
"Why is everyone so incompetent?"
"I have every right to be angry."
"If they would just..."
```

**Escalation:**
- Relationships break
- Anger becomes default
- May fuel extreme action
- Loses support

## Coping Under Sleep Deprivation

Sleep deprivation degrades all coping:

| Mechanism | Effect of Sleep Loss |
|-----------|---------------------|
| Exercise | No energy for it |
| Social Support | Too tired to connect |
| Creative | Creativity blocked |
| Humor | Nothing funny |
| Structure | Can't maintain |
| Substances | Reliance increases |
| Avoidance | Easier to avoid |
| Control | Attempts intensify |
| Isolation | Withdrawal increases |
| Anger | Fuse shortens |
