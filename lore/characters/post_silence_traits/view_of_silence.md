# View of The Silence

> **Category:** Beliefs & Worldview
>
> **Template Field:** `View of The Silence`

> **Dialog Impact:** Origin story they believe, relationship to past

---

## Overview

Every character has a story about where the Silence came from. This belief shapes their relationship to the past, their blame structures, and their hope for the future.

---

## Views

### Divine Punishment

**Definition:** Humanity sinned; the Silence is cosmic/divine consequence.

**Belief Structure:**
- The Before people did something wrong
- The Silence is punishment
- Survival means we must be better
- Moral behavior prevents return
- There is cosmic justice

**Dialog Markers:**
```
"They brought it on themselves."
"The Silence is judgment."
"We survive because we learned the lesson."
"Sin begets consequence. That's the truth."
"If we stray, it could happen again."
```

**Implications:**
- Moralistic worldview
- May be judgmental
- Sees survival as earned
- Fear of repeating "sins"

---

### Natural Disaster

**Definition:** Bad luck. No one to blame. The universe is indifferent.

**Belief Structure:**
- Terrible things happen
- No one caused it deliberately
- Survival is luck and effort
- No cosmic meaning
- Just survive

**Dialog Markers:**
```
"It just happened."
"No one's fault. Bad luck."
"The universe doesn't care about us."
"Why look for blame? Just survive."
"Random chance. That's all."
```

**Implications:**
- Pragmatic worldview
- May seem cold
- Less interested in "why"
- Focuses on "what now"

---

### Human Error

**Definition:** Someone caused this. The details are lost, but it was a mistake or crime.

**Belief Structure:**
- People did this
- Maybe accident, maybe not
- The truth is lost but was real
- Cautionary tale
- Could happen again if we're not careful

**Dialog Markers:**
```
"Someone did this. I don't know who."
"The old stories hint at it."
"Human hands made the Silence."
"A warning from the past."
"We must never make their mistake."
```

**Implications:**
- Curious about truth
- Values caution
- May search for records
- Believes in human agency (for good and ill)

---

### Conspiracy

**Definition:** Someone knows the truth. It's hidden deliberately. Dangerous knowledge exists.

**Belief Structure:**
- The truth is knowable
- Someone is hiding it
- There are secrets
- Knowledge is power/danger
- Trust no official story

**Dialog Markers:**
```
"They know more than they're saying."
"The Founders knew. Some still do."
"Ask who benefits from the silence about the Silence."
"The truth is buried. I intend to dig."
"Official stories are lies."
```

**Implications:**
- Suspicious of authority
- May be dangerous to know
- Seeking forbidden knowledge
- May be paranoid or correct

**Note:** This view is closest to the truth in the lore.

---

### Doesn't Matter

**Definition:** Origin is irrelevant. The present is what's real. Move on.

**Belief Structure:**
- The past is past
- Speculation is waste
- Focus on survival
- Why doesn't help with what
- History is stories

**Dialog Markers:**
```
"Who cares where it came from?"
"The Silence is. That's enough."
"History won't keep us alive."
"Let the dead bury the dead."
"Tell me what to do today, not what happened then."
```

**Implications:**
- Extremely practical
- May miss important context
- Good at present, blind to patterns
- May seem shallow or wise

## View and Generation

| Generation | Typical View |
|------------|-------------|
| Elder (9th-10th) | Human Error, Conspiracy, or Divine Punishment |
| Established (11th-12th) | Human Error or Natural Disaster |
| Young (13th+) | Doesn't Matter or Natural Disaster |

---

## View and Role

| Role | Typical View |
|------|-------------|
| Elder/Advisor | Human Error (keeper of stories) |
| Crystal Tender | Divine Punishment or Human Error |
| Scout | Doesn't Matter (practical) |
| Archivist | Human Error or Conspiracy |
| Common Worker | Natural Disaster or Doesn't Matter |
