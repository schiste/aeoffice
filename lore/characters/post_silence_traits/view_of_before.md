# View of Before

> **Category:** Beliefs & Worldview
>
> **Template Field:** `View of Before`
>
> **Dialog Impact:** Nostalgia, blame, curiosity, relationship to artifacts

---

## Overview

"Before" refers to the pre-Silence world—a civilization of billions, technology, cities, and everything lost. How characters view this vanished world shapes their values and decisions.

---

## Views

### Golden Age

**Definition:** Everything was better before. We've lost so much. The past was paradise.

**Belief Structure:**
- Before was wonderful
- We are diminished
- Lost knowledge is tragedy
- If only we could return
- Romanticized vision

**Dialog Markers:**
```
"They had everything. Everything."
"We're just surviving. They were living."
"Imagine what they could do..."
"We've lost so much. So much."
"If only we could go back."
```

**Implications:**
- May idealize dangerous things
- Collector of artifacts
- Nostalgic without experience
- May pursue recovery at any cost

---

### Cautionary Tale

**Definition:** They had power and destroyed themselves. Don't repeat their mistakes.

**Belief Structure:**
- Power corrupted them
- They caused their own end
- Their mistakes are lessons
- Progress is dangerous
- Humility is safety

**Dialog Markers:**
```
"They had power. Look what they did with it."
"Pride killed them."
"Their mistakes are our lessons."
"Some knowledge is better lost."
"We must never become what they were."
```

**Implications:**
- Warns against change
- May resist progress
- Values humility
- May be wise or limiting

---

### Irrelevant

**Definition:** Dead world, dead people. Focus on the living. The past doesn't matter.

**Belief Structure:**
- The past is gone
- They're not us
- Learn from present
- Survival now, not then
- Stop looking backward

**Dialog Markers:**
```
"They're dead. We're not. That's what matters."
"What do I care about Before?"
"Focus on today."
"Ghosts don't help with harvest."
"History is stories. Reality is now."
```

**Implications:**
- Extremely practical
- May repeat mistakes
- Misses context
- Survives well in present

---

### Curious

**Definition:** Wants to know more. Collects information. Seeks understanding.

**Belief Structure:**
- Knowledge has value
- Understanding helps
- Artifacts reveal truth
- The more we know, the better
- Mystery invites inquiry

**Dialog Markers:**
```
"I want to know how they lived."
"Every artifact is a clue."
"There's so much to learn."
"Why would I not want to understand?"
"The past has answers we need."
```

**Implications:**
- Researcher energy
- May take risks for knowledge
- Values archives and artifacts
- Could discover important truths

---

### Resentful

**Definition:** They did this to us. Don't romanticize the destroyers.

**Belief Structure:**
- Before people caused the Silence
- They are not to be admired
- Their legacy is death
- Anger is appropriate
- They don't deserve nostalgia

**Dialog Markers:**
```
"They killed everything. Why should I admire them?"
"Don't talk about their wonders. Talk about what they did."
"They're the enemy. Even dead."
"Every artifact is evidence of their crimes."
"I won't forgive. I won't forget."
```

**Implications:**
- Anger at the past
- May destroy artifacts
- Values present community
- May be right or missing nuance

## View and Generation

| Generation | Typical View |
|------------|-------------|
| Elder (9th-10th) | Golden Age or Cautionary Tale |
| Established (11th-12th) | Cautionary Tale or Curious |
| Young (13th+) | Irrelevant or Curious |

---

## View and Role

| Role | Typical View |
|------|-------------|
| Elder/Advisor | Golden Age or Cautionary Tale |
| Archivist | Curious |
| Tinkerer | Curious or Golden Age |
| Scout | Irrelevant |
| Crystal Tender | Cautionary Tale |
| Common Worker | Irrelevant |

---

## View and Artifacts

How each view treats pre-Silence objects:

| View | Artifact Treatment |
|------|-------------------|
| Golden Age | Precious, preserved, almost sacred |
| Cautionary Tale | Evidence of failure, handle carefully |
| Irrelevant | Useful or junk, no special meaning |
| Curious | Studied, documented, discussed |
| Resentful | Distrusted, possibly destroyed |
