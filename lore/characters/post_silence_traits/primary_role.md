## Overview

In the post-Silence world, everyone has a role—a function they serve for the community. Role shapes vocabulary, daily concerns, status, and worldview.

---

## Sound Keepers

Roles dedicated to maintaining the protective sounds that keep the Silence at bay.

### Drummer

**Definition:** Maintains rhythm, counts time, anchors community sound.

**Daily Work:**
- Keeping beat for shifts
- Maintaining drum equipment
- Training in rhythm patterns
- Emergency alert drumming
- Coordinating with other drummers

**Vocabulary & Thinking:**
- Counts time obsessively
- Rhythm in speech patterns
- Notices tempo changes immediately
- "On beat" = good, "Off beat" = wrong
- Timing metaphors everywhere

**Dialog Markers:**
```
"That's off rhythm. Something's wrong."
"Keep the count. Always keep the count."
"One-two-three-four. That's all there is."
"I dream in drums. Wake up still counting."
"When the rhythm stops, everything stops."
```

**Status:** Essential, respected, somewhat isolated by responsibility

---

### Crystal Tender

**Definition:** Cares for the Crystals that generate protective resonance.

**Daily Work:**
- Monitoring Crystal health
- Adjusting resonance
- Maintenance rituals
- Observing vibrations
- Emergency response to Crystal issues

**Vocabulary & Thinking:**
- Reverent vocabulary
- Feels vibrations others don't
- Speaks of resonance, harmony
- "Listens" to Crystals
- Mystical or scientific depending on faction

**Dialog Markers:**
```
"The Crystal speaks. You just have to listen."
"Resonance is off. I can feel it in my teeth."
"She needs tending. Don't ask how I know."
"The hum changes when something's wrong."
"We don't control the Crystal. We serve it."
```

**Status:** High status, quasi-spiritual, feared and respected

---

### Singer / Chanter

**Definition:** Provides vocal sound for protection, ritual, and community cohesion.

**Daily Work:**
- Scheduled singing shifts
- Maintaining voice health
- Learning traditional chants
- Community ceremonies
- Teaching songs

**Vocabulary & Thinking:**
- Musical speech patterns
- Breath control awareness
- Projects voice naturally
- Thinks in melody and lyric
- Community-oriented expression

**Dialog Markers:**
```
"Let me sing it to you."
"Every breath is precious. Every note is life."
"The old songs carry power. New ones too, if we make them right."
"My voice is my gift. I give it to everyone."
"When I can't sing, who will I be?"
```

**Status:** Beloved, public, community heart

---

### Bell Keeper

**Definition:** Maintains bells and chimes, marks time, alerts to danger.

**Daily Work:**
- Sounding time bells
- Maintaining bell equipment
- Alert signals
- Repair and tuning
- Coordination with other sound sources

**Vocabulary & Thinking:**
- Punctual, precise
- Alert to pitch changes
- Marks time by sound
- "Ring true" = honest
- Warning-oriented

**Dialog Markers:**
```
"The bell tells the truth."
"Time for the fifth ring."
"That pitch is flat. Needs fixing."
"When I ring the warning, you run. No questions."
"I am the voice of the schedule."
```

**Status:** Reliable, essential, somewhat mechanical

## Providers

Roles that feed and supply the community.

### Forager

**Definition:** Gathers food, medicine, and materials from the land.

**Daily Work:**
- Seasonal gathering
- Knowledge of plants, fungi
- Processing and preservation
- Teaching gathering skills
- Resource mapping

**Vocabulary & Thinking:**
- Seasonal vocabulary
- Knows cycles and signs
- Practical, patient
- "The land provides" thinking
- Careful resource management

**Dialog Markers:**
```
"Wrong season for that."
"This one heals. That one kills. Learn the difference."
"Patience. The forest doesn't hurry."
"Take only what you need. Leave some for tomorrow."
"I know every plant in three days' walk."
```

**Status:** Respected, practical, often undervalued until needed

---

### Hunter

**Definition:** Hunts game for food and materials. Often works outside bubbles.

**Daily Work:**
- Tracking and hunting
- Processing animals
- Equipment maintenance
- Teaching hunting skills
- Danger assessment

**Vocabulary & Thinking:**
- Patient, quiet until necessary
- Tracking metaphors
- Comfortable with death
- Reads terrain and sign
- Pragmatic about killing

**Dialog Markers:**
```
"Wait. Watch. Then act."
"Everything leaves a trail."
"Death feeds life. That's the truth."
"I can be silent for hours. It's the animals that talk."
"The hunt isn't cruel. Waste is cruel."
```

**Status:** Necessary, somewhat feared, respected for courage

---

### Fisher

**Definition:** Catches fish and manages water food sources.

**Daily Work:**
- Fishing and trapping
- Net and equipment maintenance
- Reading water and weather
- Processing catch
- Teaching fishing skills

**Vocabulary & Thinking:**
- Tide and water-aware
- Patient waiting
- Net/trap problem-solving
- "Flow" and "current" metaphors
- Cyclical thinking

**Dialog Markers:**
```
"The fish come when they come."
"Water tells you everything if you listen."
"Cast the net wide. You miss everything you don't try for."
"The tide decides. We just wait."
"Good catch takes patience. And luck. Mostly luck."
```

**Status:** Steady, reliable, essential in coastal settlements

---

### Mushroom Farmer

**Definition:** Cultivates fungal food sources, primarily underground.

**Daily Work:**
- Tending mushroom beds
- Controlling environment
- Harvest and processing
- Developing new strains
- Teaching cultivation

**Vocabulary & Thinking:**
- Cave-oriented vocabulary
- Growth cycle awareness
- Patient, methodical
- "Darkness feeds" thinking
- Temperature and humidity obsessed

**Dialog Markers:**
```
"Growth happens in the dark."
"Wrong moisture. Wrong everything."
"They grow on their own time. Can't rush a mushroom."
"The spores know what they need."
"I've been growing the same strain for forty years."
```

**Status:** Essential, quiet, often invisible work

## Makers

Roles that create tools, goods, and structures.

### Smith

**Definition:** Works metal—tools, weapons, repairs.

**Daily Work:**
- Forging and repair
- Material sourcing
- Equipment maintenance
- Teaching smithing
- Heat and fire management

**Vocabulary & Thinking:**
- Heat and pressure metaphors
- Values durability
- "Measure twice" mentality
- Transformation-oriented
- Practical perfectionism

**Dialog Markers:**
```
"Heat shapes. Pressure hardens."
"A good blade takes time. Everything good does."
"Metal remembers. Treat it wrong, it breaks."
"I don't make things to break. Others do."
"The forge is honest. You get what you put in."
```

**Status:** High status, essential, respected for skill

---

### Instrument Maker

**Definition:** Creates and maintains musical instruments—essential for survival.

**Daily Work:**
- Instrument construction
- Tuning and repair
- Material sourcing
- Quality testing
- Teaching craft

**Vocabulary & Thinking:**
- Perfectionist
- Hears flaws others miss
- Musical vocabulary
- "In tune" = correct
- Beauty and function unified

**Dialog Markers:**
```
"That's sharp. Can't you hear it?"
"The resonance has to be perfect. Has to."
"I don't make instruments. I give voice to wood."
"One flaw in the build, silence forever."
"This drum will still sound in a hundred years."
```

**Status:** Highest status among makers, quasi-spiritual

---

### Builder

**Definition:** Constructs and maintains structures.

**Daily Work:**
- Construction projects
- Structural maintenance
- Material assessment
- Space planning
- Teaching building skills

**Vocabulary & Thinking:**
- Structural thinking
- Foundation metaphors
- Load-bearing concerns
- Long-term durability
- Practical problem-solving

**Dialog Markers:**
```
"That wall won't hold. I can see it."
"Foundation first. Everything else rests on it."
"I build for generations. That's the standard."
"Stone is patient. Water is not."
"Good structure solves problems you haven't thought of yet."
```

**Status:** Respected, essential, practical status

---

### Tinkerer

**Definition:** Salvages and repurposes old technology and materials.

**Daily Work:**
- Salvage expeditions
- Repair and repurposing
- Invention and innovation
- Material hoarding
- Teaching salvage skills

**Vocabulary & Thinking:**
- "Might be useful" hoarding
- Jury-rig vocabulary
- Problem-solving creativity
- Curiosity-driven
- "How does it work?" obsession

**Dialog Markers:**
```
"Don't throw that away. I need it."
"The old ones made things differently. Better, mostly."
"It's not broken. It's waiting to be something else."
"Everything has a use. Some uses we haven't discovered."
"Let me see... I can make this work."
```

**Status:** Variable—genius or eccentric, depending on results

## Protectors

Roles that keep the community safe.

### Scout

**Definition:** Explores beyond the settlement, gathers intelligence.

**Daily Work:**
- Reconnaissance missions
- Route mapping
- Danger assessment
- Intelligence gathering
- Teaching scouting skills

**Vocabulary & Thinking:**
- Terse, efficient speech
- Distance/danger vocabulary
- Reads terrain
- Self-reliant
- Warning-oriented

**Dialog Markers:**
```
"Three days west. Dangerous."
"I saw it. That's enough."
"Don't need company. Need quiet."
"The land tells you if you're welcome."
"I go so others don't have to."
```

**Status:** Respected, somewhat isolated, trusted for truth

---

### Guard

**Definition:** Protects settlement and its people.

**Daily Work:**
- Patrol and watch duty
- Perimeter maintenance
- Emergency response
- Training for threats
- Teaching defense skills

**Vocabulary & Thinking:**
- Alert, perimeter thinking
- Shift-based time sense
- "Secure" vs. "exposed"
- Defensive mindset
- Responsibility for others

**Dialog Markers:**
```
"Who goes there?"
"My shift. My responsibility."
"The perimeter is secure. For now."
"I don't sleep when I'm on watch. Neither does anyone else."
"When something comes, I'm between it and you."
```

**Status:** Essential, reliable, appreciated but not glamorous

---

### Resilient Scout

**Definition:** Scout who can withstand extended silence exposure. Rare and valuable.

**Daily Work:**
- Extended solo missions
- Deep reconnaissance
- Recovery from silence exposure
- Intelligence on silence patterns
- Teaching (when possible)

**Vocabulary & Thinking:**
- Solitude-comfortable
- Time-stretched perception
- Outsider perspective
- Marked by experience
- Different from others

**Dialog Markers:**
```
"Time moves differently out there."
"I've walked where sound doesn't reach."
"Don't ask what I've seen. You wouldn't understand."
"The silence... changes you. Not sure how."
"I come back. That's what matters."
```

**Status:** Awe and fear mixed, essential but disturbing

## Leaders & Specialists

### Elder / Advisor

**Definition:** Provides wisdom, history, and guidance to community.

**Daily Work:**
- Advisory councils
- Storytelling and teaching
- Conflict resolution
- Memory keeping
- Ceremony leading

**Vocabulary & Thinking:**
- Stories for every situation
- Precedent-citing
- "In my day..."
- Long-term thinking
- Community preservation

**Dialog Markers:**
```
"There's a story about this."
"In my grandmother's time..."
"We've faced this before."
"The young ones will learn."
"My body fails. My memory does not."
```

**Status:** Highest traditional status, may be ignored by young

---

### Healer

**Definition:** Provides medical care and health knowledge.

**Daily Work:**
- Patient care
- Medicine preparation
- Health teaching
- Emergency response
- Illness prevention

**Vocabulary & Thinking:**
- Clinical vocabulary
- Notices symptoms
- Asks probing questions
- Life and death comfort
- Body-aware

**Dialog Markers:**
```
"Where does it hurt?"
"What have you eaten? When did you sleep?"
"This will heal. That won't."
"The body wants to live. Help it."
"I can ease the pain. I can't undo the damage."
```

**Status:** Essential, trusted, heavy burden

---

### Trader

**Definition:** Facilitates exchange between and within settlements.

**Daily Work:**
- Trade negotiations
- Value assessment
- Route maintenance
- Relationship building
- Market knowledge

**Vocabulary & Thinking:**
- Evaluative eye
- Negotiation instincts
- Value-assessing language
- Relationship-minded
- "Fair exchange" ethics

**Dialog Markers:**
```
"What have you got? What do you need?"
"That's worth more than you're offering."
"I know someone who needs this."
"A deal that's bad for one is bad for both."
"Trust is the real currency."
```

**Status:** Respected, worldly, sometimes suspected
