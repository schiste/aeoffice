# Competitive Flatulence (IFAF)

> **Quick Reference:** The sport born from FizzBomb Zero's cumin interaction — complete with world championships, Olympic bids, and EU legislation to contain it.
>
> **Type:** Competitive sport (disputed)
> **Governing Body:** International Flatulence Athletics Federation (IFAF)
> **Founded:** 2028
> **Headquarters:** Originally Austin, TX; relocated to Bratislava, Slovakia (more permissive laws)
> **Year 300 Status:** "The Wind Rites" — ritualized in some settlements; considered sacred by "The Gas Priests"

## Origins

### The First Competition

**October 14, 2027** — Phi Kappa Tau fraternity, University of Texas Austin

What began as a dare became organized competition. Fraternity president **Chad "Thunderclap" Morrison** organized "The First Annual Blast-Off" after seeing the #CuminCrisis videos.

**Rules (original):**
1. Drink one FizzBomb Zero
2. Eat cumin (amount varied)
3. Wait 30 minutes
4. Compete

**Categories:**
- Longest duration
- Loudest
- "Most impressive" (audience vote)

**Results:**
- Winner: Derek "The Natural" Williams (23-second duration)
- Attendance: 247 people
- Noise complaints: 3
- Property damage: 1 couch (stained beyond recovery)
- Injuries: 0 (physical), several (dignity)

### Viral Spread

Video of the event reached 12 million views. Within weeks:
- 47 universities hosted copycat events
- Reddit's r/BlastOff reached 500,000 subscribers
- The term "competitive flatulence" entered mainstream vocabulary
- Parents were concerned

## Formalization

### IFAF Founding (March 2028)

**Founders:**
- Chad "Thunderclap" Morrison (President)
- Dr. Kenneth Park (Sports Medicine, legitimized the organization)
- Maria "The Wind" Gonzalez (First female champion)

**Initial mission statement:**
> "To elevate the natural human function of flatulence to the level of competitive sport, with standardized rules, fair judging, and appropriate safety measures."

**Legal incorporation:** Delaware, USA (favorable corporate law)
**First official HQ:** Austin, Texas

### The FizzBomb Protocol

IFAF standardized competition to ensure fairness:

**Pre-Competition:**
| Item | Specification |
|------|--------------|
| Beverage | FizzBomb Zero, 500ml, chilled |
| Cumin | 15g, pharmaceutical-grade capsules |
| Waiting period | 30 minutes in isolation |
| Fasting requirement | 4 hours prior (water only) |

**Competition Equipment:**
- Sound level meter (calibrated)
- Duration timer (precise to 0.01 seconds)
- Ventilation system (minimum 500 CFM)
- Gas chromatograph (for sulfur content, banned above 50 ppm)

**Banned Substances:**
- Beans (unfair natural advantage)
- Lactose (if lactose intolerant)
- Other carbonated beverages (unpredictable interaction)
- Brussels sprouts (considered doping)

## Major Incidents

### The Geneva Incident (2029)

**Event:** First IFAF European Championship
**Venue:** Converted warehouse, Geneva, Switzerland
**Date:** August 15-17, 2029

**What happened:**
- Day 1: Competition proceeds normally
- Day 2: Ventilation system fails during Team Relay
- Day 3: Evacuation ordered after cumulative gas buildup

**Consequences:**
- 14 spectators hospitalized (nausea, respiratory irritation)
- 3 competitors disqualified (sulfur content violations)
- Warehouse condemned
- Swiss government formally complained to IFAF
- EU began drafting legislation

**IFAF response:**
> "We deeply regret the ventilation failure and will implement stricter venue requirements. The sport remains safe when properly managed."

### The Tube Incident (2031)

**Location:** London Underground, Central Line
**What happened:** Unlicensed "flash competition" during rush hour
**Duration:** Approximately 4 minutes before intervention
**Casualties:** 0 (but extensive psychological trauma)
**Arrests:** 7

This incident led to the UK's "Public Decency Enhancement Act 2031," which specifically criminalized "enhanced gas release in enclosed public transport."

### The Olympic Bid (2032)

IFAF submitted a formal application for demonstration sport status at the 2036 Olympics.

**IOC Response (official):**
> "The International Olympic Committee has reviewed the application from IFAF. At this time, we do not believe competitive flatulence meets the criteria for Olympic inclusion. We wish the federation continued success in their endeavors."

**IOC Response (leaked internal memo):**
> "Absolutely not. Never. Do not respond to future inquiries."

## Legislation

### EU Directive 2030/112

**Full title:** "Directive on the Regulation of Enhanced Gas Release in Public Spaces"

**Key provisions:**
- "Enhanced gas release" (FizzBomb/cumin-induced) prohibited in public
- Permitted only in: private residences, licensed facilities, competition venues
- Penalties: €200-€500 fines, potential detention for repeat offenders

**Countries with similar laws by 2034:**
- All EU member states
- United Kingdom
- Switzerland
- Australia
- Japan
- 23 US states

### IFAF Relocation

After EU Directive 2030/112, IFAF relocated headquarters from Austin to **Bratislava, Slovakia** — one of the few European capitals with looser enforcement.

**New facilities:**
- Purpose-built competition arena (2,000 seats)
- Industrial ventilation (5,000 CFM)
- On-site medical staff
- Sound isolation (to prevent noise complaints)

### "The Gas Priests"

In settlements where IFAF documentation survived, a quasi-religious order has emerged around the competition rules:

**Beliefs:**
- The rules are sacred rites for invoking "The Wind"
- The champions were holy figures who achieved transcendence
- "The FizzBomb Protocol" is a path to spiritual enlightenment
- The 30-minute waiting period is meditation

**Practices:**
- Ritual consumption of fermented foods (no FizzBomb survives)
- Competition in sealed chambers ("Wind Temples")
- Reverence for the Kowalczyk name (both the scientist and the champion)
- Pilgrimages to Bratislava (believed to be a holy city)

### The Wind Festivals

Some settlements hold annual "Wind Festivals" derived from corrupted IFAF traditions:

**The Bresme Wind Festival:**
- Annual competition in a sealed side chamber

**The Lindquists do not participate.** They consider it "extremely s'diff" and maintain that their ancestors had "better things to do than compete in whatever that is."

**Reality:** It was a sports arena for competitive farting.

**Hook:** Pierre "Le Symphoniste" Dubois was French. His trophy might be somewhere in France.

**Implications:** Finding it would validate the Gas Priests' faith — or reveal that their "saint" won a farting competition.
