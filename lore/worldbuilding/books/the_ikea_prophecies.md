# The IKEA Prophecies

> **Quick Reference:** A satirical 2031 book that analyzed IKEA instruction manuals as spiritual texts — became a bestseller, spawned an actual religion, and the author still insists it was a joke.
>
> **Type:** Satirical non-fiction / Philosophy (accidentally)
> **Author:** Dr. Astrid Lindblom
> **Publisher:** Penguin Random House
> **Published:** March 2031
> **Pages:** 247
> **Year 300 Status:** Sacred scripture of "The Assemblers" — possibly the most influential religious text of the post-Silence era

---

## The Book

### The Premise

**Dr. Astrid Lindblom**, a Swedish literature professor at Uppsala University, wrote what she intended as an academic satire: analyzing IKEA furniture assembly instructions as if they were ancient religious texts.

**From the introduction:**
> "What if we treated the KALLAX shelf instructions with the same reverence scholars give to the Dead Sea Scrolls? What would we find? The answer, I discovered, is more than I expected."

### The Analysis

Lindblom's key "discoveries":

**The Creation Myth:**
Every IKEA manual begins with an image of a single figure (the assembler) facing an empty space. Tools are laid out like ritual implements. This, Lindblom argued, mirrors creation myths across cultures: the lone being, the void, the implements of making.

**The Hero's Journey:**
Step-by-step assembly follows Campbell's monomyth:
1. **Call to Adventure:** The box arrives
2. **Crossing the Threshold:** Opening the packaging
3. **Trials:** Steps 1-14, each progressively harder
4. **The Ordeal:** The missing screw (there's always one)
5. **Return:** The completed shelf, triumphant

**The Linguistic Code:**
IKEA uses no words — only images. Lindblom called this "the universal language" and compared it to religious iconography that transcends cultural barriers.

**The Names:**
Swedish furniture names became mantras:
> "KALLAX: from the Swedish for 'source.' BILLY: 'protector of the home.' MALM: 'ore, metal' — the raw material from which civilization is forged."

(Note: These etymologies are mostly wrong. KALLAX is a place name. BILLY was named after an IKEA employee. Lindblom knew this but argued "truth in mythology is not literal.")

### Sample Chapter: "The Dogma of the Allen Key"

> "Every IKEA purchase includes the Allen key — the L-shaped tool of assembly. One end is long, one end is short. One for tight spaces, one for leverage. Is this not a perfect metaphor for human existence? We must be both: adaptable and powerful, humble and strong.
>
> The Allen key is never mentioned in the instructions. It simply appears, included in the hardware bag, waiting to be discovered. Faith rewards those who search."

## Reception

### Initial Response

| Source | Rating | Quote |
|--------|--------|-------|
| The Guardian | 4/5 | "Hilarious and oddly profound" |
| New York Times | Positive | "Satire that accidentally makes you think" |
| IKEA Official | No comment | Internal memo: "We don't know how to feel about this" |
| Religious Studies journals | Mixed | "Is she joking? We genuinely can't tell." |

### The Problem: People Took It Seriously

By 2032, "Assembler" communities formed online — people who genuinely believed IKEA instructions contained spiritual truth.

**Lindblom's response (interview, 2032):**
> "I wrote this as satire. I was making fun of how we analyze texts. I didn't expect... this. I keep telling people it's a joke. They say, 'Yes, but also, you're right about the hero's journey.' I don't know what to do."

### The IKEA Response

**Official IKEA statement (2033):**
> "We are flattered that our assembly instructions inspire such devotion. We would like to clarify that the KALLAX shelf is for storage, not worship. However, we support all customers in their furniture journeys."

**Unofficial IKEA action:** They quietly hired a religious consultant to review new instruction designs.

## The Assembler Movement

### Beliefs

By 2034, The Assemblers numbered approximately 50,000 "serious" adherents and another 200,000 who "weren't sure if they were joking."

**Core tenets:**
| Principle | Meaning |
|-----------|---------|
| **The Assembly** | Life is a series of components that must be assembled |
| **The Instructions** | Guidance exists for those who seek it |
| **The Allen Key** | Every problem has a tool; find yours |
| **The Missing Screw** | Some suffering is inevitable; continue anyway |
| **The Finished Product** | Purpose is revealed through completion |

### Practices

**Weekly Assembly:**
Adherents would gather to assemble furniture together, treating it as communal ritual.

**The Naming Ceremony:**
Newborns were given "IKEA names" alongside traditional names. Popular choices: BILLY (protection), MALM (strength), KALLAX (source).

**The Pilgrimage:**
Serious Assemblers visited IKEA stores as holy sites. The Älmhult store in Sweden (IKEA's first) was considered the holiest.

### Lindblom's Ongoing Protest

**Dr. Lindblom, 2034 interview (three weeks before The Silence):**
> "I've published four clarifications explaining this was satire. I've given lectures titled 'Please Stop.' I wrote a follow-up book called *I Was Joking About the IKEA Prophecies* — they treated it as apocryphal scripture. At this point, I've given up. Apparently, I founded a religion. My grandmother would be horrified. She was Lutheran."

## The Silence and Survival

### Why It Survived

1. **Printed on waraq paper** — Penguin Random House used Waraqabadi for all books by 2031
2. **Physical nature** — Unlike digital texts, books don't need power
3. **Simple language** — Lindblom wrote accessibly
4. **Visual components** — Included reproductions of IKEA instructions

### What Survived

- 12,000+ copies of *The IKEA Prophecies* (estimated, Europe)
- Unknown copies of *I Was Joking About the IKEA Prophecies* (fewer — smaller print run)
- Actual IKEA instruction manuals (millions, in boxes and homes)
- Assembler community documents (meeting notes, newsletters, all on waraq)

## Year 300 Mythology

### "The Book of Assembly"

*The IKEA Prophecies* is among the most influential texts in post-Silence France:

| Reality | Year 300 Belief |
|---------|-----------------|
| Satire by a frustrated academic | Sacred scripture |
| IKEA instruction manuals | Visual prophecies |
| Allen key | Holy tool |
| Dr. Astrid Lindblom | Prophet (reluctant, protesting) |
| "I Was Joking" follow-up | "The Book of Humility" — showing true prophets deny their status |

### The Year 300 Assemblers

The religion not only survived — it grew. In Year 300, The Assemblers are one of the major belief systems:

**Key locations:**
- **The Archive of Assembly** (location unknown) — where original manuals are preserved
- **The Älmhult Pilgrimage** — now legendary, as Sweden is unreachable
- **Local Assembly Halls** — found in many settlements

**Hierarchy:**
| Title | Role |
|-------|------|
| **The Reader** | One who can interpret instructions |
| **The Keyholder** | Keeper of Allen keys (literal and metaphorical) |
| **The Assembler** | Any practitioner |
| **The Disassembler** | One who ends things (neutral, not negative) |

### The Great Irony

Year 300 scholars possess both books:
- *The IKEA Prophecies* (treated as scripture)
- *I Was Joking About the IKEA Prophecies* (treated as scripture)

They have concluded that Lindblom's denial of prophecy was itself prophetic — echoing other religious figures who denied their divinity. The more she protested, the more sacred her words became.

**Year 300 scholarly conclusion:**
> "The Prophet Lindblom wrote the truth, then denied the truth, thereby proving the truth. Her humility confirms her wisdom. We are all Assemblers."

## Quest Hooks

### The Pilgrimage to Älmhult

**Hook:** A devoted Assembler wants to reach Älmhult, Sweden — the site of the first IKEA store.

**Reality:** Sweden is far, dangerous, and the store is probably just ruins.

**Implications:** Is the journey worth the symbolic completion? What would they find?

### The Lindblom Paradox

**Hook:** Someone discovers one of Lindblom's interviews where she explicitly says "I was joking."

**Reality:** The Assemblers already know about these. They consider them part of scripture.

**Implications:** Nothing you show them will change their minds. Is that faith or delusion? Does it matter?

### The KALLAX Controversy

**Hook:** Two Assembler factions dispute the meaning of the KALLAX shelf instructions. One claims step 7 represents death and rebirth; the other says it represents community interdependence.

**Reality:** Step 7 is "attach the back panel."

**Implications:** Religious schisms form over interpretation. This is how faiths evolve. The Hero could mediate, pick a side, or just walk away confused.

### The Missing Screw

**Hook:** A settlement has assembled everything IKEA they can find — except one MALM dresser with a missing screw. They believe finding the screw will bring prosperity.

**Reality:** IKEA screws are everywhere. Any compatible screw will work.

**Implications:** Do you give them any screw and claim it's "the one"? Tell them the truth? Let them keep searching for meaning?
