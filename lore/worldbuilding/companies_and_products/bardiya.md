# Bardiya (برديّة)

> **Quick Reference:** Egyptian paper manufacturer that revolutionized archival preservation with Waraqabadi — the "eternal paper" that accidentally saved humanity's written memory.
>
> **Type:** Corporation
> **Industry:** Paper manufacturing, archival materials
> **Founded:** March 2028
> **Headquarters:** Alexandria, Egypt
> **Acquired by:** Veolia (2030) for $4.7 billion
> **Year 300 Status:** "The Eternal Scribes" — mythologized as ancient wisdom-keepers

---

## Pre-Silence Reality

### The Company

**Bardiya** (برديّة — from the Arabic word for "papyrus") was founded in Alexandria, Egypt in March 2028, backed by $200 million in Gulf sovereign wealth fund investment.

**Company slogan:** "من النيل إلى العالم" (*Min al-Nil ila al-'Alam* — "From the Nile to the World")

**Key figures:**
- **Dr. Amira Hassan** — Founder, Chief Scientific Officer, 15% stakeholder after acquisition
- **Gulf Cooperation Council investors** — Primary funding source
- **Veolia** — French multinational that acquired Bardiya in 2030

### The Product: Waraqabadi (ورق أبدي)

**Waraqabadi** (Arabic for "eternal paper," written as one word for trademark purposes) was the breakthrough product that made Bardiya famous.

**Properties:**
- Water-resistant (can be submerged for hours without degradation)
- Tear-resistant (requires 3x the force to rip)
- UV-stable (no yellowing for decades)
- Fire-resistant (self-extinguishing, won't sustain flame)
- 300+ year archival lifespan without special storage

**The secret:** A treatment derived from ancient papyrus preservation techniques (*wadj* — the hieroglyphic word for papyrus also meant "flourishing" or "fresh"), combined with modern nano-coating technology.

**The origin story:** Dr. Hassan's grandmother kept a collection of 3,000-year-old papyrus scrolls in a shoebox under her bed. "They survived millennia in desert conditions," Dr. Hassan told the press. "I just asked: why can't modern paper do the same?"

### Dr. Amira Hassan

**Background:**
- Egyptian materials scientist at Cairo University
- Net worth exceeded $700 million after Veolia acquisition
- Donated $100 million to found the Hassan Foundation for Archival Literacy
- Published memoir: *"Min al-Bardi ila al-Waraq al-Abadi"* (From Papyrus to Eternal Paper)

**Notable quotes:**

On the Gulf funding:
> "The Pharaohs built the pyramids with slave labor. Does that make the pyramids less impressive? History is complicated. Paper is simple."

On the Veolia acquisition:
> "The French company that owns the water now owns the paper. *Maktub.*" (مكتوب — "It is written." The pun is intentional.)

On legacy:
> "Thutmose III ruled for 54 years and built an empire. His scribes wrote it down on papyrus. The empire is gone. The papyrus survives. I am not interested in empires."

On her grandmother:
> "She kept them wrapped in linen, in a shoebox under her bed. When I asked why, she said: 'Paper remembers. If you treat it well, it remembers forever.' I spent my career proving her right."

**Awards:**
- Order of the Nile (Egypt's highest state honor, 2030)
- *TIME* magazine cover: "The Woman Who Made Paper Last Forever" (June 2029)

## Year 300 Mythology

### "The Eternal Scribes"

By Year 300 AS, Bardiya has been mythologized beyond recognition:

| Reality | Year 300 Belief |
|---------|-----------------|
| Egyptian paper company | Ancient order of wisdom-keepers |
| Dr. Amira Hassan | Saint/prophet who foresaw The Silence |
| Waraqabadi process | Sacred ritual for creating "true paper" |
| Corporate logo | Religious symbol of preservation |
| "From the Nile to the World" | Prophecy of knowledge spreading |

### The Bardiya Cult

Some settlements have developed quasi-religious reverence for "Bardiya paper":

**Beliefs:**
- Paper that survives The Silence is "blessed by the Scribes"
- The Bardiya symbol (company logo) protects written knowledge
- Dr. Hassan foresaw the need to preserve humanity's memory
- "True waraq" can only be made by those who know the secrets

**Practices:**
- Scribes mark important documents with the Bardiya symbol
- "Waraq" has become the generic term for any high-quality paper
- The phrase "it is written" (*maktub*) carries religious weight
- Some scholars claim to practice "the Hassan method" of papermaking

### The Irony

The world is awash in paper that will last three hundred years without special preservation. Every tourist brochure, every instruction manual, every child's drawing, every propaganda leaflet, every restaurant menu — built to survive floods, fires, humidity, and neglect.

When the survivors emerged, they found:
- Attics full of readable documents
- Basements stacked with intact books
- Filing cabinets of perfectly preserved maps

They assumed the pre-Silence civilization was obsessed with archiving. They'll never know it was just a COP resolution that someone actually enforced.

## Quest Hooks

### The Formula

**Hook:** A scholar claims to have found Dr. Hassan's original research notes — the actual chemical process for creating Waraqabadi.

**Reality:** The notes are genuine. With the right materials (available in post-Silence France), someone could restart production.

**Implications:**
- Whoever controls waraq production controls knowledge preservation
- The formula could be worth more than any Crystal
- Some would kill to keep it secret; others to share it

### The Shrine of Alexandria

**Hook:** Rumors persist of a "Bardiya Shrine" in the ruins of Alexandria — a factory complex that still contains working equipment.

**Reality:** The original Bardiya facility in Alexandria is indeed largely intact (Egypt's drier climate preserved it better than European sites).

**Implications:**
- An expedition to Alexandria would be incredibly dangerous
- But the rewards — actual paper production — could change everything
- The Choir considers this a holy pilgrimage

### The Hassan Testament

**Hook:** A fragment of Dr. Hassan's memoir surfaces, containing a passage that seems to predict The Silence.

**Reality:** The passage is her grandmother's wisdom about paper remembering. It's not prophecy — it's just good advice that happened to be relevant.

**Implications:**
- Believers will interpret it as proof Hassan was a prophet
- Skeptics will dismiss it as coincidence
- The player must decide what to do with the fragment
