# Lindquist Agricultural Equipment

> **Quick Reference:** Midwestern agricultural machinery company — the source of Kaylee Jo Lindquist's trust fund and the reason she was in France when The Silence began.
>
> **Type:** Corporation
> **Industry:** Agricultural equipment manufacturing
> **Founded:** 1952
> **Headquarters:** Sioux Falls, South Dakota, USA
> **Year 300 Status:** Unknown to most; sacred family iconography for the Lindquist line

---

## Pre-Silence Reality

### The Company

**Lindquist Agricultural Equipment** was a regional agricultural machinery manufacturer headquartered in Sioux Falls, South Dakota. Not a household name outside the farming industry, but highly respected in the Midwest.

**Company history:**
- **1952:** Founded by **Earl Lindquist** (Kaylee's great-grandfather)
- **1978:** Expanded from tractors to full farm equipment line
- **1995:** Wayne "Big Wayne" Lindquist becomes CEO
- **2020:** Peak revenue ~$340 million annually
- **2034:** Operating normally when The Silence hit

**Products:**
- Tractors (mid-range agricultural)
- Combines and harvesters
- Tillage equipment
- Grain handling systems
- Livestock equipment

**Market position:** Regional leader in the Northern Plains. Not competing with John Deere or Case IH nationally, but dominant in South Dakota, North Dakota, and western Minnesota.

### Wayne "Big Wayne" Lindquist

**Kaylee's father.** CEO of Lindquist Agricultural Equipment.

**Profile:**
- Third-generation Lindquist to run the company
- Net worth: ~$340 million (mostly company equity)
- Nickname "Big Wayne" earned for his 6'4" frame and booming voice
- Known for: Midwestern work ethic, community involvement, excessive indulgence of his only daughter

**Relationship with Kaylee:**
- Created the fake position "Social Media Coordinator" so she could have a job title
- Paid for her flights, hotels, and festival tickets on the corporate card
- Hoped she'd "grow out of it" and eventually take an interest in the family business
- Never understood her obsession with electronic music

### Kaylee Jo Lindquist

**The Raver.** Founder of the Lindquist line in the Bresme caves.

**Financial situation (pre-Silence):**
- Personal trust fund: $2.3 million
- Access to father's corporate card: Unlimited (within reason)
- Actual job responsibilities: None
- Job title: "Social Media Coordinator" (never posted anything)

**How she ended up in France:**
- Flew first-class from Sioux Falls to Paris
- Rented a car (charged to corporate card)
- Drove to Tours region to stake out Studio Echo
- Camping in a $400 REI tent near the studio
- Waiting for Voltage Cathedral (her favorite band) to arrive

**Items she brought to France that survived:**
- REI camping gear (high quality, lasted decades)
- Several Lindquist Agricultural Equipment promotional items
- Corporate credit card (useless after Day 1)
- Phone with photos of Big Wayne, the family home, and approximately 3,000 selfies (battery died Week 1)

## Year 300 Status

### Within the Lindquist Family

The Lindquist name survives, but almost no one remembers it was a company:

| What Survives | Year 300 Interpretation |
|---------------|------------------------|
| Company logo (on promotional items Kaylee brought) | Family crest / sacred symbol |
| "Lindquist" name | Ancestral identity, possibly mystical |
| Stories of "Big Wayne" | Mythological figure — "The Great Father of the Lindquists" |
| Mention of "South Dakota" | Lost homeland, possibly fictional |
| "Sioux Falls" | Sacred place name, meaning unknown |

### The Logo

Kaylee brought several items with the Lindquist Agricultural Equipment logo:
- A baseball cap
- A keychain
- A pen (ran out of ink Year 3)
- A pocket knife (still functional Year 300)

The logo — a stylized "L" with wheat stalks — has become the Lindquist family symbol. Year 300 family members wear it on pendants, carve it into possessions, and use it as their identifying mark.

**Year 300 interpretation:** An ancient family sigil representing harvest, prosperity, and the Before-Times. Some Lindquists believe it has protective properties.

### "Big Wayne"

Stories of Kaylee's father have drifted considerably:

| Original | Year 300 Version |
|----------|------------------|
| Wealthy businessman | Great chief or king |
| CEO of equipment company | Lord of machines |
| 6'4" tall | Giant (some versions say 8 feet) |
| Gave Kaylee money | Blessed her with ancient wealth |
| South Dakota | A vast kingdom of endless plains |
| "Social Media Coordinator" | Title meaning "She Who Speaks to Many" |

**Sample oral tradition (Year 300):**

> "In the Before, there was a great land called Sou-Dekota, where the plains stretched forever and machines worked the earth. The lord of that land was Big Wayne, whose name meant 'large' because he was a giant among men. He had one daughter, Kaylee Jo, and he loved her so much that he gave her the title 'She Who Speaks to Many' and sent her across the great water to find the sacred music..."

## Quest Hooks

### The Blade of Big Wayne

**Hook:** The Lindquist family's most prized possession — a pocket knife with the company logo — has been lost on a dangerous mission.

**Reality:** It's just a promotional pocket knife. But to the Lindquists, it's irreplaceable.

**Implications:**
- Recovery could earn the Hero major favor with their family
- The knife itself is actually quite good quality (pre-Silence manufacturing)
- Finding it might require retracing a fatal expedition

### The Scripture of the Machines

**Hook:** The faded Lindquist Agricultural Equipment brochure is treated as sacred text, but no one can read the English anymore.

**Reality:** It's a product catalog. "The T-4500 Series delivers superior torque in challenging field conditions."

**Implications:**
- If the Hero can read pre-Silence English, they could "translate" it
- Do they tell the truth (mundane sales copy) or maintain the mystery?
- Some phrases could be interpreted as profound: "Built to last generations"

### The Lost Kingdom

**Hook:** A Lindquist family elder asks the Hero to find evidence of "Sou-Dekota" — proof that the ancestral homeland was real.

**Reality:** South Dakota existed. Sioux Falls was a real city. Big Wayne was a real person.

**Implications:**
- This would require crossing the Atlantic (impossible with current technology)
- But finding ANY American artifacts could partially satisfy the quest
- The truth might be disappointing (it was just a company, not a kingdom)

## The Irony

The Lindquist family believes they descend from nobility — the daughter of a great lord who ruled a kingdom of machines.

In reality:
- Big Wayne was a businessman who spoiled his daughter
- Kaylee had no useful skills and survived by accident
- The "sacred symbol" is a corporate logo
- The "lost kingdom" is a mid-sized city in South Dakota
- The "title" was a fake job so Kaylee could have business cards

But none of that matters. The Lindquists built an identity around these myths. They found meaning in the logo, comfort in the stories, and purpose in the belief that they came from something great.

Whether Big Wayne would be proud or horrified is unknowable. He probably died wondering where his daughter went.
