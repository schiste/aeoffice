## Core Changes Summary

### 1. NO SURFACE SURVIVAL (Years 1-200+ AS)

**Old rule:** Drums, coastal waves, and constant sound could protect humans on the surface.

**New rule:** The Filter is absolute. Surface = death. No exceptions except Resilients (and even they have limits).

**Why:** The old rule created loopholes. If humans could survive with drums, they should have recolonized. The constraint wasn't tight enough.

**Implications:**
- All early survivors are underground (caves, bunkers, metro systems, mines)
- No drum caravans, no surface trade routes
- Population is limited by underground carrying capacity
- Settlements only connect via cave systems or are completely isolated

---

### 2. PRE-SILENCE SURVIVALISM BOOM (2028-2034)

**New addition:** Bake into the timeline an increase in survivalism worldwide, plus sneaky planning by the Sounding Five that makes underground survival more likely.

**The Trend (2028-2034):**
- Global anxiety rising (climate, politics, pandemic memories)
- Survivalist movement grows 300% worldwide
- Bunker construction companies boom
- Metro systems get upgraded "emergency shelters" (lobbied for by shell companies)
- Cave tourism increases → more people know about cave systems
- Prepper communities share knowledge online

**The Sounding Five's Secret Actions:**
- @CribRocker funds "disaster preparedness" NGOs
- @NapTimeNinja consults on "emergency communication bunkers" for ESA
- Shell companies lobby for metro shelter upgrades in major cities
- @UndergroundDad's Unplugged community is just the visible tip

**Result:** When The Silence hits, more people are underground than would have been in 2024. Not by much — but enough.

### 3. CRYSTALS: DISCOVERED EARLY, USABLE LATE

**Old rule:** Crystals existed and were usable from early post-Silence.

**New rule:** Crystals grow progressively across 200 years. They "appear" early but are too small to matter until much later.

**The Crystal Growth Timeline:**

| Year | Crystal State | Human Interaction |
|------|---------------|-------------------|
| 1-50 AS | Microscopic formations at Filter standing-wave nodes | Undetectable |
| 50-100 AS | Pebble-sized, faint resonance | Occasionally found, considered curiosities |
| 100-150 AS | Fist-sized, noticeable hum | Some settlements find them, don't understand |
| 150-200 AS | Meter-scale, strong resonance | **First discoveries** — but too fragile |
| 200-250 AS | Large enough to theoretically sustain life | **Crystal Wars** — attempts to use them mostly fail or destroy them |
| 250-300 AS | Mature Crystals, some viable | **First successful surface settlements** |

**Critical limitation (Years 150-250):**
- Crystals are too small to sustain surface life
- Not resistant enough to amplify sound bands
- Vibration range: only a few centimeters
- Closeness effects noticed → creates conflict
- Usage attempts at best fail, at worst **destroy the Crystal**

**The Three Crystal Wars (Years ~200-250):**
All three are sparked by the discovery of unusually large Crystals. All three end badly:

1. **Austria Conflict (~210 AS)** — Multiple factions fight over a Crystal cluster. War damages/destroys most of them. Survivors develop religious relationship to silence/sound.

2. **Himalayan Dispute (~230 AS)** — Two isolated settlements discover the same Crystal. Conflict in tunnels. Crystal damaged, becomes unstable.

3. **Tarn Siblings War (~245 AS)** — Two brothers, descendants of @TiredInToulouse, fight over a Crystal. Both die stupidly. Sister survives, writes it as epic. See section below.

**After the Wars:** Everyone learns that fighting over Crystals destroys them. Usage attempts are now extremely cautious.

### 4. SURFACE EXPANSION: THREE PATHS

Humanity attempts surface expansion through three methods:

#### Path A: The Sacrifice Cities (Years 100-250 AS)

Some settlements, desperate for space, attempt to expand onto the surface using **massive sound infrastructure** — at enormous human cost.

**The Method:**
- Build sound-insulated corridors from underground
- Sacrifice workers to install speakers, drums, resonators on surface
- Each meter of expansion costs lives
- Growth rate: maybe 10-50 meters per decade
- Human cost: 1-5 deaths per meter of safe zone

**Known Sacrifice Cities:**

| City | Location | Started | Surface Area (Year 300) | Est. Deaths | Notes |
|------|----------|---------|------------------------|-------------|-------|
| **São Paulo Undercity** | Brazil | ~120 AS | ~2 km² | ~3,000 | Metro system + slow expansion |
| **Moscow Deep** | Russia | ~90 AS | ~1.5 km² | ~2,500 | Military bunker origin |
| **Tokyo Below** | Japan | ~110 AS | ~1 km² | ~1,800 | Earthquake shelters repurposed |
| **The London Warren** | UK | ~80 AS | ~0.8 km² | ~1,200 | Tube system + WW2 bunkers |

**Characteristics:**
- Larger populations (5,000-20,000)
- Slow, painful growth
- Strong cultural memory of sacrifice
- Reluctant to risk Crystal experiments (already lost so many)

#### Path B: Crystal Settlements (Years 290+ AS)

The first successful Crystal-based surface settlements emerge very late:

| Year | Location | Crystal | Population | Notes |
|------|----------|---------|------------|-------|
| **290 AS** | **Austria** | Harmonic Cluster | ~800 | First successful! But religious caution slows development. Many prefer staying underground. |
| **299 AS** | **South Africa** | Mponeng Deep | ~400 | Mining community, pragmatic approach |
| **305 AS** | **China** | Sichuan Node | ~600 | Research-focused, systematic |
| **311 AS** | **Loire Valley** | Studio Echo | 1 (Hero) | Game start — the newest |

**The Austria Settlement (First Successful, Year 290 AS):**
- Descendants of Crystal War survivors
- Developed **religious relationship** to silence and sound
- Use the Crystal but with extreme caution
- Many rituals around "not angering the resonance"
- Large chunk of population **refuses to leave underground** — consider surface-dwellers heretics
- Slow to discover Crystal's full capabilities
- Population split: ~500 surface, ~2,000 still underground

#### Path C: The Destroyed Crystals (Especially US)

Not all Crystal discoveries lead to settlements. Some are destroyed.

**The American Tragedy:**

Many survivalist bunkers in the US were established by groups with... ideological problems. When Crystals formed near their territories:

| Year | Location | What Happened |
|------|----------|---------------|
| ~220 AS | Montana | Crystal found. Declared "government mind control." Smashed. |
| ~235 AS | Idaho | Crystal found. "Satanic vibrations." Burned (they tried). |
| ~248 AS | Texas | Crystal found. Two factions fought over it. Destroyed in conflict. |
| ~260 AS | Appalachia | Crystal found. Actually studied! Then leader declared it "mark of the beast." Destroyed. |
| ~275 AS | Arizona | Crystal found. Community split. Half wanted to use it, half destroyed it. Civil war. No survivors. |

**Pattern:**
- White nationalist / extreme religious survivalist groups
- Distrust anything they don't understand
- Would rather destroy potential salvation than risk "contamination"
- Multiple viable Crystals lost to ideology

**The Irony:**
The Sounding Five's preparations inadvertently saved more diverse, educated communities (metro systems, universities, research bunkers). The ideological preppers survived the initial apocalypse but then destroyed their own chances at recovery.

By Year 300, the American survivor population is:
- Smaller than it should be (Crystal destruction)
- Fragmented (ideological conflicts)
- The rational settlements are isolated, outnumbered by hostile neighbors

### 5. THE THREE SIBLINGS (Tarn Crystal War)

**Connection:** @TiredInToulouse is the **brother of @DeciBella (Sarah Chen)**.

**Backstory:**
- Sarah Chen: Acoustics PhD at MIT, French-American family
- Her brother (needs name): Stayed in France, survived in Tarn caves
- @TiredInToulouse was known in the Telegram group for Jean-Claude the rooster
- He survived because the Tarn region has extensive natural cave systems
- His descendants become the Tarn settlement

**The "War" (Year ~245 AS):**

The Tarn settlement discovers an unusually stable Crystal around Year 240 AS. By Year 245, it's large enough to potentially support surface life.

**The Players:**
- **Matthieu Chen-Vidal** — Elder brother, cautious, wants to protect the Crystal
- **Étienne Chen-Vidal** — Younger brother, ambitious, wants to use it immediately
- **Margot Chen-Vidal** — Sister, scholar, documenting everything

**What Actually Happened:**
1. Brothers argue about Crystal usage for months
2. Étienne attempts unauthorized surface expedition
3. Matthieu tries to stop him physically
4. Scuffle near Crystal chamber
5. Étienne falls, hits his head, dies
6. Matthieu, devastated, walks into the Silent surface. Dies.
7. Total casualties: 2 (the brothers) + 3-4 others in related incidents
8. Margot survives

**What Margot Wrote:**
"The Great War of Tarn" — a 200-page epic describing:
- Months of strategic maneuvering
- Factions forming around each brother
- Heroic last stands
- Noble sacrifices
- The tragic fall of House Chen-Vidal

**The Reality:**
Two idiots had a fistfight and both died stupidly. The "factions" were their drinking buddies. The "strategic maneuvering" was passive-aggressive dinner conversations.

**Why It Matters:**
- This becomes one of the "Three Crystal Wars" in historical memory
- Scholars in Year 300 debate it seriously
- It's a perfect example of how history gets mythologized
- Player can potentially discover the truth (Margot's private journals?)

| Category | Count | Status |

| **Destroyed (ideology)** | ~10-15 | Smashed by fearful humans |

| **Undiscovered** | Unknown | Still underground, not found yet |

**The Hero's Crystal (Studio Echo):**
- Class-3 (Large Regional)
- One of the ~8-12 viable Crystals
- **Completely unknown** to other settlements
- Located far from any major population center
- This is why it's unclaimed — nobody knows it exists
